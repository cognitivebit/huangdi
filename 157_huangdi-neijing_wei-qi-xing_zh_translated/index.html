    衛氣行

    1. 黃帝問於歧伯曰：願聞衛氣之行，出入之合，何如？歧伯曰：歲有十二月，日有十二辰，子午為經，卯酉為緯。天周二十八宿，而一面七星，四七二十八星。房昴為緯，虛張為經。是故房至畢為陽，昴至心為陰。陽主晝，陰主夜。故衛氣之行，一日一夜五十周於身，晝日行於陽二十五周，夜行於陰二十五周，周於五藏1。1. 藏 : 原作「歲」。 Yellow Emperor asked Guan Pu, I would like to hear about the movement of the guardian qi, and how its entering and exiting works. Guan Pu replied, There are twelve months in a year and twelve zodiac signs. The terms sui and xia form the meridians, and chen and wu form the equator. The sky is divided into twenty-eight lunar mansions, with one face of seven stars and forty-seven other stars. Kangcheng forms the meridian, and xuan zhang forms the terms. Therefore, when yang arrives at bai, it is yin at xin. Yang governs daytime, and yin governs nighttime. Thus, the movement of guardian qi in one day and one night spans fifty cycles. During the daytime, it travels twenty-five cycles along the yang meridian, and twenty-five cycles along the yin meridian during the nighttime. It completes fifty cycles within the five viscera.

    2. 是故平旦陰盡，陽氣出於目，目張則氣上行於頭，循項下足太陽，循背下至小趾之端。其散者，別於目銳眥，下手太陽，下至手小指之間外側。其散者，別於目銳眥，下足少陽，注小趾次趾之間。以上循手少陽之分側，下至小指之間。別者以上至耳前，合於頷脈，注足陽明以下行，至跗上，入五趾之間。其散者，從耳下下手陽明，入大指之間，入掌中。其至於足也，入足心，出內踝，下行陰分，復合於目，故為一周。 Therefore, the yin energy is complete at dusk and the yang qi emerges from the eyes. When the eyes widen, the qi rises to the head, follows the governor vessel down to the great toe, follows the exceptional vessel down to the tip of the little toe. The dispersing qi separates from the acute radiance of the eyes and goes down the governor vessel to the side of the little finger's joint.

    The dispersing qi separates from the acute radiance of the eyes and goes down the exceptional vessel, following the sinew channel of the small intestine to the interval between the first and second toes. In addition, it ascends to the ear lobe and merges with the chest vessel, following the foot yang linking vessel downward, reaching the ankle, entering the interval between the five toes.

    The dispersing qi starts from the lower part of the ear, going down the yang linking vessel to the interval between the great toe and the palm. The qi arrives at the foot, enters the center of the foot and exits through the inner mound, descends along the yin linking vessel, returning to the eyes, thus forming a circle.

    1. 是故日行一舍，人氣行一周與十分身之八；日行二舍，人氣行三周於身與十分身之六；日行三舍，人氣行於身五周與十分身之四；日行四舍，人氣行於身七周與十分身之二；日行五舍，人氣行於身九周；日行六舍，人氣行於身十周與十分身之八；日行七舍，人氣行於身十二周在身與十分身之六；日行十四舍，人氣二十五周於身有奇分與十分身之二，陽盡於陰，陰受氣矣。其始入于陰，常從足少陰注于腎，腎注于心，心注于肺，肺注于肝，肝注于脾，脾復注于腎為周。是故夜行一舍，人氣行於陰藏一周與十分藏之八，亦如陽行之二十五周，而復合於目。陰陽一日一夜，合有奇分十分身之四，與十分藏之二，是故人之所以臥起之時，有早晏者，奇分不盡故也。 Therefore, in daily life, one step equals one unit of qi within a person's body and eight units away from the body. Two steps equal three units within the body and six units away. Three steps equal five units within the body and four units away. Four steps equal seven units within the body and two units away. Five steps equal nine units within the body, and six steps equal ten units within the body and eight units away. Seven steps equal twelve units within the body and six units away. Fourteen steps equal twenty-five units within the body with a rare division and two units away, yin is complete in yang, and yin receives qi. It starts from yin and proceeds from the foot's yin minor yard to the kidney, the kidney to the heart, the heart to the lung, the lung to the liver, and the liver to the spleen, returning to the kidney to complete a cycle. Therefore, in nighttime, one step equals one unit of qi within the hidden storehouse and eight units away from the hidden storehouse, which is like twenty-five units of yang. They are united at the eyes. Yin and yang occur once day and once night, uniting with a rare division of ten units and two units away. Therefore, when people wake up or lie down, there are early and late times, and this is because the rare division is not complete.

    2. 黃帝曰：衛氣之在於身也，上下往來不以期，候氣而刺之，奈何？伯高曰：分有多少，日有長短，春秋冬夏，各有分理，然後常以平旦為紀，以夜盡為始。是故一日一夜，水下百刻，二十五刻者，半日之度也，常如是毋已，日入而止，隨日之長短，各以為紀而刺之。謹候其時，病可與期，失時反候者，百病不治。故曰：刺實者，刺其來也，刺虛者，刺其去也。此言氣存亡之時，以候虛實而刺之，是故謹候氣之所在而刺之，是謂逢時。在於三陽，必候其氣在於陽而刺之，病在於三陰，必候其氣在陰分而刺之。 Yellow Emperor said: Qi resides in the body and comes and goes without restriction, waiting for the qi before needling. How should one proceed? Master Gao said: There are variations in terms of how much and how long. The seasons of spring, summer, autumn, and winter all have their own classifications. Therefore, one should always use the equinoxes as a reference, using the solstices as starting points. This is why one day equals one night, with 100 intervals in water, and 25 intervals being half a day. One should proceed accordingly without stopping, adjusting according to the length of the day, and using these as references for needling. Be careful and follow the timing precisely; illness can be anticipated by this method. If one misses the time or misjudges it, one hundred diseases cannot be cured. Thus, when needling in reality, one needs to needle according to the arrival of qi or its departure. This is discussing the time for treating life and death through waiting for the presence or absence of qi while needling. One must carefully wait for the location of qi and then needle accordingly, which means acting in accordance with the moment. One should needles at the Yang Meridian by awaiting the arrival of qi at Yang and needle it; if there is illness at the Yin Meridian, one should await the arrival of qi at Yin before needling.

    3. 水下一刻，人氣在太陽；水下二刻，人氣在少陽；水下三刻，人氣在陽明；水下四刻，人氣在陰分。水下五刻，人氣在太陽；水下六刻，人氣在少陽；水下七刻，人氣在陽明；水下八刻，人氣在陰分。水下九刻，人氣在太陽；水下十刻，人氣在少陽；水下十一刻，人氣在陽明；水下十二刻，人氣在陰分。水下十三刻，人氣在太陽；水下十四刻，人氣在少陽；水下十五刻，人氣在陽明；水下十六刻，人氣在陰分。水下十七刻，人氣在太陽；水下十八刻，人氣在少陽；水下十九刻，人氣在陽明；水下二十刻，人氣在陰分。水下二十一刻，人氣在太陽；水下二十二刻，人氣在少陽；水下二十三刻，人氣在陽明；水下二十四刻，人氣在陰分。水下二十五刻，人氣在太陽，此半日之度也。從房至畢一十四舍水下五十刻，日行半度，回行一舍，水下三刻與七分刻之四。大要曰：常以日之加於宿上也，人氣在太陽，是故日行一舍，人氣行三陽行與陰分，常如是無已，天與地同紀，紛紛紛紛，終而復始，一日一夜水下百刻而盡矣。 At any given moment underwater, the Qi is at the Taiyi. At two beats, the Qi is at the Shaoyang; at three beats, the Qi is at the Yangming; at four beats, the Qi is at the Yin division. At five beats, the Qi is at Taiyi again; at six beats, the Qi is at Shaoyang; at seven beats, the Qi is at Yangming; at eight beats, the Qi is at Yin division. At nine beats, the Qi is at Taiyi again; at ten beats, the Qi is at Shaoyang; at eleven beats, the Qi is at Yangming; at twelve beats, the Qi is at Yin division. At thirteen beats, the Qi is at Taiyi again; at fourteen beats, the Qi is at Shaoyang; at fifteen beats, the Qi is at Yangming; at sixteen beats, the Qi is at Yin division. At seventeen beats, the Qi is at Taiyi again; at eighteen beats, the Qi is at Shaoyang; at nineteen beats, the Qi is at Yangming; at twenty beats, the Qi is at Yin division. At twenty-one beats, the Qi is at Taiyi again; at twenty-two beats, the Qi is at Shaoyang; at twenty-three beats, the Qi is at Yangming; at twenty-four beats, the Qi is at Yin division. At twenty-five beats, the Qi is at Taiyi again, and this marks the end of a half day.

    From room to Fan, there are fourteen lodgings for water under fifty beats, with the sun traveling halfway and then returning one beat, water under three and seven divisions of beats. The main point is that by always adding the sun to the stars, the Qi at Taiyi, the day travels one beat, with the Qi moving through Yang and Yin divisions constantly like this without end. Heaven and Earth record it simultaneously, interweaving and intertwining ceaselessly until they return to their beginning, completing a day and a night underwater with 100 beats.
