    陰陽別論

    1: 黃帝問曰：人有四經十二從，何謂。歧伯對曰：四經應四時，十二從應十二月，十二月應十二脈。脈有陰陽，知陽者知陰，知陰者知陽。凡陽有五，五五二十五陽。所謂陰者，真藏也，見則為敗，敗必死也；所謂陽者，胃脘之陽也。別於陽者，知病處也；別於陰者，知死生之期。三陽在頭，三陰在手，所謂一也。別於陽者，知病忌時；別於陰者，知死生之期。謹熟陰陽，無與眾謀。所謂陰陽者，去者為陰，至者為陽；靜者為陰，動者為陽；遲者為陰，數者為陽。凡持真脈之藏脈者，肝至懸絕急，十八日死；心至懸絕，九日死；肺至懸絕，十二日死；腎至懸絕，七日死；脾至懸絕，四日死。 Yellow Emperor asked, What are the four canons and twelve branches? The Duke replied, The four canons correspond to the four seasons, and the twelve branches correspond to the twelve months. The twelve months correspond to the twelve pulses. The pulse has yin and yang; one who knows yang knows yin, and one who knows yin knows yang. All in all, there are five aspects of yang; five times five equals 25 yang. So-called yin refers to the true storehouse; if it is seen, it means failure, and failure must lead to death. The so-called yang refers to the yang of the abdomen and lumbar region. By distinguishing yang, one knows where the disease lies; by distinguishing yin, one knows the time of life and death. Three yangs are in the head, three yins are in the hand; so-called all is one. By distinguishing yang, one knows the taboo times of disease; by distinguishing yin, one knows the time of life and death. Be careful with yin and yang, without rivaling with the masses. The so-called yin and yang: what goes is yin, what arrives is yang; quietness is yin, motion is yang; slowness is yin, speed is yang. Those who practice the true pulse, if the liver is too full or too urgent, they will die in 18 days; if the heart is too full or too urgent, they will die in nine days; if the lung is too full or too urgent, they will die in 12 days; if the kidney is too full or too urgent, they will die in seven days; if the spleen is too full or too urgent, they will die in four days.

    2: 曰：二陽之病，發心脾，有不得隱曲，女子不月；其傳為風消，其傳為息賁者，死不治。曰：三陽為病，發寒熱，下為癰腫，及為痿厥腨㾓；其傳為索澤，其傳為頹疝。曰：一陽發病，少氣善欬善泄；其傳為心掣，其傳為隔。二陽一陰發病，主驚駭背痛，善噫善欠，名曰風厥。二陰一陽發病，善脹心滿善氣。三陽三陰發病，為偏枯痿易，四支不舉。 It is said: Diseases of the yang qi result in heart palpitations, affecting the spleen. If it does not hide and bend (the qi), a woman will not menstruate. Its transmission results in a wind that dissipates, or a disease caused by exhausting vitality, leading to death without cure. It is said: Diseases of three yang manifest as cold and heat, with the symptoms of boils and edema below, and also numbness and paralysis. Its transmission results in the loss of essence, its transmission results in piles. It is said: If one yang manifests a disease, it causes shortness of breath and sighing, and the discharge of fluids; its transmission results in constriction of the heart, and its transmission results in separation. Diseases of both yin and yang occur when shocked and experiencing back pain, causing hiccups and belching, and are known as wind stroke. If both yin and yang manifest diseases, it causes limbs that are weak, stiff, and easily paralyzed.

    3: 鼓一陽曰鉤，鼓一陰曰毛，鼓陽勝急曰絃，鼓陽至而絕曰石，陰陽相過曰溜。陰爭於內，陽擾於外，魄汗未藏，四逆而起，起則熏肺，使人喘鳴。陰之所生，和本曰和。是故剛與剛，陽氣破散，陰氣乃消亡。淖則剛柔不和，經氣乃絕。死陰之屬，不過三日而死；生陽之屬，不過四日而死。所謂生陽死陰者，肝之心，謂之生陽。心之肺，謂之死陰。肺之腎，謂之重陰。腎之脾，謂之辟陰，死不治。結陽者，腫四支。結陰者便血一升，再結二升，三結三升。陰陽結斜，多陰少陽曰石水，少腹腫。二陽結謂之消，三陽結謂之隔，三陰結謂之水，一陰一陽結謂之喉痺。陰搏陽別謂之有子。陰陽虛腸辟死。陽加於陰謂之汗。陰虛陽搏謂之崩。三陰俱搏，二十日夜半死。二陰俱搏，十三日夕時死。一陰俱搏，十日死。三陽俱搏且鼓，三日死。三陰三陽俱搏，心腹滿。發盡不得隱曲，五日死。二陽俱搏，其病溫，死不治，不過十日死。 The Yang and Yin of the drum are said to be hooks, and the Yang and Yin of the whip are said to be strings. When Yang surpasses Yin in haste, it is called stone; when Yin surpasses Yang in crossing over, it is called stream. Inside Yin fights, outside Yang disturbs. The soul sweat has not yet settled, and the four reversals arise. If they arise, they will scorch the lungs, causing the person to pant heavily. What is born from Yin is said to be harmony. Therefore, firmness and softness are compatible; the vital energy is Yang and the Yin energy will disappear. If it becomes murky, firmness and softness will not be compatible, and the channel energy will cease. Those who die from excess Yin do not survive more than three days; those who die from excess Yang do not survive more than four days. What is called dying of Yang and living on Yin is the heart of the liver. The lungs are said to be the dead Yin of the heart. The kidneys are said to be heavy Yin. The spleen is said to be dispersing Yin, and if it dies it cannot be saved. Swelling resulting from excess Yang is called results. Excess Yin is manifested by a rise in clear fluid; once it rises twice, it becomes three folds. Yang and Yin are unevenly distributed, and this is called jade water, with more Yin and less Yang. Two Yang coming together are said to dissipate. Three Yang coming together are said to be isolated. Three Yin coming together are said to be water. Two Yins coming together are said to choke. One Yin and one Yang coming together are said to paralyze the throat. Vacuity of Yin and excess Yang is called having a son. The vacuity of Yin and Yang will cause death of the intestines. Excess Yang accompanied by pounding is called sweat. Vacuity of Yang and Yin pounding is called collapse. All three Yins together, within twenty days and half a day, will die. All two Yins together, within thirteen days at dusk, will die. All one Yin together, within ten days, will die. Excess Yangs coming together and drumming is called death in three days. Excess Yang and excess Yin together, with the abdomen full, unable to hide the manifestation, will die within five days. Those who die from excessive Yin cannot be saved; this disease will not last more than ten days.
