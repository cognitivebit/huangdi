    四時氣

    1. 黃帝問于歧伯曰：夫四時之氣，各不同形，百病之起，皆有所生，灸刺之道，何者為定？歧伯答曰：四時之氣，各有所在，灸刺之道，得氣穴為定。故春取經、血脈、分肉之間，甚者，深刺之，間者，淺刺之；夏取盛經孫絡，取分間絕皮膚；秋取經俞。邪在府，取之合；冬取井滎，必深以留之。 The Yellow Emperor asked Guan Pu, The Qi of the four seasons has different forms, and all diseases arise from some source. What is the method of acupuncture and moxibustion for defining this? Guan Pu replied, The Qi of the four seasons have their respective locations. The method of acupuncture and moxibustion is to find the Qi points. Therefore, in spring, you should take the meridians, blood vessels, and muscles between, with the former being deep and the latter being shallow; in summer, you should take the full meridian and sinews, and separate the skin and subcutaneous tissue; in autumn, you should take the meridian channels. In evil cases, you should take from where they are located; in winter, you should take from the wells and ensure depth.

    2. 溫瘧汗不出，為五十九痏，風㽷膚脹，為五十七痏。取皮膚之血者，盡取之。飧泄補三陰之上，補陰陵泉，皆久留之，熱行乃止。 Sweating is not forthcoming, with 59 degrees of Yin; the complexion is sallow and swollen, at 57 degrees of Yin. When bleeding is taken from the skin, it should all be taken. Supplementing the Stomach, supplementing the Kidney Yuan, and supplementing the Kidney Spring, all can last for a long time, and the fever will subside.

    3. 轉筋於陽，治其陽；轉筋於陰，治其陰。皆卒刺之。徒㽷先取環谷下三寸，以鈹鍼鍼之，已刺而筩之，而內之，入而復之，以盡其㽷，必堅。來緩則煩悗，來急則安靜，間日一刺之，㽷盡乃止。飲閉藥，方刺之時徒飲之，方飲無食，方食無飲，無食他食，百三十五日。 Treat the tendons of the Yang and treat the Yang; treat the tendons of the Yin and treat the Yin. All are ultimately punctured. The patient must be treated with acupuncture, and the needle will be inserted and withdrawn to ensure thoroughness. If the situation worsens, it becomes uncomfortable; if it becomes urgent, it calms down. After one treatment, the patient's condition improves, and they will recover after 30-35 days. The patient is to take medicine only when being punctured, or only drink water, or only eat food. There should be no other food besides this prescribed diet

    4. 著痺不去，久寒不已，卒取其三里。骨為幹。腸中不便，取三里，盛寫之，虛補之。癘風者，素刺其腫上。已刺，以銳鍼鍼其處，按出其惡氣，腫盡乃止。常食方食，無食他食。 The ailment does not go away, and the cold lasts for a long time. It suddenly took three li. Bones are the axis. The intestines have discomfort, taking three li, summarizing it, and applying the vacuum. For those with wind abscesses, gently pierce the swollen area. After puncturing, use a sharp lancet to prick the location, press out the evil qi, and the swelling will cease when it is gone. Eat only regular food and do not eat other foods.

    5. 腹中常鳴，氣上衝胸，喘不能久立。邪在大腸，刺肓之原，巨虛上廉、三里。小腹控睪，引腰脊，上衝心。邪在小腸者，連睪系，屬于脊，貫肝肺，絡心系。氣盛則厥逆，上衝腸胃，燻肝，散于肓，結于臍，故取之肓原以散之，刺太陰以予之，取厥陰以下之，取巨虛下廉以去之，按其所過之經以調之。 The text is in classical Chinese, but it appears to describe the symptoms and treatment of an abdominal condition. Here's a translation:

    Constantly feeling pain in the abdomen, gas builds up and presses on the chest, making it difficult to breathe and stand for long. The disease is located in the large intestine, by piercing the intestines at the original site of the 'great emptiness' and three li points. For a small intestinal issue, grip the navel and pull towards the spine, pressing upwards towards the heart. If the disease is in the small intestine, tie the navel and connect it to the spine, running through the liver and lungs, connecting to the heart. When qi is abundant, it can cause dysfunction of the stomach and intestines, causing the liver to overheat and dispersing throughout the intestines, clumping in the gallbladder, hence piercing at the intestinal site to disperse it, piercing the Conception Vessel to provide it, taking from the Bubble Vessel to expel it, taking from the Great Vacuity to eliminate it, and pressing on the points along its meridian to regulate it.

    1. 善嘔，嘔有苦，長太息，心中憺憺，恐人將捕之；邪在膽，逆在胃，膽液泄，則口苦，胃氣逆，則嘔苦，故曰嘔膽。取三里以下。胃氣逆，則刺少陽血絡，以閉膽逆，卻調其虛實，以去其邪。 Good hiccup, having hiccups with pain, prolonged sighing, the mind being worried, fearing that people will capture it; evil in the gallbladder, reverse in the stomach, gallbladder fluid leakage causes the mouth to be bitter, stomach qi reversal causes hiccup bitterness, so it is called hiccup gallbladder. Take three ri below. Stomach qi reversal, then insert a needle into the small yang blood vessel to close gallbladder reversal, adjust its emptiness, and remove its evil.

    2. 飲食不下，膈塞不通，邪在胃脘，在上脘，則刺抑而下之，在下脘，則散而去之。小腹痛腫，不得小便，邪在三焦，約取之太陽大絡，視其絡脈與厥陰小絡結而血者，腫上及胃脘，取三里。 Unable to eat or drink, the qi is blocked and not flowing smoothly. The pathogenic factor is in the stomach and lower abdomen. If it's in the upper abdomen, use acupuncture to tonify and bring downward. If it's in the lower abdomen, use herbal medicine to disperse and expel. Small intestinal pain and swelling, unable to urinate, pathogenic factor is in the triple burner. Take a small amount of qi from the conception vessel and blend with the small yin vessel. If there is bleeding, the swelling will spread and affect the stomach region, take three catties of this mixture.

    3. 睹其色，察其以，知其散復者，視其目色，以知病之存亡也。一其形，聽其動靜者，持氣口人迎以視其脈，堅且盛且滑者，病日進，脈軟者，病將下，諸經實者，病三日已。氣口候陰，人迎候陽也。 By examining its color, investigating its cause, and knowing whether it is improving or deteriorating, one can discern the presence or absence of illness through observing the patient's eye color. By listening to their speech and noting their movement and stillness, one can gauge the state of the qi by observing their pulse. If the pulse is strong, abundant, and smooth, the patient is improving; if it is soft, the illness may be about to decline, and if the pulse follows the patterns described in the classics, then the illness has lasted for three days. The qi and pulse correspond to yin and yang respectively.
