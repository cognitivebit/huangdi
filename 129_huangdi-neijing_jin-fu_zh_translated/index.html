    禁服

    1: 雷公問於黃帝曰：細子得受業，通于九鍼六十篇，旦暮勤服之，近者編絕，久者簡垢，然尚諷誦弗置，未盡解於意矣。「外揣」言渾束為一，未知所謂也。夫大則無外，小則無內，大小無極，高下無度，束之奈何？士之才力，或有厚薄，智慮褊淺，不能博大深奧，自強於學若細子。細子恐其散於後世，絕於子孫，敢問約之奈何？黃帝曰：善乎哉問也。此先師之所禁，坐私傳之也，割臂歃血之盟也，子若欲得之，何不齋乎。 Lei Gong asked the Huang Di, saying: Fu Zi can be received and practiced. It runs through the nine needles and sixty chapters, morning and evening practicing it, close ones weave it up, distant ones simplify the dirt, but it is still not complete in recitation, and it does not fully resolve its meaning. The phrase external weaving refers to a single bundle, and I do not know what this means. A great principle has no outside, and small things have no inside; large and small have no extremes, high and low have no measure, how will they be bound? The abilities of a gentleman may be shallow or deep, wise or simple-minded, unable to be profound and mysterious, and must strive in their studies like Fu Zi. Fu Zi is afraid that it will scatter in later generations and disappear from descendants, daring to ask about the pact. The Huang Di said: How wonderful this question is. This first teacher's taboo has been passed down privately, a bloody covenant, and if one wants to obtain it, why not fast?

    2: 雷公再拜而起曰：請聞命於是也，乃齋宿三日而請曰：敢問今日正陽，細子願以受盟。黃帝乃與俱入齋室，割臂歃血，黃帝親祝曰：今日正陽，歃血傳方，有敢背此言者，反受其殃。雷公再拜曰：細子受之。黃帝乃左握其手，右授之書曰：慎之慎之，吾為子言之，凡刺之理，經脈為始，營其所行，知其度量，內刺五藏，外刺六府，審察衛氣，為百病母，調其虛實，虛實乃止，寫其血絡，血盡不殆矣。 Lei Gong knelt down and rose again, saying: I have heard your command; I fasted for three days and asked: 'Will you grant me an audience today when the sun is in its zenith? I would like to receive a pledge from Xiaozi.' The Yellow Emperor entered the room with him, severed his arm and shed blood. The Yellow Emperor said to him, 'Today, at the zenith of the sun, blood is passed on; whoever dares to defy this command shall suffer its consequences. Xiaozi receives it.' The Yellow Emperor then grasped his hand with his left hand and handed him a book, saying: 'Take heed, take heed! What I say to you is about needling. The channels are the starting point of all needles; regulate their flow, know their depths, needle the five organs internally and the six divisions externally, judge the vital energy, treat a hundred diseases, adjust their emptiness and fullness, and when emptiness and fullness are resolved, blood vessels will be completed, and there will be no further harm.'

    3: 雷公曰：此皆細子之所以通，未知其所約也。黃帝曰：夫約方者，猶約囊也，囊滿而弗約，則輸泄，方成弗約，則神與弗俱。雷公曰：願為下材者，勿滿而約之。黃帝曰：未滿而知約之以為工，不可以為天下師。 Master Lái said: All these are the way the Fine Son makes things work, without knowing how they are interconnected. The Yellow Emperor said: What is called 'interconnection' is like storing something in a container. If the container is full and does not close properly, it will leak; when it is properly closed, it will not leak, but if the seal is not right, then spirit will not join together. Master Lái said: For common people, I wish that you would not fill them to the point of interconnection. The Yellow Emperor said: If they are not filled and their interconnection is understood as a craft, it cannot serve as a model for all under heaven.

    4: 雷公曰：願聞為工。黃帝曰：寸口主中，人迎主外，兩者相應，俱往俱來，若引繩大小齊等。春夏人迎微大，秋冬寸口微大，如是者，名曰平人。 Lei Gong said: I wish to hear about the artisan. Emperor Huang said: The master is in charge of the inside, and the servant is in charge of the outside. Both respond to each other, going and coming as needed, with the cord neither too long nor too short. In spring and summer, the servants serve modestly, while in autumn and winter, the master serves modestly, so this is called the 'ordinary person'.

    5: 人迎大一倍於寸口，病在足少陽，一倍而躁，在手少陽。人迎二倍，病在足太陽，二倍而躁，病在手太陽。人迎三倍，病在足陽明，三倍而躁，病在手陽明。盛則為熱，虛則為寒，緊則為痛痺，代則乍甚乍間。盛則寫之，虛則補之，緊痛則取之分肉，代則取血絡，且飲藥，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之，名曰經刺。人迎四倍者，且大且數，名曰溢陽，溢陽為外格，死不治。必審按其本末，察其寒熱，以驗其藏府之病。 A person with a syndrome twice as strong as inch mouth has a disease in the foot, deficiency of Yang. Twice as severe, there is a condition in the hand, deficiency of Yang. A person with the syndrome four times as strong is called overflowing Yang, leading to external pathogenic factors and death that cannot be cured. One must carefully examine its beginning and end, discern whether it is cold or heat, and diagnose its visceral disease.

    6: 寸口大於人迎一倍，病在足厥陰，一倍而躁，在手心主。寸口二倍，病在足少陰，二倍而躁，在手少陰。寸口三倍，病在足太陰，三倍而躁，在手太陰。盛則脹滿，寒中，食不化，虛則熱中、出糜、少氣、溺色變，緊則痛痺，代則乍痛乍止。盛則寫之，虛則補之，緊則先刺而後灸之，代則取血絡，而後調之，陷下則徒灸之，陷下者，脈血結於中，中有著血，血寒，故宜灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。寸口四倍者，名曰內關，內關者，且大且數，死不治。必審察其本末之寒溫，以驗其藏府之病。 A point called Juice Mouth (Cun Kou) is located 1 cun above the tip of the foot, on the outside edge. When it is larger than the little toe and the big toe is embraced one time, the illness lies in the lower leg, in the back shin, one time agitated, in the heart of the palm. When Cun Kou is two times larger, the illness lies in the lower leg, in the small yin, two times agitated, in the heart of the little finger. When Cun Kou is three times larger, the illness lies in the lower leg, in the great yin, three times agitated, in the heart of the ring finger. If it is swollen, there will be fullness, and in the cold phase, food does not transform. If it is empty, there will be heat, ulceration, deficiency of essence, change of color, tight, and if it changes, there will be pain and numbness. If it is replaced, there will be sudden pain and then cessation. In the swollen state, write it; in the deficient state, supplement it, in the tight state, first puncture and then moxa; in the replaced state, take blood vessels and then regulate it, if sinking down, then moxa; those who sink down have stagnant blood in the middle, with blood congestion in the middle, cold blood, hence moxa is appropriate. Cun Kou four times larger is called Inner Gate (Nei Guan), and the Inner Gate is both large and complicated, without cure if dying. Must carefully examine its proximal and distal coldness and heat, and verify its storehouse palace's illness.

    7: 通其營輸，乃可傳於大數。大數曰：盛則徒寫之，虛則徒補之，緊則灸刺，且飲藥，陷下則徒灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。所謂經治者，飲藥，亦曰灸刺，脈急則引，脈大以弱，則欲安靜，用力無勞也。 To interpret the camp's strategy, it can be passed down in great numbers. Great numbers say: when it is full, it is only written; when it is empty, it is only supplemented; tighten and use acupuncture and moxibustion, and take medicine; if it falls, only use acupuncture; not full, not empty, take the classic method. The so-called classic treatment also uses acupuncture and moxibustion, and when the pulse is urgent, pull it; when the pulse is large but weak, want to calm down, using force does not require much effort.
