    八正神明論

    1: 黃帝問曰：用鍼之服，必有法則焉，今何法何則。歧伯對曰：法天則地，合以天光。帝曰：願卒聞之。歧伯曰：凡刺之法，必候日月星辰四時八正之氣，氣定，乃刺之。是故天溫日明，則人血淖液，而衛氣浮，故血易寫，氣易行；天寒日陰，則人血凝泣，而衛氣沈。月始生，則血氣始精，衛氣始行；月郭滿，則血氣實，肌肉堅；月郭空，則肌肉減，經絡虛，衛氣去，形獨居。是以因天時而調血氣也。是以天寒無刺，天溫無疑。月生無寫，月滿無補，月郭空無治，是謂得時而調之。因天之序，盛虛之時，移光定位，正立而待之。故日月生而寫，是謂藏虛；月滿而補，血氣揚溢，絡有留血，命曰重實；月郭空而治，是謂亂經。陰陽相錯，真邪不別，沈以留止，外虛內亂，淫邪乃起。 Emperor Yellow asked: There are rules for acupuncture treatment. What are the rules today? Master Qi replied: The rules are from heaven and earth, and combine the light of heaven. Emperor Yellow said: I wish to hear more about this. Master Qi said: All acupuncture techniques must wait for the gas of the sun, moon, stars, and four seasons. When the gas is stable, it is time for acupuncture. That's why when the weather is warm and sunny, human blood and fluids are liquid, and the defensive gas rises. It is easier to write blood and move gas; when it is cold and rainy, human blood thickens and hardens, and the defensive gas sinks. When the moon begins to grow, the blood and gas become more refined and the defensive gas starts to circulate; when the moon is full, the blood is rich, and the muscles are strong; when the moon wanes, the muscles shrink, the channels and collaterals are empty, the defensive gas disappears, and the body stands alone. Therefore, adjusting the blood and gas according to the time of day is necessary. That's why it's better not to acupuncture in cold weather, and there's no doubt about it in warm weather. Don't write when the moon is growing, don't supplement when the moon is full, and don't treat when the moon is waning. This is called adjusting according to the time. According to the order of heaven, when it is empty, treat it; when it is full, stand firmly and wait. That's why you write when the moon grows and supplement when the moon is full, hiding the emptiness; when the moon wanes, treating is called restoring the channels. In terms of yin and yang, confusion, true and false are not distinguished. Sinking to stop it leads to external emptiness and internal chaos, and disease begins with evil.

    2: 帝曰：星辰八正何候。歧伯曰；星辰者，所以制日月之行也。八正者，所以候八風之虛邪，以時至者也。四時者，所以分春秋冬夏之氣所在，以時調之也，八正之虛邪，而避之勿犯也。以身之虛，而逢天之虛，兩虛相感，其氣至骨，入則傷五藏，工候救之，弗能傷也，故曰：天忌不可不知也。 The emperor said: What is the sign of the eight nodes? The Grand Astrologer replied, The stars and constellations regulate the movement of the sun and moon. The eight nodes are used to mark the changes in the eight winds, which occur at certain times. The four seasons divide the energy of spring, autumn, winter, and summer, allowing us to adjust accordingly. By avoiding the effects of the eight nodes, we can avoid misfortune. When our physical bodies are aligned with the heavens, we must be careful not to offend heaven's virtue, as it can cause harm to the five organs. A skilled astrologer knows how to avoid such harm and says, 'Heaven's warnings should never be ignored.'

    3: 帝曰：善。其法星辰者，余聞之矣，願聞法往古者。歧伯曰；法往古者，先知鍼經也。驗於來今者，先知日之寒溫，月之虛盛，以候氣之浮沈，而調之於身，觀其立有驗也。觀其冥冥者，言形氣榮衛之不形於外，而工獨知之，以日之寒溫，月之虛盛，四時氣之浮沈，參伍相合而調之，工常先見之，然而不形於外，故曰觀於冥冥焉。通於無窮者，可以傳於後世也，是故工之所以異也，然而不形見於外，故俱不能見也。視之無形，嘗之無味，故謂冥冥，若神髣𨱰。虛邪者，八正之虛邪氣也。正邪者，身形若用力，汗出，腠理開，逢虛風，其中人也微，故莫知其情，莫見其形。上工救其萌牙，必先見三部九候之氣，盡調不敗而救之，故曰上工。下工救其已成，救其已敗。救其已成者，言不知三部九候之相失，因病而敗之也，知其所在者，知診三部九候之病脈處而治之。故曰守其門戶焉，莫知其情而見邪形也。 The emperor said: Good. The law of the stars and constellations, I have heard of. I wish to hear the ancient law of medicine. Zhi Bo said: Ancient medical law refers to the needle in the classics. Testing from past to present, the ancients observed the temperature and warmth of the sun and moon, the emptiness and fullness of the moon, and adjusted according to the qi's rising and sinking, observing its efficacy. They observed the mysterious and the formless. The shape and qi of honor and defense do not manifest externally, but they know how to manipulate it with the temperature and warmth of the sun and moon, the emptiness and fullness of the four seasons' qi, harmonizing the assistant and partner in adjusting it, and the practitioners always see it first, yet it does not manifest externally, thus they say observing the mysterious. It penetrates the endless, and can be passed down to future generations. This is why the practice is unique, but it remains formless and unseen externally, so neither can it be seen. Regarding it as formless, it has no taste, thus they call it the mysterious and the formless, like divine Yin and Yang. The empty and perverse are the eight regular qi. The righteous and perverse refer to when the body's shape and qi are exhausted, sweating, and the meridians opening, encountering empty winds, with people inside being microscopic, thus unknown of their situation and unable to see their form. Upper practitioners save the seedlings by first seeing the qi of the three departments and nine indicators, fully adjusting without defeat, then saving them. Lower practitioners save the already formed and the already defeated. Saving the already formed refers to not knowing the three departments and nine indicators' changes due to disease and causing it to deteriorate, knowing its location, diagnosing the three departments and nine indicators' illnesses and treating it. Thus they guard their doorways, unknown of the situation yet seeing the manifestation of evil.

    4: 帝曰：余聞補寫，未得其意。歧伯曰：寫必用方，方者，以氣方盛也，以月方滿也，以日方溫也，以身方定也，以息方吸而內鍼，乃復候其方吸而轉鍼，乃復候其方呼而徐引鍼，故曰寫必用方，其氣而行焉。補必用員，員者，行也，行者，移也，刺必中其榮，復以吸排鍼也。故員與方，非鍼也。故養神者，必知形之肥瘦，榮衛血氣之盛衰。血氣者，人之神，不可不謹養。 The Emperor said: I have heard of supplementation and inscription, but I do not understand its meaning. Touba replied: Inscription must use method, method means using qi to regulate the body's method of inhalation, and then turning the needle; using the breath to gradually pull the needle, thus it is said that inscription must use method, and the energy will flow accordingly. Supplementation must use assistant, assistant means movement, movement means mobility. If the needle penetrates the honor, it is then replaced with a suction needle. Therefore, assistant and method are not needles. Therefore, nurturing the spirit requires knowing the body's state of being thin or fat, and whether one's honor guard and blood energy are flourishing or diminished. Blood energy is what makes people spiritual, and it must be carefully nurtured.

    5: 帝曰：妙乎哉論也。合人形於陰陽四時，虛實之應，冥冥之期，其非夫子孰能通之。然夫子數言形與神，何謂形，何謂神，願卒聞之。歧伯曰：請言形，形乎形，目冥冥，問其所病，索之於經，慧然在前，按之不得，不知其情，故曰形。帝曰：何謂神。歧伯曰：請言神，神乎神，耳不聞，目明心開，而志先，慧然獨悟，口弗能言，俱視獨見，適若昏，昭然獨明，若風吹雲，故曰神。三部九候為之原，九鍼之論，不必存也。 The Emperor said: This is a wonderful discussion! The joining of yin and yang at the four seasons, the correspondence of vacuity and substance, the mysterious period, none but Confucius could explain it. However, Confucius has often spoken of form and spirit; what do you mean by form, and what do you mean by spirit? I hope to hear the explanation. Zigong said: Please explain form. Form is that which is visible as form, with eyes open one sees the mysteries, one asks about its illness, seeks it in the classics, is enlightened ahead of time, but cannot touch it and does not know its nature, so it is called form. The Emperor said: What do you mean by spirit? Zigong said: Please explain spirit. Spirit is that which is audible as spirit, with ears open one hears the mysteries, the mind is clear and open, yet the intention leads, is enlightened alone and understands independently, the mouth cannot speak it, the two [eyes and ears] see it together, but separately discern it, like the darkness of evening, manifestly bright, and like the wind blowing clouds, so it is called spirit. The Three Books and Nine Stems are its original sources; the Nine Needles' discussion does not need to exist.
