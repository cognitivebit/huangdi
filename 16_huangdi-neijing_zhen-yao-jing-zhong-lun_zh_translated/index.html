    診要經終論

    1. 黃帝問曰：診要何如。歧伯對曰：正月二月，天氣始方，地氣始發，人氣在肝。三月四月，天氣正方，地氣定發，人氣在脾。五月六月，天氣盛，地氣高，人氣在頭。七月八月，陰氣始殺，人氣在肺。九月十月，陰氣始冰，地氣始閉，人氣在心。十一月十二月，冰復，地氣合，人氣在腎。 Emperor Yellow asked, How should one diagnose? Huan Pu answered, In the second month of the first month, when the heavenly energy begins to manifest and the earthly energy emerges, the human energy is in the liver. In the third month and fourth month, when the heavenly energy is complete and the earthly energy is firmly established, the human energy is in the spleen. In the fifth month and sixth month, when the heavenly energy is abundant and the earthly energy is high, the human energy is in the head. In the seventh month and eighth month, as yin energy begins to kill, the human energy is in the lungs. In the ninth month and tenth month, as yin energy begins to freeze and the earthly energy begins to close, the human energy is in the heart. In the eleventh month and twelfth month, as the freezing repeats itself and the earthly energy merges, the human energy is in the kidneys.

    2. 故春刺散俞，及與分理，血出而止，甚者傳氣，閒者環也。夏刺絡俞，見血而止，盡氣閉環，痛病必下。秋刺皮膚，循理，上下同法，神變而止。冬刺俞竅於分理，甚者直下，閒者散下。春夏秋冬，各有所刺，法其所在。 According to the seasons, there are different acupuncture methods: In spring, prick the Sanjiao, and when blood comes out, it stops. For the serious cases, pass Qi, and for the less serious, circle around. In summer, prick the Sinew Channels, see the blood and stop, exhaust all the Qi, close the circle, and painful diseases must go away. In autumn, prick the skin and follow the reason, both up and down follow the same method, spirit stops. In winter, prick the Sanjiao at the intersection of the division reason, for the serious cases, it directly goes away, and for the idle ones, it disperses away. In spring, summer, autumn, and winter, each has a place to prick, and treat according to its location.

    3. 春刺夏分，脈亂氣微，入淫骨髓，病不能愈，令人不嗜食，又且少氣。春刺秋分，筋攣，逆氣環，為欬嗽，病不愈，令人時驚，又且哭。春刺冬分，邪氣著藏，令人脹，病不愈，又且欲言語。夏刺春分，病不愈，令人解墯。夏刺秋分，病不愈，令人心中欲無言，惕惕如人將補之。夏刺冬分，病不愈，令人少氣，時欲怒。秋刺春分，病不已，令人惕然欲有所為，起而忘之。秋刺夏分，病不已，令人益嗜臥，又且善㝱。秋刺冬分，病不已，令人洒洒時寒。冬刺春分，病不已，令人欲臥不能眠，眠而有見。冬刺夏分，病不愈，氣上，發為諸痺。冬刺秋分，病不已，令人善渴。 Spring acupuncture in the season of summer, the pulse is chaotic and the Qi is weak. It invades the bone marrow, causing a disease that cannot be cured, making people dislike eating and also being short of breath. Spring acupuncture in the season of autumn, the tendons twist, and the reversed Qi revolves, causing coughing and asthma, the disease does not subside, making people sometimes shocked and also weeping. Spring acupuncture in the season of winter, the evil Qi lurks and makes people bloated, the disease does not subside, also making people want to speak but unable to.

    Summer acupuncture in the season of spring, the disease does not subside, making people feel as though they are about to fall down. Summer acupuncture in the season of autumn, the disease does not subside, making people have a desire to lie down and also enjoy it. Summer acupuncture in the season of winter, the disease does not subside, making people short of breath and sometimes want to be angry.

    Autumn acupuncture in the season of spring, the disease is not cured, making people feel surprised and desire to do something but forget it. Autumn acupuncture in the season of summer, the disease is not cured, making people like lying down more, also enjoying it. Autumn acupuncture in the season of winter, the disease does not subside, making people thirsty.

    Winter acupuncture in the season of spring, the disease does not subside, making people want to lie down but unable to sleep, sleeping and seeing things. Winter acupuncture in the season of summer, the disease is not cured, Qi rises and causes various pains. Winter acupuncture in the season of autumn, the disease does not subside, making people desire to drink water.

    1. 凡刺胸腹者，必避五藏。中心者，環死；中脾者，五日死；中腎者，七日死；中肺者，五日死；中鬲者，皆為傷中，其病雖愈，不過一歲必死。刺避五藏者，知逆從也。所謂從者，鬲與脾腎之處，不知者反之。刺胸腹者，必以布憿著之，乃從單布上刺，刺之不愈，復刺。刺鍼必肅，刺腫搖鍼，經刺勿搖，此刺之道也。 Those who have been pierced in the chest or abdomen will definitely avoid the five organs. Those in the center will be surrounded by death, those in the spleen will die within five days, those in the kidneys will die within seven days, those in the lungs will die within five days, and those in the gallbladder will all be wounded in the chest; even if their illness recovers, they must die within a year. Knowing how to follow the principle when piercing the five organs, one knows whether it is reversed or not. The term follow refers to the positions of the gallbladder, spleen, and kidneys, and if one does not know, one will reverse them. When piercing the chest or abdomen, one must use a cloth with needles sewn in; one should not shake the needle when performing acupuncture, and this is the way of piercing.

    2. 帝曰：願聞十二經脈之終，奈何。歧伯曰：太陽之脈，其終也戴眼反折瘈瘲，其色白，絕汗乃出，出則死矣。少陽終者，耳聾百節皆縱，目𦊷絕系，絕系一日半死，其死也，色先青白，乃死矣。陽明終者，口目動作，善驚妄言，色黃，其上下經盛，不仁，則終矣。少陰終者，面黑齒長而垢，腹脹閉，上下不通而終矣。太陰終者，腹脹閉不得息，善噫善嘔，嘔則逆，逆則面赤，不逆則上下不通，不通則面黑皮毛焦而終矣。厥陰終者，中熱嗌乾，善溺心煩，甚則舌卷卵上縮而終矣。此十二經之所敗也。 The emperor said, I hope to hear about the ending of the twelve meridians. What happens when they end? Duke Pu said: The ending of the Great Sun meridian is characterized by dizziness and vision problems, with white coloration, sweating that leads to death. The ending of the Lesser Yin meridian is marked by deafness and loss of sensation in all joints, blindness followed by coma, and death from greenish-white complexion. The ending of the Yang Ming meridian is characterized by movement of the mouth and eyes, being easily frightened and speaking nonsense, yellow coloration, and a full flow of Qi in both upper and lower meridians, but if it lacks compassion, death will follow. The ending of the Shao Yin meridian is marked by dark complexion and long teeth, with bloating of the abdomen, poor circulation in the upper and lower meridians leading to death. The ending of the Tai Yin meridian is characterized by bloating of the abdomen that does not allow breathing, being good at vomiting and hiccupping, and if there is hiccoughing, the face will turn red, if there isn't, poor circulation in the upper and lower meridians leads to dark complexion and dry skin, and ultimately death. The ending of the Ren Yin meridian is marked by an internal heat that dries up the mouth and tongue, with a tendency to drown and be bothered by the heart, which can lead to the tongue rolling up towards the yolk and death. This is what happens when the twelve meridians fail.
