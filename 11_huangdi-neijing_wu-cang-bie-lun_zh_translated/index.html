    五藏別論

    1. 黃帝問曰：余聞方士，或以腦髓為藏，或以腸胃為藏，或以為府，敢問更相反皆自謂是不知其道，願聞其說。歧伯對曰：腦髓骨脈膽女子胞，此六者地氣之所生也，皆藏於陰而象於地，故藏而不寫，名曰奇恆之府。夫胃大腸小腸三焦膀胱，此五者，天氣之所生也，其氣象天故寫而不藏，此受五藏濁氣，名曰傳化之府，此不能久留輸寫者也。魄門亦為五藏，使水穀不得久藏。所謂五藏者，藏精氣而不寫也，故滿而不能實。六府者，傳化物而不藏，故實而不能滿也。所以然者，水穀入口，則胃實而腸虛；食下，則腸實而胃虛。故曰：實而不滿，滿而不實也。 Yellow Emperor asked, I have heard that some scholars say that the essence is stored in the brain and spinal cord or in the intestines. Some say it is stored in a 'palace,' but they all claim that those who do not know its path are daring to express their opinions. I would like to hear your explanation. The Duke answered, The brain, spinal cord, bones, marrow, and gallbladder of women; these six are generated by earth energy and are stored in the yin while manifesting as earthly things. Therefore, they are hidden but not written, and are called the Palace of the Strange [Qi Héng]. The large intestine, small intestine, three intestines, bladder; these five are generated by celestial energy and manifest as heavenly things. As a result, their energies are expressed but not hidden. These five organs receive the impure energy of the six organs, which is called the Palace of Transformation [Chuán Hzá]. This palace cannot be preserved and must be written, indicating that these organs have impermanence. The Duke also said that the Gate of Life is one of the five organs, controlling so that water does not linger in the body. The Five Organs are hidden while storing the essence-qi, so they cannot be full. The Six Palaces are where the transformative energies are stored without being hidden, so they can be filled but not made full. This is because when food enters the mouth, the stomach becomes filled and the intestines become empty; when food descends, the intestines become filled and the stomach becomes empty. Therefore, it is said that filled but not full, full but not real.

    2. 帝曰：氣口何以獨為五藏主。歧伯曰：胃者水穀之海，六府之大源也。五味入口，藏於胃以養五藏氣，氣口亦太陰也。是以五藏六府之氣味，皆出於胃，變見於氣口。故五氣入鼻，藏於心肺，心肺有病而鼻為之不利也。 The Emperor said: How is the Qi mouth unique as the master of the Five Organs? The Duke of Shou replied: The Stomach is like a sea of water and the source of the six ministries. The five flavors enter through the mouth, where they are stored in the Stomach to nourish the Qi of the Five Organs; the Qi mouth is also part of the Yin aspect. Therefore, the Qi of the Five Organs and Six Ministries all come from the Stomach and manifest through the Qi mouth. As a result, the five Qi enter through the nose and are stored in the Heart and Lungs. If the Heart and Lungs become diseased, this will affect the nasal passage.

    3. 凡治病必察其下，適其脈，觀其志意，與其病也。拘於鬼神者，不可與言至德。惡於鍼石者，不可與言至巧。病不許治者病必不治，治之無功矣。 In treating illness, one must investigate its root, match the pulse, observe its intentions, and be in harmony with its disease. Those who are bound by ghosts and spirits cannot be discussed in terms of great virtue. Those who dislike needles and stones cannot be discussed in terms of great skill. If a disease is not allowed to be treated, it will not be cured, and any treatment will have no effect.
