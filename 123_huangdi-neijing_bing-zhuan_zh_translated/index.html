    病傳

    1: 黃帝曰：余受九鍼于夫子，而私覽于諸方，或有導引行氣，喬摩、灸、熨、刺、焫、飲藥之一者，可獨守耶，將盡行之乎？歧伯曰：諸方者，眾人之方也，非一人之所盡行也。 Yellow Emperor said: I received nine needles from Confucius, and privately studied them in various ways. Sometimes there are guiding and directing methods for regulating the Qi, such as acupuncture, moxibustion, cupping, acupressure, cauterization, ingestion of medicine, and so on. Can one person exclusively guard and practice them all? Huzhu said: The various ways refer to the common people's methods, not something that can be fully practiced by a single individual.

    2: 黃帝曰：此乃所謂守一勿失，萬物畢者也。今余已聞陰陽之要，虛實之理，傾移之過，可治之屬，願聞病之變化，淫傳絕敗而不可治者，可得聞乎？歧伯曰：要乎哉問也，昭乎其如日醒，窘乎其如夜瞑，能被而服之，神與俱成，畢將服之，神自得之，生神之理，可著於竹帛，不可傳於子孫。 Yellow Emperor said: This is what is meant by guarding the one and not losing it, the completion of a hundred things. Now I have heard about the essence of yin and yang, the logic of emptiness and reality, the excesses of changing and moving, which can be governed and are treatable. I wish to hear about the changes in diseases that become incurable due to excessive transmission and do not lend themselves to treatment. Can you explain this? The Grand Assistant replied: What a profound question! It is as clear as daylight when one wakes up, and as perplexing as darkness when one is asleep. It can be understood and taught, allowing the spirit to succeed, and the spirit itself will attain it. The essence of the spirit, which can be written on bamboo slips, should not be passed down to one's descendants.

    3: 黃帝曰：何謂日醒？歧伯曰：明於陰陽，如惑之解，如醉之醒。黃帝曰：何謂夜瞑？歧伯曰：瘖乎其無聲，漠乎其無形，折毛發理，正氣橫傾，淫邪泮衍，血脈傳溜，大氣入藏，腹痛下淫，可以致死，不可以致生。 Yellow Emperor asked: What is meant by day wakefulness? Hui Bao replied: It means clarity of yin and yang, like the solution of confusion, like awakening from drunkenness. Yellow Emperor asked: What is meant by night torpor? Hui Bao replied: It is without sound and form, it folds the hairs, straightens the qi, adjusts the disordered and excessive, guides the blood vessels, disperses the great qi, enters the storehouse, causes abdominal pain and discharges the excess, which can lead to death but not to life.

    4: 黃帝曰：大氣入藏，奈何？歧伯曰：病先發於心，一日而之肺，三日而之肝，五日而之脾，三日不已，死。冬夜半，夏日中。 Yellow Emperor said: What if the great Qi enters the body? Huangdi said: The disease starts in the heart, and it reaches the lungs in a day. It reaches the liver in three days and the spleen in five days. If it continues for three days without stopping, one dies. Half of winter night or mid-summer day.

    5: 病先發於肺，三日而之肝，一日而之脾，五日而之胃，十日不已，死。冬日入，夏日出。 The illness first manifested in the lungs, and by the third day it had affected the liver. In a day, it reached the spleen. By five days, it extended to the stomach. Ten days later, it still persisted and resulted in death. It occurred during winter and resolved during summer.

    6: 病先發於肝，三日而之脾，五日而之胃，三日而之腎，三日不已，死。冬日入，夏蚤食。 The disease first appears in the liver, and by the third day it has spread to the spleen, by the fifth day to the stomach, by the third day to the kidneys, where it continues without cease until death. It is contracted in winter and cured in summer with a diet of insects.

    7: 病先發於脾，一日而之胃，二日而之腎，三日而之膂膀胱，十日不已，死。冬人定，夏晏食。 The illness begins in the spleen, moves to the stomach in one day, to the kidneys in two days, to the muscles and bladder in three days, and continues for ten days without stopping, resulting in death. This is a seasonal diet: in winter, refrain from eating; in summer, be cautious with food.

    8: 病先發於胃，五日而之腎，三日而之膂膀胱，五日而上之心，二日不已，死，冬夜半，夏日昳。 The illness first began in the stomach, and after five days it affected the kidneys. After three days it spread to the liver, spleen, and intestines. Five days later, it rose up to the heart. Within two days, it did not stop, leading to death. Halfway through the winter night or a summer day's heat.

    9: 病先發於腎，三日而之膂膀胱，三日而上之心，三日而之小腸，三日不已，死。冬大晨，夏晏晡。 The illness first appeared in the kidney, and after three days it reached the bladder, three days later it rose to the heart, three days later it affected the small intestine, and it did not stop there. The person died. In winter, during the large morning, and in summer, during the late afternoon heat

    10: 病先發於膀胱，五日而之腎，一日而之小腸，一日而之心，二日不已，死。冬雞鳴，夏下晡。 The illness first began in the bladder, and by the fifth day it had reached the kidneys. By the next day, it affected the small intestine, then the heart, where it lingered for two days before causing death. Chickens cackled in winter, and in summer they shed feathers.

    11: 諸病以次相傳，如是者，皆有死期，不可刺也；間一藏及二、三、四藏者，乃可刺也。 Various diseases that come in succession, if they are like this, all have a time of death and cannot be punctured; between the lower abdomen and the first, second, third, and fourth storerooms, they can be punctured.
