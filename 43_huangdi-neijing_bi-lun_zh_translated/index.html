    痺論

    1. 黃帝問曰：痺之安生。歧伯對曰：風寒濕三氣雜至，合而為痺也。其風氣勝者為行痺，寒氣勝者為痛痺，濕氣勝者為著痺也。帝曰：其有五者，何也。歧伯曰：以冬遇此者為骨痺，以春遇此者為筋痺，以夏遇此者為脈痺，以至陰遇此者為肌痺，以秋遇此者為皮痺。帝曰：內舍五藏六府，何氣使然。歧伯曰：五藏皆有合病，久而不去者，內舍於其合也。故骨痺不已，復感於邪，內舍於腎；筋痺不已，復感於邪內舍於肝；脈痺不已，復感於邪內舍於心；肌痺不已，復感於邪內舍於脾；皮痺不已，復感於邪，內舍於肺，所謂痺者，各以其時，重感於風寒濕之氣也。 Yellow Emperor asked: What is the cause of paralysis? [Taiwan] Bo said: It is due to the combination of three types of Qi - wind, cold and damp. If wind Qi predominates, it causes limb paralysis; if cold Qi predominates, it causes painful paralysis; if damp Qi predominates, it causes swollen paralysis. Yellow Emperor asked: Are there five types of this? [Taiwan] Bo said: In the winter, it is bone paralysis; in the spring, it is muscle paralysis; in the summer, it is tendon paralysis; in the autumn, it is skin paralysis. Yellow Emperor asked: The internal organs have six divisions; what Qi causes this? [Taiwan] Bo said: All internal organs have a condition of merging diseases, which persists and does not go away.     2. 凡痺之客五藏者，肺痺者，煩滿喘而嘔；心痺者，脈不通，煩則心下鼓，暴上氣而喘，嗌乾善噫，厥氣上則恐；肝痺者，夜臥則驚，多飲數小便，上為引如懷；腎痺者，善脹，尻以代踵，脊以代頭；脾痺者，四支解墯，發欬嘔汁，上為大塞；腸痺者，數飲而出不得，中氣喘爭，時發飱泄；胞痺者，少腹膀胱，按之內痛，若沃以湯，澀於小便，上為清涕。 All cases of organ stagnation:

    3. Lung stagnation causes wheezing, shortness of breath, and nausea.
    4. Heart stagnation leads to irregular pulse, palpitations, shortness of breath, coughing, and fear.
    5. Liver stagnation results in nighttime awakenings with fright, excessive urination, and symptoms similar to carrying a child.
    6. Kidney stagnation causes bloating, walking with difficulty like someone pulling on the hips, and neck stiffness.
    7. Spleen stagnation results in weak limbs, hiccups, nausea, and difficulty swallowing.
    8. Stomach stagnation leads to frequent drinking without being able to relieve oneself, shortness of breath, and occasional diarrhea.

    9. Uterus stagnation causes lower abdominal pain and bloating, pressing on the abdomen induces pain, discharging watery fluid when applying heat, and clear vaginal discharge.

    10. 陰氣者，靜則神藏，躁則消亡。飲食自倍，腸胃乃傷。淫氣喘息，痺聚在肺；淫氣憂思，痺聚在心；淫氣遺溺，痺聚在腎；淫氣乏竭，痺聚在肝；淫氣肌絕，痺聚在脾。諸痺不已，亦益內也，其風氣勝者，其人易已也。帝曰：痺，其時有死者，或疼久者，或易已者，其故何也。歧伯曰：其入藏者死，其留連筋骨閒者疼久，其留皮膚閒者易已。 The person with yin (internal) energy, when quiet, the spirit is hidden, and when agitated, it perishes. Excessive food and drink harm the stomach and intestines. Dissipated qi (vital energy) causes shortness of breath, accumulating in the lungs; dissipated qi causing worry, accumulating in the heart; dissipated qi leading to drowning, accumulating in the kidneys; dissipated qi causing weakness, accumulating in the liver; and dissipated qi causing muscle atrophy, accumulating in the spleen. The accumulations do not stop, and it also harms internally, with the dominant winds and qi leading to harm in people. The Emperor said: In the case of accumulation, there are those who die, those who suffer for a long time, and those who are easily harmed. What is the reason for this? Zhi Bo said: Those who enter and hide (the qi) die; those who linger on the tendons and bones suffer for a long time; and those who linger on the skin and flesh are easily harmed.

    11. 帝曰：其客於六府者何也。歧伯曰：此亦其食飲居處，為其病本也。六府亦各有俞，風寒濕氣中其俞，而食飲應之，循俞而入，各舍其府也。帝曰：以鍼治之，柰何。歧伯曰：五藏有俞，六府有合，循脈之分，各有所發，各隨其過，則病瘳也。 The Emperor asked, What are the six ministries? The Duke of Zhou replied, This refers to their food and drink and their place of residence, which are the origins of their diseases. Each of the six ministries has its own affinity, and when affected by wind, cold, or dampness, they act according to their affinity and take refuge in their respective ministries. The Emperor asked, How would you treat it with a lancet? The Duke of Zhou replied, The Five Canisters have affinities, and the six ministries have correspondences. By following the pulse divisions, each has its own manifestation, following its path, and then the disease will resolve.

    12. 帝曰：榮衛之氣，亦令人痺乎。歧伯曰：榮者水穀之精氣也，和調於五藏，灑陳於六府，乃能入於脈也；故循脈上下，貫五藏絡六府也。衛者水穀之悍氣也，其氣慓疾滑利，不能入於脈也；故循皮膚之中，分肉之閒，熏於肓膜，散於胸腹，逆其氣則病，從其氣則愈。不與風寒濕氣合，故不為痺。帝曰：善。痺或痛，或不痛或不仁，或寒，或熱，或燥，或濕，其故何也。歧伯曰：痛者，寒氣多也，有寒故痛也。其不痛不仁者，病久入深，榮衛之行濇，經絡時踈，故不通，皮膚不營，故為不仁。其寒者，陽氣少，陰氣多，與病相益，故寒也；其熱者，陽氣多，陰氣少，病氣勝陽遭陰，故為痺熱。其多汗而濡者，此其逢濕甚也，陽氣少，陰氣盛，兩氣相感，故汗出而濡也。帝曰：夫痺之為病，不痛何也。歧伯曰：痺在於骨則重，在於脈則血凝而不流，在於筋則屈不伸，在於肉則不仁，在於皮則寒，故具此五者，則不痛也。凡痺之類，逢寒則蟲，逢熱則縱。帝曰：善。 Emperor said: The Qi of glory is also lethargic for people. Master Of Gi said: Glory refers to the Qi of grains, regulated in the Five Zang and poured into the Six Fu, which can enter the Meridians. Therefore, by following the Meridians up and down and penetrating the Five Zang and Six Fu. Glory is the fierce Qi of grains, with its Qi agile, swift, slippery, and unable to enter the Meridians. So, by circulating within the skin and flesh, dispersing in the muscles, fumigating on the Lung and Stomach, opposite the Qi will cause disease, following the Qi will cure. If it does not merge with the Wind, Cold, Dampness Qi, it will not be lethargic.

    Emperor asked: Why is there pain or no pain, or numbness in some cases? Master Of Gi said: Pain indicates that Cold Qi is abundant, and therefore causes pain. Numbness without pain or sensitivity is due to the disease becoming deep-seated, and the disordered flow of Qi and blood at the meridians and collaterals. The skin does not nourish properly, resulting in insensitivity. Cold indicates that Yang Qi is deficient and Yin Qi is abundant, which benefit each other, causing cold. Heat indicates that Yang Qi is abundant and Yin Qi is deficient, and the pathogenic factor attacks Yang or Yin, causing numbness and heat. Sweating and becoming damp indicates meeting excessive Dampness, with Yang Qi deficient and Yin Qi abundant, causing the two Qis to feel each other, resulting in sweating and becoming damp.

    Emperor asked: What causes numbness without pain? Master Of Gi said: Numbness resides in the bones, making it heavy; numbness resides in the meridians, making blood congeal and not flow; numbness resides in the tendons, making them unable to bend and stretch; numbness resides in the flesh, causing insensitivity; so if all five of these are present, there is no pain. All cases of numbness have different treatments: encountering Cold will cause constriction, encountering Heat will cause stretching.
