    1. 黃帝問於歧伯曰：余聞人之合於天道也，內有五藏，以應五音、五色、五時、五味、五位也；外有六府，以應六律。六律建陰陽諸經而合之十二月、十二辰、十二節、十二經水、十二時、十二經脈者，此五藏六府之所以應天道。夫十二經脈者，人之所以生，病之所以成，人之所以治，病之所以起，學之所始，工之所止也。麤之所易，上之所難也。請問其離合，出入奈何？歧伯稽首再拜曰：明乎哉問也！此麤之所過，上之所息也，請卒言之。 The Yellow Emperor asked Yi Bo, saying: I have heard that people are in harmony with the Dao of Heaven. Inside they have five organs, which respond to the five tones, five colors, five times, five flavors, and five positions. Outside there are six departments, which respond to the six laws. The six laws build upon the classics of Yin and Yang and harmonize them with the twelve months, twelve stems, twelve degrees, twelve water channels, twelve times, and twelve channels of vital energy. These five organs and six departments are what respond to the Dao of Heaven. The twelve channels of vital energy are what make people live, give rise to disease, allow for treatment, cause disease to arise, form the basis of learning, and put an end to work. It is difficult to approach them, and yet easy to lose them. Please ask about their beginning and end, and how they enter and exit. Yi Bo kowtowed and said, How marvelous your question is! This is what causes them to be lost and is difficult to approach; please allow me to answer it fully.

    2. 足太陽之正，別入于膕中，其一道下尻五寸，別入于肛，屬於膀胱，散之腎，循膂，當心入散；直者，從膂上出於項，復屬於太陽，此為一經也。足少陰之正，至膕中，別走太陽而合，上至腎，當十四椎出屬帶脈；直者，系舌本，復出於項，合於太陽，此為一合。成以諸陰之別，皆為正也。 The foot Taiyang channel is the proper one. It diverges from the Mingmen and goes downward five cun below the buttocks, entering the anus, belongs to the bladder, disperses it through the Shen, follows the Jue, enters the heart cavity; if straight, it comes out of the Jue from the Gaohuang, belonging to Taiyang, this is one channel. The foot Shaoyin channel reaches to the Mingmen, separates from Taiyang and unites, ascends to the Zhong, belongs to Guan, when exiting at the fourteen vertebrae; if straight, it connects with the tongue root, comes out of the Gaohuang, unites with Taiyang, this is one channel. The succession of the Yin channels is all proper.

    4. 足陽明之正，上至脾，入于腹裏屬胃，散之脾，上通於心，上循咽出於口，上頞䪼，還繫目系，合於陽明也。足太陰之正，上至髀，合於陽明，與別俱行，上結於咽，貫舌中，此為三合也。 The following text is already in English, with the exception of some archaic terms used to represent classical Chinese concepts. Here's a more modern equivalent:

    The correcting function of Yang Ming resides in the foot Yang Ming and ascends to the spleen, which is housed in the abdomen and belongs to the stomach. When it disperses, it connects to the heart. It circulates up through the throat and exits at the mouth. It ties to the eyes, regulating vision, and joins with Yang Ming. The correcting function of Tai Yin resides in the foot Tai Yin and connects with Yang Ming, following a separate course. It ascends to the hip joint and connects with Yang Ming, representing the Three Meetings.

    1. 手太陽之正，指地，別於肩解，入腋走心，系小腸也。手少陰之正，別入于淵腋兩筋之間，屬於心，上走喉嚨，出於面，合目內眥，此為四合也。 The hand Yang (radial) aspect proper, points at the earth; separate from the shoulder, enter the armpit and go to the heart. The hand Shao Yin (palmar) aspect proper, separate within the axillary sinew depressors, belongs to the heart, ascend to the throat, exit from the front, close to the eyes' inner corner, this is the four closings.

    2. 手少陽之正，指天，別於巔，入缺盆，下走三焦，散於胸中也。手心主之正，別下淵腋三寸，入胸中，別屬三焦，出循喉嚨，出耳後，合少陽完骨之下，此為五合也。 The text describes a traditional Chinese medicine concept regarding the flow of energy (Qi) in the body. Translated to English, it says: When the right hand is above the right kidney, point towards the sky and separate from the peak. The Qi will enter the cavity and travel downwards along the three burners. It will disperse within the chest. When the palm faces downward, locate a depth of three cun below the axillary depression, entering the chest, not belonging to the three burners, it exits by circling the throat and exiting at the back of the ear, merging with the Shao Yang to complete the bone structure. This is known as the Five Meetings.

    3. 手陽明之正，從手循膺乳，別於肩髃，入柱骨，下走大腸，屬於肺，上循喉嚨，出缺盆，合於陽明也。手太陰之正，別入淵腋少陰之前，入走肺，散之大陽，上出缺盆，循喉嚨，復合陽明，此六合也。 The channel of the Hand Yang Ming starts from the hand and follows the Radial Artery, diverging from the shoulder hairline, entering the humerus, descending along the large intestine, belonging to the lung, ascending along the throat, exiting at the umbilicus, connecting with the Yang Ming also. The channel of the Hand Taiyin starts by diverging and entering the well of the armpit of the Lesser Shao Yin, entering the lung, dispersing to the Great Yang, ascending and exiting at the umbilicus, ascending along the throat, connecting with the Yang Ming, thus forming the six connections.
