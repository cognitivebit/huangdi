    太陰陽明論

    1. 黃帝問曰：太陰陽明為表裏，脾胃脈也，生病而異者何也。歧伯對曰：陰陽異位，更虛更實，更逆更從，或從內，或從外，所從不同，故病異名也。帝曰：願聞其異狀也。歧伯曰：陽者，天氣也，主外；陰者，地氣也，主內。故陽道實，陰道虛。故犯賊風虛邪者，陽受之；食飲不節起居不時者，陰受之。陽受之，則入六府，陰受之，則入五藏。入六府，則身熱不時臥，上為喘呼；入五藏則䐜滿閉塞，下為飱泄，久為腸澼。故喉主天氣，咽主地氣。故陽受風氣，陰受濕氣。故陰氣從足上行至頭，而下行循臂至指端；陽氣從手上行至頭，而下行至足。故曰：陽病者，上行極而下，陰病者，下行極而上。故傷於風者，上先受之；傷於濕者，下先受之。 The Yellow Emperor asked, The Yin and Yang are clear in the exterior and interior; what is the cause of disease when it is not in harmony? Huan Dynasty replied, When Yin and Yang are out of alignment, they become deficient or excessive, and reverse or follow in either the inner or outer, with differing sources, thus causing diseases to manifest differently. The Emperor asked, May I hear about these different states? Huan Dynasty said, Yang represents heavenly Qi, governing the exterior, while Yin represents earthly Qi, governing the interior. Therefore, the path of Yang is real and the path of Yin is vacuous. Thus, when one contracts wind evil, Yang is affected; when one eats unrefined food and does not regulate rest or movement, Yin is affected. When Yang is affected, it enters the six ministries, and when Yin is affected, it enters the five organs. Entering the six ministries results in fever and restlessness at night; entering the five organs leads to nausea and diarrhea, which can persist as dysentery. Therefore, the throat governs heavenly Qi, and the palate governs earthly Qi. Thus, Yang receives wind Qi, and Yin receives damp Qi. The flow of Yin Qi rises from the foot to the head and descends along the arms to the tips of the fingers; the flow of Yang Qi rises from the hand to the head and descends to the foot. Hence, it is said that in diseases of Yang, the ascent is extreme while the descent is weak, and in diseases of Yin, the descent is extreme while the ascent is weak. Therefore, when one is injured by wind, the upper part is affected first; when one is injured by dampness, the lower part is affected first

    2. 帝曰：脾病而四支不用，何也。歧伯曰：四支皆稟氣於胃，而不得至經，必因於脾，乃得稟也。今脾病不能為胃行其津液，四支不得稟水穀氣，氣日以衰，脈道不利，筋骨肌肉，皆無氣以生，故不用焉。 The emperor asked, Why are the four limbs not nourished in cases of pemphigus (脾病), and what is the reason for this? Qi Bo replied, The four limbs all obtain qi from the stomach. If they cannot reach the meridians, it is due to a problem with the spleen. Hence, they can obtain qi. Now, in cases of pemphigus, the spleen cannot provide nourishment for the stomach, and the four limbs cannot obtain water and grain qi. The qi declines daily, the meridian channels are not beneficial, and the tendons, bones, muscles, and flesh all lack qi to generate life, so they are not used.

    3. 帝曰：脾不主時，何也。歧伯曰：脾者土也，治中央，常以四時長四藏，各十八日寄治，不得獨主於時也。脾藏者，常著胃土之精也，土者，生萬物而法天地，故上下至頭足，不得主時也。 The Emperor asked: Why is the spleen not in charge of the seasons? Hui-bо responded: The spleen is like earth, governing from the center, and always regulating the four seasons for forty days each, with each period consisting of eighteen days. It should not be solely responsible for managing the seasons. The spleen's function, which involves connecting with the nourishing energy of the earth, extends throughout both the upper and lower realms; thus, it should not be in charge of the seasons

    4. 帝曰：脾與胃以膜相連耳，而能為之行其津液，何也。歧伯曰：足太陰者三陰也，其脈貫胃屬脾絡嗌，故太陰為之行氣於三陰。陽明者，表也，五藏六府之海也，亦為之行氣於三陽。藏府各因其經而受氣於陽明，故為胃行其津液。四支不得稟水穀氣，日以益衰，陰道不利，筋骨肌肉無氣以生，故不用焉。 The emperor asked: The spleen and stomach are connected by a membrane, and yet they can cause the fluids to flow. Why is this so? Táibó replied: The Chíyuè channel is the three Yín channels, and its pulse connects to the stomach and belongs to the spleen's network of the Língtóng. Thus, the Chíyue channel causes Qì to flow in the three Yín. The Yángmíng is the exterior, and the sea of the five Zāng and six Fǔ. It also causes Qì to flow in the three Yáng. Each storehouse receives Qì according to its meridian from the Yángmíng, thus causing the stomach to move the fluids. If the four limbs are not properly balanced, water and grain Qì will decrease each day, the Yīn pathway will not be favorable, muscles, bones, and flesh will have no Qì to generate life, and therefore will not be used.
