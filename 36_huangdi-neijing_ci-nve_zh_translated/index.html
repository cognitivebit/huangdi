    刺瘧

    1: 足太陽之瘧，令人腰痛頭重，寒從背起，先寒後熱，熇熇暍暍然，熱止汗出，難已，刺郄中出血。足少陽之瘧，令人身體解㑊，寒不甚，熱不甚，惡見人，見人心惕惕然，熱多汗出甚，刺足少陽。足陽明之瘧，令人先寒洒淅洒淅寒甚久乃熱，熱去汗出喜見日月光火氣，乃快然，刺足陽明跗上，足太陰之瘧，令人不樂，好大息，不嗜食，多寒熱汗出，病至則善嘔，嘔已乃衰，即取之。足少陰之瘧，令人嘔吐甚，多寒熱，熱多寒少；欲閉戶牖而處，其病難已。足厥陰之瘧，令人腰痛少腹滿，小便不利，如癃狀，非癃也，數便，意恐懼氣不足，腹中悒悒，刺足厥陰。 The ailments associated with the foot [Yang] channel are as follows: 1. Ailments of the foot [Yangming] channel: This leads to pain in the head and waist, with chills starting from the back, fever coming later, with symptoms of mild perspiration. Once the fever stops, there is sweating. The condition is difficult to treat, and bleeding occurs upon puncturing the Jing-well point. 2. Ailments of the foot [Shaoyang] channel: This causes the body to feel weak and tired, with chills but not very severe, and fever that is also not very intense. This is a bad sign. The patient seems nervous, and their heart is anxious. There is excessive sweating during fever, and the foot [Shaoyang] point is punctured. 3. Ailments of the foot [Yangming] channel: This leads to chills that last for a long time before the onset of fever. Once the fever subsides, there is sweating, which brings relief and happiness. The foot [Yangming] and [Jueyin] points are punctured, respectively. 4. Ailments of the foot [Yin] channel: This causes displeasure, a desire for deep breathing, loss of appetite, and excessive chills and fever with sweating. If the disease progresses, there is a tendency to vomit, followed by recovery, but then deterioration. Therefore, it is treated promptly. 5. Ailments of the foot [Yin] channel: This causes vomiting to occur frequently, with excessive chills and fever, where chills are more prevalent than fever. It feels as if one wants to close the window shades, but the disease is difficult to treat. 6. Ailments of the foot [Ren] channel: This leads to pain in the waist and lower abdomen, with difficulty urinating, like the condition of cystitis but not cystitis. The patient feels anxious about their qi not being sufficient, with a tense feeling in the abdomen. The foot [Ren] point is punctured.

    Note: This translation uses the Wade-Giles romanization system for Chinese characters. Modern translations would likely use Pinyin.

    2: 肺瘧者，令人心寒，寒甚熱，熱間善驚，如有所見者，刺手太陰陽明。心瘧者，令人煩心甚欲得清水反寒多，不甚熱，刺手少陰。肝瘧者，令人色蒼蒼然太息，其狀若死者，刺足厥陰見血。脾瘧者，令人寒，腹中痛，熱則腸中鳴，鳴已汗出，刺足太陰。腎瘧者，令人洒洒然，腰脊痛宛轉大便難，目眴眴然，手足寒，刺足太陽少陰。胃瘧者，令人且病也，善飢而不能食，食而支滿腹大，刺足陽明太陰橫脈出血。 For someone with a fever: It is chilling to the heart, and the chill is intense. Fevers can be very alarming, similar to actually seeing something. For this, use the hand technique of Taiyang (Yang Meridian of the Hand).

    For someone with heart fever: It makes people's hearts uneasy and anxious, wanting to drink cold water very much, not overly hot, and use the hand technique of Shaoyang (Shao Yang Meridian of the Hand).

    For someone with liver fever: It makes people appear pale and short of breath, their condition resembling that of a dead person. For this, use the foot technique of Weiyang (Yang Meridian of the Foot).

    For someone with spleen fever: It makes people feel cold, have abdominal pain, and when hot, there will be rumbling in the intestines, followed by sweating. For this, use the foot technique of Taiyang (Yang Meridian of the Foot).

    For someone with kidney fever: It makes people sweaty, have lower back and lumbar pain, difficulty passing stool, have dizzy eyes, cold hands and feet, for which use the foot technique of Taiyin (Yin Meridian of the Foot) and Shaoyang.

    For someone with stomach fever: It makes people feel unwell, have a good appetite but cannot eat, or eat and feel bloated in the abdomen, for this use the foot technique of Jueyin (Yin Meridian of the Foot) and Taiyang.

    3: 瘧發身方熱，刺跗上動脈，開其空出其血，立寒，瘧方欲寒刺手陽明太陰足陽明太陰。 The patient's fever is high, and there is a pulse in the Shu (Lung) position. Open the Bleeding Vessels to let out the blood, then apply cold to it. When the fever is about to become cold, use needles to stimulate Hand Jiao (Ren 17) and Shenque (Ren 6).

    4: 瘧脈滿大，急刺背俞，用中鍼傍伍胠俞各一，適肥瘦出其血也。瘧脈小實，急灸脛少陰，刺指井。瘧脈滿大，急刺背俞，用五胠俞背俞各一，適行至於血也。瘧脈緩大虛，便宜用藥，不宜用鍼，凡治瘧先發，如食頃乃可以治，過之則失時也，諸瘧而脈不見，刺十指閒出血，血去必已，先視身之赤如小豆者盡取之。 When the pulse is full and large, in an urgent situation, insert a central needle at the back Shu point, using one needle each at the five Shu points of the affected area to let out blood. If the pulse is small and real, use moxa at the Shenmen point on the foot, and insert a needle into the Jing Well. In cases where the pulse is full and large, in an urgent situation, insert one needle each at the five Shu points of the affected area, and proceed to extract blood. If the pulse is somewhat full but empty, it is more suitable to use medicine rather than needles; needles should not be used. In treating febrile diseases as soon as they appear, within a short time they can be cured, but if one misses this opportunity, it will be too late. When diagnosing other febrile diseases and the pulse cannot be seen, insert ten superficial needles at idle points and let blood out; once the blood is let out, it is already completed. For those with a red complexion resembling small beans, take them all away immediately.

    5: 瘧者，其發各不同時，察其病形，以知其何脈之病也。先其發時如食頃而刺之，一刺則衰，二刺則知，三刺則已，不已，刺舌下兩脈出血，不已，刺郄中盛經出血，又刺項已下俠脊者必已。舌下兩脈者，廉泉也。 In the case of a patient experiencing convulsions, examine the appearance of the convulsion to determine which meridian is affected. Before the onset of the convulsion, insert a needle at the appropriate location. One insertion results in weakness, two insertions allow identification of the affected meridian, and three insertions resolve the issue. If the issue persists, insert a needle below the tongue to bleed the meridians; if it still persists, insert a needle into the center of the chest to release the meridians. Additionally, if a needle is inserted at the point located below the shoulder blade on the affected side, the issue will be resolved. The two meridians beneath the tongue are referred to as pure springs.

    6: 刺瘧者，必先問其病之所先發者，先刺之。先頭痛及重者，先刺頭上及兩額兩眉閒出血。先項背痛者，先刺之。先腰脊痛者，先刺郄中出血，先手臂痛者，先刺手少陰陽明十指閒。先足脛痠痛者，先刺足陽明十指閒出血。風瘧，瘧發則汗出惡風，刺三陽經背俞之血者，䯒痠痛甚按之不可名曰胕髓病，以鑱鍼鍼絕骨出血，立已。身體小痛，刺至陰。諸陰之井無出血，閒日一刺。瘧不渴，閒日而作，刺足太陽。渴而閒日作，刺足少陽。溫瘧汗不出，為五十九刺。 For a person with chills, first inquire about the location where the illness first manifested and then acupuncture that area. For those with headaches and severe pain, acupuncture the top of the head, as well as the forehead and eyebrows, allowing for a small amount of bleeding. For those with neck and back pain, acupuncture the middle point of the Bladder channel and allow for some bleeding. For those with lower back pain, acupuncture the Conception Vessel and allow for a small amount of bleeding. For those with arm pain, acupuncture the hand Shao Yin and Jing Ming points. For those with leg pain, acupuncture the Yang Ming point on the foot and allow for some bleeding. For wind-induced chills, if fever arises accompanied by sweating from evil winds, then acupuncture the Shen Men, Ren Mai, and the Governing Vessel at Bai Hui. For severe cases of chills where it cannot be named and is similar to damage of the marrow, use an awl to puncture the bone and allow for bleeding to stop. For minor body aches, acupuncture the Ren Mai. If there is no bleeding from the Well of Longevity, re-acupuncture the next day. For chills without thirst, acupuncture the foot Tai Yin. For chills with thirst, acupuncture the foot Shao Yin. For fever without sweating, perform 59 punctures.
