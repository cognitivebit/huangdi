    癩狂病

    1. 目眥外決於面者，為銳眥；在內近鼻者，為內眥；上為外眥，下為內眥。 The outer corner of the eye determines the sharpness of the gaze, being outside close to the cheek; inside near the nose, it is the inner corner; above it is the outer corner and below it is the inner corner.

    2. 癲疾始生，先不樂，頭重痛，視舉目赤，甚作極，已而煩心。候之於顏。取手太陽、陽明、太陰，血變為止。 Initially, the patient was in a hurry and not happy. They had a heavy headache, red eyes, and were extremely uncomfortable, causing their heart to be restless. Waiting for the face to change, they took the Radiant Sun, Yang Brightness, and Yin Migration formulas. The bleeding was stopped.

    3. 癲疾始作，而引口啼呼喘悸者，候之手陽明、太陽。左強者，攻其右；右強者，攻其左，血變為止。癲疾始作，先反僵，因而脊痛，候之足太陽、陽明、太陰、手太陽，血變為止。 When the mania and madness begin, with one urging their mouth to cry out, gasp, and pant, wait until the hand Yang Brightness and Great Sun appear. If it is strong on the left side, attack its right; if it is strong on the right side, attack its left, and the bleeding will stop. When mania and madness begin, first stiffen and then become rigid, causing a backache, wait until the foot Yang Brightness, Great Sun, Kidney Yang Brightness, and Hand Yang Brightness appear. The bleeding will stop.

    4. 治癲疾者，常與之居，察其所當取之處。病至，視之有過者寫之，置其血於瓠壺之中，至其發時，血獨動矣，不動，灸窮骨二十壯。窮骨者，骶骨也。 Treating the insane, one should always live with them and examine the proper places to take. When the illness is severe, note any abnormalities and write them down. Place their blood in a bowl, when it begins to move on its own, it will stop moving without manipulation, and needling the bone marrow twenty times. Needling the bone marrow refers to the vertebrae.

    5. 骨癲疾者，顑、齒諸腧、分肉皆滿而骨居，汗出、煩悗，嘔多沃沫，氣下泄，不治。 A patient with a fracture, the bones are filled but there is no flesh, they sweat and itch, vomit much foam, release gas, and cannot be cured.

    6. 筋癲疾者，身倦攣急大，刺項大經之大杼脈，嘔多沃沫，氣下泄，不治。 The patient with convulsions, exhausted and suffering from great haste, had needles inserted into the Jiao Xu points of the large intestine meridian, with copious vomiting and gas release, but to no avail.

    7. 脈癲疾者，暴仆，四肢之脈皆脹而縱，脈滿，盡刺之出血，不滿，灸之挾項太陽，灸帶脈於腰相去三寸，諸分肉本輸。嘔吐沃沫，氣下泄，不治。癲疾者，疾發如狂者，死不治。 For a rapid pulse, violent vomiting of saliva and gastric fluids, release of qi, and failure to be treated, the condition is severe. This disease is similar to madness; if not treated, it will result in death.

    The patient has a quick pulse and violent vomiting, with qi being released but no blood being drawn out. The pulse is full and upon puncturing, bleeding occurs; if not full, moxibustion should be applied at the governor vessel and conception vessel points, with the moxa stick used 3 inches away from the pulse at the waist, stimulating the muscles to release excess heat. This treatment is effective for vomiting and releasing qi, but has failed to resolve the issue. The rapid onset of symptoms in this case is comparable to insanity; if left untreated, it will lead to death.

    1. 狂始生，先自悲也，喜忘、苦怒、善恐者得之憂飢，治之取手太陽、陽明，血變而止，及取足太陰、陽明。狂始發，少臥不飢，自高賢也，自辯智也，自尊貴也，善罵詈，日夜不休，治之取手陽明太陽太陰舌下少陰，視之盛者，皆取之，不盛，釋之也。 In the case of mania onset, one initially feels sadness and then experiences joy, anger, anxiety, fear, and eventually becomes troubled by hunger. Treatment involves taking the pulse at the Taiyang (Radiant Sun) and Yangming (Brightness) points, with blood flow normalizing and stopping after a short time. Additionally, taking the pulse at the Taiyang, Yangming, and Tongzhong Yimen (Gathering Life Gate) points is necessary for cases where there is persistent lying in bed without appetite. The patient feels proud and wise, debating and respectful of themselves, prone to verbal abuse without rest day or night. Treatment involves taking the pulse at Yangming, Taiyang, Taiyang (Radiant Sun), and Shao Shang (Little Pulse) points, with those who respond being treated while others are dismissed.

    2. 狂言，驚，善笑，好歌樂，妄行不休者，得之大恐，治之取手陽明太陽太陰。狂，目妄見，耳妄聞，善呼者，少氣之所生也；治之取手太陽太陰陽明，足太陰頭兩顑。 The text translates to: Insanity, fear, skillful laughter, good songs and music, those who act recklessly without stopping, are greatly feared. Treating them involves using the Yang Ming (Hand Yang) and Taiyang (Great Yang), as well as the Yin Ming (Hand Yin) and Taiyin (Great Yin). Madness is characterized by hallucinations of the eyes and hearing delusions through the ears; those who can call upon it have a deficiency of Qi. Treatment involves using the Hand Yang, Great Yang, Hand Yin, and Great Yin, as well as the Jueyin (Mingmen) point.

    3. 狂者多食，善見鬼神，善笑而不發於外者，得之有所大喜，治之取足太陰太陽陽明，後取手太陰太陽陽明。狂而新發，未應如此者，先取曲泉左右動脈，及盛者見血，有頃已，不已，以法取之，灸骨骶二十壯。 Persons who are mad often eat too much, can see spirits and gods, and smile without showing it on the outside. People are very pleased when such a person appears, and they treat them by taking sufficient Kidney-Yang and Bright Yang and then taking Hand-Kidney-Yang and Bright Yang. If the madness is new and not as described above, first take Curved Vessel Left and Right channels and Blood Vessels, and when it becomes apparent, stop immediately. Do not continue, use this method to treat them, press the bones and vertebrae twenty times.

    4. 風逆，暴四肢腫，身漯漯，唏然時寒，飢則煩，飽則善變，取手大陰表裏，足少陰陽明之徑，肉清取滎，骨清取井、經也。 Against the wind, with four limbs swollen, body trembling, feeling cold at times, hungry irritable and satisfied easily, taking hands as an example, the palm side is the way of yin, the back side is the way of yang. The meat is clear when taken in a flood, and the bones are clear when taken from a well or a spring (referring to meridians).

    5. 厥逆為病也，足暴清，胸若將裂，腸若將以刀切之，煩而不能食，脈大小皆澀，暖取足少陰，清取足陽明，清則補之，溫則寫之。厥逆腹脹滿，腸鳴，胸滿不得息，取之下胸二脅，咳而動手者，與背輸，以手按之，立快者是也。 The reversal of the channels is a disease. The feet are numb and cold, the chest seems about to rupture, the intestines seem about to be cut with a knife, and one is bothered but cannot eat. Both pulse readings are irregular. Take warm from the Kidney Yin and clear from the Foot-Yangming. If it clears, reinforce; if it warms, disperse. The reversal of the channels causes abdominal distention, the feet feel heavy, and the chest is full and cannot be exhaled; take from the lower abdomen at both Kidney positions, make the person cough and move their hands, and that's it.

    6. 內閉不得溲，刺足少陰太陽，與抵上以長鍼。氣逆，則取其太陰、陽明、厥陰，甚取少陰、陽明，動者之經也。 Internal blockage, not allowed to be released. Puncture the foot's Kidney-1 (Bubbling Spring) and join with the long needle. If the Qi is reversed, then take its Spleen-6 (Three Mile Point), Stomach-40 (Joining Vessel), Liver-8 (Governor Vessel) and even more so for Spleen-10 (Liv 3), Stomach-44 (Communion Point). These are the moving channels.

    7. 少氣，身漯漯也，言吸吸也，骨痠體重，懈惰不能動，補足少陰。短氣息短，不屬，動作氣索，補足少陰，去血絡也。 Weak, the body trembling and weak-voiced, bones aching and heavy, unable to move due to laziness. Supplements the kidney Yang. Short breath and short exhalation, not fitting, movements lacking in Qi, supplementing the kidney Yang, expelling blood stagnation.
