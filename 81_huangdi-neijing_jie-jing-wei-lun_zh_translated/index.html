    解精微論

    1. 黃帝在明堂，雷公請曰：臣授業傳之，行教以經論，從容形法，陰陽刺灸，湯藥所滋，行治有賢不肖，未必能十全。若先言悲哀喜怒，燥濕寒暑，陰陽婦女，請問其所以然者，卑賤富貴，人之形體所從，群下通使，臨事以適道術，謹聞命矣。請問有毚愚仆漏之問，不在經者，欲聞其狀。帝曰：大矣。公請問：哭泣而淚不出者，若出而少涕，其故何也。帝曰：在經有也。復問：不知水所從生，涕所從出也。帝曰：若問此者，無益於治也，工之所知，道之所生也。 Yellow Emperor was in the Hall of Clear Illumination, Thunder Master asked: I humbly submit to transmit the Classic of Changes. I will teach through the Book of Changes, focusing on explaining the inner workings of yin and yang, acupuncture and moxibustion techniques, formulating prescriptions that nourish life, and skillfully practicing medicine. There may be sages who are not inferior. As for the question of why some people can easily grasp this classic while others cannot, it is due to differences in their social status, and the physical forms of people come from this as well. The masses can use these methods when faced with situations, and they will listen to my command. I have a question about my humble understanding: if there are any mistakes or misunderstandings in my explanation, please correct me. Yellow Emperor said: That is very good. Thunder Master asked: What about those who cry but do not shed tears? Why is this the case? Yellow Emperor replied: There is a principle in the Classic of Changes. Further asking: I don't know where water comes from or where tears come from. Yellow Emperor said: If you ask about these things, it will be of no benefit to your practice. These are techniques that craftsmen know and principles that arise from the Dao.

    2. 夫心者，五藏之專精也，目者，其竅也，華色者，其榮也，是以人有德也，則氣和於目，有亡，憂知於色。是以悲哀則泣下，泣下水所由生。水宗者，積水也，積水者，至陰也，至陰者，腎之精也。宗精之水，所以不出者，是精持之也。輔之裹之，故水不行也。夫水之精為志，火之精為神，水火相感，神志俱悲，是以目之水生也。故諺言曰：心悲名曰志悲，志與心精，共湊於目也。是以俱悲則神氣傳於心，精上不傳於志，而志獨悲，故泣出也。 The heart is the essence of the five organs, the eyes are their orifices, and the radiant color is their glory. Thus, when a person has virtue, the qi harmonizes with the eyes, and there is no sorrow and knowledge of color. Therefore, when sadness and grief arise, one cries and sheds tears. The water that arises from crying is due to the accumulation of water. Accumulated water is the ultimate yin, and the ultimate yin is the essence of the kidneys. The essence of the kidneys gives rise to water, which does not go out because it is held firmly in place. It is wrapped and contained, so the water does not move. The essence of water gives rise to intention, and the essence of fire gives rise to spirit. Water and fire mutually inform each other, and both intention and spirit are sorrowful, giving rise to the watery tears of the eyes. Hence, there is a saying: The heart's sadness is called intention, and intention and the heart's essence blend together in the eyes. Therefore, when both are sorrowful, the vital qi flows to the heart, and the essence does not rise to the intention, while the intention alone is sorrowful, and this causes tears to flow out.

    3. 泣涕者，腦也，腦者，陰也，髓者，骨之充也，故腦滲為涕。志者，骨之主也，是以水流而涕從之者，其行類也。夫涕之與泣者，譬如人之兄弟，急則俱死，生則俱生，其志以早悲，是以涕泣俱出而橫行也。夫人涕泣俱出而相從者，所屬之類也。 Those who weep and shed tears, it is due to the brain. The brain is yin energy, and the marrow fills the bones. Thus, when the brain is filled with water, tears flow forth. The will, residing in the bones, causes the water to flow and produces tears. Comparing tears and weeping to brothers, if they are hurried, both will die; if they live, both will live. Their shared desire for early grief leads tears and weeping to come out together and run horizontally. Those who weep and produce tears following one another, share the same category.

    4. 雷公曰：大矣！請問人哭泣而淚不出者，若出而少涕，不從之何也？帝曰：夫泣不出者，哭不悲也；不泣者，神不慈也。神不慈則志不悲，陰陽相持，泣安能獨來？夫志悲者惋，惋則沖陰，沖陰則志去目，志去則神不守精，精神去目，涕泣出也。且子獨不誦不念夫經言乎？厥則目無所見，夫人厥則陽氣并於上，陰氣并於下。陽并於上則火燭光也，陰并於下則足寒，足寒則脹也。夫一水不勝五火，故目眥盲。是以衝風，泣下而不止。夫風之中目也，陽氣內守於精，是火氣燔目，故見風則泣下也。有以比之，夫火疾風生，乃能雨，此之類也。 Lei Gong said: Great indeed! The emperor asked, If a person weeps but the tears do not come out, and if they come out but are insufficient, why are they not in accord with this principle? The emperor replied: Weeping that does not produce tears is not mourning; not weeping shows that the spirit is not compassionate. If the spirit is not compassionate, then the will is not sorrowful. Yin and Yang balance each other; how can weeping come out on its own? The will is sorrowful, causing a leak of Yin, which leads to the loss of the will, causing the spirit not to guard the spirit-stuff, and the spirit-stuff goes out with the tears. Moreover, Confucius, you alone do not recite or think on the Classics? When the person is exhausted, the eyes have nothing to see; when a woman is exhausted, Yang-qi is concentrated above and Yin-qi below. When Yang is concentrated above, there is light from the fire torch; when Yin is concentrated below, there is coldness in the feet, which causes swelling. Since water cannot overcome five fires, the eyes are blinded. Therefore, rushing against wind, one weeps but does not stop. Wind is born from fire, and Yang-qi is guarded within the spirit, so the fire-qi burns the eyes, causing one to weep when encountering wind. There is a comparison: just as fire produced by rapid wind can produce rain, this is its type.
