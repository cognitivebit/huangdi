    雜病

    1. 厥挾脊而痛者，至頂，頭沉沉然，目𥆨𥆨然，腰脊強。取足太陽膕中血絡。 For a person with pain in the spine, when they arrive at the top [of the condition], their head feels heavy and their eyes are sunken. Their waist and back feel strong. Apply pressure to the Joint Well point on the sole of the foot.

    2. 厥胸滿面腫，唇漯漯然，暴言難，甚則不能言，取足陽明。 The chest is bloated, the face swollen, the mouth drools, speech is difficult, sometimes it's impossible to speak, and taking the Yang Ming pulse.

    3. 厥氣走喉而不能言，手足清，大便不利，取足少陰。 The qi is not circulating through the throat, making it difficult to speak. The hands and feet are cold, bowel movements are infrequent, and the pulse is shallow in the left foot.

    4. 厥而腹嚮嚮然，多寒氣，腹中穀穀，便溲難，取足太陰。 The abdomen was moving and tumbling, there was much cold qi, grains in the abdomen were scattered, defecation was difficult, and the foot too yang was taken.

    5. 嗌乾，口中熱如膠，取足少陰。 Dry up the leaky faucet, the saliva in your mouth is as hot as glue, and take a little of the Small Yin.

    This passage appears to be an idiomatic or colloquial expression. The first part seems to refer to stopping something that is dripping or leaking. The phrase saliva in your mouth is as hot as glue could be a figure of speech meaning to have difficulty speaking, while the mention of Small Yin might refer to an herbal remedy used in traditional Chinese medicine for treating certain conditions.

    1. 膝中痛，取犢鼻，以員利鍼，發而間之。鍼大如氂，刺膝無疑。 The knee hurts, take a rooster comb, use an employee's acupuncture needle, and insert it between. The needle is as large as a weasel, without a doubt, the needle penetrates the knee.

    2. 喉痺不能言，取足陽明；能言，取手陽明。 If one cannot speak due to a throat obstruction, take the Yang Ming from the foot; if one can speak, take the Yang Ming from the hand.

    3. 瘧不渴，間日而作，取足陽明；渴而日作，取手陽明。 If you're thirsty and your body is hot during the day, take Yang Ming from the foot. If you're thirsty and your body is hot during the night, take Yang Ming from the hand.

    4. 齒痛，不惡清飲，取足陽明；惡清飲，取手陽明。 Toothache, not averse to clear drinks, take the Jiao Yin; averse to clear drinks, take the Shé Yín.

    5. 聾而不痛者，取足少陽；聾而痛者，取手陽明。 For those who are deaf but not in pain, take the foot Jueyin; for those who are deaf and in pain, take the Hand Yangming.

    6. 衄而不止，衄血流，取足太陽；衄血，取手太陽。不已，刺宛骨下；不已，刺膕中出血。 Bleeding does not stop, bleeding blood, take foot Taiyang; Bleeding, take hand Taiyang. Not yet done, acupuncture Yuan point below; Not yet done, acupuncture Ligament channel bleeding.

    7. 腰痛，痛上寒，取足太陽陽明；痛上熱，取足厥陰；不可以俛仰，取足少陽。中熱而喘，取足少陰膕中血絡。 Back pain, with pain cold rising, take foot Taiyang and Yangming; with pain hot rising, take foot Wei Yin.     8. 喜怒而不欲食，言益小，刺足太陰；怒而多言，刺足少陽。 Desiring food but not eating, speaking less, and needing more acupuncture at the small intesture; getting angry and talking a lot, needing more acupuncture at the bladder.

    9. 顑痛，刺手陽明與顑之盛脈出血。 Achilles, the doctor Yang Ming pierced his pulse and blood flowed out.

    10. 項痛不可俛仰，刺足太陽；不可以顧，刺手太陽也。 If there is pain in the area of the armpit, do not lean over; acupuncture the sun at the foot; and do not look back, also acupuncture the sun at the hand.

    11. 小腹滿大，上走胃，至心，淅淅身時寒熱，小便不利，取足厥陰。 The stomach is distended and full, the qi ascends to the chest, disturbing the heart, causing intermittent feelings of coldness and heat, difficult urination, and use the foot-Taiyang (Exterior) method.

    12. 腹滿，大便不利，腹大，亦上走胸嗌，喘息喝喝然，取足少陰。 Full, constipated bowel movements, abdomen enlarged, also ascending and descending urethral discharge, wheezing and swallowing, taking little yin foot.

    13. 腹滿食不化，腹嚮嚮然，不能大便，取足太陰。 Full of food but unable to transform, the stomach is rumbling and one cannot defecate. Take sufficient Pinellia.

    14. 心痛引腰脊，欲嘔，取足少陰。 Heartache causing pain in the back, wanting to vomit, taking the foot-Taiyang.

    Translation: Heartache causing back pain and nausea, use Taiyang (foot) acupuncture.

    1. 心痛，腹脹，牆牆然，大便不利，取足太陰。 Heartache, bloated abdomen, feeling confined, difficult bowel movements, take the foot Taiyin.

    2. 心痛，引背不得息，刺足少陰；不已，取手少陽。 Heartache, causing one to take shallow breaths and lean back; accompanied by stabbing pain in the soles of the feet. This does not subside, leading one to grasp at the wrist joint.

    3. 心痛引小腹滿，上下無常處，便溲難，刺足厥陰。 Heartache causing pain in the lower abdomen, unsteady on both sides, difficult to relieve, insert needles at the foot Jueyin.

    4. 心痛，但短氣不足以息，刺手太陰。 Heartache, but the breath is too short to sigh, the pulse is too weak.

    5. 心痛，當九節刺之，按，已刺按之，立已；不已，上下求之，得之立已。 Heartache, when it is at the nine orifices and pressed, if pressed and pierced, it is done; if not done, seek up and down until found, and then it is done

    6. 顑痛，刺足陽明曲周動脈，見血，立已；不已，按人迎於經，立已。 Insert the needle quickly, and stimulate the Yangming channel at the Zhongdu point on the wrist. Observe for bleeding, then cease. If there is no bleeding, press the person firmly against the meridian, observe for any reaction, then cease.

    7. 氣逆上，刺膺中陷者，與下胸動脈。 Upon reversal of Qi, when piercing the diaphragm and sinking into the lower chest, connect with the chest's dynamic vessels.

    8. 腹痛，刺臍左右動脈，已刺按之，立已；不已，刺氣街，已刺按之，立已。 Abdominal pain, puncture the umbilicus left and right to locate the pulse. Already done? Stand up; if not yet, puncture Qi Street and when you have located the pulse, stand up.

    9. 痿厥為四末束悗，乃疾解之，日二；不仁者，十日而知，無休，病已止。 Paralysis caused by the four extremities being numb and bound is treated urgently, resolving within two days; if not compassionate, it will be known in ten days, without rest, and once the illness has ceased.

    10. 歲以草刺鼻，嚏，嚏而已；無息，而疾迎引之，立已；大驚之，亦可已。 Year after year, grass pricks the nose, sneezes, sneezes and that's all; without rest, hastily rushing to greet and catch it, standing already; greatly surprised, it can also be enough.
