    水熱穴論

    1. 黃帝問曰：少陰何以主腎，腎何以主水。歧伯對曰：腎者，至陰也，至陰者，盛水也。肺者，太陰也，少陰者，冬脈也，故其本在腎，其末在肺，皆積水也。帝曰：腎何以能聚水而生病。歧伯曰：腎者，胃之關也，關門不利，故聚水而從其類也，上下溢於皮膚，故為胕腫，胕腫者，聚水而生病也。 Emperor asked: How does the Shao Yin govern the kidney, and how does the kidney govern water? Huang Di answered: The kidney is the root of the Yin, and the Yin is responsible for storing water. The lung is the Ta Yin, Shao Yin is the small intestine channel, so its source is in the kidney and its end is in the lung, all of which are involved in storing water. Emperor asked: How can the kidney be able to gather water and cause disease? Huang Di answered: The kidney is the gateway of the stomach, and if the gateway is not functioning properly, it will gather water according to its kind, and the accumulation will overflow upwards and downwards, causing the skin to swell, and swelling is a disease caused by gathering water.

    2. 帝曰：諸水皆生於腎乎。歧伯曰：腎者，牝藏也，地氣上者屬於腎，而生水液也，故曰至陰。勇而勞甚則腎汗出，腎汗出逢於風，內不得入於藏府，外不得越於皮膚，客於玄府，行於皮裏，傳為胕腫，本之於腎，名曰風水。所謂玄府者，汗空也。 Emperor said: All waters are born from the kidneys. Hui Bao said: The kidneys are the place of yin, anything that is on the surface of the earth belongs to the kidneys and produces water fluid, thus it is called the source of yin. When one is courageous and works hard, the kidney sweats come out, when the kidney sweat encounters wind, it cannot enter the interior of the kidney nor go beyond the skin, it resides in the mysterious palace, moves within the skin, and transfers to the swelling, its origin is from the kidneys, and it is called wind water. As for the mysterious palace, it is the space for sweat.

    3. 帝曰：水俞五十七處者，是何主也。歧伯曰：腎俞五十七穴，積陰之所聚也，水所從出入也。尻上五行行五者，此腎俞。故水病，下為胕腫大腹，上為喘呼，不得臥者，標本俱病，故肺為喘呼，腎為水腫，肺為逆不得臥，分為相輸俱受者，水氣之所留也。伏菟上各二行行五者，此腎之街也。三陰之所交結於腳也。踝上各一行行六者，此腎脈之下行也，名曰太衝。凡五十七穴者，皆藏之陰絡，水之所客也。 The emperor asked: Which ruler governs the fifty-seven points of water? Master Jī said: The renal points number fifty-seven orifices, where energy is stored and from which water enters and exits. When the water channel is diseased, below it there is distention and fullness in the abdomen, above it there is wheezing and panting, and one cannot lie down; this is a classic case of the renal points. Consequently, water pathology results in the lungs causing wheezing and panting, and the kidneys causing water retention. The lungs are unable to rest, with symptoms spreading to other channels as well. This is where water energy gathers. Six meridian points lie above the lower abdomen, which are the renal channel street. The three Yin intersect at the feet. At the ankle, there is one meridian point with six lines, which is the lower pathway of the kidney's meridian, known as Taiyi. All fifty-seven points are associated with hidden yin channels and serve as storage for water energy.

    4. 帝曰：春取絡脈分肉，何也。歧伯曰：春者，木始治，肝氣始生，肝氣急，其風疾，經脈常深，其氣少，不能深入，故取絡脈分肉間。帝曰：夏取盛經分腠，何也。歧伯曰：夏者，火始治，心氣始長，脈瘦氣弱，陽氣留溢，熱熏分腠，內至於經，故取盛經分腠絕膚而病去者，邪居淺也，所謂盛經者，陽脈也。帝曰：秋取經俞，何也。歧伯曰：秋者，金始治，肺將收殺，金將勝火，陽氣在合，陰氣初勝，濕氣及體，陰氣未盛，未能深入，故取俞以寫陰邪，取合以虛陽邪，陽氣始衰，故取於合。帝曰：冬取井榮，何也。歧伯曰：冬者，水始治，腎方閉，陽氣衰少，陰氣堅盛，巨陽伏沈，陽脈乃去，故取井以下陰逆，取榮以實陽氣。故曰：冬取井榮，春不鼽衄，此之謂也。 The Emperor asked: Why do you take the Chong channel in spring? [Response] The Duke said: In spring, wood is beginning to be treated, the liver's Qi is starting to emerge, the liver's Qi is urgent, its wind and rapidity, the channels are always deep, its Qi is insufficient, it cannot penetrate deeply, so you take the Chong channel and divide the flesh. The Emperor asked: Why do you take the abundant channel in summer? [Response] The Duke said: In summer, fire is beginning to be treated, the heart's Qi is starting to grow larger, the pulse becomes thin and the Qi is weak, the Yang Qi accumulates and overflows, the heat scatters in the channels, it is located within the meridians, so you take the abundant channel and divide the tendons while the disease disappears, this evil resides shallowly, what is called an abundant channel is the Yang meridian. The Emperor asked: Why do you take the lung channel in autumn? [Response] The Duke said: In autumn, metal is beginning to be treated, the lungs are about to withdraw and suppress, gold is about to prevail over fire, Yang Qi is present, Yin Qi is starting to win, dampness Qi also enters the body, Yin Qi is not yet full, it cannot penetrate deeply, so you take the Gu for expelling Yin evil, and take the merger for emptying Yang evil, as Yang Qi begins to decline. The Emperor asked: Why do you take the well and brilliance in winter? [Response] The Duke said: In winter, water is beginning to be treated, the kidneys are about to close, Yang Qi declines and is insufficient, Yin Qi is firm and full, grand Yang conceals itself, the Yang meridian has gone, so you take the well for expelling lower Yin reversal, and take brilliance for nourishing Yang Qi. Therefore, it is said: In winter, take the well and brilliance; in spring, there is no harm, this is what is meant.

    5. 帝曰：夫子言治熱病五十九俞，余論其意，未能領別其處，願聞其處，因聞其意。歧伯曰：頭上五行行五者，以越諸陽之熱逆也。大杼膺俞缺盆背俞此八者，以寫胸中之熱也。氣街三里巨虛上下廉此八者，以寫胃中之熱也。雲門髃骨委中髓空此八者，以寫四支之熱也。五藏俞傍五此十者，以寫五藏之熱也。凡此五十九穴者，皆熱之左右也。帝曰：人傷於寒而傳為熱，何也。歧伯曰：夫寒盛，則生熱也。 The Emperor said: Master Zhang asks about the treatment of fever, and I have discussed the meaning of his words, but I am unable to distinguish their significance. I would like to hear more so that I can understand their meaning. Qi Bo replied: There are five lines on the head, representing the reversal of Yang's heat. Eight points involving the chest, shoulder, and back correspond to heat in the chest. Three lines at Gas Street, on a giant vacuity, correspond to heat in the stomach. The eight points at Yunmen's hair, bones, marrow, and hollow represent heat in the arms. Ten points around the five viscera correspond to heat within the viscera. In total, there are fifty-nine points. All of these points are associated with left and right heat. The Emperor asked: If a person is injured by cold, how can it lead to fever? Qi Bo replied: When cold is excessive, it generates heat.
