    五禁

    1: 黃帝問於歧伯曰：余聞刺有五禁，何謂五禁？歧伯曰：禁其不可刺也。黃帝曰：余聞刺有五奪。歧伯曰：無寫其不可奪者也。黃帝曰：余聞刺有五過。歧伯曰：補寫無過其度。黃帝曰：余聞刺有五逆。歧伯曰：病與脈相逆，命曰五逆。黃帝曰：余聞刺有九宜。歧伯曰：明知九鍼之論，是謂九誼。 Yellow Emperor asked Yi Bo, I have heard of five prohibitions in acupuncture. What are the five prohibitions? Yi Bo replied, They refer to what should not be pierced. Yellow Emperor said, I have heard of five things that must be taken away in acupuncture. Yi Bo replied, There is no writing about what should not be taken away. Yellow Emperor said, I have heard of five excesses in acupuncture. Yi Bo replied, It is necessary to supplement without exceeding the limit. Yellow Emperor said, I have heard of five reversals in acupuncture. Yi Bo replied, Disease and pulse are inversely related, and they are called the five reversals. Yellow Emperor said, I have heard of nine indications in acupuncture. Yi Bo replied, Knowing the theories behind the nine needles is what is meant by the nine indications.

    2: 黃帝曰：何謂五禁，願聞其不可刺之時。歧伯曰：甲乙日自乘，無刺實，無發蒙於耳內。丙丁日自乘，無振埃於肩喉廉泉。戊己日自乘四季，無刺腹，去爪寫水。庚辛日自乘，無刺關節於股膝。壬癸日自乘，無刺足脛，是謂五禁。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the meaning of the five prohibitions? I wish to hear when it is not permitted to pierce. Duke of Shao replied: On days beginning with yin, there is no piercing of the ears and no shaving of hair inside the ears. On days beginning with Yang, there is no vigorous brushing at the shoulders, throat, or springs. On days governed by Wood, there is no piercing of the abdomen, and claws are removed to write water. On days governed by Metal, there is no piercing of joints in the knees and elbows. On days governed by Earth, there is no piercing of the heels and Achilles tendons. This is what is known as the five prohibitions.

    3: 黃帝曰：何謂五奪？歧伯曰：形肉已奪，是一奪也；大奪血之後，是二奪也；大汗出之後，是三奪也；大泄之後，是四奪也；新產及大血之後，是五奪也。此皆不可寫。 Yellow Emperor asked, What is meant by the five losses? The Master of Difficulties replied: When the body and flesh have been lost, it constitutes one loss; when the blood has flowed out, it constitutes a second loss; when sweat has been secreted, it constitutes a third loss; when excrement has passed, it constitutes a fourth loss; when new life has emerged along with great blood, it constitutes the fifth loss. All of these are not to be written

    4: 黃帝曰：何謂五逆？歧伯曰：熱病脈靜，汗已出，脈盛躁，是一逆也；病泄，脈洪大，是二逆也；著痺不移䐃肉破，身熱，脈偏絕，是三逆也；淫而奪形、身熱，色夭然白，乃後下血衄，血衄篤重，是謂四逆也；寒熱奪形，脈堅搏，是謂五逆也。 Yellow Emperor asked: What are the five reversals? Huang Bo replied: When a person has a fever and their pulse is still, sweat has already come out, and their pulse is full of agitation, it represents one reversal. When the disease is relieved, the pulse becomes large and surging, this represents the second reversal. When paralysis is not moving and the muscles are breaking down, the person is hot, and the pulse is deviated, this represents the third reversal. When one indulges and loses their shape, the person is hot, the color turns pale and white, and then there is bleeding from below, and the bleeding is heavy, representing the four reversals. When fever leads to loss of shape, and the pulse is rigid and spasmodic, this represents the five reversals.
