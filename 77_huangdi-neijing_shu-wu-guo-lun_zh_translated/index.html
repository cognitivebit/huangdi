    疏五過論

    1: 黃帝曰：嗚呼遠哉。閔閔乎若視深淵，若迎浮雲，視深淵尚可測，迎浮雲莫知其際。聖人之術，為萬民式，論裁志意，必有法則，循經守數，按循醫事，為萬民副，故事有五過四德，汝知之乎。雷公避席再拜曰：臣年幼小，蒙愚以惑，不聞五過與四德，比類形名虛引其經，心無所對。 Yellow Emperor said: Ah, so far away! Like gazing into the abyss and trying to embrace the floating cloud. Looking into the abyss can still be measured, but embracing the floating cloud is uncertain. The art of a sage serves all people, has rules and principles, discerns people's intentions by interpreting the classics, adheres to ancient methods in medicine, benefits all people, and thus there are five constant virtues and four virtuous qualities. You know this, right? Lord of Thunder, offering the second obeisance said: I am young and ignorant, confused by my lack of understanding, never having heard about the five virtues and the four virtues, unable to compare their categories or give them names, let alone understand their meaning.

    2: 帝曰：凡未診病者，必問嘗貴後賤，雖不中邪，病從內生，名曰脫營。嘗富後貧，名曰失精，五氣留連，病有所并。醫工診之，不在藏府，不變軀形，診之而疑，不知病名。身體日減，氣虛無精，病深無氣，洒洒然時驚，病深者，以其外耗於衛，內奪於榮。良工所失，不知病情，此亦治之一過也。凡欲診病者，必問飲食居處，暴樂暴苦，始樂後苦，皆傷精氣，精氣竭絕，形體毀沮。暴怒傷陰，暴喜傷陽，厥氣上行，滿脈去形。愚醫治之，不知補寫，不知病情，精華日脫，邪氣乃并，此治之二過也。善為脈者，必以比類奇恒從容知之，為工而不知道，此診之不足貴，此治之三過也。診有三常，必問貴賤，封君敗傷，及欲侯王。故貴脫勢，雖不中邪，精緻神內傷，身必敗亡。始富後貧，雖不傷邪，皮焦筋屈，痿躄為攣。醫不能嚴，不能動神，外為柔弱，亂至失常，病不能移，則醫事不行，此治之四過也。凡風診者，必知終始，有知餘緒，切脈問名，當合男女。離絕菀結，憂恐喜怒，五藏空虛，血氣離守，工不能知，何術之語。嘗富大傷，斬筋絕脈，身體復行，令澤不息。故傷敗結留，薄歸陽，膿積寒炅。粗工治之，亟刺陰陽，身體解散，四支轉筋，死日有期，醫不能明，不問所發，唯言死日，亦為粗工，此治之五過也。 The emperor said: All those who have not been diagnosed with a disease should be asked if they have ever experienced poverty or wealth, even if they have not fallen ill from evil influences, the illness arises from within and is called detachment of camp. Those who have experienced poverty and wealth are named loss of vitality. The five energies are retained and there is a simultaneous onset of illness. A physician diagnoses this without being in the palace, does not change the shape of the body, doubts the diagnosis and does not know the name of the disease. As time goes by, one's body gradually weakens, vital energy is deficient without strength, the disease is deep and there is shock, and the disease is deep, causing external loss in the guards and internal loss in reputation. A skilled physician who fails to recognize the condition does not know the patient's state, and this is also a shortcoming in treatment.

    Those who wish to diagnose diseases should ask about diet, food, residence, excessive enjoyment or severe suffering, starting with pleasure and ending with suffering, all causing damage to vital energy and strength. Vital energy is exhausted to the extreme, the body is ruined and wasted. Rash anger damages yin, rash joy damages yang, excessive qi rises upward and dissipates the form of the pulse. Foolish physicians treat it without knowing how to supplement or rectify, or knowing the patient's condition, and the treasures continue to dissipate, allowing evil qi to be entangled, resulting in poor treatment.

    Skillful practitioners of the pulse should ask about the noble and humble, cover kings and ministers defeated, and those who desire to become marquises or dukes. Therefore, if one is noble and detached, even without falling ill from evil influences, vital energy is restrained and damaged within, and the body must be destroyed and lost. Those who have experienced wealth and poverty, even without damaging evil influences, skin is withered and tendons are twisted, muscles are stiffened. Physicians who cannot be strict do not know how to mobilize spirit, and externally they are weak and disordered, making it difficult to move the disease, so medical treatment does not work, resulting in poor treatment.

    Those who practice wind diagnosis must know past and future, have knowledge of remnants, examine the pulse and ask for the name, and match men and women. Leaving off accumulation and restraint, fearing excitement and anger, the five organs are empty and vacant, blood and qi are disordered, physicians do not know how to treat, and their language is incomprehensible. There have been those who have experienced great injury, cutting tendons and severing vessels, restoring the body but causing unrest and accumulation of cold, filling the interior with heat. Coarse physicians treat it by hastily piercing yin and yang, disintegrating the body, and turning muscles into joints, dying at a set time. Physicians do not understand why this happens, do not ask where it comes from, they only say that death will occur at a set time, which is also a shortcoming in treatment.

    3: 凡此五者，皆受術不通，人事不明也。故曰：聖人之治病也，必知天地陰陽四時經紀五藏六府雌雄表裏刺灸砭石毒藥所主，從容人事，以明經道，貴賤貧富，各異品理，問年少長，勇怯之理，審於分部，知病本始，八正九候，診必副矣。治病之道，氣內為寶，循求其理，求之不得，過在表裏。守數據治，無失俞理，能行此術，終身不殆。不知俞理，五藏菀熟，癰發六府。診病不審，是謂失常，謹守此治，與經相明，上經下經，揆度陰陽，奇恒五中，決以明堂，審於終始，可以橫行。 These five [aspects] are all lacking in knowledge of the art and not clear about human affairs. Thus it is said: The sages' treatment of illness requires knowing the Heaven and Earth yin and yang four seasons' meridians, five organs, six departments, masculine and feminine, surface and interior points of acupuncture, moxibustion, stone, poisonous drugs, the master, being calm about human affairs, illuminating the classics and the way, valuing nobility and poverty, wealth and destitution, each according to their different principles, asking whether young or old, courageous in their reasoning, assessing them at the sections, knowing the root of the disease, the eight regularities and nine symptoms, making a diagnosis. The method of treating illness involves the Qi inside being precious, seeking its principle, seeking it without success lies in the exterior and interior. Guard the numbers in treatment, without failing the principles, those who can practice this art will not be exhausted in their lifetime. Not knowing the principles, the five organs wither and bloom, sores appear in the six departments. Diagnosing diseases is not thorough, that is called deviating from the norm, carefully guarding this treatment, corresponding to the classics, above the classics and below the classics, measuring the yin and yang, the strange persistence of the five centers, deciding by the clear hall, assessing at the beginning and end, it can be crossed horizontally.
