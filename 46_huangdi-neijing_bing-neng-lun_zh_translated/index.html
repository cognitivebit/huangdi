    病能論

    1. 黃帝問曰：人病胃脘癰者，診當何如。歧伯對曰：診此者，當候胃脈，其脈當沈細，沈細者氣逆，逆者，人迎甚盛，甚盛則熱，人迎者胃脈也，逆而盛，則熱聚於胃口而不行，故胃脘為癰也。 Emperor Yellow asked: How should one diagnose a person with abdominal pain and jaundice? Master Qi replied: To diagnose this condition, one must examine the pulse at the lower abdomen. The pulse should be shallow and weak. Weakness indicates that qi is flowing in reverse, causing the symptoms to be quite pronounced. When these symptoms are intense, heat accumulates in the stomach region and does not disperse properly, resulting in jaundice. Thus, the name abdominal pain and jaundice.

    2. 帝曰：善。人有臥而有所不安者何也。歧伯曰：藏有所傷，及精有所之寄則安，故人不能懸其病也。 The Emperor said: Good. What kind of person is someone who lies down but feels uneasy? Duke Zhou said: When there is something hidden that hurts and the vitality has a place to rest, then one can be at ease, so people cannot simply hang their illnesses

    3. 帝曰：人之不得偃臥者，何也。岐伯曰：肺者，藏之蓋也，肺氣盛則脈大，脈大則不得偃臥，論在奇恒陰陽中。 The Emperor asked: Why can't some people lie down and rest? Qi Bo replied: The lungs are the container for the vital energy. When the lung energy is abundant, the pulse becomes strong, causing difficulty in lying down or resting, and this relates to the discussion of the odd and even principles of yin and yang

    4. 帝曰：有病厥者，診右脈沈而緊，左脈浮而遲，不然病主安在。歧伯曰：冬診之右脈固當沈緊，此應四時，左脈浮而遲，此逆四時。在左當主病在腎，頗關在肺，當腰痛也。帝曰：何以言之。歧伯曰：少陰脈貫腎絡肺，今得肺脈，腎為之病，故腎為腰痛之病也。 The emperor asked: A patient with a severe illness, examining the right radix and finding it sunken and tense while the left radix is floating and slow - if this is not the case, where is the disease located?

    Huang Di responded: In winter, the right radix should be sunken and tense; this is consistent with the four seasons. The left radix floats and is slow - this is contrary to the four seasons. If it is on the left side, then the disease is located in the kidneys; if it is on the right side, it may relate to the lungs, and it could be a case of lower back pain.

    The emperor asked: How do you know this? Huang Di replied: The yang ming vessel passes through both the kidneys and lungs. With an examination of the lung radix, there is disease in the kidneys, hence the kidney is related to lower back pain.

    1. 帝曰：善。有病頸癰者，或石治之，或鍼灸治之，而皆已，其真安在。歧伯曰：此同名異等者也。夫癰氣之息者，宜以鍼開除去之；夫氣盛血聚者，宜石而寫之。此所謂同病異治也。 Emperor: Good. For someone with a sore throat and tonsillitis, one may treat it with stones or acupuncture and moxibustion, but in all cases the true nature of the disease remains unknown. Duke Mu said: This is an example of same name, different treatment. In the case of a condition caused by stagnation of qi, acupuncture should be used to open and remove it; in the case of an abundance of qi and blood, stones should be used for cupping therapy. This is called treating the same disease with different methods

    2. 帝曰：有病怒狂者此病安生。歧伯曰：生於陽也，帝曰：陽何以使人狂。歧伯曰：陽氣者因暴折而難決，故善怒也，病名曰陽厥。帝曰：何以知之。歧伯曰：陽明者常動，巨陽少陽不動，不動而動大疾，此其候也。帝曰：治之柰何。歧伯曰：奪其食即已。夫食入於陰，長氣於陽，故奪其食即已。使之服以生鐵洛為飲，夫生鐵洛者，下氣疾也。 The Emperor said, What about someone who is ill and angry? Tongbo said, Such a person is born under the influence of Yang. The Emperor asked, How does Yang cause this? Tongbo replied, Yang qi is easily agitated and difficult to control after being jarred, which makes people prone to anger. This condition is known as Yang excess. The Emperor asked, How do you know this? Tongbo said, The brilliance of Yang constantly changes, but the large Yang and small Yang remain unchanged. They move quickly when they are not supposed to, which indicates the presence of this condition. The Emperor asked, What is the cure? Tongbo replied, Withhold their food, and it will be cured. Food enters through Yin, nourishes Yang, so withholding their food will cure them. Have them drink a decoction of Genihulu, as this herb calms the qi rapidly.

    3. 帝曰：善。有病身熱解墯，汗出如浴，惡風少氣，此為何病。歧伯曰：病名曰酒風。帝曰：治之奈何。歧伯曰：以澤瀉朮各十分，麋銜五分，合以三指撮為後飯。 The Emperor said: Good. There is a disease with fever and sweating like taking a bath, weak breathing due to bad wind, what kind of disease is this? Duke Huan said: The name of the disease is wine wind. The Emperor asked: How to treat it? Duke Huan said: Take 10 parts of Zexie and 10 parts of Mu Tong, mix them with 5 parts of Ge Gen. Boil in water, then strain the decoction into a bowl as dinner.

    4. 所謂深之細者，其中手如鍼也，摩之切之，聚者堅也；博者大也。上經者，言氣之通天也，下經者，言病之變化也，金匱者，決死生也，揆度者，切度之也，奇恒者，言奇病也。所謂奇者，使奇病不得以四時死也，恒者得以四時死也。所謂揆者，方切求之也，言切求其脈理也，度者得其病處，以四時度之也。 Regarding the subtle and profound, its touch is as sharp as a needle, delicate and precise, and firm when assembled; it is broad. Mastering the classics refers to understanding the flow of qi throughout heaven, while mastering the lower classics refers to understanding the changes in disease. Golden needles decide between life and death, while regulating the depth of insertion decides the degree of illness. The constant ones refer to managing strange diseases so that they do not lead to death during the four seasons, while odd ones are able to cause death at the four seasons. Regarding the odd, it prevents strange diseases from causing death during the four seasons; regarding the constant, one can cause death at the four seasons. Regarding regulating, it is a matter of seeking the precise depth, understanding the subtlety of the meridians, and accurately locating the disease before regulating it according to the four seasons.
