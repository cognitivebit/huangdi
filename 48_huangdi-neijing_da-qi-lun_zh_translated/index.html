    大奇論

    1. 肝滿腎滿肺滿皆實，即為腫。肺之雍喘而兩胠滿，肝雍兩胠滿，臥則驚，不得小便；腎雍腳下至少腹滿，脛有大小，髀䯒大跛，易偏枯。 The liver and kidneys are both full, resulting in swelling. The lungs become wheezy, causing the abdomen to be distended, the liver also causes the abdomen to be distended, lying down brings about surprise, one cannot urinate; the kidneys become swollen to the soles of the feet, the ankles have large and small protrusions, the hips are painful and stiff, walking is difficult and there may be withering.

    2. 心脈滿大，癇瘛筋攣。肝脈小急，癇瘛筋攣。肝脈騖暴，有所驚駭，脈不至若瘖，不治自已。腎脈小急，肝脈小急，心脈小急，不鼓皆為瘕。腎肝并沈為石水，并浮為風水，并虛為死，并小絃欲驚。 Great force in the pulse, trembling and spasms. Liver pulse is small and rapid, trembling and spasms. Liver pulse suddenly violent, with shock and awe, the pulse does not reach the extent of palpitation, self-cures without treatment. Kidney pulse small and rapid, liver pulse small and rapid, heart pulse small and rapid, all lacking fullness are called pallor. Kidney and liver combined sink as stone water, combined float as wind water, combined empty as death, and combined thin desire to shock.

    3. 腎脈大急沈，肝脈大急沈，皆為疝，心脈搏滑急為心疝。肺脈沈搏為肺疝。三陽急為瘕，三陰急為疝，二陰急為癇厥，二陽急為驚。 The liver pulse is large and rapid, and it is deficient; the kidney pulse is also large and rapid, indicating urinary incontinence. The heart pulse is irregular and rapid, indicating heart palpitations. The lung pulse is deep and irregular, indicating lung disease. When the yang pulses are rapid, it indicates consumption (or wasting). When the yin pulses are rapid, it indicates convulsions or stroke.

    4. 脾脈外鼓，沈為腸澼，久自已，肝脈小緩為腸澼易治。腎脈小搏，沈為腸澼，下血血溫身熱者死。心肝澼亦下血，二藏同病者可治。其脈小沈濇為腸澼，其身熱者死，熱見七日死。 The pulse on the left side of the abdomen is weak, the bladder is sinking, and it has been like this for a long time. The liver pulse is slightly delayed, causing the bladder to sink easily and can be treated. The kidney pulse is faint, causing the bladder to sink and internal bleeding leading to death. If both heart and liver are involved in the sinking of the bladder, they can be treated together. A weak pulse with slight sinking in the abdomen causes the bladder to sink and the patient's body temperature becomes high. The fever lasts for seven days and leads to death.

    5. 胃脈沈鼓濇胃外鼓大，心脈小堅急，皆鬲偏枯。男子發左，女子發右，不瘖舌轉，可治，三十日起。其從者瘖，三歲起。年不滿二十者，三歲死。 The stomach pulse is weak and faint, the outer pulse is large and throbbing. The heart pulse is small, tight, and urgent, all showing signs of being out of balance. Male patients have a left-sided pulse, while female patients have a right-sided pulse. If the tongue is not smooth and changes color, it can be treated, starting on the third day. For those who follow this treatment, if they have rashness, it starts on the third day. For children under two years old, they will die on the third day

    6. 脈至而搏，血衂身熱者死，脈來懸鉤浮為常脈。脈至如喘名曰暴厥，暴厥者，不知與人言。脈至如數，使人暴驚，三四日自已。 When the pulse arrives, it struggles; when the blood congeals and the body is hot, death ensues. When the pulse comes, it hangs and floats like a common pulse. When the pulse arrives like gasping, it is called violent apoplexy, and those suffering from it cannot communicate with others. When the pulse arrives like counting, it startles people and lasts for three or four days.

    7. 脈至浮合，浮合如數，一息十至以上，是經氣予不足也，微見九十日死，脈至如火薪然，是心精之予奪也，草乾而死；脈至如散葉，是肝氣予虛也，木葉落而死；脈至如省客，省客者，脈塞而鼓，是腎氣予不足也，懸去棗華而死；脈至如丸泥是胃精予不足也，榆莢落而死；脈至如橫格，是膽氣予不足也，禾熟而死；脈至如弦縷，是胞精予不足也，病善言，下霜而死，不言可治；脈至如交漆，交漆者，左右傍至也，微見三十日死；脈至如涌泉浮鼓，肌中太陽氣予不足也，少氣味，韭英而死。脈至如頹土之狀，按之不得，是肌氣予不足也，五色先見黑白壘發死；脈至如懸雍，懸雍者浮，揣切之益大，是十二俞之予不足也，水凝而死；脈至如偃刀，偃刀者，浮之小急，按之堅大急，五藏菀熟，寒熱獨并於腎也，如此其人不得坐，立春而死，脈至如丸滑不直手，不直手者，按之不可得也，是大腸氣予不足也，棗葉生而死；脈至如華者，令人善恐，不欲坐臥，行立常聽，是小腸氣予不足也，季秋而死。 The pulse is described as being in various states, leading to different deaths.

    8. Pulse at the state of floating together, like numbers, one breath of ten counts, it indicates insufficient Qi from the channels; a hint of death in ninety days. The person dies when the grass dries up.
    9. Pulse at the state of scattered leaves, indicates deficient liver Qi; the leaves fall and the person dies.
    10. Pulse at the state of stalls, the stall being when the pulse is blocked but the armpits pound; insufficient kidney Qi; the peach blossoms wither and the person dies.
    11. Pulse at the state of baked clay, indicates deficient stomach Qi; the jiuweed withers and the person dies.
    12. Pulse at the state of planks, indicates deficient gallbladder Qi; the wheat ripens and the person dies.
    13. Pulse at the state of twisted strings, indicates deficient uterine Qi; the patient's speech is good but has a white discharge and dies.
    14. Pulse at the state of lacquered joinery, means that the left and right are about to happen; hint of death in thirty days.
    15. Pulse at the state of gushing springs and pounding, it's the Yang Qi from the muscles insufficient; shortness of breath, mugwort flowers bloom and the person dies.
    16. Pulse at the state of rotten earth, press it and it doesn't sink in, indicates insufficient muscle Qi; five colors appear earlier than black and white, hint of death seen in black and white.
    17. Pulse at the state of suspended jade, hangs but is not stable, indicates insufficient Qi of the twelve meridians; water condenses and the person dies.
    18. Pulse at the state of lying sword, means floating in a small urgency, pressing it is strong and urgent, the five viscera are ripe, cold and heat are mixed only in the kidney, like this the person cannot sit, stands in spring and dies. Pulse at the state of curved but not straight hands, indicates insufficient large intestine Qi; peach blossoms bloom and the person dies.
    19. Pulse at the state of flowers, makes people feel fearful, do not want to sit or lie down, walking standing always listens, indicates deficient small intestine Qi; autumn hint seen, dies in autumn.
