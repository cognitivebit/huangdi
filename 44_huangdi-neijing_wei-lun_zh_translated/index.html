    痿論

    1. 黃帝問曰：五藏使人痿，何也。歧伯對曰：肺主身之皮毛，心主身之血脈，肝主身之筋膜，脾主身之肌肉，腎主身之骨髓。故肺熱葉焦，則皮毛虛弱，急薄著則生痿躄也；心氣熱，則下脈厥而上，上則下脈虛，虛則生脈痿，樞折挈，脛縱而不任地也；肝氣熱，則膽泄口苦筋膜乾，筋膜乾則筋急而攣，發為筋痿；脾氣熱，則胃乾而渴，肌肉不仁，發為肉痿；腎氣熱，則腰脊不舉，骨枯而髓減，發為骨痿。 Yellow Emperor asked, The Five Organs cause impotence, why is that? The Grand Officer replied, The Lung governs the skin and hair of the body, the Heart governs the blood vessels of the body, the Liver governs the tendons and membranes of the body, the Spleen governs the muscle tissue of the body, and the Kidney governs the bone marrow of the body. Therefore, when Lung is heated, the skin and hair become weak, and if they are not properly nourished, it leads to impotence and involuntary discharge; when Heart's Qi is heated, the blood vessels below become deficient while those above become excessive, leading to deficiency of blood vessels below, which results in weak pulse, causing impotence, limp erections, and inability to stand upright; when Liver's Qi is heated, the Gallbladder leaks, causing bitterness, drying up of tendons and membranes, resulting in stiff and spasmodic muscles, leading to impotence; when Spleen's Qi is heated, the Stomach becomes dry and one experiences thirst, muscle tissue not being nourished properly leads to muscle weakness, causing atrophy of muscle tissue; when Kidney's Qi is heated, the backbone does not support properly, bones become brittle and marrow decreases, resulting in impotence due to weak bones.

    2. 帝曰：何以得之。歧伯曰：肺者藏之長也，為心之蓋也；有所失亡，所求不得，則發肺鳴，鳴則肺熱葉焦，故曰：五藏因肺熱葉焦，發為痿躄，此之謂也。悲哀太甚，則胞絡絕，胞絡絕，則陽氣內動，發則心下崩數溲血也。故《本病》曰：大經空虛，發為肌痺，傳為脈痿。思想無窮，所願不得，意淫於外，入房太甚，宗筋弛縱，發為筋痿，及為白淫。故《下經》曰：筋痿者，生於肝使內也。有漸於濕以水為事，若有所留居處相濕，肌肉濡漬，痺而不仁，發為肉痿。故《下經》曰：肉痿者，得之濕地也。有所遠行勞倦，逢大熱而渴，渴則陽氣內伐，內伐則熱舍於腎，腎者，水藏也，今水不勝火，則骨枯而髓虛，故足不任身，發為骨痿。故《下經》曰，骨痿者，生於大熱也。帝曰：何以別之。歧伯曰：肺熱者，色白而毛敗；心熱者，色赤而絡脈溢，肝熱者，色蒼而爪枯；脾熱者，色黃而肉蠕動；腎熱者，色黑而齒槁。 The Emperor asked, How can it be obtained? The Duke of Tang replied: The lungs are the storehouse of Qi and serve as a cover for the heart; when there is something lost or missing and one cannot obtain what they seek, the lungs will emit a sound, and once there is a sound, the lungs will heat and burn the leaves, thus causing it to be called 'five storehouses due to lung heat and burning of the leaves.' It is said: when grief is too great, the channels will stop, and once the channels have stopped, the Yang Qi will move internally, leading to palpitations and bleeding below the heart. Therefore, in the treatise on Originating Diseases, it is said: 'Great vacuity leads to muscle paralysis, and this is called the disease.' Contemplation without end results in desires not being fulfilled, and one's intentions become excessive outside, leading to a dwelling place that is too wet. The muscles become flaccid and unable to tendon reflex, resulting in muscle weakness. Therefore, in the treatise on Diseases of the Extremities, it is said: 'Muscle weakness comes from a damp environment.' When one engages in distant travel and becomes tired, encountering great heat while thirsty, the Yang Qi retreats internally, causing the heat to reside in the kidneys, which are the water storehouse. If water cannot withstand fire, then bones will dry up and marrow will be depleted, resulting in weakness of the limbs. Therefore, in the treatise on Diseases of the Extremities, it is said: 'Bone weakness comes from great heat.' The Emperor asked, How can they be distinguished? The Duke of Tang replied: Lung heat manifests as a pale complexion and hair falling; heart heat manifests as a reddish complexion with overflowing channels and vessels; liver heat manifests as a blue-green complexion with nails becoming dry; spleen heat manifests as a yellow complexion with muscle tremors; kidney heat manifests as a dark complexion with teeth becoming worn.

    3. 帝曰：如夫子言可矣，論言治痿者獨取陽明何也。歧伯曰：陽明者，五藏六府之海，主閏宗筋，宗筋主束骨而利機關也。衝脈者經脈之海也，主滲灌谿谷，與陽明合於宗筋，陰陽揔宗筋之會，會於氣街，而陽明為之長，皆屬於帶脈，而絡於督脈。故陽明虛則宗筋縱，帶脈不引，故足痿不用也。帝曰：治之柰何。歧伯曰：各補其滎而通其俞，調其虛實，和其逆順，筋脈骨肉，各以其時受月，則病已矣。帝曰：善。 Emperor said: If Master's words are true, how can one take the Yangming for treating paralysis? Hui Bo said: The Yangming is the sea of the Five Zang and Six Fu organs, which governs the Yuan Shen and Gu Qi, and the Yuan Shen governs binding the bones and nourishing the Jing Luo vessels. The Jing vessels are the sea of the Conception Vessel, which governs the Gui and Luo rivers, and merge with the Yangming at the Yuan Shen, where the Yin and Yang meet the Yuan Shen, meeting at the Qi Street. In this case, the Yangming provides length, and all belong to the Conception Vessel, and are governed by the Governor Vessel. Therefore, if the Yangming is deficient, the Yuan Shen is disordered, the Conception Vessel does not guide, and thus the paralysis occurs. Emperor asked: How can it be treated? Hui Bo said: Each organ should be supplemented and opened accordingly, regulate its excess and deficiency, harmonize its ascending and descending, sinews, vessels, bones, and flesh should each be governed by their respective time to receive the moon phase, then the illness will resolve. Emperor said: Good.
