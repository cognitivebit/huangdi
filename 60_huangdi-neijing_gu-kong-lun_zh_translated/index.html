    骨空論

    1: 黃帝問曰：余聞風者百病之始也，以鍼治之，柰何。歧伯對曰：風從外入，令人振寒，汗出頭痛，身重惡寒，治在風府，調其陰陽，不足則補，有餘則寫。 Yellow Emperor asked: I have heard that wind is the beginning of a hundred diseases, and it is treated with acupuncture. What do you think? Huang Di replied: Wind enters from outside, causing people to shiver, sweating headache, feeling heavy and uncomfortable due to cold, treatment lies in the Wind Palace, regulating Yin and Yang, not enough then supplemented, and if there is excess then remove it.

    2: 大風頸項痛，刺風府，風府在上椎。大風汗出，灸譩譆，譩譆在背下俠脊傍三寸所，厭之令病者呼譩譆，譩譆應手。從風憎風，刺眉頭。失枕在肩上棋骨閒，折使榆臂齊肘正，灸脊中。䏚絡季脇引少腹而痛脹，刺譩譆。腰痛不可以轉搖，急引陰鯤刺八髎與痛上，八髎在腰尻分閒。 Great Wind Neck Pain, Stab Wind Palace, Wind Palace is located in the Bladder Channel. Great Wind Sweat, Gua Sha the Shoulder Well, the Shoulder Well is three cun behind the shoulder blade on the back side of the scapula. Dislike Wind, needle between the eyebrows. Failing to find the pillow on the shoulder, move the arm to have the elbow straight and center at the wrist, Gua Sha the midline. The patient feels like a knot, twist the seasonal channels, pinch the small intestine channel, causing pain in the lower abdomen. Urgently needle the Uterus Channel and the 8 Gathering points where there is pain, the 8 Gathering points are located at the hip joints.

    Translation: In case of severe wind-cold, needle the Wind Palace, which is located on the Bladder Meridian. When suffering from a great wind, needle between the eyebrows. If unable to find a pillow on the shoulder, move the arm so that the elbow is straight and the wrist is in line with the centerline of the back. Gua Sha the midline. In cases where the patient feels like there is a knot, twist the seasonal channels, pinch the small intestine channel, causing pain in the lower abdomen. In case of hip pain that cannot be rotated or swayed, urgently needle the Uterus Channel and the 8 Confluence points where there is pain, with the 8 Confluence points located at the hip joints.

    3: 鼠瘻寒熱，還刺寒府，寒府在附膝外解營。取膝上外者使之拜，取足心者使之跪。任脈者，起於中極之下，以上毛際循腹裏上關元至咽喉，上頤循面入目。衝脈者，起於氣街，並少陰之經，俠齊上行，至胸中而散。任脈為病，男子內結七疝，女子帶下瘕聚。衝脈為病，逆氣裏急。督脈為病，脊強反折。督脈者，起於少腹以下骨中央，女子入繫廷孔，其孔，溺孔之端也，其絡循陰器合篡閒繞篡後，別繞臀至少陰，與巨陽中絡者合，少陰上股內後廉，貫脊屬腎，與太陽起於目內眥，上額交巔，上入絡腦，還出別下項循肩髆，內俠脊抵腰中入循膂絡腎。其男子循莖下至篡，與女子等。其少腹直上者貫齊中央，上貫心入喉，上頤環脣，上繫兩目之下中央，此生病從少腹上衝心，而痛不得前後，為衝疝，其女子不孕，癃痔遺溺嗌乾。督脈生病治督脈，治在骨上，甚者在齊下營，其上氣有音者，治其喉，中央在缺盆中者，其病上衝喉者治其漸，漸者上俠頤也。 The text describes various channels or meridians in the body, their locations and functions, as well as the symptoms associated with illnesses affecting them. Here is a rough translation:

    1. (Taiyin) Spleen and Lung Channel: The Spleen channel starts at the inner corner of the knee, ascends along the medial aspect of the leg, crosses the genitals and ascends the anterior abdominal wall to the umbilicus, where it connects with the Lung channel. It then courses up the lateral costal margin to enter the supraclavicular fossa, passes upward through the neck and terminates at the jaw angle.
    2. (Yangming) Large Intestine and Lung Channel: The Large Intestine channel originates at the outer corner of the elbow, ascends along the radial aspect of the forearm to the wrist, where it connects with the Lung channel, passes along the radial border of the hand to the finger tip. It then courses downward through the face and enters the nasal cavity via the nostril.
    3. (Yangwei) Stomach Channel: The Stomach channel starts at the medial aspect of the great toe, ascends the medial side of the leg to the genital area, where it connects with the Spleen and Large Intestine channels. It then courses upward along the anterior midline of the neck and enters the mouth cavity via the upper lip.
    4. (Yangyao) Small Intestine Channel: The Small Intestine channel originates at the outer corner of the eye, courses downward through the face to the shoulder, where it connects with the Stomach channel. It then descends along the lateral aspect of the body to the hip region and ascends the medial aspect of the leg to the medial malleolus, where it connects with the Bladder channel.
    5. (Yangming) Urinary Bladder Channel: The Bladder channel starts at the medial aspect of the medial malleolus, ascends the medial aspect of the leg and spine to the occuput, where it connects with the Small Intestine channel. It then courses downward through the face to the inner corner of the eye.
    6. (Yinwei) Kidney Channel: The Kidney channel starts at the inner aspect of the medial malleolus, ascends along the medial aspect of the leg to the genital area, where it connects with the Spleen and Large Intestine channels. It then courses upward along the anterior midline of the spine and terminates at the occuput.
    7. (Yinqiao) Gallbladder Channel: The Gallbladder channel originates at the outer aspect of the medial malleolus, ascends the lateral aspect of the leg to the hip region, where it connects with the Small Intestine channel. It then courses upward along the posterior midline of the body to the occuput, where it connects with the Liver and Governing Vessel channels.
    8. (Yinbai) Liver Channel: The Liver channel starts at the outer aspect of the great toe, ascends the lateral side of the leg to the hip region, where it connects with the Gallbladder channel. It then courses upward along the anterior midline of the body to the occuput, where it connects with the Governing Vessel and Gallbladder channels.
    9. (Shaoyang) Triple Burner Channel: The Triple Burner channel starts at the inner aspect of the elbow, ascends the medial side of the arm to the wrist, where it connects with the Small Intestine channel. It then courses downward through the face and neck to the axillary region, where it connects with the Gallbladder channel.
    10. (Taiyang) Governor Vessel: The Governing Vessel starts at the occuput, descends along the anterior midline of the body to the perineum, then loops upward through the genitals and ascends the medial aspect of the leg to the medial malleolus, where it connects with the Liver and Gallbladder channels.

    The text also describes various symptoms associated with illnesses affecting these meridians. For example, if the Taiyin (Spleen and Lung) channel is affected, there may be pain in the lower abdomen, shortness of breath, cough, and a sore throat. If the Yangming (Large Intestine and Lung) channel is affected, there may be symptoms such as a runny nose, sneezing, fever, and sore throat.

    4: 蹇膝伸不屈治其楗，坐而膝痛治其機。立而暑解，治其骸關。膝痛，痛及拇指治其膕。坐而膝痛如物隱者治其關。膝痛不可屈伸，治其背內。連䯒若折，治陽明中俞髎。若別治巨陽少陰滎。淫濼脛痠，不能久立，治少陽之維，在外上五寸。輔骨上橫骨下為楗，俠髖為機，膝解為骸關，俠膝之骨為連骸，骸下為輔，輔上為膕，膕上為關，頭橫骨為枕。 Knee pain, bent and cannot straighten; treat the knee. Sitting with knee pain, treating the mechanism. Standing and sweating treated the tendon passage. Knee pain, painful and sore like the little finger, treat the joint. Sitting with knee pain as if hidden by an object, treat the tendon. Knee pain unable to bend or stretch, treat the internal back. Toxic dampness in the kidney channel, as if broken, treat the Yang Ming middle Jiao. If separate, treat the Jue Yin luo. Licentious vaginal discharge, unable to stand for long, treat the Shao Yang source vessel, located outside five cun up. Assistant bone is horizontal above and below the crossbones, Luo connects as the mechanism, knee pain treated as hui guan, the connective bone as connect hui, hui down as wei, wei up as yao, yao up as guan, guan as ting.

    5: 水俞五十七穴者，尻上五行，行五，伏菟上兩行，行五，左右各一行，行五，踝上各一行，行六穴。 The fifty-seven water channels are located: five on the top of the vertebrae, five in each row of the small intestine (which runs above the vertebrae), one line each on the left and right sides (of the body), one line each at the heels, six channels at the ankles.

    6: 髓空在腦後三分，在顱際銳骨之下，一在齗基下，一在項後中復骨下，一在脊骨上空在風府上。脊骨下空在尻骨下空。數髓空在面俠鼻，或骨空在口下當兩肩。兩髆骨空在髆中之陽。臂骨空在臂陽，去踝四寸兩骨空之間。股骨上空在股陽出上膝四寸。䯒骨空在輔骨之上端。股際骨空在毛中動下。尻骨空在髀骨之後相去四寸。肩骨有滲理湊，無髓孔，易髓無空。 The marrow is three cun behind the brain, at the sharp end of the nose and in the hollow beneath the upper teeth, at the back of the collarbone, and on the top of the windpipe. Below the spine is empty space at the level of the sacrum. There are empty spaces in the cheekbones and sometimes in the bones below the lip, near the shoulders. The two pelvic bones have empty spaces at their ends. The bones above the tendons at the crest of the hip joints have empty spaces four cun above the hip joints. Above the sacrum is an empty space in the sacral crest and a space behind the ilium. In the hollow of the ischium, there is an empty space. There is no marrow at the ends of the clavicle, and the bones of the shoulder have no holes for the marrow to be present.

    7: 灸寒熱之法，先灸項大椎，以年為壯數，次灸橛骨，以年為壯數，視背俞陷者灸之，舉臂肩上陷者灸之，兩季脇之間灸之，外踝上絕骨之端灸之，足小指次指間灸之，腨下陷脈灸之，外踝後灸之，缺盆骨上切之堅痛如筋者灸之，膺中陷骨間灸之，掌束骨下灸之，齊下關元三寸灸之，毛際動脈灸之，膝下三寸分閒灸之，足陽明跗上動脈灸之，巔上一灸之，犬所囓之處灸之三壯，即以犬傷病法灸之，凡當灸二十九處。傷食灸之，不已者，必視其經之過於陽者，數刺其俞而藥之。 Acupuncture and Cold-Heat Treatment:

    First, treat according to the nature of the disease (cold or heat), and then proceed as follows: 1. Begin by needling the front junction and great shu points, using age as a criterion for determining the number of needles. 2. Next, needle the yang jing points, again using age as a guide. 3. Needle where the shen renders the back depressed, where the arms are lifted and the shoulders depressed, between the two shu, on the outer ankle where the limbs meet the bone ends, between the first and second toes, at the lower part of the guan where there is a weak pulse, at the outer ankle where there is pain, on the missing pubic bone, at the place where the jin is stiff and painful like muscle, in the depressions of the jing and shu points of the breastbone, at the lower part of the tendon, between the guan and the lower part of the conception vessel three cun, at the hairy margins and on the large blood vessels, at the hollows of the knee three cun apart, on the foot jue where there is a moving blood vessel, on the top one, at the place bitten by dogs, for a total of 29 needling points. 4. For cases of injury to food, continue needling without ceasing, and if necessary, view the channel's tendency to be excessively Yang, and puncture the jing several times and administer medicine
