    營衛生會

    1: 黃帝問于歧伯曰：人焉受氣？陰陽焉會？何氣為營？何氣為衛？營安從生？衛于焉會？老壯不同氣，陰陽異位，願聞其會。歧伯答曰：人受氣于穀，穀入于胃，以傳與肺，五藏六府，皆以受氣，其清者為營，濁者為衛，營在脈中，衛在脈外，營週不休，五十而復大會，陰陽相貫，如環無端，衛氣行于陰二十五度，行于陽二十五度，分為晝夜，故氣至陽而起，至陰而止。故曰日中而陽隴，為重陽，夜半而陰隴為重陰，故太陰主內，太陽主外，各行二十五度分為晝夜。夜半為陰隴，夜半後而為陰衰，平旦陰盡而陽受氣矣。日中而陽隴，日西而陽衰，日入陽盡而陰受氣矣。夜半而大會，萬民皆臥，命曰合陰，平旦陰盡而陽受氣，如是無已，與天地同紀。 Emperor Yellow asked Confucius: Do people receive qi? How do yin and yang meet? What is the qi for defense? What is the qi for nourishment? Is the nourished securely established? Does defense meet with it? The elderly have different qi; yin and yang are not in harmony. I wish to hear about their meeting. Confucius replied: People receive qi from grains, which enter the stomach and pass them on to the lungs. Five zang and six fu are all nourished by receiving qi. The clear qi is for defense, and the turbid qi is for nourishment. Defense remains unrested in the vessels, and after fifty years it returns to a great meeting. Yin and yang intertwine, like a never-ending cycle. The defense qi circulates through the yin 25 times and the yang 25 times, divided into day and night. When the qi reaches yang, it rises; when it reaches yin, it stops. Thus, in the daytime, there is a peak yang, called the 'reinforcing yang,' and at midnight, there is a peak yin, called the 'reinforcing yin.' Therefore, the Taiyin governs inside and the Taiyang governs outside, each having 25 cycles divided into day and night. At midnight, it becomes a peak yin, followed by yin deficiency. At dawn, the yin is exhausted, and the qi begins to receive yang. In the daytime, there is a peak yang as the sun sets, and when it enters the yang, the qi receives yin. At midnight, there is a great meeting, and all people rest; the command is called 'merging yin.' At dawn, the yin is exhausted, and the qi begins to receive yang, just like the harmony between heaven and earth without end.

    2: 黃帝曰：老人之不夜瞑者，何氣使然？少壯之人，不晝瞑者，何氣使然？歧伯答曰：壯者之氣血盛，其肌肉滑，氣道通，營衛之行不失其常，故晝精而夜瞑。老者之氣血衰，其肌肉枯，氣道澀，五藏之氣相搏，其營氣衰少而衛氣內伐，故晝不精，夜不瞑。 Yellow Emperor asked: Why do the elderly not sleep at night, and what Qi makes this possible? Why do the young and vigorous not sleep at night, and what Qi makes this possible? The Duke answered: The young and vigorous have abundant Qi and blood, their muscles are smooth, their meridians are open, and their regulation of internal organs is not deviated from the norm, so they rest during the day and sleep at night. The elderly have weakened Qi and blood, their muscles are withered, their meridians are blocked, the Qi in the five organs fight against each other, and their regulation of internal organs becomes weak while their defensive Qi attacks internally, so they do not rest during the day and sleep at night.

    3: 黃帝曰：願聞營衛之所行，皆何道從來？歧伯答曰：營出于中焦，衛出于下焦。黃帝曰：願聞三焦之所出。歧伯答曰：上焦出於胃上口，並咽以上，貫膈，而布胸中，走腋，循太陰之分而行，還至陽明，上至舌，下足陽明，常與營俱行于陽二十五度，行于陰亦二十五度一周也。故五十度而復太會于手太陰矣。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the functions of the warmer and weaker, and where they come from. Master Qi replied: The warmer comes from the center focus and the weaker comes from the lower focus. Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the three focuses where they come from. Master Qi replied: The upper focus comes out from above the stomach and the throat above, passing through the conception vessel, spreading over the chest, going across the armpit, following the kidney channel, and running its course until it returns to Yang Ming. It ascends to the tongue, goes down to the sole, always accompanying Yang Ming in its course of 25 degrees, and also in its Yin course of 25 degrees in a week. Hence, it meets at the hand of the kidney channel again.

    4: 黃帝曰：人有熱，飲食下胃，其氣未定，汗則出，或出於面，或出於背，或出於身半，其不循衛氣之道而出，何也？歧伯曰：此外傷於風，內開腠理，毛蒸理泄，衛氣走之，固不得循其道，此氣慓悍滑疾，見開而出，故不得從其道，故命曰漏泄。 Yellow Emperor said: In humans, there is heat. Food and drink enter the stomach, but its energy has not yet stabilized, and then sweat will come out, sometimes from the face, sometimes from the back, or from half of the body. This does not follow the path of defensive Qi, why? Duke of Shao said: This is an external injury caused by wind, opening the brain and marrow, causing the sweat glands to release, and making the defensive Qi move uncontrollably. Therefore, it cannot follow its path. This Qi is unruly, quick, and slippery, and appears when opened, so it cannot follow its path. Hence, it is called leakage.

    5: 黃帝曰：願聞中焦之所出。歧伯答曰：中焦亦並胃中，出上焦之後，此所受氣者，泌糟粕，蒸津液，化其精微，上注于肺脈乃化而為血，以奉生身，莫貴於此，故獨得行於經隧，命曰營氣。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the middle Focal point. Zhong Feng answered: The middle Focal point is also in the center of the Stomach, emerging from the Upper Focal point. The substances it receives are transformed by the Spleen and are turned into essence and rise up to the lungs where they become blood. This is extremely precious and unique, so it can only be transmitted through the Meridians, its name being Governing Vessel.

    6: 黃帝曰：夫血之與氣，異名同類。何謂也？歧伯答曰：營衛者，精氣也，血者，神氣也，故血之與氣，異名同類焉。故奪血者無汗，奪汗者無血，故人生有兩死而無兩生。 The Yellow Emperor said: Blood and Qi have different names, but they are of the same kind. What does this mean? The Grand Prefect answered: The Guards and Ministers are the spiritual Qi, and blood is the material Qi; therefore, blood and Qi, although they have different names, belong to the same category. Therefore, if one loses blood, there will be no sweat, and if one loses sweat, there will be no blood; hence, a person has two ways to die but only one way to be born.

    7: 黃帝曰：願聞下焦之所出。歧伯答曰：下焦者，別回腸，注于膀胱，而滲入焉；故水穀者，常并居于胃中，成糟粕，而俱下于大腸而成下焦，滲而俱下。濟泌別汁，循下焦而滲入膀胱焉。 The Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the origin of piles. Huangdi replied: Piles refer to hemorrhoids, which are connected to the colon and attached to the bladder. They exude fluid; thus, cereal grains are always present in the stomach, forming husks and chaff, which are then excreted by the large intestine and form piles. The fluid is secreted through the piles and enters the bladder.

    8: 黃帝曰：人飲酒，酒亦入胃，穀未熟，而小便獨先下，何也？歧伯答曰：酒者，熟穀之液也。其氣悍以清，故後穀而入，先穀而液出焉。黃帝曰：善。余聞上焦如霧，中焦如漚，下焦如瀆，此之謂也。 The Yellow Emperor asked: When a person drinks alcohol, the alcohol enters the stomach; before the grain is ripe, urine comes out first. Why? The Duke of Zhou replied: Alcohol is the essence of ripened grain. Its energy is strong and purifying, so it goes in after the grain and comes out ahead of the grain. The Yellow Emperor said: Good. I have heard that when the upper abdomen is like fog, the middle abdomen is like a tree leaf, and the lower abdomen is like a clam; these are what they refer to.
