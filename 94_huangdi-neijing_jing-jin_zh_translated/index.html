    1. 足太陽之筋，起於足小趾，上結于踝，邪上結于膝，其下循足外側，結于踵，上循跟，結於膕；其別者，結于腨外，上膕中內廉，與膕中并上結于臀，上挾脊上項；其支者，別入結于舌本；其直者，結于枕骨，上頭，下顏，結于鼻；其支者，為目上網，下結于頄；其支者，從腋后外廉結于肩髃；其支者，入腋下，上出缺盆，上結於完骨；其支者，出缺盆，邪上出于頄。其病小趾支跟腫痛，膕攣，脊反折，項筋急，肩不舉，腋支缺盆中紐痛，不可左右搖。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰仲春痺也1。1. 也 : 舊脫。 The sinew of the foot's Yellow Emperor begins at the tip of the small toe, ascends to the heel, and its path upward follows the outer edge of the foot, ending at the ankle; it then loops around the heel, ending at the malleolus. Its other branch ascends alongside the lateral condyle of the tibia, connects with the tendon of the adductor muscle of the thigh, and joins its fellow tendons to connect with the buttock; its supplementary branch enters the resultant and ends at the medial condyle of the humerus. Its direct branch results in the tendon of the supraorbital muscle, connecting to the skull above, descending to the cheekbone and ending at the nose. Its supplementary branch supports the eyebrow above, descending and ending at the nape of the neck; its supplementary branch enters the axilla and ascends to connect with the coracoid process; its supplementary branch enters the inframammary area, ascends to connect with the crest of the ilium, and descends from there. Its supplementary branch exits the crest of the ilium, deviates upward to connect at the nape of the neck. This sinew's disease involves swelling and pain in the tip of the small toe and heel, aching in the waist, bending of the spine, neck pain, inability to raise the shoulders, pain in the axillary tendon, and inability to swing from left to right; it is treated by acupuncture at the Jueyin hole. The number of needles used indicates the severity of the condition, and it is called the Zhong Chun Yao. Note: 也 - Old: Remove

    2. 足少陽之筋，起於小指次指，上結外踝，上循脛外廉，結於膝外廉；其支者，別起外輔骨，上走髀，前者結於伏兔之上，後者，結於尻；其直者，上乘䏚季脅，上走腋前廉，繫於膺乳，結於缺盆；直者，上出腋，貫缺盆，出太陽之前，循耳後，上額角，交巔上，下走頷，上結於頄；支者，結於目眥為外維。其病小指次指支轉筋，引膝外轉筋，膝不可屈伸，膕筋急，前引髀，後引尻，即上乘䏚季脅痛，上引缺盆、膺乳、頸維筋急。從左之右，右目不開，上過右角，并蹻脈而行，左絡於右，故傷左角，右足不用，命曰維筋相交。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰孟春痺也。 The meridian of the small and ring fingers begins at the shallow yang channels, rises from the little and ring fingers, ascends along the outer side of the calf, loops around the crest of the pubic bone, and terminates at the outer side of the groin; its supporting branch starts from the outer aspect of the ilium, ascends to the sacrum, one ending at the top of the scapula and the other at the nape, its direct branch rises over the axilla along the outer aspect of the anterior crest, attaches to the pectoralis major, and terminates in the inguinal region; the direct branch ascends from the axilla, pierces the inguinal ring, exits through the upper opening of the abdomen, runs along the auricle, loops around the superior margin, descends at the jaw, and terminates at the throat; its supporting branch terminates at the lacrimal sac as an external tendon. Its disease involves the little and ring fingers' support and transformation of channels, referring to the inguinal region's transformation channel, causing the knee to be unable to bend or straighten, straining the groin, referring to the ilium, and referring to the sacrum and nape, resulting in pain at the level of the axilla, breast

    3. 足陽明之筋，起於中三指，結於跗上，邪外上加於輔骨，上結於膝外廉，直上結於髀樞，上循脅屬脊；其直者，上循骭，結於膝；其支者，結於外輔骨，合少陽；其直者，上循伏兔，上結於髀，聚於陰器，上腹而布，至缺盆而結，上頸，上挾口，合于頄，下結於鼻，上合於太陽。太陽為目上網，陽明為目下網；其支者，從頰結於耳前。其病足中指支脛轉筋，腳跳堅，伏兔轉筋，髀前踵，㿉疝，腹筋急，引缺盆及頰，卒口僻；急者，目不合，熱則筋縱，目不開，頰筋有寒，則急，引頰移口，有熱則筋弛縱，緩不勝收，故僻。治之以馬膏，膏其急者；以白酒和桂，以塗其緩者，以桑鉤鉤之，即以生桑炭置之坎中，高下以坐等。以膏熨急頰，且飲美酒，敢美炙肉，不飲酒者，自強也，為之三拊而已。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰季春痺也。 The Jīn Chūn Yuán is an acupuncture point located on the foot Yang Míng channel, starting at the middle finger's metacarpophalangeal joint and ending at the heel. Pathological external factors may accumulate above the Cánguān, joining with the Shǎo Yáng channel, ascending to the knee, circling around the sacrum and connecting with the spine; its ascending branch goes up along the Zhènshū, connecting at the pubic region, moves across the abdomen and reaches the mons veneris, then gathers at the genitals. Its supporting branch connects at the pubic region and merges with the Shǎo Yáng channel, while its ascending branch goes up along the Fútù, connecting at the hip, gathers at the yin orifice, moves across the lower abdomen and reaches the pubic region, then passes by the neck and embraces the mouth, merging with the Tàiyáng. The Tàiyáng represents the brows, while the Yang Míng represents the eyes below the brows; its supporting branch starts at the chin and connects at the ear lobe. Its pathology involves the middle finger, ankle, knee joint, inguinal region (pubic area), abdomen, and urogenital system, with symptoms including painful and difficult movement of the hip, pain in the low back and waist, abdominal pain and distension, and swelling and pain in the genitals, face, and mouth. In severe cases, the eyes do not meet, the tongue is deviated, and there may be cold or heat sensations. To treat this condition, apply horse liniment to the urgent areas; use white wine and cinnamon paste for the sluggish areas, and use a loach fishhook on them. Apply fresh loach charcoal in the hollows and have the patient sit with both legs folded or straightened. Apply ointment to the urgent areas around the mouth, and have the patient drink sweet wine, eat roasted meat, and avoid drinking alcohol if possible; this should be done three times for a complete recovery. This treatment is performed during the Jīn Chūn period, and has been called Jīn Chūn Yún for short.

    4. 足太陰之筋，起於大指之端內側，上結於內踝；其直者，絡於膝內輔骨，上循陰股，結於髀，聚於陰器，上腹結於臍，循腹裏，結於肋，散於胸中；其內者，著於脊。其病足大指支內踝痛，轉筋痛，膝內輔骨痛，陰股引髀而痛，陰器紐痛，上引臍兩脅痛，引膺中脊內痛。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，命曰孟秋痺也。 The ligament of the foot's yang minor originates from inside the tip of the great toe and ascends to the inner ankle; its straight part runs through the knee's auxiliary bone, ascending along the yin channel to the thigh, gathering at the pudendum, ascending to the abdomen and connecting at the umbilicus, circulating within the abdomen, and connecting at the ribs, dispersing in the chest; its interior part is attached to the spine. Its disorder involves pain at the great toe supporting the inner ankle, turning tendon pain, pain at the knee's auxiliary bone, referring pain to the thigh, pudendum pain, pain at the umbilicus referring to both hypochondria, and pain at the chest and spine. Treatment is by moxibustion or acupuncture needling at Jueyin, knowing pain as a departure, and called Meng Qiu Yan.

    5. 足少陰之筋，起於小指之下，并足太陰之筋，邪走內踝之下，結於踵，與太陽之筋合，而上結於內輔之下，并太陰之筋，而上循陰股，結於陰器，循脊內挾膂上至項，結於枕骨，與足太陽之筋合。其病足下轉筋，及所過而結者皆痛及轉筋。病在此者，主癎瘈及痙，在外者不能挽，在內者不能仰。故陽病者，腰反折不能俛，陰病者，不能仰。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸。在內者熨引飲藥，此筋折紐，紐發數甚者死不治，名曰仲秋痺也。 Translation: The channels of the foot yang minor and foot shao yin originate from below the little toe and unite with the great yang channel, ascending to join the jue yin channel at the renal depression, forming a loop around the genitals and joining the bladder channel at the occuput, uniting with the great yang channel. Patients with pain in these channels or along the course of the channels will have pain. Diseases arising from within cannot be pulled outward, and those arising internally cannot be elevated. Therefore, diseases of yang channels result in a reversed lumbar curvature that cannot be straightened, while diseases of yin channels result in an inability to be elevated. Treatment involves needle insertion at the appropriate points, using a counter-irritant and draining method, and internally administering heat-producing medicine. If this channel becomes twisted and knotted, the cord will emit signs of danger; if severe, it is fatal and is known as qu zhi yin.

    6. 足厥陰之筋，起於大指之上，上結於內踝之前，上循脛，上結內輔之下，上循陰股，結於陰器，絡諸筋。其病足大指支內踝之前痛，內輔痛，陰股痛轉筋，陰器不用，傷於內則不起，傷於寒則陰縮入，傷於熱則縱挺不收，治在行水清陰氣；其病轉筋者，治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，命曰季秋痺也。 The ligament of the foot's yin (Flexor Hallucis Longus) begins at the top of the big toe, ascends to the front of the inner ankle, loops around the tibia, connects to the adductor muscles, loops around the pubic bone, and attaches to the genitals. When this ligament is injured, there will be pain at the big toe supporting the inner ankle, the adductor muscles, the pubic bone, and difficulty using the genitals. If injured internally, it will not rise; if injured by cold, the yin will retract; if injured by heat, it will stand erect and not retract. Treatment for this condition involves regulating water and clearing the yin energy; for those with pain that refers to other areas (referred pain), treatment involves needing and cupping at strategic points, identifying the pain as Seasonal Imbalance of Qi.

    7. 手太陽之筋，起於小指之上，結於腕，上循臂內廉，結於肘內銳骨之後，彈之應小指之上，入結於腋下；其支者，後走腋後廉，上繞肩胛，循頸出走太陽之前，結於耳後完骨；其支者，入耳中；直者，出耳上，下結於頷，上屬目外眥。其病小指支肘內銳骨後廉痛，循臂陰，入腋下，腋下痛，腋後廉痛，繞肩胛引頸而痛，應耳中鳴痛引頷，目瞑良久乃得視，頸筋急，則為筋瘻頸腫，寒熱在頸者。治在燔鍼劫刺之，以知為數，以痛為輸。其為腫者，復而銳之。本支者，上曲牙，循耳前屬目外眥，上頷結於角，其痛當所過者支轉筋。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰仲夏痺也。 The hand Yang meridian begins at the upper part of the little finger, passes through to the wrist, ascends along the inner aspect of the arm and terminates at the interior sharp bone behind the elbow, twists towards the little finger above and enters into the axillary fossa. Its branch goes downward following the rear aspect of the arm, ascends around the shoulder blade, loops around the clavicle and exits to the front of the yang meridian, terminating at the mastoid process behind the skull bone. Its branch goes into the ear canal. The directing vessel goes out from the top of the ear, descends and terminates at the lower lip, belonging to the outer aspect of the eyebrow. If there is disease affecting the little finger, wrist, interior sharp bone, inner aspect of the arm, axillary fossa, shoulder blade, clavicle, or skull bone, it causes pain. When it spreads downward and causes pain in the throat, it refers to the ear, causing ear pain and causing pain at the lower lip, leading to temporary loss of vision before recovery. If the throat muscle is tight, this can cause yang muscle swelling and neck inflammation, with cold or heat in the neck area. Treatment involves using acupuncture and moxibustion on the jueyin and shaoyang points along the meridian, following the pain for guidance. For swelling, needle in the opposite direction. This is known as the Xia Zhi syndrome.

    8. 手少陽之筋，起於小指次指之端，結於腕，中循臂，結於肘，上繞臑外廉、上肩、走頸，合手太陽；其支者，當曲頰入繫舌本；其支者，上曲牙，循耳前，屬目外眥，上乘頷，結於角。其病當所過者，即支轉筋，舌卷。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰季夏痺也。 The hand's Yang channel begins at the root of the little and ring fingers, passes through the wrist, ascends along the arm, terminates at the elbow, goes around the outside of the elbow, across the shoulder, down the neck, and joins with the other hand's Yang channel. Its connecting point is at the lower lip, attached to the root of the tongue; another connecting point is at the upper gum, ascending in front of the ear, belonging to the external sense organ of the eye, ascending to the mouth, terminating at the horn. When disease occurs along this channel, it manifests as numbness and turning of the affected limb, with the tongue rolling up. The treatment involves needling at the Luo connective point, eliciting a sensation of distending fullness, and releasing, in the context of the Jisheng (Seasonal Evil) invasion. This is known as the Jisheng Syndrome.

    9. 手陽明之筋，起於大指次指之端，結於腕，上循臂，上結於肘外，上臑，結於髃；其支者，繞肩胛，挾脊；直者，從肩髃上頸；其支者，上頰，結於頄；直者，上出手太陽之前，上左角，絡頭，下右頷。其病當所過者，支痛及轉筋，肩不舉，頸不可左右視。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰孟夏痺也。 The Hand Yang Ming Meridian starts at the tip of the thumb and index finger, ascends along the arm, terminates outside the elbow, runs to the wrist, connects with the hair. The branch is wrapped around the shoulder blade and hugs the spine; it goes straight from the nape of the neck to the top of the head; its branch goes up to the forehead, connects at the temples. It goes directly out from the hand Shao Yang point, ascends the left cheek, connects with the hair root, descends the right cheek. If there is a disease along its course, there will be pain and numbness in the support, the shoulder cannot be raised, and the head cannot turn to the left or right. Treatment is available in fire needles and knife-like poking, knowing numbers, bleeding based on pain, called Mengxia Syndrome.

    10. 手太陰之筋，起於大指之上，循指上行，結於魚後，行寸口外側，上循臂，結肘中，上臑內廉，入腋下，出缺盆，結肩前髃，上結缺盆，下結胸裏，散貫賁，合賁下抵季脅。其病當所過者，支轉筋，痛甚成息賁，脅急吐血。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸。名曰仲冬痺也。 The sinew of the hand's yin is located above the great finger, and follows along the finger, terminates at the fish's tail, goes outward from the inch, loops up the arm, terminates in the elbow's center, enters the armpit's interior, exits the uterus' basin, terminates at the shoulder in front of the hair, loops up at the uterus' basin, and terminates inside the chest cavity. It disperses to the tendons and connects to the seasonal gates. When there is illness along its course, the sinew transforms and becomes painful, causing difficulty in breathing and urging vomiting of blood. Treatment involves inserting a needle at Gushing Spring and Bamboo Mound, counting the number of insertions, and treating according to pain. It is called Zhongtan (Middle Winter) Jian.

    11. 手心主之筋，起於中指，與太陰之筋並行，結於肘內廉，上臂陰，結腋下，下散前後挾脅；其支者，入腋，散胸中，結於臂。其病當所過者，支轉筋前及胸痛息賁。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸，名曰孟冬痺也。 The sinew of the hand's heart, originates from the middle finger, runs parallel with the Conception channel, connects at the inner elbow crease, reaches the anterior aspect of the upper arm, spreads downward and extends across the chest; its connecting point is located within the axilla. Its course follows a path beneath the breastbone, connects to the arm, and disperses above and below the chest. When it encounters disease, it manifests as turning cold, pain in the sinew channel, and chest pain with difficulty breathing. Treatment lies in needle insertion at the appropriate points along the sinew channel, following the direction of the pain, and is called Meng Dong Jian.

    12. 手少陰之筋，起於小指之內側，結於銳骨，上結肘內廉，上入腋，交太陰，挾乳裏，結於胸中，循臂下繫於臍。其病內急心承伏梁，下為肘網。其病當所過者，支轉筋，筋痛。治在燔鍼劫刺，以知為數，以痛為輸。其成伏梁唾血膿者，死不治。經筋之病，寒則反折筋急，熱則筋弛縱不收，陰痿不用。陽急則反折，陰急則俛不伸。焠刺者，刺寒急也，熱則筋縱不收，無用燔鍼，名曰季冬痺也。 The hand's yin muscles, beginning inside the little finger and ending at the acromion, pass through the armpit, unite with the conception vessel, embrace the breast, and connect to the chest at the serratus anterior muscle, and descend along the medial side of the arm. When there is disease along its course, it turns and causes pain in the muscles. Treatment involves piercing with an awl or needle, using knowledge as the method and pain as the guide. If the disease leads to a collapse of the trapezius muscle and bleeding of blood and pus, it cannot be cured. The diseases of the channel of the yin muscle are characterized by pain when cold, causing the muscles to contract and spasm. When hot, the muscles become flaccid and do not retract. Deficiency of yin leads to a Yang urgency that causes contraction, and deficiency of yang leads to an inability to stretch. In the case of piercing with an awl or needle, it is for treating pain caused by cold, but if there is heat, the muscles do not retract and are untreatable with moxibustion. This is known as seasonal rigidity (Ji Dong Li).
