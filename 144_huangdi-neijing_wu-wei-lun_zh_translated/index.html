    五味論

    1: 黃帝問於少俞曰：五味入于口也，各有所走，各有所病，酸走筋，多食之，令人癃；鹹走血，多食之，令人渴；辛走氣，多食之，令人洞心；苦走骨，多食之，令人變嘔；甘走肉，多食之，令人挽心。余知其然也，不知其何由？願聞其故。少俞答曰：酸入于胃，其氣澀以收，上之兩焦，弗能出入也，不出即留於胃中，胃中和溫，則下注膀胱，膀胱之胞薄以懦，得酸則縮綣，約而不通，水道不行，故癃。陰者，積筋之所終也，故酸入而走筋矣。 Yellow Emperor asked Shao Yong, Five flavors enter the mouth, each has its own channel and each has its own disease. Sour enters the sinews, eating too much of it causes pain; salty enters the blood, eating too much causes thirst; pungent enters qi, eating too much causes one to feel hollow; bitter enters the bones, eating too much causes vomiting; sweet enters the flesh, eating too much causes palpitations. I know this to be true, but do not know how it works. I hope to hear its cause. Shao Yong replied, Sour enters the stomach, its qi becomes turbid and collects, rising upward to the two fu organs, unable to enter or exit. If it does not exit, it remains in the stomach, which has a balanced environment, causing water to flow downward to the bladder. The bladder's essence is weak, so if there is sour, it contracts and tightens, binds and does not communicate, disrupting the waterway, thus causing pain. Yin refers to the endpoint of filling the sinews, so sour enters and walks the sinews.

    2: 黃帝曰：鹹走血，多食之，令人渴，何也？少俞曰：鹹入于胃；其氣上走中焦，注于脈，則血氣走之，血與鹹相得，則凝，凝則胃中汁注之，注之則胃中竭，竭則咽路焦，故舌本干而善渴。血脈者，中焦之道也，故鹹入而走血矣。 Yellow Emperor said: Why does salty taste cause thirst? Shao Yong said: Salt enters the stomach. Its Qi rises and acts on the spleen, causing the blood and Qi to circulate. When blood and salt meet, they coagulate, and when they coagulate, the fluids in the stomach are exhausted. When they are exhausted, the throat pathway becomes parched, thus the tongue is inherently dry and good at being thirsty. The blood vessels are the channel of the spleen, so salt enters and acts on the blood.

    3: 黃帝曰：辛走氣，多食之，令人洞心，何也？少俞曰：辛入于胃，其氣走於上焦，上焦者，受氣而營諸陽者也，薑韭之氣熏之，營衛之氣，不時受之，久留心下，故洞心。辛與氣俱行，故辛入而與汗俱出。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the reason that cinnamon promotes the flow of Qi, and when people eat more of it, their hearts become clear? Shao Yong replied: Cinnamon enters the spleen, its Qi moves upwards to the middle energy center. The middle energy center is where the Qi is received and nourishes the yang, and ginger and scallion's Qi fumigate it. By timely replenishing the defensive Qi, it stays in the heart area for a long time, thus making hearts clear. When cinnamon and Qi both move, cinnamon enters and goes out with sweating.

    4: 黃帝曰：苦走骨，多食之，令人變嘔，何也？少俞曰：苦入于胃，五穀之氣，皆不能勝苦，苦入下脘，三焦之道，皆閉而不通，故變嘔。齒者，骨之所終也，故苦入而走骨，故入而復出，知其走骨也。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the reason for the bitter walking of the bones and causing people to vomit after eating too much? Shao Yong said: Bitter enters the stomach, the Qi of five grains cannot overcome the bitterness. Bitterness enters the lower chest, the passageways of the three Yang organs are all closed and do not communicate, therefore causing vomiting. In response to this, I say that the teeth are the end of the bones, so when bitterness walks and spreads, it enters and then exits, knowing it is walking the bones.

    5: 黃帝曰：甘走肉，多食之。令人悗心，何也？少俞曰：甘入于胃，其氣弱小，不能上至於上焦，而與谷留於胃中者，令人柔潤者也，胃柔則緩，緩則蟲動，蟲動則令人悗心。其氣外通於肉，故甘走肉。 Yellow Emperor said: Eat more ginseng. Why would it make people's heart palpitate? Shao Yong said: Ginseng enters the stomach and its energy is weak and slight, unable to rise up to the upper abdomen. It stays in the stomach with grains, making people feel softened and relaxed. If the stomach is softened, it calms things down, and when things are calm, insects will move, causing people's heart palpitations. Its energy circulates outside in meat, so ginseng can make one's flesh ache
