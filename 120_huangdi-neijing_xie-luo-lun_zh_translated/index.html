    血絡論

    1: 黃帝曰：願聞其奇邪而不在經者。歧伯曰：血絡是也。 Yellow Emperor said: I want to hear about its strangeness and evil, which is not in the classics. Huang Bo said: It is the network of blood vessels.

    2: 黃帝曰：刺血絡而仆者，何也？血出而射者，何也？血少黑而濁者，何也？血出清而半為汁者，何也？拔鍼而腫者，何也？血出若多若少而面色蒼蒼者，何也？拔鍼而面色不變而煩悗者，何也？多出血而不動搖者，何也？願聞其故。 Yellow Emperor said, What does it mean when there is a puncture wound and the attendant? What does it mean when blood comes out and shoots? What does it mean when there is little black blood and it becomes turbid? What does it mean when the blood comes out clear and half turns into fluid? What does it mean when you pull out the lancet and it swells? What does it mean when the blood comes out in a lot or little, and the face becomes pale? What does it mean when you pull out the lancet and the complexion does not change but feels uncomfortable? What does it mean when there is excessive bleeding but no shaking? I want to hear its cause.

    3: 歧伯曰：脈氣盛而血虛者，刺之則脫氣，脫氣則仆。 Obi said: If the pulse is full and the blood is deficient, when pierced, there will be a sudden release of breath, and if suddenly released, it will lead to collapse.

    4: 血氣俱盛而陰氣多者，其血滑，刺之則射；陽氣蓄積，久留而不寫者，其血黑以濁，故不能射。 Those with abundant vital energy and an excess of yin energy have slippery blood, and when pricked it gushes forth; those with accumulated yang energy, who hold it for a long time without dispersing it, have black and turbid blood, hence they cannot shoot.

    5: 新飲而液滲于絡，而未合和于血也，故血出而汁別焉；其不新飲者，身中有水，久則為腫。 If one drinks new liquids and they permeate the veins but have not yet mixed with the blood, then the blood flows out and separates from the fluid; for those who do not drink new liquids, there is water in their body, which over time leads to swelling.

    6: 陰氣積于陽，其氣因于絡，故刺之血未出而氣先行，故腫。 Yin qi accumulates in Yang, its qi is stored in the Luo vessels, therefore when needling, blood has not yet come out but qi precedes it, thus causing swelling.

    7: 陰陽之氣，其新相得而未和合，因而寫之，則陰陽俱脫，表裏相離，故脫色而蒼蒼然。 The yin and yang qi, when their new aspects meet but are not yet harmonized, if one writes them, then the yin and yang both depart, the front and back become separate, hence they lose color and appear blue-green.

    8: 刺之血出多，色不變而煩悗者，刺絡而虛經，虛經之屬于陰者，陰脫，故煩悗。 If the blood coming out is profuse, and yet the color does not change but one feels annoyed and vexed, it is because the needle has pierced the superficial layer and the Channels of Yin, so one feels annoyed and vexed.

    9: 陰陽相得而合為痺者，此為內溢于經，外注于絡。如是者，陰陽俱有餘，雖多出血而弗能虛也。 When yin and yang are harmonious and meet, it is because there is an excess of internal qi (vital energy) and external blood. In this case, both yin and yang have some surplus, although there may be a lot of bleeding, it cannot cause emptiness (deficiency).

    10: 黃帝曰：相之奈何？歧伯曰：血脈者，盛堅橫以赤，上下無常處，小者如鍼，大者如筋，則而寫之萬全也，故無失數矣。失數而反，各如其度。 Yellow Emperor said: How about the correspondence? Master Gao replied: The blood vessels are like thick cords running red, without boundaries above or below. Small ones are like needles, large ones like tendons; by this they write the myriad changes, so there are no missing patterns. If a pattern is missed and reversed, each one adapts to its degree.

    11: 黃帝曰：鍼入而肉著者，何也？歧伯曰：熱氣因于鍼，則鍼熱，熱則內著于鍼，故堅焉。 Yellow Emperor asked: What does it mean when the needle enters and penetrates the flesh? Guan Pu replied: The heat generated by the needle causes the needle to become hot. The heat then penetrates deeply into the needle, thus making it firm.
