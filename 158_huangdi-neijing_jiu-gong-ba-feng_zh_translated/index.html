    九宮八風

    1: 大1一常以冬至之日，居葉蟄之宮四十六日，明日居天留四十六日，明日居倉門四十六日，明日居陰洛四十五日，明日居天宮四十六日，明日居玄委四十六日，明日居倉果四十六日，明日居新洛四十五日，明日復居葉蟄之宮，曰冬至矣。1. 大 : 或作「太」。《四庫全書》本作「太」。 Great One: It is customary to dwell in the Palace of Insects for sixteen days after the winter solstice. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of Clear and Radiant. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of Grain Stores. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of Yinuoluo. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of Heavenly Court. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of Mysterious Filiations. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of Granary. On the next day, dwell in the Palace of New Luo. On the next day, return to dwell in the Palace of Insects, saying Winter Solstice is today. 1. Great: May also be translated as Ta. The version in the Siku Quanshu reads Ta.

    2: 太一日遊，以冬至之日，居葉蟄之宮，數所在日，從一處至九日，復返於一。常如是無已，終而復始。 On the first day, I travel. On the winter solstice day, I reside in the Palace of Leaf Flying insects. On the day when it occurs, starting from one place and lasting for nine days, returning again to one. It is always so with no end, ultimately returning to the beginning.

    3: 太一移日，天必應之以風雨，以其日風雨則吉，歲美民安少病矣。先之則多雨，後之則多汗。太一在冬至之日有變，占在君；太一在春分之日有變，占在相；太一在中宮之日有變，占在吏；太一在秋分之日有變，占在將；太一在夏至之日有變，占在百姓。所謂有變者，太一居五宮之日，病風折樹木，揚沙石，各以其所主，占貴賤。因視風所從來而占之，風從其所居之鄉來為實風，主生，長養萬物；從其沖後來為虛風，傷人者也，主殺，主害者。謹候虛風而避之，故聖人日避虛邪之道，如避矢石然，邪弗能害，此之謂也。 When the Great One moves to another day, heaven will respond with wind and rain according to its day, so that when there is wind and rain, it is auspicious; there will be good weather, and people will have few diseases. If it comes beforehand, there will be much rain; if it comes afterward, there will be much sweat. The Great One changes on the day of the winter solstice and is consulted for the ruler; the Great One changes on the day of the spring equinox and is consulted for the minister; the Great One changes on the day of the middle palace and is consulted for the official; the Great One changes on the day of the autumn equinox and is consulted for the general; the Great One changes on the day of the summer solstice and is consulted for the common people. What is called a change refers to the days when the Great One dwells in the five palaces, causing illness, withering trees, raising sand and stones, each according to what they govern. By observing the direction from which the wind comes, one can determine whether it is real or virtual wind. If it comes from where it resides, it is a true wind, nurturing life and growing countless things; if it comes from its rear, it is a virtual wind that harms people, governing killing and harm. Being cautious of the virtual wind and avoiding it, the sage person avoids the evil path, like avoiding arrows in flight, so that evil cannot hurt, and this is what they mean by it.

    4: 是故太一入徙立於中宮，乃朝八風，以占吉凶也。風從南方來，名曰大弱風，其傷人也，內舍於心，外在於脈，氣主熱。風從西南方來，名曰謀風，其傷人也，內舍於脾，外在於肌，其氣主為弱。風從西方來，名曰剛風，其傷人也，內舍於肺，外在於皮膚，其氣主為燥。風從西北方來，名曰折風，其傷人也，內舍於小腸，外在於手太陽脈，脈絕則溢，脈閉則結不通，善暴死。風從北方來，名曰大剛風，其傷人也，內舍於腎，外在於骨與肩背之膂筋，其氣主為寒也。風從東北方來，名曰凶風，其傷人也，內舍於大腸，外在於兩脅腋骨下及肢節。風從東方來，名曰嬰兀風，其傷人也，內舍於肝，外在於筋紐，其氣主為身濕。風從東南方來，名曰弱風，其傷人也，內舍於胃，外在肌肉，其氣主體重。此八風皆從其虛之鄉來，乃能病人。三虛相搏，則為暴病卒死。兩實一虛，病則為淋露寒熱。犯其兩濕之地，則為痿。故聖人避風，如避矢石焉。其有三虛而偏中於邪風，則為仆偏枯矣。 Therefore, the Great Ultimate enters and stands in the Middle Palace, representing the Eight Trigrams and used for divination of auspiciousness. The wind coming from the south is called the Great Yin Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the heart and externally in the pulse, governing heat. The wind coming from the southwest is called the Willow Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the spleen and externally in the muscles, governing weakness. The wind coming from the west is called the Rigid Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the lungs and externally in the skin, governing dryness. The wind coming from the northwest is called the Twisting Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the small intestine and externally in the hand yang channel, with the pulse blocked it causes drowning, and with the pulse closed it causes blockage and obstruction leading to a sudden death. The wind coming from the north is called the Great Rigid Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the kidneys and externally in the bones and shoulder blade tendons, governing coldness. The wind coming from the northeast is called the Disaster Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the large intestine and externally at the lower part of the pectoral girdle joints and limb joints. The wind coming from the east is called the Newborn Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the liver and externally in the tendons, governing bodily moisture. The wind coming from the southeast is called the Weak Wind, which harms people, dwelling internally in the stomach and externally in the muscles, governing corporeality. These Eight Winds all come from their deficiency regions and can harm people. When the three deficiencies struggle, it results in a violent fatal illness. When there are two realities and one deficiency, the person becomes ill with sweating and fever. If they offend the two moist areas, it leads to paralysis. Therefore, the sage avoids the wind as if avoiding arrows or rocks. If there are three deficiencies focusing on the evil wind, then the limbs become withered.
