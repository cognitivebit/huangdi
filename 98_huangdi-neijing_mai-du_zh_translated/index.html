    脈度

    1. 黃帝曰：願聞脈度。歧伯答曰：手之六陽，從手至頭，長五尺，五六三丈。手之六陰，從手至胸中，三尺五寸，三六一丈八尺，五六三尺，合二丈一尺。足之六陽，從足上至頭，八尺，六八四丈八尺。足之六陰，從足至胸中，六尺五寸，六六三丈六尺，五六三尺合三丈九尺。蹻脈從足至目，七尺五寸，二七一丈四尺，二五一尺，合一丈五尺。督脈、任脈，各四尺五寸，二四八尺，二五一尺，合九尺。凡都合一十六丈二尺，此氣之大經隧也。 Yellow Emperor said: I want to hear about the pulse. Gongsun Long replied: The greater Yang pulse is from the hand to the head, five chi, five six li. The greater Yin pulse is from the hand to the chest, three chi and five cun, three six li eight chi, five six three chi, total two li one chi. The lesser Yang pulse is from the foot to the head, eight chi, six eight li eight chi. The lesser Yin pulse is from the foot to the chest, six chi and five cun, six six li six chi, five six three chi, total three li nine chi. The Bladder Meridian pulse is from the foot to the eye, seven chi and five cun, two seven li four chi, two five one chi, total one li five chi. Governor Vessel and Conception Vessel, each four chi and five cun, two four eight li, two five one chi, total nine chi. In sum, all together is sixteen li two chi. This is the Great Classic of the Channel Vessels.

    2. 經脈為裏，支而橫者為絡，絡之別者為孫，盛而血者疾誅之，盛者寫之，虛者飲藥以補之。五藏常內閱於上七竅也。故肺氣通於鼻，肺和則鼻能知臭香矣；心氣通於舌，心和則舌能知五味矣；肝氣通於目，肝和則目能辨五色矣；脾氣通於口，脾和則口能知五穀矣；腎氣通於耳，腎和則耳能聞五音矣。五藏不和，則七竅不通；六府不合則留為癰。故邪在府則陽脈不和，陽脈不和則氣留之，氣留之則陽氣盛矣。陽氣太盛，則陰不利，陰脈不利則血留之，血留之則陰氣盛矣。陰氣太盛則陽氣不能榮也，故曰關。陽氣太盛，則陰氣弗能榮也，故曰格。陰陽俱盛，不得相榮，故曰關格。關格者，不得盡期而死也。 The vital energy of the meridians is divided into three categories: branches, collaterals, and sinews. When there is a fullness of blood, it is dispelled by puncturing; when there is a deficiency, medicine is taken to strengthen. The five viscera are always within the cavity above the seven orifices. Therefore, lung energy circulates through the nose, so that when the lung is harmonious, the nose can sense odors and fragrances. Heart energy circulates through the tongue, so that when the heart is harmonious, the tongue can taste the five flavors. Liver energy circulates through the eyes, so that when the liver is harmonious, the eyes can distinguish the five colors. Spleen energy circulates through the mouth, so that when the spleen is harmonious, the mouth can discern the five grains. Kidney energy circulates through the ears, so that when the kidney is harmonious, the ears can hear the five tones. If the viscera are not in harmony, then the seven orifices will not be open. If the six bowels do not harmonize, then there will be a swelling. Thus, if pathogenic factors reside in the bowels, the Yang meridians will not be in harmony, and when the Yang meridians are not in harmony, energy will accumulate. Accumulation of energy leads to the fullness of Yang energy. When Yang energy is excessive, Yin energy is obstructed, and when Yin energy is obstructed, blood accumulates, leading to the fullness of Yin energy. Excessive Yin energy prevents the Yang from ascending, hence it is called relationship. Excessive Yang energy prevents the Yin from ascending, hence it is called constraint. When both Yin and Yang are excessive and cannot ascend, it is called relationship constraint. Relationship constraint means that death will not come at the proper time.

    3. 黃帝曰：蹻脈安起安止，何氣榮水？歧伯答曰：蹻脈者，少陰之別，起於然骨之後。上內踝之上，直上循陰股，入陰，上循胸裏，入缺盆，上出人迎之前，入頄，屬目內眥，合於太陽，陽蹻而上行，氣并相還，則為濡，目氣不榮，則目不合。 Emperor Yellow said: If the Bubble Vessel is established and secured, where does the energy and prestige of water come from? Duke of Shao replied: The Bubble Vessel is the separate part of the Shao Yin. It begins at the rear bone of the kidneys and ascends along the Yin path, entering the Yin, rising through the chest cavity, entering the missing basin, exiting in front of the person's embrace, entering the throat, belonging to the inner eye, and connecting with the Tai Yang. When the Yang Bubble rises and circulates, there is a balance of energy, but if the energy of the eyes does not bring prestige, then the eyes do not unite.

    4. 黃帝曰：氣獨行五藏，不榮六府，何也？歧伯答曰：氣之不得無行也，如水之流，如日月之行不休，故陰脈榮其藏，陽脈榮其府，如環之無端，莫知其紀，終而復始，其流溢之氣，內溉藏府，外濡腠理。 Yellow Emperor said: What does it mean that Qi acts alone, the six palaces not honored? Huang Bo answered: The Qi cannot be without movement, like water flowing, like the unceasing motion of the sun and moon, therefore the Yin channel honors its stores (cavities), and the Yang channel honors its manors (organs), like a ring with no beginning or end, ultimately it returns to its source, its overflowing Qi irrigates the interior stores and manors, and nourishes the exterior head hair.

    5. 黃帝曰：蹻脈有陰陽，何脈當其數？歧伯曰：男子數其陽，女子數其陰，當數者為陰，其不當數者為絡也。 Yellow Emperor said: The Ying and Yang of the Chong Mai are present in the Bao Mai. Which one should be counted? Hui Bo replied: For males, count the Yang, for females, count the Yin. Counting correctly is called the Yin Mai; counting incorrectly is called the Lian Mai.
