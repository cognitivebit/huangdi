    長刺節論

    1: 刺家不診，聽病者言在頭，頭疾痛，為藏鍼之，刺至骨病已，上無傷骨肉及皮，皮者道也。 The acupuncturist did not examine, listening to the patient's words and noting that there was a headache. They inserted the needle to treat the bone disease, avoiding any injury to the bones, flesh, or skin. The term skin refers to the meridian channels.

    2: 陰刺，入一傍四處。治寒熱。深專者，刺大藏，迫藏刺背，背俞也。刺之迫藏，藏會，腹中寒熱去而止，與刺之要，發鍼而淺出血。 Acupuncture, insert the needle one inch deep at four locations. Treat cold and heat. For specialists, insert the needle into the Dazhong, press and manipulate the Dazhong, which is located behind the point. When needling the Dazhong, the Qi will rise, and the cold and heat in the abdomen will resolve, corresponding to the Deqi sensation and shallow bleeding out of the needle.

    3: 治腐腫者刺腐上，視癰小大深淺刺，刺大者多血，小者深之，必端內鍼為故止。 The text is in classical Chinese, which is not exactly literary Chinese as it is a vernacular form of written Chinese. However, I will attempt to provide a translation. 治腐腫者 (Treating tumors)，刺腐上 (insert needle into the tumor)，視癰小大深淺刺 (observing whether the swelling is small or large, deep or shallow, and inserting the needle accordingly)，刺大者多血，小者深之，必端內鍼為故止 (for larger ones, there will be more blood; for smaller ones, go deeper until it stops)。

    4: 病在少腹有積，刺皮𩩻以下，至少腹而止，刺俠脊兩傍四椎閒，刺兩髂髎季脇肋閒，導腹中氣熱下已。 The illness resides in the lower abdomen and accumulates. Puncture the skin below the navel, proceed to the lower abdomen and stop. Puncture the kidneys at both sides of the four [vertebrae], puncture the hip bones and the ribs on both sides, lead the Qi in the lower abdomen downward and expel the heat.

    5: 病在少腹，腹痛不得大小便，病名曰疝，得之寒，刺少腹兩股閒，刺腰髁骨閒，刺而多之，盡炅病已。 The illness is in the lower abdomen, causing abdominal pain and difficulty with both urination and defecation. This condition is called yinyang and is caused by cold. To treat it, needle the lower abdomen and the area between the two groins, and also needle the lumbar region near the hip bones. Needle this area frequently until the illness has resolved.

    Note: This text appears to be a description of a traditional Chinese medical condition and its treatment.

    6: 病在筋，筋攣節痛，不可以行，名曰筋痺，刺筋上為故，刺分肉閒，不可中骨也，病起筋炅，病已止。 The illness is in the sinew, the sinews and joints are painful, making it impossible to move. This condition is called Sinew Contraction. To treat this, you can needle the surface of the sinew, but do not needle the muscle fibers too deeply, as there should be no bone involvement. When the disease has subsided in the sinew, the illness will cease.

    7: 病在肌膚，肌膚盡痛，名曰肌痺，傷於寒濕，刺大分小分，多發鍼而深之，以熱為故，無傷筋骨，傷筋骨，癰發若變，諸分盡熱，病已止。 The illness is in the flesh, the flesh is painful, it is called muscle spasm, injured by cold and dampness, needle large and small division, often insert a needle deep, due to heat as the cause, no injury to muscles and bones, injure muscles and bones, nodes are swollen like changes, all divisions are hot, the disease has stopped.

    8: 病在骨，骨重不可舉，骨髓酸痛，寒氣至，名曰骨痺，深者刺無傷脈肉為故，其道大分小分，骨熱病已止。 The disease lies in the bones; the bones are too heavy to be lifted, the bone marrow is acidic and painful, the cold energy has reached a peak, and this condition is called bone paralysis. When the disease is deeply rooted, acupuncture must be performed without causing pain in the muscles and blood vessels, and the treatment path is divided into large and small divisions; the fire of bone disease has stopped.

    9: 病在諸陽脈，且寒且熱，諸分且寒且熱，名曰狂，刺之虛脈，視分盡熱，病已止。 The illness is in the Zheng Yang (Zhengjiang) meridian, and it is both cold and hot, with the branches also being cold and hot. This disease is called Kuang (madness). When needling the empty channel, observe the division until there is heat, and the disease will be cured.

    10: 病初發，歲一發，不治月一發，不治，月四五發，名曰癲病，刺諸分諸脈，其無寒者以鍼調之，病止。 At the onset of the disease, there is one case per year. If untreated, there are cases every month. This condition is known as convulsive mania. Stick needles at various points along the pulse, and if there is no cold, use a lancet to stimulate it, and the disease will cease

    11: 病風且寒且熱，炅汗出，一日數過，先刺諸分理絡脈，汗出且寒且熱，三日一刺，百日而已。 The illness is both cold and hot, profuse sweating occurs, a day passes in counting. First, puncture the divisions of the channels and the network vessels, as there is still sweating that is both cold and hot, one puncturing per day, after one hundred days it is done.

    12: 病大風，骨節重，鬚眉墮，名曰大風，刺肌肉為故，汗出百日，刺骨髓，汗出百日，凡二百日，鬚眉生而止鍼。 The patient had a fever, was heavy in weight, and had hair loss. He was named Da Feng (大風). He suffered from puncturing his muscles, sweating for 100 days, and then puncturing his bone marrow, sweating for another 100 days. After 200 days, the patient's hair grew back and stopped being lanced.
