    經水

    1: 黃帝問於歧伯曰：經脈十二者，外合於十二經水，而內屬於五藏六府。夫十二經水者，其有大小、深淺、廣狹、遠近各不同；五藏六府之高下、大小、受谷之多少亦不等，相應奈何？夫經水者，受水而行之；五藏者，合神氣魂魄而藏之；六府者，受穀而行之，受氣而揚之；經脈者，受血而營之。合而以治，奈何？刺之深淺，灸之壯數，可得聞乎？ Yellow Emperor asked Guan Pu: The twelve channels are externally united with the twelve channel vessels, while internally they belong to the five zang and six fu organs. The twelve channel vessels have differences in depth, shallowness, width, narrowness, distance, and proximity. The levels of the five zang and six fu organs also differ in height, size, amount of receiving the grains, and so on. How are they correlated? Channels are the paths that receive water and flow with it; the zang organs combine the spirit, qi, soul, and body to store them; the fu organs receive grains and raise them, receiving qi and elevating them. Channels harmonize and regulate, how is it done? Is it possible to hear about needling at different depths, moxibustion with numerous numbers?

    2: 歧伯答曰：善哉問也！天至高不可度，地至廣不可量，此之謂也。且夫人生於天地之間，六合之內，此天之高，地之廣也，非人力之所能度量而至也。若夫八尺之士，皮肉在此，外可度量切循而得之，其死可解剖而視之。其藏之堅脆，府之大小，穀之多少，脈之長短，血之清濁，氣之多少，十二經之多血少氣，與其少血多氣，與其皆多血氣，與其皆少血氣，皆有大數。其治以鍼艾，各調其經氣，固其常有合乎。 Huang Di replied: Your question is indeed good! The sky is too high to be measured, and the earth is too vast to be calculated; this is what you are referring to. Moreover, humans live within the space between heaven and earth and inside the six covers (i.e., six sense organs). The height of heaven and the breadth of earth are not within human ability to measure. If an eight-foot man has his skin and flesh here, one can measure it and cut it around to obtain it; his death can be dissected and examined. His hiding place is rigid and brittle, the extent of his organs, the abundance of his grains, the length of his veins, the clarity or turbidity of his blood, the abundance of his Qi, the state of his twelve meridians with more or less blood and Qi, whether it is mostly abundant in blood and Qi or deficient in both, or solely deficient in blood, all have specific numbers. Treatment involves acupuncture and moxibustion, adjusting the meridian Qi according to each case, and fixing their constitutional balance.

    3: 黃帝曰：余聞之，快於耳不解於心，願卒聞之。歧伯答曰：此人之所以參天地而應陰陽也，不可不察。足太陽外合清水，內屬膀胱，而通水道焉。足少陽外合于渭水，內屬于膽。足陽明外合于海水，內屬于胃。足太陰外合于湖水，內屬于脾。足少陰外合于汝水，內屬于腎。足厥陰外合于澠水，內屬于肝。手太陽外合淮水，內屬小腸，而水道出焉。手少陽外合于漯水，內屬于三焦。手陽明外合于江水，內屬于大腸。手太陰外合于河水，內屬于肺。手少陰外合于濟水，內屬于心。手心主外合于漳水，內屬于心包。凡此五藏六府十二經水者，外有源泉，而內有所稟，此皆內外相貫，如環無端，人經亦然。故天為陽，地為陰，腰以上為天，腰以下為地。故海以北者為陰，湖以北者為陰中之陰；漳以南者為陽，河以北至漳者為陽中之陰；漯以南至江者，為陽中之太陽，此一隅之陰陽也，所以人與天地相參也。 The Yellow Emperor said: I have heard of this, quick to the ear and difficult to understand in the heart. I wish to hear it once more. The Duke answered: This person is classified according to the Yin-Yang of heaven and earth, and cannot be overlooked. The foot Jueyin outside merges with clear water, inside belongs to the Bladder, and connects to the waterway. The foot Shaoyin outside merges with river water, inside belongs to the Gallbladder. The foot Yangming outside merges with sea water, inside belongs to the Stomach. The foot Taiyin outside merges with lake water, inside belongs to the Spleen. The foot Shayin outside merges with Swatow water, inside belongs to the Kidneys. The foot Jueyin outside merges with Gobi water, inside belongs to the Liver. The hand Jueyin outside merges with the Huai River water, inside belongs to the Small Intestine, and the waterway comes out of it. The hand Shaoyin outside merges with the Luo River water, inside belongs to the Triple Burner. The hand Yangming outside merges with river water, inside belongs to the Large Intestine. The hand Taiyin outside merges with the Yangtze River water, inside belongs to the Lungs. The hand Shayin outside merges with the Tongpu River water, inside belongs to the Heart. The hand Jingwell outside merges with the Chu River water, inside belongs to the Pericardium. All of these five viscera and six bowels have twelve waterways. Externally, there is a source of water, and internally, there is a place for it to be stored; all are interconnected between internal and external, like a circle without an end, just as human channels are the same. Therefore, heaven is yang, and earth is yin; above the waist is yang, and below the waist is yin. The Duke further said: The North Sea is yin, and the Lake is yin within the yin; the South River is yang, and from the North to the South is yin within the yang; the Luo River extends to the Yangtze River, which is yin within the yang of this corner. Therefore, people are related to heaven and earth by virtue of this yin and yang.

    4: 黃帝曰：夫經水之應經脈也，其遠近淺深，水血之多少，各不同，合而以刺之奈何？歧伯答曰：足陽明，五藏六府之海也，其脈大，血多氣盛，熱壯，刺此者不深勿散，不留不寫也。足陽明刺深六分，留十呼。足太陽深五分，留七呼。足少陽深四分，留五呼。足太陰深三分，留四呼。足少陰深二分，留三呼。足厥陰深一分，留二呼。手之陰陽，其受氣之道近，其氣之來疾，其刺深者，皆無過二分，其留，皆無過一呼。其少長、大小、肥瘦，以心擦之，命曰法天之常，灸之亦然。灸而過此者，得惡火則骨枯脈澀，刺而過此者，則脫氣。 Yellow Emperor said: The meridians correspond to water channels, and the depth and distance of these meridians vary according to the amount of blood and qi. How should they be pierced when they are all different? Huang Di answered: The foot Yangming is where the six ministries and five zang meet, with large vessels and abundant blood and qi, it is warm and strong. If you pierce this area, do not go too deep and do not disperse or dissipate. When piercing the foot Yangming, go 6 cun deep and leave for ten breaths. When piercing the foot Taiyang, go 5 cun deep and leave for seven breaths. When piercing the foot Shaoyang, go 4 cun deep and leave for five breaths. When piercing the foot Taiyin, go 3 cun deep and leave for four breaths. When piercing the foot Shao yin, go 2 cun deep and leave for three breaths. When piercing the foot Jueyin, go 1 cun deep and leave for two breaths. For the hand Yangming and Shaoyang, as their channels receive qi more closely and their qi arrives more quickly, the depth of piercing should not exceed two cun, and the duration should not exceed one breath. The length, size, and fatness (of a person) are determined by intention; this is known as the constant way of law heaven, and acupuncture is similar in principle. If you go beyond this, it will result in evil fire consuming the bones and vessels, and if you pierce beyond this, the qi will escape.

    5: 黃帝曰：夫經脈之大小，血之多少，膚之厚薄，肉之堅脆及膕之大小，可為量度乎？歧伯答曰：其可為度量者，取其中度也。不甚脫肉，而血氣不衰也。若夫度之人，消瘦而形肉脫者，惡可以度量刺乎。審、切、循、捫、按，視其寒溫盛衰而調之，是謂因適而為之真也。 Yellow Emperor said: Can the size of the meridians, the amount of blood, the thickness of the skin, the hardness or softness of the muscles, and the size of the veins be measured? Duke of Zhou replied: That which can be measured is doing so by degree. One should not strip away the flesh, as the vital energy will not decline. As for those who measure by degree, if they become thin and their body flesh strips away, they are bad candidates for acupuncture. Examination, puncture, examination, looping, palpation - these methods should be adjusted according to whether it is cold or warm, abundant or diminished. This is what it means to act appropriately in accordance with the principle of true medicine.
