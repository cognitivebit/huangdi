    衛氣失常

    1. 黃帝曰：衛氣之留於腹中，搐積不行，菀蘊不得常所，使人支脅胃中滿，喘呼逆息者，何以去之？伯高曰：其氣積於胸中者，上取之，積於腹中者，下取之，上下皆滿者，旁取之。 Yellow Emperor asked: Qi accumulates in the abdomen, causing discomfort and difficulty in movement. The potential is not easily accessible and supports the fullness of the stomach, resulting in shortness of breath and reversed breathing. How can this be addressed? Bo Gao replied: If the qi accumulates in the chest, take it upwards; if it accumulates in the abdomen, take it downwards; if both upper and lower are full, take it from the side.

    2. 黃帝曰：取之奈何？伯高對曰：積於上，寫人迎、天突、喉中；積於下者，寫三里與氣街；上下皆滿者，上下取之，與季脅之下一寸；重者，雞足取之。診視其脈大而弦急，及絕不至者，及腹皮急甚者，不可刺也。黃帝曰：善。 Yellow Emperor said: How shall I take it? Bo Gao replied: Accumulate it above, focusing on the Ren Mai, Du Mai, and the pharynx; accumulate it below, focusing on the San Jiao and Qi Jing; when both upper and lower are full, take it from either region, and match it to the depth of the seasonal lesion; if it is heavy, take from the Jiao Sui. If upon examination, the pulse is large and urgent, and not yet resolved, or if there is severe urgency in the abdominal region, do not puncture. Yellow Emperor said: Good.

    3. 黃帝問於伯高曰：何以知皮肉氣血筋骨之病也？伯高曰：色起兩眉薄澤者，病在皮；唇色青黃赤白黑者，病在肌肉；營氣濡然者，病在血氣；目色青黃赤白黑者，病在筋；耳焦枯受塵垢，病在骨。 Emperor Yellow asked Bo Gao: How do you know whether a disease is of the skin, flesh, qi, blood, sinews, or bones? Bo Gao replied: If the color changes above the eyebrows, the disease is in the skin; if the lips are colored green, yellow, red, white, and black, the disease is in the muscles; if the qi is damp, the disease is in the blood and qi; if the eye color is green, yellow, red, white, and black, the disease is in the sinews; if the ears are burnt, dried, and dusty, the disease is in the bones.

    4. 黃帝曰：病形何如，取之奈何？伯高曰：夫百病變化，不可勝數，然皮有部，肉有柱，血氣有輸，骨有屬。黃帝曰：願聞其故。伯高曰：皮之部，輸於四末；肉之柱，有臂脛諸陽分肉之間，與足少陰分間；血氣之輸，輸於諸絡，氣血留居，則盛而起，筋部無陰無陽，無左無右，候病所在；骨之屬者，骨空之所以受益而益腦髓者也。 Yellow Emperor said: What are the manifestations of diseases, and how to take them? Bo Gao said: There are numerous varieties of a hundred diseases that cannot be counted. However, there are parts of skin, tendons have pillars, blood and qi have channels, and bones have attributes. Yellow Emperor said: I would like to hear about their origins. Bo Gao said: The parts of the skin have channels at the four extremities; the tendon pillars have between the arm-yang division and the leg-shao yin division; the blood and qi channels have channels throughout the body, if blood and qi stay, it will be abundant and rise up. If there are no yin or yang, no left or right, and the disease is in its location; the bones attribute is where empty spaces benefit and improve the brain marrow.

    5. 黃帝曰：取之奈何？伯高曰：夫病變化，浮沉深淺，不可勝究，各在其處，病間者淺之，甚者深之，間者小之，甚者眾之，隨變而調氣，故曰上工。 Yellow Emperor said, How should it be done? Bo Gao replied, A disease changes and fluctuates in depth and shallowness, and cannot be fully understood. Each person experiences it differently: those with a mild disease experience a shallow one, while those with severe symptoms experience a deeper one. Those in between may have varying degrees of symptoms depending on the situation. By adapting to the changes and adjusting one's Qi, this is known as 'upright technique.'

    6. 黃帝問于伯高曰：人之肥瘦大小溫寒，有老壯少小，別之奈何？伯高對曰：人年五十已上為老，二十已上為壯，十八已上為少，六歲已上為小。 Yellow Emperor asked Confucius: How can one distinguish between the fat, thin, tall and short, old, robust, young and small in humans? Confucius replied: By the age of fifty, a person is considered old; by the age of twenty, they are considered robust; by the age of eighteen, they are considered young; and by the age of six, they are considered small.

    7. 黃帝曰：何以度知其肥瘦？伯高曰：人有肥、有膏、有肉。黃帝曰：別此奈何？伯高曰：膕肉堅，皮滿者，肥。膕肉不堅，皮緩者，膏。皮肉不相離者，肉。 The Yellow Emperor said: How can one tell if a person is fat or thin? Bo Gao replied: A person has fat, grease, and flesh. The Yellow Emperor asked: What about the others? Bo Gao said: If the flesh is tough and the skin is full, it is fat. If the flesh is not tough and the skin is loose, it is grease. If the flesh and skin do not separate, it is flesh.

    8. 黃帝曰：身之寒溫何如？伯高曰：膏者，其肉淖而麤理者，身寒，細理者，身熱。脂者，其肉堅，細理者熱，麤理者寒。 Yellow Emperor said: How is the body's cold and heat? Bo Gao said: Ointment, if the flesh becomes slimy and smooth, the body feels cold, and if finely powdered, the body feels hot. Tallow, if the flesh becomes hard, finely powdered it feels hot, and smooth it feels cold

    9. 黃帝曰：其肥瘦大小奈何？伯高曰：膏者，多氣而皮縱緩，故能縱腹垂腴。肉者，身體容大。脂者，其身收小。 Yellow Emperor asked: What about being fat, thin, large or small? Bo Gao replied: The one who is greased, has a lot of energy and the skin is loose and relaxed, so they can hang their belly and move their buttocks. The one with meat, has a big body shape. The one with fat, has a smaller figure.

    10. 黃帝曰：三者之氣血多少何如？伯高曰：膏者，多氣，多氣者，熱，熱者耐寒。肉者，多血則充形，充形則平。脂者，其血清，氣滑少，故不能大。此別於眾人者也。 Yellow Emperor asked: How much are the Qi and blood in these three? Master Gao answered: The lubricant has more Qi, more Qi means warmth, warmth is resistant to cold. The flesh has more blood, which makes the form full, a full form means balance. The fat has blood and essence, Qi is smooth but less, so it cannot expand. This is different from ordinary people.

    11. 黃帝曰：眾人奈何？伯高曰：眾人皮肉脂膏，不能相加也，血與氣，不能相多，故其形不小不大，各自稱其身，命曰眾人。 Yellow Emperor said: What about the common people? Duke Mu said: The common people have skin, flesh, fat, and grease, which cannot be added together. Blood and Qi cannot be increased, so their forms are not too tall or too short, each person boasts of his own body, and they are called the common people

    12. 黃帝曰：善。治之奈何？伯高曰：必先別其三形，血之多少，氣之清濁，而後調之，治無失常經。是故膏人縱腹垂腴，肉人者，上下容大，脂人者，雖脂不能大者。 Yellow Emperor said: Good. How to regulate it? Duke of Zhou replied: First, differentiate its three forms, the amount of blood, and whether the qi is clear or turbid; then adjust it. Treatment without fail will be based on the Correcting Injury Classic. Therefore, for massaging people, let their abdomen hang down and suspend their limbs, for flesh people, their upper and lower parts should be broad, and for fat people, even if they cannot become broad.
