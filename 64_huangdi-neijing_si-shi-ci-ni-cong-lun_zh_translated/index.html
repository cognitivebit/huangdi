    四時刺逆從論

    1: 厥陰有餘病陰痺，不足病生熱痺，滑則病狐疝，風濇則病少腹積氣。少陰有餘病皮痺，隱軫不足病肺痺，滑則病肺風疝，濇則病積溲血。太陰有餘病肉痺，寒中不足病脾痺，滑則病脾風疝，濇則病積心腹時滿。陽明有餘病脈痺身時熱，不足病心痺，滑則病心風疝，濇則病積時善驚。太陽有餘病骨痺身重，不足病腎痺，滑則病腎風疝，濇則病積善時巔疾。少陽有餘病筋痺脇滿，不足病肝痺，滑則病肝風疝，濇則病積時筋急目痛。 Yin is excessive and leads to illness of the Yin, characterized by lower abdominal pain; insufficiency leads to feverish pain in the lower abdomen. If it slides, there will be pain in the groin, and if it stagnates due to wind, there will be pain in the lower abdomen with accumulated gas. Yang is excessive and leads to illness of the Yang, characterized by palpitation of the pulse and bodily heat; insufficiency leads to heart pain. If it slides, there will be pain in the heart due to wind, and if it stagnates, there will be accumulation of blood at unusual times. Yang Ming is excessive and leads to illness of the Yang Ming, characterized by palpitation of the pulse and bodily heat; insufficiency leads to heart pain. If it slides, there will be pain in the heart due to wind, and if it stagnates, there will be accumulation at unusual times, causing sudden fright. Taiyang is excessive and leads to illness of the Taiyang, characterized by pain in the bones and bodily heaviness; insufficiency leads to kidney pain. If it slides, there will be pain in the kidneys due to wind, and if it stagnates, there will be accumulation at unusual times, causing sudden pain and stiffness of the neck. Shao Yang is excessive and leads to illness of the Shao Yang, characterized by pain in the muscles and body heaviness on the flanks; insufficiency leads to liver pain. If it slides, there will be pain in the liver due to wind, and if it stagnates, there will be accumulation at unusual times, causing sudden pain in the muscles and eyes. Yin is excessive and leads to illness of the Yin, characterized by pain in the lower abdomen; insufficiency leads to feverish pain in the lower abdomen. If it slides, there will be pain in the groin, and if it stagnates due to wind, there will be pain in the lower abdomen with accumulated gas. Yang is excessive and leads to illness of the Yang, characterized by palpitation of the pulse and bodily heat; insufficiency leads to heart pain. If it slides, there will be pain in the heart due to wind, and if it stagnates, there will be accumulation of blood at unusual times.

    2: 是故春氣在經脈，夏氣在孫絡，長夏氣在肌肉，秋氣在皮膚，冬氣在骨髓中。帝曰：余願聞其故。歧伯曰：春者，天氣始開，地氣始泄，凍解冰釋，水行經通，故人氣在脈。夏者，經滿氣溢，入孫絡受血，皮膚充實。長夏者，經絡皆盛，內溢肌中。秋者，天氣始收，腠理閉塞，皮膚引急。冬者蓋藏，血氣在中，內著骨髓，通於五藏。是故邪氣者，常隨四時之氣血而入客也，至其變化不可為度，然必從其經氣，辟除其邪，除其邪，則亂氣不生。 Therefore, the Qi of spring is in the meridians, the Qi of summer is in the muscles, the Qi of long summer is in the flesh, the Qi of autumn is in the skin, and the Qi of winter is in the bone marrow. The Emperor said, I wish to hear about its origins. Huan Tui replied: Spring is when heavenly Qi begins to manifest and earthly Qi begins to disperse, melting ice and allowing waterways to flow freely. Therefore, human Qi resides in the meridians. Summer occurs when Qi fills the meridians and overflows into the sinews, nourishing the skin. Long summer is when the meridians and sinews are all abundant, with excess Qi flowing into the muscles. Autumn is when heavenly Qi begins to contract and the network of channels closes up, causing the skin to tighten. Winter is when things are hidden away, with Qi and blood in the center, settling into the bone marrow and connecting with the five organs. Thus, pathogenic Qi always follows the Qi and blood of the seasons and enters the body, changing and transforming in unpredictable ways. However, one must treat it by targeting its meridians and expelling its pathogenic influence. Once the pathogenic influence is expelled, disorderly Qi will not arise.

    3: 帝曰：逆四時而生亂氣，柰何。歧伯曰：春刺絡脈，血氣外溢，令人少氣。春刺肌肉，血氣環逆，令人上氣。春刺筋骨，血氣內著，令人腹脹。夏刺經脈，血氣乃竭，令人解㑊。夏刺肌肉，血氣內卻，令人善恐。夏刺筋骨，血氣上逆，令人善怒。秋刺經脈，血氣上逆，令人善忘。秋刺絡脈，氣不外行，令人臥不欲動。秋刺筋骨，血氣內散，令人寒慄。冬刺經脈，血氣皆脫，令人目不明。冬刺絡脈，內氣外泄，留為大痺。冬刺肌肉，陽氣竭絕，令人善忘。凡此四時刺者大逆之病，不可不從也，反之則生亂氣相淫病焉。故刺不知四時之經，病之所生，以從為逆，正氣內亂，與精相薄，必審九候，正氣不亂，精氣不轉。 Emperor said: Inverse with the four seasons, resulting in chaotic qi, how come? Master Qi said: In spring, pierce the meridian, blood and qi overflow, causing people to have a shortage of qi. Pierce the muscles in spring, blood and qi flow counterclockwise, causing people to have too much qi. Pierce the bones and tendons in spring, blood and qi are inside, causing people to feel bloated. In summer, pierce the meridian, blood and qi exhaust, causing people to dissipate. Pierce the muscles in summer, blood and qi are inside, causing people to be easily frightened. Pierce the bones and tendons in summer, blood and qi are upside down, causing people to be prone to anger. In autumn, pierce the meridian, blood and qi are upside down, causing people to be prone to forgetfulness. Pierce the vessels in autumn, qi does not go outwards, causing people to want to lie down and not move. Pierce the bones and tendons in autumn, blood and qi are scattered inside, causing people to feel cold sweat. In winter, pierce the meridian, blood and qi all detach, causing people to have blurred vision. Pierce the vessels in winter, inner qi leaks outwards, leading to severe edema. Pierce the muscles in winter, Yang qi exhausts completely, causing people to be prone to forgetfulness. All diseases caused by piercing at these four seasons are great reversals that should not be ignored. On the contrary, they will generate chaotic qi and produce disorders such as diseases. Therefore, when piercing, one must know the meridians of the four seasons, where the disease comes from, treat it accordingly to reverse the qi, regulate the inner qi, and maintain the essence, avoiding nine possible manifestations and ensuring that qi does not become chaotic and jing does not transform.

    4: 帝曰：善。刺五藏，中心一日死，其動為噫。中肝五日死，其動為語。中肺三日死，其動為欬。中腎六日死，其動為嚏欠。中脾十日死，其動為吞。刺傷人五藏必死，其動，則依其藏之所變候知其死也。 The emperor said: Good. Strike the Five Organs, and if it hits the center, one will die in a day, its movement becoming a sigh. If it strikes the middle of the liver, one will die in five days, its movement becoming speech. If it strikes the lungs, one will die in three days, its movement becoming coughing and panting. If it strikes the kidneys, one will die in six days, its movement becoming coughing and gasping for breath. If it strikes the spleen, one will die in ten days, its movement becoming swallowing. Striking any of the Five Organs of a person will cause them to die, and its movements can be known by their symptoms
