    刺禁論

    1. 黃帝問曰：願聞禁數。歧伯對曰：藏有要害，不可不察，肝生於左，肺藏於右，心部於表，腎治於裏，脾為之使，胃為之巿。鬲肓之上，中有父母，七節之傍，中有小心，從之有福，逆之有咎。 The Yellow Emperor asked, I wish to hear about the secrets of the numbers. The Duke answered, There are hidden dangers within it, which should not be overlooked. The liver is born on the left, the lungs are stored on the right, the heart is located on the surface, the kidneys are treated internally, the spleen controls it, and the stomach houses it. Above the gates, there are parents in the center, around the seven apertures, there is a small heart, through which comes fortune, and by reversing it brings punishment.

    2. 刺中心，一日死，其動為噫。刺中肝，五日死，其動為語。刺中腎，六日死，其動為嚏。刺中肺，三日死，其動為欬。刺中脾，十日死，其動為吞。刺中膽，一日半死，其動為嘔。 The heart, when pierced, dies in a day and its movement is a sigh. When the liver is pierced, it dies in five days and its movement is speech. When the kidney is pierced, it dies in six days and its movement is coughing. When the lung is pierced, it dies in three days and its movement is groaning. When the spleen is pierced, it dies in ten days and its movement is swallowing. When the gallbladder is pierced, it almost dies in a day and its movement is vomiting. *Note: This text appears to be describing the movements or reactions of someone who has been wounded or attacked at different vital organs.

    3. 刺跗上，中大脈，血出不止，死。刺面，中溜脈，不幸為盲。刺頭中腦戶，入腦立死。刺舌下中脈太過，血出不止為瘖。刺足下布絡中脈，血不出為腫。刺郄中大脈，令人仆脫色。刺氣街中脈，血不出，為腫鼠僕。刺脊閒，中髓為傴。刺乳上，中乳房為腫根蝕。刺缺盆中內陷，氣泄令人喘欬逆。刺手魚腹內陷為腫。無刺大醉，令人氣亂。無刺大怒，令人氣逆。無刺大勞人，無刺新飽人，無刺大饑人，無刺大渴人，無刺大驚人。刺陰股中大脈，血出不止，死。刺客主人內陷中脈，為內漏為聾。刺膝髕出液為跛。刺臂太陰脈，出血多立死。刺足少陰脈，重虛出血，為舌難以言。刺膺中陷中肺，為喘逆仰息。刺肘中內陷，氣歸之，為不屈伸。刺陰股下三寸內陷，令人遺溺。刺掖下脇間內陷，令人欬。刺少腹中膀胱溺出，令人少腹滿。刺腨腸內陷為腫。刺匡上陷骨中脈，為漏為盲。刺關節中液出，不得屈伸。 Puncturing the Skin:

    4. Puncturing the heart causes death.

    5. Puncturing the optic nerve leads to blindness.
    6. Puncturing the forehead causes a fatal stroke.
    7. Puncturing the tongue, causing bleeding that doesn't stop, results in ulceration.
    8. Puncturing the sole causes swelling due to bleeding that doesn't stop.
    9. Puncturing the navel results in gasping and choking.
    10. Puncturing the belly of a fish causes dizziness.
    11. Overworking without rest leads to fatigue.
    12. Feeling hungry after a feast leads to discomfort.
    13. Feeling thirsty after drinking too much water leads to discomfort.
    14. Feeling frightened in shock leads to unease.
    15. Puncturing the yin gate (the genitals) causes bleeding that doesn't stop, resulting in death.
    16. Puncturing the internal organs of a client results in internal injury and deafness.
    17. Puncturing the knee joint causes fluid to leak out, resulting in stiffness.
    18. Puncturing the arm pit leads to difficulty in speaking.
    19. Puncturing the lung causes coughing and shortness of breath.
    20. Puncturing the elbow joint leads to immobility.
    21. Puncturing the lower abdomen below the three palpable parts results in drowning.
    22. Puncturing the ribcage causes the diaphragm to rupture, leading to difficulty breathing.
    23. Puncturing the bladder causes bloating and fullness.
    24. Puncturing the intestine near the bone causes swelling.
    25. Puncturing the joints causes fluid to leak out, making it impossible to bend or straighten.
