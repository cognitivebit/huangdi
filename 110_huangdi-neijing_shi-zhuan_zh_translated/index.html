    師傳

    1. 黃帝曰：余聞先師，有所心藏，弗著於方，余願聞而藏之，則而行之，上以治民，下以治身，使百姓無病，上下和親，德澤下流，子孫無優，傳於後世，無有終時，可得聞乎？歧伯曰：遠乎哉問也。夫治民與自治，治彼與治此，治小與治大，治國與治家，未有逆而能治之也，夫惟順而已矣。順者，非獨陰陽脈，論氣之逆順也，百姓人民皆欲順其志也。 Yellow Emperor said: I have heard that the esteemed master has a secret heart, which is not revealed in the four directions, and I wish to hear it and put it into practice. It will govern the upper and the lower, govern the people and govern oneself so that there are no diseases among the common folk; relations between the upper and lower are harmonious, virtues permeate downstream, having no descendants in inferior positions, being passed down to future generations without end, can this be heard? Duke of Zhou said: How distant is this matter! Governing others and governing oneself, governing that which is outside and governing that which is within, governing the state and governing the family, none who are not in accordance with it will be able to govern. Only those who follow it can achieve success. Following refers not only to the yin and yang channels, but also discusses the order of qi. All common people desire to follow their aspirations.

    2. 黃帝曰：順之奈何？歧伯曰：入國問俗，入家問諱，上堂問禮，臨病人問所便。 Yellow Emperor asked, How about following? Duke of Zhou replied, When entering a country, one should inquire about customs; when entering a family, one should inquire about traditions; when entering a hall, one should inquire about etiquette; and when facing illness, one should ask what is appropriate.

    3. 黃帝曰：便病人奈何？歧伯曰：夫中熱消癉，則便寒；寒中之屬，則便熱。胃中熱則消谷，令人懸心善飢。臍以上皮熱，腸中熱，則出黃如糜。臍以下皮寒，胃中寒，則腹脹；腸中寒，則腸鳴飧泄。胃中寒，腸中熱，則脹而且泄，胃中熱，腸中寒，則疾飲，小腹痛脹。 Yellow Emperor asked, What can be done for a person who is not feeling well? Qi Bo replied, If there is internal heat, it causes cold; if it is cold inside, then it causes heat. When the stomach has heat, it consumes grain, causing the person to have a hollow appetite and feel hungry. If the gallbladder and liver are heated, then there will be yellow stool like diarrhea. If the lower layer of the skin and muscles are cold, and the stomach is cold, there will be bloating; if the intestines are cold, there will be abdominal rumbling, loose stools, and pain. If the stomach is cold and the intestines are hot, then there is bloating with loose stools. If the stomach is hot and the intestines are cold, there is rapid drinking, lower abdominal pain, bloating, and diarrhea.

    4. 黃帝曰：胃欲寒飲，腸欲熱飲，兩者相逆，便之奈何？且夫王公大人，血食之君，驕恣從欲輕人，而無能禁之，禁之則逆其志，順之則加其病，便之奈何？治之何先？歧伯曰：人之情，莫不惡死而喜生，告之以其敗，語之以其善，導之以其所便，開之以其所苦，雖有無道之人，惡有不聽者乎？ Yellow Emperor said: The stomach desires warmth in drinks, while the intestines desire coolness in drinks. If these two desires are opposite to each other, how can one regulate them? As for the king, noblemen, and great men, who govern the empire through blood and food, they cannot help but indulge their desires and lightly humble the people. If they try to control this, it will go against their intentions, and if they follow along with it, they will only increase the illnesses. How can one regulate it? The Duke asked: People all dislike death and love life. Inform them of their defeat, teach them what is good, lead them according to what suits them, open them to what causes them pain, even though there are those who do not follow the way, would anyone dislike this?

    5. 黃帝曰：治之奈何？歧伯曰：春夏先治其標，後治其本；秋冬先治其本，後治其標。 Yellow Emperor asked: How should one govern? Huan Bo replied: First govern according to the seasons, treating the manifestations before addressing the roots during spring and summer; in autumn and winter, first address the roots before treating the manifestations.

    6. 黃帝曰：便其相逆者奈何？歧伯曰：便此者，食飲衣服，亦欲適寒溫，寒無淒愴，暑無出汗。食飲者，熱無灼灼，寒無滄滄。寒溫中適，故氣將持，乃不致邪僻也。 Yellow Emperor asked: What about those who find it hard to follow the natural order? Guo Bo replied: Those who follow it, want their food, drink, clothing to be appropriate for the cold or heat, and want the cold without frostbite and the heat without excessive sweating. With suitable food and drink, they can maintain balance and avoid internal harm

    7. 黃帝曰：本藏以身形肢節䐃肉，候五藏六府之大小焉。今夫王公大人，臨朝即位之君，而問焉，誰可捫循之，而後答乎？歧伯曰：身形肢節者，藏府之蓋也，非面部之閱也。 Yellow Emperor said: The essential spirit resides in the body's shape and limbs, and interacts with the five viscera and six bowels. Now, you are a ruler who has ascended to the throne at this time, and yet you ask this question. Who can guide you, and then provide an answer? Duke of Zhou said: The body's shape and limbs are the receptacles of the viscera, not merely the features of the face.

    8. 黃帝曰：五藏之氣，閱於面者，余已知之矣，以肢節知而閱之，奈何？歧伯曰：五藏六府者，肺為之蓋，巨肩陷咽，候見其外。黃帝曰：善。 Yellow Emperor said: I know the Qi of the Five Organs, as read in The Face. I have known it through the movements of my limbs and studied it. What shall I do now? Qi Bo said: The Five Organs and Six Depots are covered by the Lung. The groove at the base of the neck is where it resides, and one can observe its exterior. Yellow Emperor said: Good

    9. 歧伯曰：五藏六府，心為之主，缺盆為之道，骷骨有余以候𩩲𩨗。黃帝曰：善。 Huang Di said: Good.

    10. 歧伯曰：肝者，主為將，使之候外，欲知堅固，視目小大。黃帝曰：善。 Bo says: The liver is in charge of the general, making it timely and sending it outward. If you want to know if it is firmly established, look at the size of the pupils. Emperor Huang says: Good.

    11. 歧伯曰：脾者，主為衛，使之迎糧，視唇舌好惡，以知吉凶。黃帝曰：善。 Bo said: The spleen is responsible for governing the defense and ensuring that it receives nourishment, observes whether the lips and tongue are good or bad, and thus discerns good and evil omens. Yellow Emperor said: Good.

    12. 歧伯曰：腎者，主為外，使之遠聽，視耳好惡，以知其性。黃帝曰：善。願聞六府之候。 Huang Di said: Good. I would like to hear about the weather conditions of the Six Ministries.

    13. 歧伯曰：六府者，胃為之海，龐骸、大頸、張胸，五穀乃容。鼻隧以長，以候大腸。唇厚、人中長，以候小腸。目下果大，其膽乃橫。鼻孔在外，膀胱漏泄。鼻柱中央起，三焦乃約，此所以候六府者也。上下三等，藏安且良矣。 Duke Zhou said: The six palaces are the sea of the abdomen, which includes the hunchback, the large neck, and the spread chest; these five grains are their nourishment. The nasal passage is long and the large intestine is long, indicating the health of the large intestine. The lips are thick and the person is tall, indicating the health of the small intestine. The eyes are large, which indicates the strength of the gallbladder. The nostrils are on the outside, and the bladder is leaking. When the nasal column is in the center, the three burners are regulated, and this is why the six palaces are mentioned. Up and down, there are three levels, and they are safe and beneficial.
