    脹論

    1: 黃帝曰：脈之應于寸口，如何而脹？歧伯曰：其脈大堅以濇者，脹也。黃帝曰：何以知藏府之脹也。歧伯曰：陰為藏，陽為府。 Yellow Emperor said: The pulse responds at the wrist, how can it be palpated? Master of Difficult Issues said: When the pulse is large and strong, it is a sign of palpitation. Yellow Emperor asked: How do you know that it is the palpitation of the Kidney palace? Master of Difficult Issues said: The Yin is responsible for storing and the Yang is responsible for the palace.

    2: 黃帝曰：夫氣之令人脹也，在于血脈之中耶，藏府之內乎？歧伯曰：三者皆存焉，然非脹之舍也。黃帝曰：願聞脹之舍。歧伯曰：夫脹者，皆在於藏府之外，排藏府而郭胸脅，脹皮膚，故命曰脹。 Yellow Emperor said: Is it the vital energy that makes people bloated, within the blood vessels and in the internal organs? Master of the Three said: All three are present, but not due to bloating. Yellow Emperor asked: May I hear about the cause of bloating? Master of the Three said: Bloating is caused by vital energy outside the internal organs, which presses against the internal organs and surrounds the chest and abdomen. That's why it's called bloating.

    3: 黃帝曰：藏府之在胸脅腹里之內也，若匣匱之藏禁器也，名有次舍，異名而同處，一域之中，其氣各異，願聞其故。黃帝曰：未解其意，再問。歧伯曰：夫胸腹，藏府之郭也。膻中者，心主之宮城也；胃者，太倉也；咽喉、小腸者，傳送也；胃之五竅者，閭里門戶也；廉泉、玉英者，津液之道也。故五藏六府者，各有畔界，其病各有形狀。營氣循脈，衛氣逆為脈脹；衛氣並脈循分為膚脹。三里而寫，近者一下，遠者三下，無問虛實，工在疾寫。 Yellow Emperor said: The internal organs, such as the heart, liver, spleen, lungs, and kidneys, are called the Five Zang Organs. They are all in one domain, yet they have different qualities of energy. I would like to know their origins. Yellow Emperor said: I do not understand your meaning, so I ask again. The Duke of Tang replied: The chest and abdomen are where the internal organs reside. The diaphragm is the capital city of the spirit lord; the stomach is the warehouse; the throat and small intestine are the transit centers; the five orifices of the stomach are the market gates; the renal cortex and the adrenal glands are the channels for fluids. Thus, each of the Five Zang Organs and Six Fu Organs has its own boundary, and their diseases have different manifestations. Regulating Qi through the meridians and defending against pathogenic factors through the meridians can cause Qi stagnation or blockage; defending against pathogenic factors through the meridians can also lead to skin blockages. Three li away, closer ones are written with one stroke, and farther ones with three strokes. There is no asking about truth or falsity, and the skill lies in rapid writing.

    4: 黃帝曰：願聞脹形。歧伯曰：夫心脹者煩心短氣，臥不安；肺脹者，虛滿而喘咳；肝脹者，脅下滿而痛引小腹；脾脹者，善穢，四肢煩俛，體重不能勝衣，臥不安；腎脹者，腹滿引背央央然，腰髀痛。六府脹，胃脹者，腹滿，胃脘痛，鼻聞焦臭，妨於食，大便難；大腸脹者，腸鳴而痛濯濯，冬日重感於寒，則餐泄不化；小腸脹者，少腹慎脹，引腰而痛；膀胱脹者，少腹滿而氣癃；三焦脹者，氣滿於皮膚中，輕輕然而不堅；膽脹者，脅下痛脹，口中苦，善太息。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about distention. Duke of Shao replied: One who is distended in the heart feels restless and short-tempered, unable to rest peacefully; one who is distended in the lungs coughs and wheezes; one who is distended in the liver feels a fullness below the navel and experiences pain that refers to the lower abdomen; one who is distended in the spleen feels uncomfortable, limbs are stiff, cannot bear the weight of clothing, and cannot rest peacefully; one who is distended in the kidneys feels full in the abdomen, referring pain to the back, the hips hurt; one who is distended in the large intestine has a sounding colon, causing lower abdominal pain, has a strong smell from the nose, which interferes with eating and defecation; one who is distended in the small intestine has a distended lower abdomen that refers to the hips and causes pain; one who is distended in the bladder feels full in the lower abdomen and gasps; one who is distended in the three burners feels full under the skin, and moves lightly but without stability; one who is distended in the gallbladder feels pain below the navel and bloated.

    5: 凡此諸脹者，其道在一，明知逆順，鍼數不失，寫虛補實，神去其室，致邪失正，真不可定，麤之所敗，謂之天命；補虛寫實，神歸其室，久塞其空，謂之良工。 Regarding these various texts, their method is one, clearly knowing yin and yang, not missing the acupuncture points, writing the virtual and supplementing the real, when the spirit departs its chamber, causing deviation from the proper way, that which cannot be defined is called destiny; supplementing the virtual and writing the real, the spirit returns to its chamber, lingering in its void for a long time, this is called a skilled craftsman.

    6: 黃帝曰：脹者焉生？何因而有？歧伯曰：衛氣之在身也，常然並脈，循分肉，行有逆順，陰陽相隨，乃得天和，五藏更始，四時循序，五穀乃化。然後厥氣在下，營衛留止，寒氣逆上，真邪相攻，兩氣相搏，乃合為脹也。黃帝曰：善。何以解惑？歧伯曰：合之於真，三合而得。帝曰：善。 Yellow Emperor said: How can a swelling arise? What is the reason for it? Ho Po said: The defensive Qi is within the body, constantly following the channels, penetrating the flesh, having both upward and downward movement, Yin and Yang accompany each other, thus achieving harmony with heaven, the Five Organs are established, the Four Seasons follow their proper sequence, and the Five Grains become transformed. Later, the Excessive Qi is below, the defense of the defensive Qi becomes stationary, cold Qi rises upwards, true and false attack each other, the two Qis struggle together, thus forming a swelling. Yellow Emperor said: Good. How can I dispel confusion? Ho Po said: Unite them with the true, and after three unifications, one will obtain it. Yellow Emperor said: Good.

    7: 黃帝問於歧伯曰：脹論言無問虛實，工在疾寫，近者一下，遠者三下，今有其三而不下者，其過焉在？歧伯對曰：此言陷於肉肓，而中氣穴者也。不中氣穴，則氣內閉，鍼不陷肓，則氣不行，上越中肉，則衛氣相亂，陰陽相逐。其於脹也，當寫不寫，氣故不下，三而不下，必更其道，氣下乃止，不下復始，可以萬全，烏有殆者乎？其於脹也，必審其彌，當寫則寫，當補則補，如鼓應桴，惡有不下者乎？ Yellow Emperor asked Master Qi Bo: Regarding the treatment of [a disease called] hacking cough, there is no inquiry about whether it is real or not. The method involves rapid writing, nearer to it in one strike, farther away in three strikes. If today it has three strikes but does not fall, where does its error lie? Master Qi Bo replied: This speech is trapped in the flesh and tendons, but it is related to the acupuncture points of the body's vital energy. If you do not hit the acupuncture point, then the vital energy will be blocked inside, the needle will not penetrate the flesh, and the vital energy will not flow, causing disorder between defensive Qi and nutritive Yin, with Yang following Yin. In regard to hacking cough, one should definitely write or not write based on the situation; if the vital energy does not descend because of this, it will not fall even after three strikes, one must change the method, and once the vital energy descends and settles, it will be cured completely, with no risk of recurrence. In regard to hacking cough, one must carefully consider the situation, act accordingly by writing or supplementing [acupuncture points], like a drum responding to a lute; is there any risk of not hitting the mark?
