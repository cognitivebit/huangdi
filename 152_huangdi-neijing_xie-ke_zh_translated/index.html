    邪客

    1. 黃帝問于伯高曰：夫邪氣之客人也，或令人目不瞑不臥出者，何氣使然？伯高曰：五穀入于胃也，其糟粕津液宗氣，分為三隧。故宗氣積於胸中，出於喉嚨，以貫心脈，而行呼吸焉。營氣者，泌其津液，注之於脈，化以為血，以榮四末，內注五藏六府，以應刻數焉。節氣者，出其悍氣之慓疾，而先行於四末分肉皮膚之間，而不休者也。晝日行於陽，夜行於陰，常從足少陰之分間，行於五藏六府，今厥氣客於五藏六府，則衛氣獨衛其外，行於陽，不得入于陰。行於陽則陽氣盛，陽氣盛則陽橋陷，不得入于陰，陰虛，故目不瞑。 The Yellow Emperor asked Confucius, Regarding the evil qi that is a guest, it may cause people to not sleep day and night. What kind of qi brings this about? Confucius replied, Five grains enter the stomach, their dregs and fluids nourish the vital qi, dividing into three channels. Therefore, the vital qi accumulates in the chest and issues forth from the throat, permeating the heart vessels and guiding respiration. The managed qi causes the fluids to flow through the vessels, transforming them into blood, irrigating the four extremities and nourishing the five organs and six palaces, responding to changes in numbers. The regulated qi emerges from the sharp qi's urgency and precedes in the gaps between the flesh, skin, and pores of the four extremities, unceasingly. During the day it acts in the Yang, at night in the Yin, always traversing the five organs and six palaces through the dividing channel of the little yin of the foot, today the qi of the five organs and six palaces is deficient, so the defensive qi guards its exterior, acting in the Yang and not entering the Yin. Acting in the Yang causes the Yang to be abundant, and the Yang being abundant causes the Yang Bridge to collapse, not entering the Yin, the Yin is empty, thus causing one not to sleep.

    2. 黃帝曰：善。治之奈何？伯高曰：補其不足，寫其有餘，調其虛實，以通其道，而去其邪。飲以半夏湯一劑，陰陽已通，其臥立至。黃帝曰：善。此所謂決瀆壅塞，經絡大通，陰陽和得者也。願聞其方。伯高曰：其湯方以流水千里以外者八升，揚之萬遍，取其清五升，煮之，炊以葦薪火，沸置秫米一升，治半夏五合，徐炊，令竭為一升半，去其滓，飲汁一小杯，日三稍益，以知為度，故其病新發者，復杯則臥，汗出則已矣。久者，三飲而已也。 Yellow Emperor said: Good. How should I treat it? Duke of Gao said: Supplement its deficiencies, rectify its excesses, regulate its reality, and harmonize its virtues to guide its course, while eliminating its deviations. Drink a dose of Xiajiao soup, and the yin and yang will be connected, returning to normal sitting and standing postures. Yellow Emperor said: Good. This is what is called resolving stagnation and unblocking obstructions, regulating the meridians, harmonizing yin and yang, and achieving a balanced state. I hope to hear its method. Duke of Gao said: The soup formula consists of 8 ounces of water from a river over a thousand li away, boiled a hundred times, taking five ounces of the clear liquid, boiling it, steaming it with reeds and willows, adding one cup of millet rice, simmering half an Xiajiao, cooking until it becomes one and a half cups, removing excess liquid, drinking the broth in small sips, gradually increasing by three cups per day to determine the appropriate dosage. In the early stages of illness, if you drink again, the patient will return to bed, and if sweating occurs, recovery is achieved. If prolonged, three drinks will be sufficient.

    3. 黃帝問於伯高曰：願聞人之肢節以應天地奈何？伯高答曰：天圓地方，人頭圓足方以應之。天有日月，人有兩目；地有九州，人有九竅；天有風雨，人有喜怒；天有雷電，人有聲音；天有四時，人有四肢；天有五音，人有五藏；天有六律，人有六府；天有冬夏，人有寒熱；天有十曰，人有手十指；辰有十二，人有足十指，莖垂以應之，女子不足二節，以抱人形；天有陰陽，人有夫妻；歲有三百六十五日，人有三百六十節；地有高山，人有肩膝；地有深谷，人有腋膕；地有十二經水，人有十二經脈；地有泉脈，人有衛氣；地有草蓂，人有毫毛；天有晝夜，人有臥起；天有列星，人有牙齒；地有小山，人有小節；地有山石，人有高骨；地有林木，人有募筋；地有聚邑，人有膕肉；歲有十二月，人有十二節；地有四時不生草，人有無子。此人與天地相應者也。 Yellow Emperor asked Bo Gao, I wish to hear how the human body corresponds with heaven and earth? Bo Gao replied, Heaven is round and wide, and humans have round heads and feet to correspond with it. Heaven has the sun and moon, humans have two eyes; Earth has nine regions, humans have nine orifices; Heaven has wind and rain, humans have joy and anger; Heaven has thunder and lightning, humans have sounds; Heaven has the four seasons, humans have four limbs; Heaven has the five tones, humans have five organs; Heaven has the six laws, humans have six departments; Heaven has winter and summer, humans have cold and heat; Heaven is called Ten, humans have ten fingers; The pole has twelve, humans have ten toes, their hairs falling down corresponding; Women lacking two limbs embrace human forms; Heaven has yin and yang, humans have husband and wife; A year has three hundred and sixty-five days, humans have three hundred and sixty joints; High mountains in the Earth have humans' shoulders and knees; Deep valleys in the Earth have humans' armpits and elbows; The twelve meridians of water in the Earth have humans' twelve meridian vessels; The earth has Qi channels, humans have defensive Qi; The earth has grasses and weeds, humans have hair follicles; Heaven has day and night, humans have lying down and getting up; Heaven has starry constellations, humans have teeth; Small mountains in the Earth have small joints; Rocks and stones in the Earth have hard bones; Forests and trees in the Earth have tense muscles; Gathered cities in the Earth have fleshy muscles; Twelve months in a year, humans have twelve joints. Humans correspond with heaven and earth.

    4. 黃帝問於歧伯曰：余願聞持鍼之數，內鍼之理，縱舍之意，扞皮開腠理，奈何？脈之屈折，出入之處，焉至而出，焉至而止，焉至而徐，焉至而疾，焉至而入，六府之輸於身者，余願盡聞，少序別離之處，離而入陰，別而入陽，此何道而從行，願盡聞其方。歧伯曰：帝之所問，鍼道畢矣。 Yellow Emperor asked Qi Bo, saying: I wish to hear about the techniques of acupuncture, the principles of internal acupuncture, the intentions of abandoning or retaining, and the methods of opening the skin and separating the sinews. What about the bending, stretching, slowing, hastening, entering, and the six palaces' inputs to the body? I wish to fully understand these aspects, the order of succession, the points of departure and entry, leaving to enter yin and departing to enter yang. How do these methods follow a certain path? I wish to fully understand their approach. Qi Bo replied: The Emperor's questions have completed the theory of acupuncture.

    5. 黃帝曰：願卒聞之。歧伯曰：手太陰之脈，出於大指之端，內屈，循白肉際，至本節之後太淵，留以澹，外屈，上於本節下，內屈，與陰諸絡會於魚際，數脈並注，其氣滑利，伏行壅骨之下，外屈，出於寸口而行，上至於肘內廉，入于大筋之下，內屈，上行臑陰，入腋下，內屈，走肺。此順行逆數之屈折也。心主之脈，出於中指之端，內屈，循中指內廉以上，留於掌中，伏行兩骨之間，外屈，出兩筋之間，骨肉之際，其氣滑利，上二寸，外屈，出行兩筋之間，上至肘內廉，入于小筋之下，留兩骨之會，上入于胸中，內絡於心脈。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about it. Guan Ping replied: The Conception vessel (Ren Mai) is a channel that begins at the tip of the little finger, travels along the outer edge of the white part of the palm, and ends at the hollow below the thumb. It then turns inward, ascends along the Ren Mai, passes through the muscle layer, enters the chest, and connects with the Lung channel. This is a description of the Conception vessel's changes in direction

    6. 黃帝曰：手少陰之脈，獨無俞，何也？歧伯曰：少陰，心脈也。心者，五藏六府之大主也，精神之所舍也，其藏堅固，邪弗能容也。容之則心傷，心傷則神去，神去則死矣。故諸邪之在於心者，皆在於心之包絡。包絡者，心主之脈也，故獨無俞焉。 Yellow Emperor said: The lesser yang channel, what is this? Huan Bo said: Lesser yang, it refers to the heart channel. The heart is the supreme of the five zang and six fu organs, the residence of the spirit, its location is firm and unyielding, and evil cannot dwell therein. If evil does dwell therein, then the heart will be injured; if the heart is injured, then the spirit will depart, and with the spirit's departure comes death. Thus, all evils lie within the heart's domain, which are under the mastery of the heart channel's connecting vessel. The connecting vessel is the heart channel's master branch, hence there is no point of connection (no Luo connective vessel).

    7. 黃帝曰：少陰獨無俞者，不病乎？歧伯曰：其外經病而藏不病，故獨取其經於掌後銳骨之端。其餘脈出入屈折，其行之徐疾，皆如手少陰心主之脈行也。故本俞者，皆因其氣之虛實疾徐以取之，是謂因沖而寫，因衰而補，如是者，邪氣得去，真氣堅固，是謂因天之序。 Yellow Emperor said: If there is no Yin in Shao, will there be no illness? Hua Tuo said: When the exterior has passed through illness and concealed without sickness, one focuses on taking its course at the sharp end of the styloid process of the radius. The rest of the pulse exits and enters, bends and stretches, all as if reflecting the pulse of the heart governor. Therefore, those who originally have no Yin, all do so because their Qi is rapid or slow to obtain it, thus seeking harmony when weakened and supplementing when declining; this is known as following the natural order. When pathogenic Qi departs and true Qi becomes firm, this is known as following the order of Heaven.

    8. 黃帝曰：持鍼縱舍奈何？歧伯曰：必先明知十二經脈之本末，皮膚之寒熱，脈之盛衰滑澀。其脈滑而盛者，病日進；虛而細者，久以持；大以澀者，為痛痺。陰陽如一者，病難治。其本末尚熱者，病尚在；其熱以衰者，其病亦去矣。持其尺，察其肉之堅脆，大小滑澀，寒溫燥濕。因視目之五色，以知五藏，而決死生。視其血脈，察其色，以知其寒熱痛痺。 Yellow Emperor said: Holding a lancet, what should one do? Duke of Shou replied: One must first clearly understand the origins and ends of the twelve meridians, as well as the skin's cold and heat, and the fullness and weakness of the pulse. If the pulse is slippery and strong, the illness will progress; if it is empty and thin, one should persist; if it is large and turbid, it will cause pain and numbness. If yin and yang are equal, treating the disease will be difficult. If the origins and ends of the meridians are still hot, the illness has not yet resolved; if the heat dissipates along with the weakness, then the illness is gone as well. Holding the zhang, examine the flesh's firmness and suppleness, large or small, slippery or turbid, cold or warm. Examine the five colors of the eyes to know the five organs and determine life and death. Look at the blood and pulse, examine their colors, to know whether it is cold, hot, painful, or numb.

    9. 黃帝曰：持鍼縱舍，餘未得其意也。歧伯曰：持鍼之道，欲端以正，安以靜。先知虛實而行疾徐。左手執骨，右手循之。無與肉果。寫欲端以正，補必閉膚。輔鍼導氣，邪得淫泆，真氣得居。 Yellow Emperor said: Holding the lancet, one can pierce and let go; it is not yet at its purpose. Duke of Zhou said: The way of holding the lancet aims to be precise and accurate, how can it be still? Sage knows whether it is true or false and acts swiftly or slowly. With the left hand, grasp the bone, and with the right hand, follow along. There is no match with flesh and fruit. One desires to make it precise and accurate, but must close the skin. Aiding the lancet to guide the qi, if there are pathological factors, true qi can reside; if there are not, true qi can settle down.

    10. 黃帝曰：扞皮開腠理奈何？歧伯曰：因其分肉，左別其膚，微內而徐端之，適神不散，邪氣得去。 Yellow Emperor asked: How does one flay the skin and separate the sinews? Hua Tuo replied: Because it is divided into muscles, the skin is separated from the left side, gently pushing inward while extending slowly. This allows the spirit to adapt without dispersing, and expels pathogenic Qi

    11. 黃帝問於歧伯曰：人有八虛，各何以候？歧伯答曰：以候五藏。黃帝曰：候之奈何？歧伯曰：肺心有邪，其氣留於兩肘；肝有邪，其氣流於兩腋；脾有邪，其氣留於兩髀；腎有邪，其氣留於兩膕。凡此八虛者，皆機關之室，真氣之所過，血絡之所遊。邪氣惡血，固不得住留。住留則傷筋絡骨節；機關不得屈伸，故痀攣也。 Yellow Emperor asked Huang Di: What are the eight emptinesses? How does one attend to them? Huang Di answered: Attend to the five organs. Yellow Emperor asked: What is attending like? Huang Di said: If there is pathogenic factor in the lungs and heart, the qi will accumulate at the elbows; if there is pathogenic factor in the liver, the qi will flow to the shoulders; if there is pathogenic factor in the spleen, the qi will accumulate at the hips; if there is pathogenic factor in the kidneys, the qi will accumulate at the ankles. All eight emptinesses are related to the channels and collaterals, along which true qi flows and blood vessels circulate. Pathogenic qi and bad blood must not remain. If they stay, it will damage the muscles and bones; the channels cannot bend or stretch freely, so there is rigidity and pain.
