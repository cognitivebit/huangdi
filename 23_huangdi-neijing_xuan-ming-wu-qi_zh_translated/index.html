    宣明五氣

    1. 味所入；酸入肝，辛入肺，苦入心，鹹入腎，甘入脾，是謂五入。 The flavors enter accordingly; sour enters the liver, bitter enters the heart, salty enters the kidneys, sweet enters the spleen, and pungent enters the lungs. This is called the Five Enter.

    2. 氣所病：心為噫，肺為欬，肝為語，脾為吞，腎為欠為嚏，胃為氣逆為噦為恐，大腸小腸為泄，下焦溢為水，膀胱不利為癃，不約為遺溺，膽為怒，是謂五病。 Diseases of Qi: The heart is manifested as sighing, the lungs as groaning, the liver as speech, the spleen as swallowing, the kidneys as shivering, the stomach as nausea from reversed qi, the large intestine and small intestine as diarrhea, the lower abdomen as overflow of water, the bladder as difficult urination, convulsions as not being bound, the gallbladder as anger, these are the five diseases.

    3. 精所并：精氣并於心則喜，并於肺則悲，并於肝則憂，并於脾則畏，并於腎則恐，是謂五并，虛而相并者也。 The text translates to: The gathering of the spirit (精): when it gathers in the heart, there is joy; when it gathers in the lungs, there is sorrow; when it gathers in the liver, there is worry; when it gathers in the spleen, there is fear; when it gathers in the kidneys, there is terror. These are the five gatherings, and they occur when the spirit is empty.

    4. 藏所惡：心惡熱，肺惡寒，肝惡風，脾惡濕，腎惡燥，是謂五惡。 The text in literary Chinese can be translated to English as: Evil of the hidden: heart evil heat, lung evil cold, liver evil wind, spleen evil dampness, kidney evil dryness, are called the five evils.

    5. 藏化液：心為汗，肺為涕，肝為淚，脾為涎，腎為唾，是謂五液。 Literary Chinese Translation: The heart is sweat, the lungs are tears, the liver is mucus, the spleen is saliva, and the kidneys are sputum. These are called the five fluids. English Translation: The heart is sweat, the lungs are tears, the liver is phlegm, the spleen is saliva, and the kidneys are sputum. These are known as the five essences.

    6. 味所禁：辛走氣，氣病無多食辛；鹹走血，血病無多食鹹；苦走骨，骨病無多食苦；甘走肉，肉病無多食甘；酸走筋，筋病無多食酸；是謂五禁，無令多食。 In accordance with one's taste: bitter moves Qi, Qi diseases are not common in those who eat much bitter; salty moves blood, blood diseases are not common in those who eat much salty; acrid moves bones, bone diseases are not common in those who eat much acrid; sweet moves flesh, flesh diseases are not common in those who eat much sweet; sour moves tendons, tendon diseases are not common in those who eat much sour; this is called the Five Prohibitions, do not let people eat too much.

    7. 病所發：陰病發於骨，陽病發於血，陰病發於肉，陽病發於冬，陰病發於夏，是謂五發。 According to the text: The disease arises from the Yin (internal organs and their functions), while Yang (external factors such as weather or pathogens) affects the blood, the Yin manifests in summer, while the Yang appears in winter. Thus are described the five manifestations of disease

    8. 邪所亂：邪入於陽則狂，邪入於陰則痺，搏陽則為巔疾，搏陰則為瘖，陽入之陰則靜，陰出之陽則怒，是謂五亂。 Wickedness Disorder: Evil enters the Yang and causes madness, evil enters the Yin and causes paralysis, struggling with Yang leads to severe pain, struggling with Yin leads to rigidity. When Yang invades the Yin, it becomes quiet, when Yin emerges from Yang, it becomes angry. This is known as the Five Disorders

    9. 邪所見：春得秋脈，夏得冬脈，長夏得春脈，秋得夏脈，冬得長夏脈，名曰陰出之陽，病善怒不治，是謂五邪，皆同命，死不治。 Evil observations: Obtaining the pulse of autumn in spring, the pulse of summer in summer, a long summer obtaining the pulse of spring, obtaining the pulse of autumn in summer, obtaining the pulse of winter in long summer; called yin producing yang, diseases of anger are not curable; these are the five evils, all the same disease, untreatable and dying.

    10. 藏所藏：心藏神，肺藏魄，肝藏魂，脾藏意，腎藏志，是謂五藏所藏。 Hidden within: The heart houses the spirit, the lungs house the soul, the liver houses the soul essence, the spleen houses the mind, and the kidneys house the willpower; this is what is referred to as the five internal organs.

    Note that the translation provided uses more traditional terminology for the internal organs and their functions. The text refers to the heart housing the spirit, lungs housing the soul, liver housing the soul essence, spleen housing the mind, and kidneys housing the willpower, which are part of traditional Chinese medicine's concept of the five internal organs (五藏).

    1. 藏所主：心主脈，肺主皮，肝主筋，脾主肉，腎主骨，是謂五主。 The Cave Master: The heart governs the vessels, the lungs govern the skin, the liver governs the tendons, the spleen governs the flesh, and the kidneys govern the bones. This is known as the five organs.

    2. 勞所傷：久視傷血，久臥傷氣，久坐傷肉，久立傷骨，久行傷筋，是謂五勞所傷。 Labor-induced injury: Prolonged visual strain leads to blood damage; prolonged bed rest results in qi damage; prolonged sitting causes muscle damage; prolonged standing causes bone damage; prolonged walking causes sinew damage. These are the five types of injuries caused by labor.

    3. 脈應象：肝脈絃，心脈鉤，脾脈代，肺脈毛，腎脈石，是謂五藏之脈。 Pulse Characteristics: The liver pulse is like silk, the heart pulse is like a claw, the spleen pulse is like coins, the lung pulse is like fur, and the kidney pulse is like stone. These are the pulse characteristics of the five organs.
