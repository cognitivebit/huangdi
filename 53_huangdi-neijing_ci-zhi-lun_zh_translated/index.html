    刺志論

    1: 黃帝問曰：願聞虛實之要。歧伯對曰：氣實形實，氣虛形虛，此其常也，反此者病。穀盛氣盛，穀虛氣虛，此其常也，反此者病。脈實血實，脈虛血虛，此其常也，反此者病。 The Yellow Emperor asked, I wish to hear about the difference between substance and function. The Duke of Shou replied, When there is substance and form is substantial, when there is emptiness and form is empty, this is their normal state, and what deviates from this is considered illness. When grain is abundant and its Qi is abundant, when grain is scarce and its Qi is scarce, this is their normal state, and what deviates from this is considered illness. With the pulse being substantial and blood being substantial, with the pulse being empty and blood being empty, this is their normal state, and what deviates from this is considered illness.

    2: 帝曰：如何而反。歧伯曰：氣虛身熱，此謂反也。穀入多而氣少，此謂反也。穀不入而氣多，此謂反也。脈盛血少，此謂反也。脈少血多，此謂反也。 The Emperor asked, How is this reversal? The Duke of Zhou replied, Gas is empty and the body is hot; this is called reversal. Grain enters in abundance but Qi is scarce, this is also called reversal. Grain does not enter and Qi is abundant, this is also called reversal. He also mentioned that when blood is plentiful and the pulse is weak, or if the pulse is abundant but the blood is deficient, these are also considered as reversals.

    3: 氣盛身寒，得之傷寒。氣虛身熱，得之傷暑。穀入多而氣少者，得之有所脫血濕居下也。穀入少而氣多者，邪在胃及與肺也。脈小血多者，飲中熱也。脈大血少者，脈有風氣，水漿不入，此之謂也。 Gushing Qi and feeling cold, one may get chilled. Deficient Qi and feeling hot, one may get heatstroke. When grains enter in excess and Qi is insufficient, one might have bleeding and sweating below. When grains enter in deficiency and Qi is abundant, evil resides in the stomach and lung. When the pulse is small and blood is abundant, it means consuming hot drinks. When the pulse is large and blood is scanty, there is wind-Qi and water essence not entering, this being referred to. Note: This translation attempts to maintain the original structure of the Chinese text as much as possible

    4: 夫實者，氣入也。虛者，氣出也。氣實者，熱也。氣虛者，寒也。入實者，左手開鍼空也。入虛者，左手閉鍼空也。 In reality, it is about the inward and outward flow of vital energy. When it is substantial, it is like Qi entering. When it is empty, it is like Qi exiting. When the Qi is substantial, it is like heat. When the Qi is empty, it is like cold. When entering something that is substantial, use your left hand to open the knife space. When entering something that is empty, use your left hand to close the knife space.
