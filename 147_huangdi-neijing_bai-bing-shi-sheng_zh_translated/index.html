    百病始生

    1: 黃帝問于歧伯曰：夫百病之始生也，皆生於風雨寒暑，清濕喜怒，喜怒不節則傷藏，風雨則傷上，清濕則傷下。三部之氣所傷異類，願聞其會，歧伯曰：三部之氣各不同或起於陰或起於陽請言其方，喜怒不節則傷藏，藏傷則病起於陰也，清濕襲虛，則病起於下，風雨襲虛，則病起於上，是謂三部，至於其淫泆，不可勝數。 Emperor Yellow asked Master Qi Bo: All hundred diseases are caused by external factors such as wind, rain, heat and cold, joy and anger. If joy and anger are not regulated, they will harm the interior; if there is an attack of wind and rain, it harms the exterior; if there is an attack of cold and dampness, it harms the lower part. I want to know how the three departments of Qi are affected differently, and how they may arise from Yin or Yang. Master Qi Bo replied: The three departments of Qi have different manifestations depending on whether they originate from Yin or Yang. If joy and anger are not regulated, it harms the interior, thus giving rise to diseases in the Yin; if there is an attack by cold and dampness, it targets the lower part, thus giving rise to diseases below; if there is an attack by wind and rain, it targets the upper part, thus giving rise to diseases above. This is how the three departments of Qi can be affected, and their excesses are countless.

    2: 黃帝曰：余固不能數，故問先師願卒聞其道，歧伯曰：風雨寒熱不得虛，邪不能獨傷人。卒然逢疾風暴雨而不病者，蓋無虛，故邪不能獨傷人。此必因虛邪之風，與其身形，兩虛相得，乃客其形。兩實相逢，眾人肉堅，其中於虛邪也因於天時，與其身形，參以虛實，大病乃成，氣有定舍，因處為名，上下中外，分為三員。 Yellow Emperor said: I am indeed unable to count [on the body's defensive Qi], so I ask Master Qi to please explain its function. Qi Bo said: Wind and rain, cold and heat cannot harm [the body] when it is empty (without defensive Qi). Those who encounter sudden fever, windstorm, and heavy rain but do not fall ill are because they are empty, so the pathogenic factor cannot harm them. This must be due to the relationship between the defensive and pathogenic Qi and their bodily form. When one's body is robustly healthy, the pathogenic factor will have no place to attach. The disease arises from the interaction of defensive and pathogenic Qi at a particular time in accordance with one's bodily form. Therefore, there are three divisions: upper, middle, and lower.

    3: 是故虛邪之中人也，始於皮膚，皮膚緩則腠理開，開則邪從毛髮入，入則抵深，深則毛髮立，毛髮立則淅然，故皮膚痛。留而不去，則傳舍於絡脈，在絡之時，痛於肌肉，故痛之時息，大經乃代，留而不去，傳舍於經，在經之時，洒淅喜驚。留而不去，傳舍於輸，在輸之時，六經不通四肢，則肢節痛，腰脊乃強，留而不去，傳舍於伏衝之脈，在伏衝之時體重身痛，留而不去，傳舍於腸胃，在腸胃之時，賁響腹脹，多寒則腸鳴飱泄，食不化，多熱則溏出糜。留而不去，傳舍於腸胃之外，募原之間，留著於脈，稽留而不去，息而成積，或著孫脈，或著絡脈，或著經脈，或著輸脈，或著於伏衝之脈，或著於膂筋，或著於腸胃之募原，上連於緩筋，邪氣淫泆，不可勝論。 Therefore, people who are possessed by demons and spirits, start from the skin. When the skin is loose, the hair-pores open up, and when they open up, the evil enters through the hairs. Once it has entered deeply, the hairs stand on end, which causes a state of bewilderment. Hence, there is pain in the skin. If one does not remove it, it passes into the sinews, at the time of the sinews there is pain in the muscles, thus at the time of the muscles one sighs, and the Great Classic speaks metaphorically. If one does not remove it, it passes into the channels, at the time of the channels there is dizziness, joy, and shock. If one does not remove it, it passes into the network vessels, at the time of the network vessels, the six channels do not connect to the limbs, causing joint pain, the loins and back become strong. If one does not remove it, it passes into the buried tendons' vessels, at the time of the buried tendons' vessels, the body feels heavy and aches in the entire body, if one does not remove it, it passes into the intestines and stomach, at the time of the intestines and stomach, there is distension in the abdomen. If there is cold, then the intestines make gurgling sounds and leak, digestion is impaired. If there is heat, then there is discharge of pus. If one does not remove it, it passes into the outside of the intestines and the original region, lodging in the vessels, accumulating further and sometimes attaching to the branch vessel, sometimes to the network vessel, sometimes to the channel vessel, sometimes to the drainage vessel, sometimes to the buried tendons' vessel, sometimes to the sinews and muscles, sometimes to the intestines and stomach's original region, ascending to the relaxed tendons, evil qi flows wantonly and cannot be described in detail.

    4: 黃帝曰：願盡聞其所由然。歧伯曰：其著孫絡之脈而成積者，其積往來上下，臂小孫絡之居也，浮而緩，不能句積而止之，故往來移行腸胃之間，水湊滲注灌，濯濯有音，有寒則䐜䐜滿雷引，故時切痛，其著於陽明之經則挾臍而居，飽食則益大，飢則益小。其著於緩筋也，似陽明之積，飽食則痛，飢則安。其著於腸胃之募原也，痛而外連於緩筋，飽食則安，飢則痛。其著於伏沖之脈者，揣之應手而動，發手則熱氣下於兩股，如湯沃之狀。其著於膂筋，在腸後者飢則積見，飽則積不見，按之不得。其著於輸之脈者，閉塞不通，津液不下，孔竅干壅，此邪氣之從外入內，從上下也。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear its origin clearly. Guan Pu said: It is due to the accumulation of Sun SiMiao's channels, which come and go up and down. The Small Intestine channel is related to Sun SiMiao, floating and slow, unable to stop the accumulation, so it comes and goes, moving between the stomach and intestines, regulating water, and generating sound and cold, causing pain when full. When it reaches the Yang Ming Meridian, it lodges in the connective tissue, and when one is overfed, it increases, while when hungry, it decreases. It lodges in the Shen Jin (tendons) and seems similar to the Yang Ming accumulation. When overfed, it causes pain, and when hungry, it becomes comfortable. It lodges in the source of the intestines and connects with the tendons outside, causing discomfort when hungry and pain when full. It lodges in the Fuai Meridian's channel, where it can be felt as a lump when hungry and not seen when full, and pressing on it is painful. It lodges in the vessel (Yin Wei) channel, where it can be felt by pressing the hand and moving the hand generates heat that goes down to the knees, like boiling water. It lodges in the Jue Yin Meridian, where pressing it causes discomfort at the back of the muscles of the intestines when hungry and not seen when full, and pressing on it is painful. It lodges in the Xuan Yin channel, which is blocked and does not circulate, leading to a buildup of body fluids, stuffiness in the orifices, and this is external pathogenic qi invading internally from outside and from above and below.

    5: 黃帝曰：積之始生，至其已成，奈何？歧伯曰：積之始生，得寒乃生，厥乃成積也，黃帝曰：其成積奈何？歧伯曰：厥氣生足悗，悗生脛寒，脛寒則血脈凝澀，血脈凝澀則寒氣上入于腸胃，入于腸胃則䐜脹，䐜脹則腸外之汁沫迫聚不得散，日以成積。卒然多食飲，則腸滿，起居不節，用力過度，則絡脈傷，陽絡傷則血外溢，血外溢則衄血，陰絡傷則血內溢，血內溢則後血。腸胃之絡傷則血溢於腸外，腸外有寒，汁沫與血相搏，則併合凝聚不得散，而積成矣。卒然中外於寒，若內傷於憂怒，則氣上逆，氣上逆則六俞不通，溫氣不行，凝血蘊裏而不散，津液澀滲，著而不去，而積皆成矣。 Yellow Emperor asked: What do you mean by accumulating something that is just beginning to form, and what happens when it has been completed? Qi Bo replied: When something is just beginning to form and accumulates, it requires cold to be generated, and it reaches completion through yin. Yellow Emperor asked: And how does it become complete? Qi Bo replied: When the qi becomes abundant, the kidney qi generates cold, which causes the blood vessels to congeal and thicken. Congealed and thickened blood vessels cause cold qi to ascend into the stomach and intestines, leading to nausea and bloating. Nausea and bloating cause digestive fluids to clump together and not be able to disperse, eventually resulting in accumulation. If one eats and drinks excessively, the stomach becomes full, leading to irregular posture and overstraining of the tendons and vessels, causing injury to the yang vessels, which results in bleeding externally. Injury to the yin vessels causes bleeding internally, leading to dark blood. Injury to the stomach and intestines' vessels causes bleeding outside of the stomach, where cold exists, and digestive fluids clash with blood, causing them to coagulate and not disperse, eventually resulting in accumulation. In cases of excess internal and external cold, if one injures oneself internally due to worry and anger, qi will rise reversely, leading to the six channels becoming blocked, with stagnant qi not circulating, clotting blood within the vessels and not dispersing, causing fluids to become thick and sticky, leading to accumulation.

    6: 黃帝曰：其生於陰者，奈何？歧伯曰：憂思傷心，重寒傷肺，忿怒傷肝，醉以入房，汗出當風傷脾，用力過度，若入房汗出洛，則傷腎，此內外三部之所生病者也。 Yellow Emperor asked: What about the one born of Yin? Huangdi replied: Sorrow, grief, and anxiety strain the heart; anger strains the liver; drinking leads to entering a room, sweating in the wind strains the spleen; overexertion can lead to sweating in Liao, thereby straining the kidneys. These are the diseases caused by internal and external organs.

    7: 黃帝曰：善治之奈何？歧伯答曰：察其所痛，以知其應，有餘不足，當補則補，當寫則寫，毋逆天時，是謂至治。 Yellow Emperor asked, What is the way of good governance? The Duke of Zhou replied, Inspect what causes suffering and know what it requires. If there is not enough, one should supplement; if something needs to be written, then write it. Do not oppose heaven's decrees. This is the ultimate in governance.
