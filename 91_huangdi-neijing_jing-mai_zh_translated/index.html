    經脈

    1. 雷公問於黃帝曰：「禁脈」之言，凡刺之理，經脈為始，營其所行，制其度量，內次五藏，外別六府，願盡聞其道。 Master Lái asked Emperor Yellow: Regulating the pulse is a topic, in which the theory of needling pertains. The pulse originates the pathway, charts its course, measures it, arranges its internal divisions (the Five Organs) and its external areas (the Six Departments), wishing to hear about its way.

    2. 黃帝曰：人始生，先成精，精成而腦髓生，骨為幹，脈為營，筋為剛，肉為牆，皮膚堅而毛髮長，穀入于胃，脈道以通，血氣乃行。 The Yellow Emperor said: When a person is first born, they are initially pure. This purity leads to the development of their brain and spinal cord. Their bones serve as the framework, their vessels serve as their territory, their tendons provide strength, their flesh serves as the walls, their skin is firm while their hair and fur grow long. Their grains enter the stomach, their channels circulate throughout, and blood and qi function properly.

    3. 雷公曰：願卒聞經脈之始生。黃帝曰：經脈者，所以能決死生、處百病、調虛實，不可不通。 Lei Gong said: I wish to hear about the origins of the meridians. Huang Di said: The meridians are what allow us to determine life and death, treat a hundred diseases, and regulate real and fake, and cannot be ignored.

    4. 肺手太陰之脈，起於中焦，下絡大腸，還循胃口，上膈屬肺，從肺系橫出腋下，下循臑內，行少陰心主之前，下肘中，循臂內上骨下廉，入寸口，上魚，循魚際，出大指之端；其支者，從腕後直出次指內廉出其端。 The lung channel's conception vessel pulse, originating from the middle burner, passes through the large intestine, ascends along the stomach opening, connects to the lung, exits horizontally from the axilla, descends inside the muscle, proceeds along the small intestine channel, reaches the heart governor's front, descends at the elbow's center, follows the arm's interior along the bone and shin, enters the foot's portal, ascends along the flexor tendon, follows the Achilles tendon, exits at the tip of the great toe; its connecting vessel is from the wrist back, straight out to the side of the ring finger's base.

    5. 是動則病肺脹滿，膨脹而喘咳，缺盆中痛，甚則交兩手而瞀，此為臂厥。是主肺所生病者，咳上氣，喘渴，煩心，胸滿，臑臂內前廉痛厥，掌中熱。氣盛有餘，則肩背痛，風寒汗出中風，小便數而欠。氣虛則肩背痛，寒，少氣不足以息，溺色變。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，寸口大三倍於人迎，虛者，則寸口反小於人迎也。 The text describes the symptoms and treatment of a disease affecting the lung, characterized by fullness, wheezing, coughing, pain in the lower abdomen, and other related issues. It also provides instructions for diagnosing and treating the condition based on whether it is excessive or deficient, hot or cold, and whether it has sunk down or not. The text concludes with details about how the pulse at a specific acupuncture point should appear under different circumstances.

    Translation:

    A disease of lung Qi stagnation presents with fullness and pain, causing bloating and wheezing, accompanied by coughing. There is pain in the lower abdomen, and in severe cases, the patient may clutch their chest and gasp for breath, manifesting as Qi stagnation. This condition affects those with lung disorders. The patient experiences coughing up Qi, shortness of breath, restlessness, fullness in the chest, pain in the inner aspect of the arm, and heat in the palm. If there is an excess of Qi, there will be pain in the shoulders and back, sweating due to wind-heat, and frequent urination with dribbling. If Qi is deficient, there will be pain in the shoulders and back, coldness, and insufficient Qi to dispel pathogens, leading to a changed appearance.

    To treat these various illnesses, first determine if they are excessive or deficient, hot or cold, and whether they have sunk down. If there is an excess, the pulse at a specific acupuncture point will be large, three times the size of a normal pulse. If there is a deficiency, the pulse will be smaller than normal. The treatment approach varies depending on whether it is excessive or deficient, hot or cold, and whether it has sunk down or not.

    1. 大腸手陽明之脈，起於大指次指之端，循指上廉，出合谷兩骨之間，上入兩筋之中，循臂上廉，入肘外廉，上臑外前廉，上肩，出髃骨之前廉，上出於柱骨之會上，下入缺盆，絡肺，下膈，屬大腸。其支者，從缺盆上頸，貫頰，入下齒中，還出挾口，交人中，左之右，右之左，上挾鼻孔。 The Large Intestine Channel of the Yang Ming meridian begins at the root of the index finger, ascends along the back of the index finger, emerges between the two bones in the crease of the wrist, enters the muscle and rises to the surface, passing over the outer aspect of the elbow, up to the outer aspect of the shoulder, emerging from the front of the mastoid process, exiting at the junction of the axis bone, descending into the hollow of the inguinal ligament, connecting with the lungs, moving downward, it connects with the Conception Vessel and nourishes the large intestine. Its branch (the Channel of the Yang Ming) starts from the inguinal ligament, ascends to the back of the neck, passes through the mouth, exits the throat, crosses in front of the nostrils, left over right, right over left, and ascends to the nasal opening.

    2. 是動則病齒痛，頸腫。是主津液所生病者，目黃，口乾，鼽衄，喉痺，肩前臑痛，大指次指痛不用，氣有餘則當脈所過者熱腫；虛則寒慄不復。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，人迎大三倍於寸口；虛者，人迎反小於寸口也。 If the condition is caused by movement, there will be toothache and neck swelling. The patient suffers from a disease associated with the production of body fluids and exhibits symptoms such as yellow eyes, dry mouth, nosebleeds, a sore throat, pain in the shoulder blade, and pain in the second and third fingers that are difficult to use. If there is an excess of Qi, the pulse will be rapid at specific points along the meridian, accompanied by heat and swelling; if there is deficiency, there will be chills and no recovery.

    For these symptoms, if there is an excess or a deficiency, write down the prescription; if there is heat or cold, act accordingly; if there is stagnation, use acupuncture; and if there is nonexcess or deficiency, apply the meridian points according to the classics. In cases of excess, the patient will be greeted by the Lung, Large Intestine, and Stomach meridians three times as large as the Conception Vessel at the point; in cases of deficiency, the patient will be greeted by the Conception Vessel, which is smaller than the point on the Conception Vessel.

    1. 胃足陽明之脈，起於鼻之交頞中，旁納太陽之脈，下循鼻外，入上齒中，還出挾口環唇，下交承漿，卻循頤後下廉，出大迎，循頰車，上耳前，過客主人，循髮際，至額顱；其支者，從大迎前下人迎，循喉嚨，入缺盆，下膈，屬胃，絡脾；其直者，從缺盆下乳內廉，下挾臍，入氣沖中；其支者，起於胃口，下循腹裏，下至氣沖中而合，以下髀關，抵伏兔，下膝臏中，下循脛外廉，下足跗，入中指內間；其支者，下廉三寸而別下入中趾外間；其支者，別跗上，入大趾間出其端。 The Pulse: Yangming, also called the Governing Vessel, begins at the intersection of the nostrils. It runs alongside the Great Vessel, descending externally along the nose, entering the upper gum between the lips, emerging below the cheekbone, passing in front of the ear, crossing the temple, and ending at the hairline; its branch is called Jueyin, which originates from the juncture just below the Great Vessel's external end, descends along the throat, enters the hollow under the breast, connecting to the stomach, uniting with the spleen; its direct branch starts from the hollow beneath the breast and descends along the inguinal region, entering the bladder hole, connecting to the diaphragm, relating to the stomach, nourishing the spleen; its branch begins at the end of the stomach, descending through the abdomen, reaching the bladder hole, and merging there, then proceeding down the outer edge of the shin, below the little toe, entering the middle space between the toes; its branch originates from below the ankle, enters the big toe's outer corner and exits.

    2. 是動則病洒洒振寒，善呻，數欠，顏黑，病至則惡人與火，聞木聲則惕然而惊，心欲動，獨閉戶塞牖而處。甚則欲上高而歌，棄衣而走，賁嚮腹脹，是為骭厥。是主血所生病者，狂瘧溫淫，汗出，鼽衄，口喎，唇胗，頸腫，喉痺，大腹水腫，膝臏腫痛，循膺乳、氣沖、股、伏兔、骭外廉、足跗上皆痛，中趾不用，氣盛則身以前皆熱，其有餘於胃，則消穀善飢，溺色黃；氣不足則身以前皆寒慄，胃中寒則脹滿。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，人迎大三倍於寸口，虛者，人迎反小於寸口也。 The disease caused by excessive movement is characterized by trembling, shivering, and a pale complexion. As the disease worsens, the patient becomes hostile and uncooperative, jumps at the sound of footsteps, and their heart palpitates. They close themselves in a dark room. In severe cases, they want to climb high and sing, discard their clothes and run around, experiencing abdominal pain and distension, this is called jia yi. This disease is caused by excessive bleeding and is characterized by madness, fever, sweating, bloody discharge, a hoarse voice, swollen lips, sore throat, goiter, and difficulty swallowing. Patients also experience abdominal swelling, knee pain, lower back pain, swelling and pain in the hui, lao, qi, kou, and chen regions, pain in the ankle joints, aching soles of the feet, and the big toe is unable to bear weight. If there is an excess of this condition, patients may have excessive hunger and yellow complexion, or if there is not enough Qi, they will feel cold and tremble, with distension in the abdomen. For these diseases, when there is an excess, you should write down the symptoms, when there is a deficiency, you should supplement it, when there is heat, you should release it, and when there is cold, you should retain it. If the disease sinks down, you should use acupuncture. If there is neither excess nor deficiency, you should treat according to the classical texts. When there is an excess, the patient will present symptoms three times greater than at the guan (governor) vessel point, and when there is a deficiency, the patient will present symptoms smaller than at the guan vessel point.

    3. 脾足太陰之脈，起於大趾之端，循趾內側白肉際，過核骨後，上內踝前廉，上踹內，循脛骨後，交出厥陰之前，上膝股內前廉，入腹，屬脾，絡胃，上膈，挾咽，連吞本，散舌下；其支者，復從胃，別上膈、注心中。 The conception vessel, starting at the center of the foot where the spleen and kidney yin meet, follows the inner white side of the big toe, crossing the exocrine bone behind, ascends to the inner crook in front, goes along the back of the shankbone, connects with the beginning of the penile vessel outside the pubic arch, enters the abdomen, belongs to the spleen, nourishes the stomach, lifts the throat, links the origin of swallowing, disperses below the tongue; its connecting vessel, again comes from the stomach, separates upward to regulate the heart.

    4. 是動則病舌本強，食則嘔，胃脘痛，腹脹，善噫，得後與氣，則快然如衰，身體皆重。是主脾所生病者，舌本痛，體不能動搖，食不下，煩心，心下急痛，溏瘕泄，水閉，黃疸，不能臥，強立，股膝內腫厥，足大趾不用。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，寸口大三倍於人迎，虛者，寸口反小於人迎。 This passage discusses the diagnosis and treatment of various illnesses in traditional Chinese medicine, based on the condition of the patient's tongue.

    Translation: When there is movement (of the body), it is due to illness of the sinew; when eating, there is vomiting, pain in the abdomen, distension in the belly, burping, and quick recovery after defecation, and the body feels heavy. This pertains to patients with illnesses caused by the spleen. The tongue is painful, the body cannot move freely, and food does not descend. The mind is restless, there is pain below the heart, there is discharge of pus, urinary retention, jaundice, hemorrhoids, and an inability to lie down. When standing, there is swelling and numbness in the groin and knees, and the toes cannot be lifted.

    For these illnesses, if they are predominant, write them down; if they are deficient, supplement them; if they are warm, disperse them; if they are cold, retain them; if they have sunken, use acupuncture; if they do not fit any of the above, use the classics. For the predominant ones, the pulse at the right spring (location) is three times larger than that of a normal person; for the deficient ones, it is smaller than that of a normal person.

    1. 心手少陰之脈，起於心中，出屬心系，下膈，絡小腸；其支者，從心系，上挾咽，系目系；其直者，復從心系卻上肺，下出腋下，下循臑內後廉，行太陰心主之後，下肘內，循臂內後廉，抵掌後銳骨之端，入掌內後廉，循小指之內，出其端。 The heart meridian of the spleen and stomach starts at the center of the chest, belonging to the heart channel. It goes down to the abdomen and connects with the small intestine. Its branch goes from the heart channel up to the throat, connecting with the governor vessel; its direct course goes from the heart channel but up to the lung, exiting at the inframammary crease, descending along the inner rear crest of the oblique muscles, following the sinewy conduit of the small intestine, and entering the rear of the wrist's sharp end. It goes along the inner part of the little finger and exits at its tip.

    2. 是動則病嗌干，心痛，渴而欲飲，是為臂厥。是主心所生病者，目黃，脅痛，臑臂內後廉痛厥，掌中熱痛。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，寸口大再倍於人迎，虛者，寸口反小於人迎也。 If there is movement and pain in the tongue, heart palpitations, chest pain, thirst, and a desire to drink, it is due to a weakness of the arm. This pertains to patients with diseases of the heart. The symptoms include yellow eyes, pain behind the navel, pain in the inside of the elbow, pain in the palm, and pain when grasping something. For these diseases, if there is an excess, write about it; if there is a deficiency, supplement it; if there is heat, disperse it; if there is cold, retain it; if it sinks down, use acupuncture; do not have an excess without a deficiency, and take from the meridians according to the Classic of Medicine. If there is an excess, the pulse at the Influential point (LU-1) will be large, double that of a normal person; if there is a deficiency, it will be small compared to a normal person's pulse.

    3. 小腸手太陽之脈，起於小指之端，循手外側，上腕，出踝中，直上循臂骨下廉，出肘內側兩筋之間，上循臑外後廉，出肩解，繞肩胛，交肩上，入缺盆，絡心，循咽，下膈，抵胃，屬小腸；其支者，從缺盆循頸上頰，至目銳眥，卻入耳中；其支者，別頰上䪼，抵鼻，至目內眥，斜絡於顴。 The small intestine channel of the foot Yang Ming begins at the tip of the little finger, follows the outer side of the hand, ascends to the wrist, exits from the crook of the elbow, moves along the outside of the upper arm, exits from the shoulder hollow, encircles the collarbone, crosses the shoulder joint, enters the pit between the two shoulder muscles, connects the heart, follows the throat, descends to the diaphragm, opposes the stomach, belongs to the small intestine. Its connecting point is from the pit of the stomach along the top of the neck to the sharp end of the eye and enters the ear canal. The other connecting point diverges from the upper part of the forehead, opposes the nose, reaches the inner corner of the eye, slantwise connects at the cheekbone.

    4. 是動則病嗌痛，頷腫，不可以顧，肩似拔，臑似折。是主液所生病者，耳聾、目黃，頰腫，頸、頷、肩、臑、肘、臂外後廉痛。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，人迎大再倍於寸口，虛者，人迎反小於寸口也。 The patient has symptoms of qi stagnation and blood stasis, such as pain in the tongue, swollen throat, difficulty swallowing, shoulder pain similar to pulling, elbow pain similar to bending. The chief manifestations of this disease are tinnitus, deafness, yellow eyes, swelling of the cheek, neck, throat, shoulders, elbows, forearms, and numbness on the outer side of the limbs. For these diseases, one should prescribe according to their predominance; if abundant, record it in the case history, if deficient, supplement it. If there is heat, treat it urgently; if cold, let it be. If it sinks downward, use acupuncture and moxibustion. Do not simply rely on the meridians; take the pulse. When abundant, the pulse at the wrist will be twice as strong as that at the heart position, and when deficient, it will be smaller than that at the heart position.

    5. 膀胱足太陽之脈，起於目內眥，上額，交巔；其支者，從巔至耳上角；其直者，從巔入絡腦，還出別下項，循肩髆內，挾脊，抵腰中，入循膂，絡腎，屬膀胱；其支者，從腰中下挾脊，貫臀，入膕中；其支者，從髆內左右，別下，貫胛，挾脊內，過髀樞，循髀外，從後廉，下合膕中，以下貫踹內，出外踝之後，循京骨，至小趾外側。 The pulse of the Chong (Renishing) is located at the interior gate of the eyes, and runs up to the forehead and the vertex; its branch proceeds from the vertex to the top of the ear. Its direct flow goes from the vertex into the network vessel, exits from another lower abdomen, follows the lumbar region, presses on the loin, and arrives at a point in the middle of the back that loops with the sinew vessel, connects with the kidney, and belongs to the Chong. Its branch proceeds from the middle of the back down to the lumber region, penetrates the buttocks, enters the urinary bladder, presses on the inside of the lumbar region, passes the knee joint, loops around the outside of the thigh, from the pubic tuberosity downward merges with the urinary bladder's interior, exits outward from the heel, and follows the shin bone to the little toe's outer edge.

    6. 是動則病沖頭痛，目似脫，項如拔，脊痛，腰似折，髀不可以曲，膕如結，踹如裂，是為踝厥。是主筋所生病者，痔、瘧、狂、癲疾、頭𩕄項痛，目黃、淚出，鼽衄，項、背、腰、尻、膕踹、腳皆痛，小趾不用。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。盛者，人迎大再倍於寸口，虛者，人迎反小於寸口也。 The condition is caused by overexertion, and presents with headache, dizziness, stiffness in the neck, back pain, painful hips that cannot be flexed, knees that feel like they are locked, and ankles that feel like they are split. This is called Gua disease. It affects those who have illnesses of the sinews, such as boils, furuncles, madness, mania, headaches, aching necks, yellow and watery eyes, nasal discharge, stiffness in the neck, back, hips, waist, knees, ankles, and toes that all hurt, and small toe weakness. For this condition, if it is excessive, write it; if it is deficient, tonify it; if there is heat, expel it; if there is cold, retain it; if it sinks downward, use moxibustion; if it neither excesses nor deficiencies, use acupuncture according to the classics. Excess is when the patient faces the practitioner at a distance twice that of the Cun point, and deficiency is when the patient faces the practitioner at a distance smaller than the Cun point.

    7. 腎足少陰之脈，起於小趾之下，邪走足心，出於然谷之下，循內踝之後，別入跟中，以上踹內，出膕內廉，上股內後廉，貫脊，屬腎，絡膀胱；其直者，從腎上貫肝膈，入肺中，循喉嚨，挾舌本；其支者，從肺出絡心，注胸中。 The pulse of the Spleen and Kidney Yang is situated at the bottom of the small toe. If pathogenic factors travel to the heart, it comes out from the groove below, following the inner calf, not entering the heel, rising above the pubic tubercle, coming out before the inguinal ligament, penetrating the groin and entering the kidney region, passing through the spine. It belongs to the Spleen and nourishes the Bladder. Its direct path is from the Spleen, passing through the Liver and Stomach, entering the Lung, following the throat and grasping the root of the tongue. Its branch path is from the Lung, descending to nourish the Heart, lodging in the chest.

    8. 是動則病飢不欲食，面如漆柴，咳唾則有血，喝喝而喘，坐而欲起，目𥆨𥆨如無所見，心如懸若飢狀。氣不足則善恐，心惕惕如人將捕之，是為骨厥。是主腎所生病者，口熱，舌乾，咽腫，上氣，嗌乾及痛，煩心，心痛，黃疸，腸澼，脊股內後廉痛，痿厥，嗜臥，足下熱而痛。為此諸病，盛則寫之，虛則補之，熱則疾之，寒則留之，陷下則灸之，不盛不虛，以經取之。灸則強食生肉，緩帶披發，大杖重履而步。盛者，寸口大再倍於人迎，虛者，寸口反小於人迎也。 When there is movement, it becomes illness due to hunger and lack of appetite, with a pale face like lacquer on tinder. When coughing up sputum, there is blood. Panting after drinking, sitting and wanting to get up, the eyes roll around like seeing nothing, and the heart feels suspended as if in a state of hunger. Insufficient Qi causes one to be easily frightened, with the mind being alert, like someone about to be caught, which is known as Jade Tear. This governs diseases generated by the Kidney, marked by a red tongue, a dry throat, swollen lymph nodes, a sensation of stuffiness in the chest, a dry and painful tongue, restlessness, heart pain, jaundice, abdominal bloating, lower back and inner thigh pain, impotence, liking to lie down, and hot and painful soles of the feet. For these diseases, if there is excess, regulate it; if deficient, supplement it; if there is heat, disperse it; if cold, retain it; if sunken in, apply acupuncture; if not excess or deficiency, use the Meridians. When applying acupuncture, the size of the opening at the Conception Vessel should be doubled in those with excess and half that in those with deficiency.

    9. 心主手厥陰心包絡之脈，起於胸中，出屬心包絡，下膈，歷絡三焦；其支者，循胸出脅，下腋三寸，上抵腋下，循臑內，行太陰、少陰之間，入肘中，下臂，行兩筋之間，入掌中，循中指，出其端；其支者，別掌中，循小指次指，出其端。 The radial artery is the pulse taken at the wrist. It is derived from the brachial artery, which branches off from the axillary artery (a continuation of the subclavian artery).
