    決氣

    1. 黃帝曰：余聞人有精、氣、津、液、血、脈，余意以為一氣耳，今乃辨為六名，余不知其所以然。歧伯曰：兩神相搏，合而成形，常先身生，是謂精。何謂氣？歧伯曰：上焦開發，宣五穀味，熏膚、充身、澤毛，若霧露之溉，是謂氣。何謂津？歧伯曰：腠理發泄，汗出溱溱，是謂津。何謂液？歧伯曰：穀入氣滿，淖澤注于骨，骨屬屈伸，泄澤補益腦髓，皮膚潤澤，是謂液。何謂血？歧伯曰：中焦受氣，取汁變化而赤，是謂血。何謂脈？歧伯曰：壅遏營氣，令無所避，是謂脈。 Yellow Emperor said: I have heard that there are the spirit, qi, fluids, body fluids, blood, and pulse. I thought they were all one qi, but now they are distinguished as six names, and I do not know why. Hui Bo said: The two spirits struggle, combine to form a shape, constantly preceding the body's birth, which is called the spirit. What is qi? Hui Bo said: The upper burner refines the grain flavors, disperses them through the skin and fills the body, like the mist on dew-covered grass. What is fluids? Hui Bo said: The sinews discharge waste, sweat drips droplet by droplet, this is called fluids. What is body fluids? Hui Bo said: The grain enters the qi and nourishes the bones, the bones belong to bending and stretching, releasing fluids to supplement and nurture the marrow and brain, making the skin moist and soft, this is called body fluids. What is blood? Hui Bo said: The middle burner receives qi and transforms it into red, this is called blood. What is pulse? Hui Bo said: Accumulated stagnation of qi, causing no place to avoid, this is called the pulse.

    2. 黃帝曰：六氣有，有餘不足，氣之多少，腦髓之虛實，血脈之清濁，何以知之？歧伯曰：精脫者，耳聾；氣脫者，目不明；津脫者，腠理開，汗大泄；液脫者，骨屬屈伸不利，色夭，腦髓消，脛痺，耳數鳴；血脫者，色白，夭然不澤，其脈空虛，此其候也。 Yellow Emperor said: There are six types of energy. How can one tell whether there is an excess or deficiency, the abundance or scarcity of energy, the truth or falseness of the brain and marrow, and the clarity or turbidity of blood and vessels? Bo Yi replied: Those who lose their vital essence will have hearing problems; those who lose their energy will not see clearly; those who lose their body fluids will have diarrhea and excessive sweating; those who lose their bodily fluids will have bones that are rigid, withered, and unable to bend or stretch, pale complexion, brain and marrow deterioration, paralysis of the limbs, and frequent ringing in the ears; those who lose blood will have a pale complexion, withering without vitality, their pulse is empty and weak, and this is indicative of their condition.

    3. 黃帝曰：六氣者，貴賤何如？歧伯曰：六氣者，各有部主也，其貴賤善惡，可為常主，然五穀與胃為大海也。 Yellow Emperor said: What about the six energies, noble and base? Duke of Zhou said: The six energies each have their own master, and their nobility and baseness can be a constant master. However, five grains and the stomach are like a great sea.
