    周痺

    1: 黃帝問于歧伯曰：周痺之在身也，上下移徒隨脈，其上下左右相應，間不容空，願聞此痛，在血脈之中邪？將在分肉之間乎？何以致是？其痛之移也，間不及下鍼，其慉痛之時，不及定治，而痛已止矣。何道使然？願聞其故？歧伯答曰：此眾痺也，非周痺也。 The Yellow Emperor asked Chi Bo: In the case of the 'Week-recurrent pain,' it moves up and down following the pulse, with corresponding changes above and below. There is no room for anything in between, and I wish to know if this pain is within the blood vessels or in the muscles. How did this occur? The moving pain does not allow time for needle insertion, nor does there seem to be a proper time for treatment, yet the pain has already subsided. What can be said about this? Please explain the cause. Chi Bo replied: This is a case of 'massive pain,' not 'week-recurrent pain.'

    2: 黃帝曰：願聞眾痺。歧伯對曰：此各在其處，更發更止，更居更起，以右應左，以左應右，非能周也。更發更休也。黃帝曰：善。刺之奈何？歧伯對曰：刺此者，痛雖已止，必刺其處，勿令復起。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about various joint pains. Hua Tuo replied: Each one is located in its place, with treatment and rest, moving and raising, right responding to left and left responding to right, not in the circle. Treating with needles, how should it be done? Hua Tuo said: The one who is pricked feels pain and stops, but must be pricked at the location to prevent it from rising again.

    3: 帝曰：善。願聞周痺何如？歧伯對曰：周痺者，在於血脈之中，隨脈以上，隨脈以下，不能左右，各當其所。黃帝曰：刺之奈何？歧伯對曰：痛從上下者，先刺其下以過之，後刺其上以脫之。痛從下上者，先刺其上以過之，後刺其下以脫之。 The Emperor asked: Good. I wish to hear about the jaundice of the Zhou Dynasty? Taoist master Qi Bo replied: The jaundice of the Zhou Dynasty is within the blood vessels, moving along with the blood flow above and below, not being able to turn left or right, each according to its place. Emperor asked: How should it be needled? Taoist master Qi Bo said: If the pain comes from above and below, first needle below and then above to expel it; if the pain comes from below and above, first needle above and then below to expel it.

    4: 黃帝曰：善。此痛安生？何因而有名？歧伯對曰：風寒濕氣，客於外分肉之間，迫切而為沫，沫得寒則聚，聚則排分肉而分裂也，分裂則痛，痛則神歸之，神歸之則熱，熱則痛解，痛解則厥，厥則他痺發，發則如是。帝曰：善。余已得其意矣。此內不在藏，而外未發於皮，獨居分肉之間，真氣不能周，故名曰周痺。故刺痺者，必先切循其下之六經，視其虛實，及大絡之血結而不通，及虛而脈陷空者而調之，熨而通之。其瘈堅轉引而行之。黃帝曰：善。余已得其意矣，亦得其事也。九者經巽之理，十二經脈陰陽之病也。 Yellow Emperor said: Good. Is this pain related to a new generation? What caused it to be named as such? Huang Di replied: Cold, dry winds and dampness, invading from the outside and acting between layers of flesh, causing a coating that becomes cold, which then accumulates and separates the flesh, leading to pain, and the spirit returns to this area, causing heat, heat resolves the pain, pain dissipates and leads to collapse, collapse triggers paralysis, paralysis occurs as such. Yellow Emperor said: Good. I have now understood it completely. This is not inside the stores, but outside manifesting on the skin, uniquely situated between layers of flesh, where true qi cannot circulate fully, so it is called circumferential paralysis. Therefore, when needling circumferential paralysis, one must first cut along its course below the six channels, observe its emptiness or fullness, and address any blood stagnation blocking the great vessels that do not flow freely and any empty veins causing a sunken pulse, using moxa to open them up. Finally, lead it in a firm, turning manner. Yellow Emperor said: Good. I have now understood completely, and also know how to apply this. These nine channels are related to the Pure Yang channel, and the twelve channels correspond to Yin-Yang diseases
