    舉痛論

    1: 黃帝問曰：余聞善言天者，必有驗於人；善言古者，必有合於今；善言人者，必有厭於己。如此，則道不惑而要數極，所謂明也。今余問於夫子，令言而可知，視而可見，捫而可得，令驗於己而發蒙解惑，可得而聞乎。歧伯再拜稽首對曰：何道之問也。 The Yellow Emperor asked: I have heard that the words of the sage who speaks of heaven are verified in people; the words of the ancient sage are applicable to today; and the words of a human sage must be weary of oneself. If so, then the Way is not confused and has a clear extreme, which is called clarity. Now, I ask Master Zhou about this: let me explain it so that it can be understood, see it so that it can be seen, cultivate it so that it can be obtained, let it be verified in oneself and illuminate confusion, obtain it and hear about it. Huan He bowed twice, knelt down, and said, What is the meaning of asking about the Way?

    2: 帝曰：願聞人之五藏卒痛，何氣使然。歧伯對曰：經脈流行不止，環周不休，寒氣入經而稽遲，泣而不行，客於脈外則血少，客於脈中則氣不通，故卒然而痛。 The emperor said, I wish to hear the reason why a person's five viscera suffer from acute pain. The Duke of Tang replied, The channels and network vessels are not at rest, and the cold Qi invades the channels, causing the person to cry but unable to stand. If the disorder is outside the channels, there will be insufficient blood; if it is within the channels, the Qi will not circulate freely, leading to acute pain.

    3: 帝曰：其痛或卒然而止者，或痛甚不休者，或痛甚不可按者，或按之而痛止者，或按之無益者，或喘動應手者，或心與背相引而痛者，或脇肋與少腹相引而痛者，或腹痛引陰股者，或痛宿昔而成積者，或卒然痛死不知人，有少閒復生者，或痛而嘔者，或腹痛而後泄者，或痛而閉不通者，凡此諸痛，各不同形，別之柰何。 The emperor said: Some pains may suddenly stop, some pains are too severe to rest, some pains cannot be pressed, or pressing alleviates the pain, or pressing is of no benefit, or gasping and extending a hand, or pain with the heart and back pulling each other, or the side ribs and lower abdomen pulling each other causing pain, or pain in the abdomen referring to the yin channel, some pains that were sudden but became chronic, or pains that suddenly cause death without knowing the person, are there any who recover a little, or pains that cause vomiting, or pain in the abdomen that then causes diarrhea, or pain that causes difficulty breathing, all these different types of pain, how can they be distinguished?

    4: 歧伯曰：寒氣客於脈外，則脈寒，脈寒則縮踡，縮踡則脈絀急，則外引小絡，故卒然而痛，得炅則痛立止，因重中於寒，則痛久矣。寒氣客於經脈之中，與炅氣相薄，則脈滿，滿則痛而不可按也。寒氣稽留，炅氣從上，則脈充大而血氣亂，故痛甚不可按也。寒氣客於腸胃之閒，膜原之下，血不得散，小絡急引故痛，按之則血氣散，故按之痛止。寒氣客於俠脊之脈，則深按之不能及，故按之無益也。寒氣客於衝脈，衝脈起於關元，隨腹直上，寒氣客則脈不通，脈不通則氣因之，故喘動應手矣。寒氣客於背俞之脈則脈泣，脈泣則血虛，血虛則痛，其俞注於心，故相引而痛，按之則熱氣至，熱氣至則痛止矣。寒氣客於厥陰之脈，厥陰之脈者，絡陰器繫於肝，寒氣客於脈中，則血泣脈急，故脇肋與少腹相引痛矣。厥氣客於陰股，寒氣上及少腹，血泣在下相引，故腹痛引陰股。寒氣客於小腸膜原之間，絡血之中，血泣不得注於大經，血氣稽留不得行，故宿昔而成積矣。寒氣客於五藏，厥逆上泄，陰氣竭，陽氣未入，故卒然痛死不知人，氣復反，則生矣。寒氣客於腸胃，厥逆上出，故痛而嘔也。寒氣客於小腸，小腸不得成聚，故後泄腹痛矣。熱氣留於小腸，腸中痛，癉熱焦渴，則堅乾不得出，故痛而閉不通矣。帝曰：所謂言而可知者也。 Master said: When the cold Qi is outside the pulse, the pulse is cold, and when the pulse is cold, it contracts and tightens, causing the pulse to be urgent and leading to external referral of the small vessels. Thus, there is sudden pain. When warmed, the pain stops, because heating is more effective in this case. Cold Qi residing within the channels is thinner than fire Qi, resulting in a full pulse, which hurts but cannot be pressed, and when cold Qi ascends from above, the pulse is large and disorderly, causing great pain that cannot be pressed. When cold Qi resides in the intestines, the ligament of the mucosa does not allow blood to disperse, causing small vessel referral pain; pressing it causes blood to disperse, stopping the pain. Cold Qi residing at the lumbar vertebrae causes a deep pulse that cannot be reached, making pressing ineffective. Cold Qi residing in the Chong channel, originating from the Ren channel and ascending along the middle line, results in an obstructed pulse when cold Qi is present, causing breathing difficulty and shortness of breath. When cold Qi resides at the Bladder channel, the pulse weeps, indicating blood deficiency, which causes pain when it is pulled together by the Bladder; pressing leads to the arrival of fire Qi, stopping the pain. Cold Qi residing in the liver channel causes the reining Qi to be obstructed at the kidneys. Kidney yin is depleted, but yang Qi has not yet entered, causing sudden death without anyone knowing. Reversal of Qi leads to life again. Cold Qi residing in the intestines causes reining Qi to rise, resulting in pain with vomiting. Cold Qi residing in the small intestine causes it not to gather, resulting in diarrhea and lower abdominal pain. When fire Qi remains in the small intestine, there is pain in the intestines, fever, and scorched thirst, leading to constipation and an inability to release them, causing pain and closure without flow. All that is said can be understood.

    5: 視而可見，柰何。歧伯曰：五藏六府，固盡有部，視其五色，黃赤為熱，白為寒，青黑為痛，此所謂視而可見者也。帝曰：捫而可得，柰何。歧伯曰：視其主病之脈堅而血，及陷下者，皆可捫而得也。 Upon seeing, it is clear. How much more so? The Duke of Zhou said: The Five Canons and Six Palaces are indeed complete with their respective divisions. Upon looking at the five colors, yellow and red are hot, white is cold, green and black are painful; this is what is meant by upon seeing it can be known. The Emperor said: If one can obtain them by touching, how much more so? The Duke of Zhou said: By looking at the master's disease pulse and finding it tight and blood-filled, as well as sunken below, these can all be obtained through touch.

    6: 帝曰：善。余知百病生於氣也，怒則氣上，喜則氣緩，悲則氣消，恐則氣下，寒則氣收，炅則氣泄，驚則氣亂，勞則氣耗，思則氣結，九氣不同，何病之生。歧伯曰：怒則氣逆，甚則嘔血及飱泄，故氣上矣。喜則氣和志達，榮衛通利，故氣緩矣。悲則心系急，肺布葉舉，而上焦不通，榮衛不散，熱氣在中，故氣消矣。恐則精卻，卻則上焦閉，閉則氣還，還則下焦脹，故氣不行矣。寒則腠理閉，氣不行，故氣收矣。炅則腠理開，榮衛通汗大泄，故氣泄。驚則心無所倚，神無所歸，慮無所定，故氣亂矣。勞則喘息汗出，外內皆越，故氣耗矣。思則心有所存，神有所歸，正氣留而不行，故氣結矣。 Emperor said: Good. I know that all diseases arise from Qi, anger causes Qi to rise, joy causes Qi to become calm, sorrow causes Qi to dissipate, fear causes Qi to descend, cold causes Qi to contract, heat causes Qi to discharge, shock causes Qi to be disordered, labor causes Qi to deplete, and thought causes Qi to congeal. Qi has nine different manifestations; how can diseases arise?

    Marquis Wen said: When angry, Qi rises up excessively, causing vomiting of blood and purging of the bowels, so it is Qi that rises. When joyful, Qi becomes harmonious and spirit moves smoothly, causing the defense qi to flow freely through the channels, so it is Qi that becomes calm. When sorrowful, the heart is bound tightly, the lungs spread out their leaves and raise them up, yet the middle jiao does not communicate, and the defensive qi does not scatter, so it is Qi that dissipates. When afraid, the vital essence scatters, causing the middle jiao to close, and when closed, the Qi returns and causes the lower jiao to distend, so it is Qi that does not circulate. Cold causes the networks and vessels to contract, preventing Qi from circulating, so it is Qi that contracts. When heated, the networks and vessels open, causing the defense qi to sweat profusely, so it is Qi that discharges. When shocked, the heart has nowhere to rely on, the spirit has nowhere to return, the mind has no place to settle, so it is Qi that becomes disordered. When working hard, there is panting and sweating, both internally and externally are out of balance, so it is Qi that depletes. When thinking, the heart has a place to store, the spirit has a place to return, the genuine qi remains but does not circulate, so it is Qi that congeals
