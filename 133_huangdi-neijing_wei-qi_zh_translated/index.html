    衛氣

    1: 黃帝曰：五藏者，所以藏精神魂魄者也；六府者，所以受水谷而行化物者也。其氣內干五藏，而外絡肢節。其浮氣之不循經者，為衛氣；其精氣之行於經者，為營氣。陰陽相隨，外內相貫，如環之無端。亭亭淳淳乎，孰能竊之。然其分別陰陽，皆有標本虛實所離之處。能別陰陽十二經者，知病之所生；候虛實之所在者，能得病之高下；知六府之氣街者，能知解結契紹於門戶；能知虛石之堅軟者，知補寫之所在；能知六經標本者，可以無惑於天下。 The Yellow Emperor said: The Five Organs are where the spirit and soul reside; the Six Departments are where the transformation of water and grain occurs. Its Qi is internally stored in the five organs, while its tendons and joints are externally connected. The floating Qi that does not follow the channels serves as defense Qi; the circulating Qi within the channels serves as nourishing Qi. Yin and Yang follow each other, and the internal and external influence each other, like a circle without end. Pure and unblemished, who could dare to steal it? However, dividing Yin and Yang has its basis for true and false, which can distinguish between health and disease. Knowing the twelve channels of Yin and Yang can identify the source of illness; understanding where virtuality is located can discern high and low states of illness; knowing the Six Departments' Qi flow can understand the treatment and resolution of knots at doors; knowing the firmness and softness of the empty stone can identify supplements and tonics; and knowing the six channels' foundation can eliminate confusion throughout the world.

    2: 歧伯曰：博哉！聖帝之論。臣請盡意悉言之。足太陽之本，在限以上五寸中，標在兩絡命門。命門者，目也。足少陽之本，在竅陰之間，標在窗籠之前。窗籠者，耳也。足少陰之本，在內踝下上三寸中，標在背輸與舌下兩脈也。足厥陰之本，在行間上五寸所，標在背腧也。足陽明之本，在厲兌，標在人迎，頰挾頏顙也。足太陰之本，在中封前上四寸之中，標在背腧與舌本也。 The Duke said: It is profound! The discourse of the sacred Emperor. I request that you explain this thoroughly to me. The foot Jueyin is located in the upper 5 cun of the Mingmen, marked at the front of the anus. The Mingmen is the eye. The foot Shaoyang is located between the Guan and marked at the front of the window shade. The window shade is the ear. The foot Shaoyin is located in the lower 3 cun of the Yujiapoint, marked at the Sanjiao and Jueyinshu points. The Sanjiao point is the tongue. The foot Taiyin is located in the upper 5 cun of the Guan, marked at the Bladder channel and Congpoint. The foot Yangming is located at the Mingmen, marked at the Renying, between the lips and the cheek. The foot Yinwei is located in the middle 4 cun of the Zhongming, marked at the Bladder channel and Jingwell point.

    3: 手太陽之本，在外踝之後，標在命門之上一寸也。手少陽之本，在小指次指之間上二寸，標在耳後上角下外眥也。手陽明之本，在肘骨中，上至別陽，標在顏下合鉗上也。手太陰之本，在寸口之中，標在腋內動也。手少陰之本，在銳骨之端，標在背腧也。手心主之本，在掌後兩筋之間二寸中，標在腋下下三寸也。 The location of the hand Jupiter is at the root of the thumb, above the heel of the little finger, an inch away. The location of the hand Venus is between the small and index fingers, two inches up from the base of the ear on the outer canthus of the eye. The location of the hand Radiant is in the hollow of the elbow, reaching to the tip of the arm, at the crease below the jaw. The location of the hand Mysterious is in the hollow under the armpit. The location of the hand Receptive is at the tip of the spine, beneath the buttock. The location of the hand Conception is between the tendons of the palm, two inches away from the hollow below the armpit.

    4: 凡候此者，下虛則厥，下盛則熱；上虛則眩，上盛則熱痛。故石者，絕而止之，虛者，引而起之。 For this ailment, if the qi is deficient below, there will be cold; if it is excessive below, there will be fever. If the qi is deficient above, there will be dizziness; if it is excessive above, there will be fever pain. Therefore, for stone, one must stop and expel it, and for the empty (deficiency), one must regulate and lift it up.

The text appears to describe symptoms related to a specific illness or condition and suggests corresponding treatment approaches.

    5: 請言氣街，胸氣有街，腹氣有街，頭氣有街，脛氣有街。故氣在頭者，止之於腦；氣在胸者，止之膺與背腧；氣在腹者，止之背腧，與沖脈於臍左右之動脈者；氣在脛者，止之於氣街，與承山踝上以下。取此者，用毫鍼，必先按而在久應於手，乃刺而予之。所治者，頭痛眩仆，腹痛中滿暴脹，及有新積。痛可移者，易已也；積不痛，難已也。 The text in literary Chinese describes the principles and techniques of acupuncture for treating various conditions. It translates to:

    Ask for the qi on the street, chest qi has a street, abdominal qi has a street, head qi has a street, hip qi has a street. Since the qi is in the head, stop it at the brain; when the qi is in the chest, stop it at the diaphragm and back muscles; when the qi is in the abdomen, stop it at the diaphragm and connect with the great vessel at the left and right lower abdominal dynamic vessels; when the qi is at the hip, stop it on the qi street and connect with the gathering mountain on the soles of the feet. To treat this condition, use a needle and first press with the probe, and if it lasts for a long time, it will be effective in the hand. Those to be treated are headaches and dizziness, abdominal pain and fullness, and swelling and hardness. Pain that can move is easily cured; swelling without pain is difficult to cure.
