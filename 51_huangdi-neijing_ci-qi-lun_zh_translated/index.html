    刺齊論

    1: 黃帝問曰：願聞刺淺深之分。歧伯對曰：刺骨者無傷筋，刺筋者無傷肉，刺肉者無傷脈，刺脈者無傷皮，刺皮者無傷肉，刺肉者無傷筋，刺筋者無傷骨。 The Yellow Emperor asked, I would like to hear about the distinction between shallow and deep needling. Hua Tuo replied, Superficial needling does not harm the tendons, needle insertion into tendons does not harm the flesh, needle insertion into the flesh does not harm the vessels, needle insertion into the vessels does not harm the skin, needle insertion into the skin does not harm the flesh, needle insertion into the flesh does not harm the tendons, and needle insertion into the tendons does not harm the bones.

    2: 帝曰：余未知其所謂，願聞其解。歧伯曰：刺骨無傷筋者，鍼至筋而去，不及骨也。刺筋無傷肉者，至肉而去，不及筋也。刺肉無傷脈者，至脈而去，不及肉也。刺脈無傷皮者，至皮而去，不及脈也。所謂刺皮無傷肉者，病在皮中，鍼入皮中，無傷肉也。刺肉無傷筋者，過肉中筋也。刺筋無傷骨者，過筋中骨也。此之謂反也。 The Emperor said: I do not know what you mean, please explain it to me. Tongboyi said: When piercing the bone without injuring the tendon, you must remove it before reaching the bone. When piercing the tendon without injuring the flesh, you must remove it before reaching the tendon. When piercing the flesh without injuring the blood vessel, you must remove it before reaching the flesh. When piercing the blood vessel without injuring the skin, you must remove it before reaching the blood vessel. To pierce the skin without injuring the flesh means that the disease is in the skin, and by entering the skin, you do not harm the flesh. Piercing the flesh without injuring the tendon indicates that there is a problem with the tendon within the flesh. Piercing the tendon without injuring the bone indicates that there is a problem with the bone within the tendon. This is what is called reversal.
