    示從容論

    1: 黃帝燕坐召雷公而問之曰：汝受術誦書者，若能覽觀雜學，及於比類，通合道理，為余言子所長，五藏六府，膽胃大小腸脾胞膀胱，腦髓涕唾，哭泣悲哀，水所從行，此皆人之所生，治之過失，子務明之，可以十全，即不能知，為世所怨。雷公曰：臣請誦《脈脛》上下篇甚眾多矣，別異比類，猶未能以十全，又安足以明之。帝曰：子別試通五藏之過，六府之所不和，鍼石之敗，毒藥所宜，湯液滋味，具言其狀，悉言以對，請問不知。雷公曰：肝虛腎虛脾虛，皆令人體重煩冤，當投毒藥刺灸砭石湯液，或已，或不已，願聞其解。帝曰：公何年之長而問之少，余真問以自謬也。吾問子窈冥，子言上下篇以對，何也。夫脾虛浮似肺，腎小浮似脾，肝急沈散似腎，此皆工之所時亂也，然從容得之。若夫三藏土木水參居，此童子之所知問之，何也。 Emperor Yellow asked Lei Gong: You are a master of techniques, able to comprehend diverse studies and be proficient in comparison, understand principles and harmonize them. Your teachings should be valuable for my heirs, with the five organs and six bowels, gallbladder, pancreas, kidneys, saliva, tears, sadness - all aspects of human life that need treatment when they go astray. Can you not make them complete? If you do not know how to treat them, you will be criticized by the world.

    Lei Gong replied: I have studied the 'Pulse' and 'Awakening to Life' chapters, which contain many complexities and differences that I have not yet been able to master with perfection. How can I explain it clearly? Emperor Yellow said: Try to understand the deficiencies of the five organs and the six bowels. Explain how they are out of harmony, how needles, stones, and medicine should be applied, and how the taste of broths and fluids should be understood. Please ask me any questions you don't know the answers to.

    Lei Gong said: When the liver, spleen, and kidneys are weak, they cause discomfort in the human body, and I hope to apply acupuncture, moxibustion, stone needles, or medicine. But have I already applied it or not? I wish to know the solution. Emperor Yellow said: Your age is long and you still ask these questions. I truly feel embarrassed to ask you.

    What about your question? asked Lei Gong. Emperor Yellow explained: When the spleen is deficient, it resembles the lungs; when the kidneys are small and deficient, they resemble the spleen; and when the liver is agitated and dispersed, it resembles the kidneys. All of these are temporary imbalances caused by work, but they can be rectified with tranquility.

    Lei Gong then said: The five organs and six bowels follow the phases of earth, metal, fire, and water. How do you know this?

    2: 雷公曰：於此有人，頭痛筋攣骨重怯然少氣，噦噫腹滿，時驚不嗜臥，此何藏之發也。脈浮而弦，切之石堅，不知其解，復問所以三藏者，以知其比類也。帝曰：夫從容之謂也。夫年長則求之於府，年少則求之於經，年壯則求之於藏。今子所言皆失，八風菀熟，五藏消爍，傳邪相受。夫浮而弦者，是腎不足也。沈而石者，是腎氣內著也。怯然少氣者，是水道不行，形氣消索也。欬嗽煩冤者，是腎氣之逆也。一人之氣，病在一藏也。若言三藏俱行，不在法也。雷公曰：於此有人，四支解墯，喘欬血泄，而愚診之，以為傷肺，切脈浮大而緊，愚不敢治，粗工下砭石，病愈多出血，血止身輕，此何物也。帝曰：子所能治，知亦眾多，與此病失矣。譬以鴻飛亦沖於天，夫聖人之治病，循法守度，援物比類，化之冥冥，循上及下，何必守經。今夫脈浮大虛者，是脾氣之外絕，去胃外歸陽明也。夫二火不勝三水，是以脈亂而無常也。四支解墯，此脾精之不行也。喘欬者，是水氣并陽明也。血泄者，脈急血無所行也。若夫以為傷肺者，由失以狂也。不引比類，是知不明也。夫傷肺者，脾氣不守，胃氣不清，經氣不為使，真藏壞決，經脈傍絕，五藏漏泄，不衂則嘔，此二者不相類也。譬如天之無形，地之無理，白與黑相去遠矣。是失吾過矣，以子知之，故不告子，明引比類從容，是以名曰診輕，是謂至道也。 Lei Gong said: In this case, there is a person who suffers from headache and weakness of the sinews and bones, feels full in the abdomen, is frightened and cannot rest at certain times. What sort of exacerbation has led to this manifestation? The pulse is superficial and tight, I do not know its resolution, and again ask about the three stores to know their comparative category. The Emperor said: That which pertains to serenity. If one grows old, he seeks it in the ministry; if young, in the canon; if mature, in the storehouses. Now, what the son says is all lost, as the eight winds are ripe and the five stores are consumed, transmitting falsehoods. The superficial and tight pulse refers to the kidneys not being sufficient. The sinking and hard pulse refers to the kidneys holding their vital force within. The weak in sinews and bones with deficient vitality refers to the disruption of the water passages and the exhaustion of the formative force. The groaning, moaning, and annoyance refer to the reversal of the vital force of the kidneys. One person's vital energy has a disease located in one storehouse. If one speaks of the three stores being all functional, it is not in accordance with the truth.

    Lei Gong said: In this case, there is a person who has weakness and stiffness of the four limbs, gasps and coughs with blood leakage, and misdiagnoses the situation, believing it to be lung damage. He cuts the pulse, which is large and tight, and does not dare to treat, crude practitioners use stone needles, and the illness worsens with increased bleeding, bleeding stops and the person becomes weak. What sort of thing is this? The Emperor said: That which you can treat, I also know to be numerous. With this disease, it differs from what you have described. Compare it to a goose soaring in the sky; a sage's method of treating illness follows the laws, relies on material comparisons, transforms according to ancient models, and adheres to the classics both above and below. How can one not follow the classics?

    Now, with the pulse being large and empty, it refers to the vital energy of the spleen being blocked outside. The two fires not being able to overcome the three waters refer to the disorder of the pulse and lack of constancy. Weakness and stiffness of the four limbs refer to the disruption of water energy and the depletion of formative force. Gasping and coughing, with blood leakage, refers to urgent blood without a path to go. If one believes it to be lung damage, it is due to misunderstanding. Without referring to material comparisons, how can one know?

    The damaging of the lungs refers to the spleen's vital energy not being contained and the stomach's vital energy not being clear, channel vital force not guiding, the true storehouses being destroyed, the meridians of the channels being on the verge of blockage, the five stores leaking, and without restoration, leading to vomiting. This is not in accordance with the two aspects. Compare it to something formless like heaven or rationality like earth, which are far apart. My mistake lies here; since I know what my son says, I do not tell him, but clarify through material comparisons and serenity, this is called light diagnosis, the ultimate way.
