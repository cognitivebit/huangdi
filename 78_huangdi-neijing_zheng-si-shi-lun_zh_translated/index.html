    徵四失論

    1: 黃帝在明堂，雷公侍坐。黃帝曰：夫子所通書受事眾多矣，試言得失之意，所以得之，所以失之。雷公對曰：循經受業，皆言十全，其時有過失者，請聞其事解也。帝曰：子年少智未及邪，將言以雜合耶。夫經脈十二，絡脈三百六十五，此皆人之所明知，工之所循用也。所以不十全者，精神不專，志意不理，外內相失，故時疑殆。診不知陰陽逆從之理，此治之一失矣。受師不卒，妄作雜術，謬言為道，更名自功，妄用砭石，後遺身咎，此治之二失也。不適貧富貴賤之居，坐之薄厚，形之寒溫，不適飲食之宜，不別人之勇怯，不知比類，足以自亂，不足以自明，此治之三失也。診病不問其始，憂患飲食之失節，起居之過度，或傷於毒，不先言此，卒持寸口，何病能中，妄言作名，為粗所窮，此治之四失也。是以世人之語者，馳千里之外，不明尺寸之論，診無人事。治數之道，從容之葆，坐持寸口，診不中五脈，百病所起，始以自怨，遺師其咎。是故治不能循理，棄術於市，妄治時愈，愚心自得。嗚呼，窈窈冥冥，熟知其道。道之大者，擬於天地，配於四海，汝不知道之諭受以明為晦。 Emperor Yellow in the Hall of Illumination, Master Thunder attended. Emperor Yellow asked: Your knowledge of the classics is profound, and your accomplishments numerous. Can you explain how you gained this knowledge and how you lost it? Master Thunder replied: Studying the canon and practicing the arts, all speak of perfection; if at any time one falls short or makes an error, I would like to hear about it to learn from my mistake. Emperor Yellow said: My child, your wisdom has not yet reached maturity; will you mix confused teachings? The classics on the channels and acupoints number twelve channels and 365 collateral vessels; these are well-known and widely used by practitioners. Why are they not all perfect? This is because the spirit is not focused, intentions are unclear, internal and external are out of harmony, leading to doubt and suspicion. If a physician does not understand the principles of yin and yang in diagnosing, this is one mistake. A teacher who does not practice diligently creates mixed treatments and speaks falsely about the way, claiming success for themselves and using oracles, leaving behind a legacy of error, resulting in a second mistake. Not adapting to the differing fortunes of the poor and wealthy, noble and humble, being inconsistent in treating the cold and warm, not suited to the appropriate diets and drinks, not knowing the classics, leading to self-confusion and unable to discern clarity, resulting in a third mistake. A physician who does not ask about the onset of an illness, worries about the patient's improper diet and excessive activity, perhaps causing harm with poisonous medicines, without first mentioning this, hastily prescribes a remedy, leading to confusion about the disease, speaking falsely as if in desperation, resulting in a fourth mistake. Therefore, the language of human beings is heard from afar, not understanding the reasoning behind measurements and prescriptions; medicine cannot be practiced by anyone. The path of treatment must come from a calm and tranquil state of mind, holding the needle with care, and not hitting the correct acupoints, with all diseases originating from this mistake. A hundred illnesses arise, and one begins to blame oneself, leaving behind a legacy of error for future generations. Thus, medicine cannot follow reason, abandoning its methods in despair, practicing falsely at times, with an ignorant heart that derives satisfaction. Alas! How deeply mysterious and profound, truly understanding the way is beyond my comprehension. I beseech you to make it clear for me so that I may understand
