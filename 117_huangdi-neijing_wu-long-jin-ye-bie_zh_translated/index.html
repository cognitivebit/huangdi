    五癃津液別

    1: 黃帝問於歧伯曰：水穀入于口，輸於腸胃，其液別為五，天寒衣薄，則為溺與氣，天熱衣厚則為汗，悲哀氣並則為泣，中熱胃緩則為唾。邪氣內逆，則氣為之閉塞而不行，不行則為水脹，余知其然也，不知其何由生？願聞其道。 The Yellow Emperor asked Yi Yin: Water grains enter the mouth, exit at the stomach and intestines. Their fluids are divided into five types. When it's cold and one is wearing thin clothes, it leads to drowning and sweat; when one is sad, it leads to tears; when the stomach is hot and sluggish, it leads to spittle. Evil qi inside turning over leads to qi being blocked without moving, leading to edema; I know this to be true, but do not know where it comes from. I wish to hear its method.

    2: 歧伯曰：水穀皆入于口，其味有五，各注其海。津液各走其道，故三焦出氣，以溫肌肉，充皮膚，為其津，其流而不行者為液。 Obo said: All grains depend on the mouth for water, and their flavors have five varieties, with each registering its sea. The body fluids each follow their own path, so the three Yang Meridians expel Qi, warming the muscles and tendons, nourishing the skin, serving as their fluids, while those that flow but do not move are classified as fluids

    3: 天暑衣厚則腠理開，故汗出，寒留於分肉之間，聚沫則為痛。 If the clothing for summer is too thick, the pores will open, leading to sweat, and cold will accumulate between the skin layers, forming clumps that cause pain.

    4: 天寒則腠理閉，氣濕不行，水下留於膀胱，則為溺與氣。 If it is cold in heaven, the marrow will close up; if the qi is damp and cannot move, water will remain in the bladder, resulting in drowning and Qi.

    5: 五藏六府，心為之主，耳為之聽，目為之候，肺為之相，肝為之將，脾為之衛，腎為之主外。故五藏六府之津液，盡上滲於目，心悲氣並，則心系急。心系急則肺舉，肺舉則液上溢。夫心系與肺，不能常舉，乍上乍下，故欬而泣出矣。 Five Organs and Six Palaces, the heart governs it, the ears listen to it, the eyes are its attendants, the lungs form it, the liver commands it, the spleen guards it, and the kidneys govern it externally. Therefore, the fluid of the Five Organs and Six Palaces is all led up to the eyes, when the heart-soul qi is stirred and distressed, then the heart becomes troubled. When the heart becomes troubled, the lungs will rise, when the lungs rise, the fluid will overflow. Therefore, when the heart and lungs cannot be constantly raised and are alternately rising and falling, one sighs and weeps.

    6: 中熱則胃中消穀，消穀則蟲上下作。腸胃充郭，故胃緩，胃緩則氣逆，故唾出。 If one is too hot, it will burn the grains in the stomach, and if the grains are burnt, worms will invade. When the intestines and stomach are full, therefore the stomach becomes sluggish, and when the stomach is sluggish, the Qi rebels, thus causing vomiting.

    7: 五穀之津液，和合而為膏者，內滲入于骨空，補益腦髓，而下流於陰股。 The five grains' essence, mixed and combined to form a salve, can be injected internally into the cavities of the bones. It supplements and nourishes the brain and marrow, and flows downward to the yin passage (genital region).

    8: 陰陽不和，則使液溢而下流於陰，髓液皆減而下，下過度則虛，虛，故腰背痛而脛痠。 If yin and yang are not in harmony, qi will overflow and flow downward into the yin, leading to a deficiency of essence and causing excessive downward flow. This can lead to emptiness, which results in pain in the lower back and knees.

    9: 陰陽氣道不通，四海閉塞，三焦不寫，津液不化，水穀並行腸胃之中，別於回腸，留於下焦，不得滲膀胱，則下焦脹，水溢則為水脹，此津液五別之逆順也。 When the yin-yang qi channels are not open, the four seas are closed off, the three faucets do not write, and the fluids do not transform. The water grains are aligned in the middle of the intestines and colon, separate from the returning colon, and remain in the lower faucet, unable to enter the bladder. This causes the lower faucet to bloat, and if there is an overflow, it becomes a water bloating. This is the reversal and disorder of the five types of fluids
