    經脈別論

    1: 黃帝問曰：人之居處動靜勇怯，脈亦為之變乎。歧伯對曰：凡人之驚恐恚勞動靜，皆為變也。是以夜行則喘出於腎，淫氣病肺。有所墮恐，喘出於肝，淫氣害脾。有所驚恐，喘出於肺，淫氣傷心。度水跌仆，喘出於腎與骨，當是之時，勇者氣行則已，怯者則著而為病也。故曰：診病之道，觀人勇怯骨肉皮膚，能知其情，以為診法也。故飲食飽甚，汗出於胃。驚而奪精，汗出於心。持重遠行，汗出於腎。疾走恐懼，汗出於肝。搖體勞苦，汗出於脾。故春秋冬夏，四時陰陽，生病起於過用，此為常也。 Yellow Emperor asked, People's dwelling places and activities are both dynamic and still, courageous and timid; does the pulse also change accordingly? The Grand Perfecter replied, In general, people's shock, fear, and exhaustion in response to their activities all constitute changes. Thus, when practicing martial arts at night, one may gasp from the kidneys, causing kidney energy to damage the lungs. If one experiences a fall or feels afraid, one may gasp from the liver, causing liver energy to harm the spleen. When experiencing shock or fear, one may gasp from the lungs, causing liver energy to injure the heart. Falling into water or tripping may cause gasping from both the kidneys and bones, at which point, if one is courageous, their vital energy will circulate properly, but if one is timid, they will become ill. Therefore, it is said that diagnosing illness involves observing a person's courage, timidity, flesh, skin, and being able to discern their condition to prescribe treatment. Thus, when one overeats or drinks, sweat may emerge from the stomach. When shocked, one loses vital energy, causing sweat to emerge from the heart. When carrying heavy loads and walking far, sweat may emerge from the kidneys. When rushing in fear or panic, sweat may emerge from the liver. When exhausted from physical exertion, sweat may emerge from the spleen. In spring, summer, autumn, and winter, the changing yin and yang of the four seasons give rise to illness, which is a constant occurrence.

    2: 食氣入胃，散精於肝，淫氣於筋。淫氣入胃，濁氣歸心，淫精於脈。脈氣流經，經氣歸於肺，肺朝百脈，輸精於皮毛。毛脈合精，行氣於腑。腑精神明，留於四臟，氣歸於椎衡。權衡以平，氣口成寸，以決死生。飲入於胃，遊溢精氣，上輸於脾。脾氣散精，上歸於肺，通調水道，下輸膀胱。水精四布，五經並行，合於四時五臟陰陽，揆度以為常也。 Food Qi enters the stomach, disperses essence in the liver, and dissipates lewd Qi in the muscles. Lewd Qi enters the stomach, turbid Qi returns to the heart, and lewd essence in the vessels. Vessel Qi circulates throughout the body, and lung Qi returns to the lungs, connecting with the hundred vessels and nourishing the skin and hair. Hair vessels join essence, regulating Qi in the liver. The liver spirit is enlightened, residing in the four organs, and Qi returns to the vertebral plexus. The vertebral plexus balances and establishes the meridians, leading to the formation of an inch of Qi, and deciding life and death. Drinking enters the stomach, circulating essence and Qi, ascending and nourishing the spleen. Spleen Qi disperses essence, rising to the lungs, regulating the waterways, and descending to nourish the bladder. Essence spreads in the five pathways, with the five organs aligning in pairs according to yin and yang, regulating with the seasons, establishing constancy.

    3: 太陽藏獨至，厥喘虛氣逆，是陰不足陽有餘也，表裏當俱寫，取之下俞。陽明藏獨至，是陽氣重并也，當寫陽補陰，取之下俞。少陽藏獨至，是厥氣也，蹻前卒大，取之下俞，少陽獨至者，一陽之過也。太陰藏搏者，用心省真，五脈氣少，胃氣不平，三陰也，宜治其下俞，補陽寫陰。一陽獨嘯，少陽厥也，陽并於上，四脈爭張，氣歸於腎，宜治其經絡，寫陽補陰。一陰至，厥陰之治也，真虛㾓心，厥氣留薄，發為白汗，調食和藥，治在下俞。 When the sun is hidden and alone, when the breath is weak and the energy is reversed, it is because yin is insufficient and yang has excess. Both inside and outside should be written, and the treatment is to supplement yin with moxa below. When the light of yang is hidden and alone, it is because yang energy is strong and dense. It should be written to supplement yin and treat with moxa below. When the lesser yang is hidden and alone, it is the energy of the kidneys, crippled in front, large treatment with moxa below. When the lesser yang is alone, it is the deficiency of one yang. The great yin is difficult to grasp, five vessels are weak, the stomach qi is not stable, and it is the three yins. It should be treated at the lower jiao, supplementing yang with moxa. One yang alone, lesser yang, yang is concentrated above, the four vessels struggle to stretch, the qi returns to the kidneys, and it should be treated by regulating the channels and meridians, supplementing yin. When one yin is present, it is a treatment for the kidneys. The heart is true and false, pondering, the kidney energy is thin, sweating white sweat, regulate the diet and take medicine, treating at the lower jiao.

    4: 帝曰：太陽藏何象。歧伯曰：象三陽而浮也。帝曰：少陽藏何象。歧伯曰：象一陽也，一陽藏者，滑而不實也。帝曰：陽明藏何象。歧伯曰：象大浮也，太陰藏搏，言伏鼓也。二陰搏至，腎沈不浮也。 The Emperor asked, What does the sun hide? Duke Huan replied, The sun hides the image of three yangs and one floating yang. The hidden yang is a small, insubstantial yang. The Emperor asked, What does Yang Ming hide? Duke Huan replied, It hides the image of a great floating yang. The Yin hides struggle, indicating the hiding of the drum. When the two yins reach their limit, the kidneys sink and do not float.
