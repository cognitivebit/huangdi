    移精變氣論

    1: 黃帝問曰：余聞古之治病，惟其移精變氣，可祝由而已。今世治病，毒藥治其內，鍼石治其外，或愈或不愈，何也。歧伯對曰：往古人居禽獸之閒，動作以避寒，陰居以避暑，內無眷慕之累，外無伸官之形，此恬憺之世，邪不能深入也。故毒藥不能治其內，鍼石不能治其外，故可移精祝由而已。當今之世不然，憂患緣其內，苦形傷其外，又失四時之從，逆寒暑之宜，賊風數至，虛邪朝夕，內至五藏骨髓，外傷空竅肌膚，所以小病必甚，大病必死，故祝由不能已也。 Yellow Emperor asked: I have heard that in ancient times, treating diseases involved regulating the flow of vital energy, and this was accomplished through the Five Sacred Rituals. Nowadays, we treat diseases by administering toxic medicines internally and using needles and stones externally, with some patients recovering while others do not, why is that? The Duke of Zhou replied: In ancient times, people lived like animals, moving to avoid cold and staying indoors to avoid heat. They had no accumulation of desires within them and no external pressure from officials, which was a time of humility and simplicity. Therefore, diseases could not penetrate deeply, so toxic medicines could not treat internally, and needles and stones could not treat externally. Nowadays, however, illnesses are caused by internal factors and damage the external body, as well as disregarding the proper seasonal treatments of cold and heat. Diseases from malevolent pathogens attack constantly, reaching deep into the five vital organs and bone marrow, as well as damaging the pores, muscles, and skin. This leads to minor illnesses becoming severe, and major illnesses leading to death. Thus, the Five Sacred Rituals cannot be effective anymore

    2: 帝曰：善。余欲臨病人，觀死生，決嫌疑，欲知其要，如日月光，可得聞乎。歧伯曰：色脈者，上帝之所貴也，先師之所傳也。上古使僦貸季，理色脈而通神明，合之金木水火土四時八風六合，不離其常，變化相移，以觀其妙，以知其要，欲知其要，則色脈是矣。色以應日，脈以應月，常求其要，則其要也。夫色之變化，以應四時之脈，此上帝之所貴，以合於神明也，所以遠死而近生。生道以長，命曰聖王。中古之治病，至而治之，湯液十日，以去八風五痺之病，十日不已，治以草蘇草荄之枝，本末為助，標本已得，邪氣乃服。暮世之治病也則不然，治不本四時，不知日月，不審逆從，病形已成，乃欲微鍼治其外，湯液治其內，粗工兇兇，以為可攻，故病未已，新病復起。 Emperor: Good. I wish to observe a diseased person, watch life and death, judge suspicion, and want to know the essence, like the light of the sun and moon, can it be heard? Zhi Bó said: The color and pulse are what the ancient sages greatly valued, and they were transmitted down through generations. In ancient times, they understood the color and pulse, combined them with gold, wood, water, and fire of the four seasons and eight winds, not deviating from their nature, changing and moving with transformation, observing its subtleties to know its essence, wanting to know its essence, then the color and pulse are it. The color changes in response to the day, and the pulse changes in response to the moon, constantly seeking its essence, and thus its essence appears. The change of color is in response to the four seasons' pulse, which is what the ancients greatly valued and combined with the spiritual light. This is why they can avoid death and approach life. The way of treating diseases in the middle period was to treat them at their root, after ten days of Tang fluid to eliminate eight winds and five pains of diseases, not stopping for ten days, and then treating them with twigs of grass and weeds, root and branch as assistants, the basis has been established, and the pathogenic qi will be swallowed. In later times, the treatment of diseases is different, not treating at the root of the four seasons, not knowing the sun and moon, not discerning between following and leading, when the disease shape has already formed, they want to treat superficially with needles, and treat internally with Tang fluid, rough workmanship and crudeness, regarding it as possible attack, so the disease is not yet cured, new diseases arise.

    3: 帝曰：願聞要道。歧伯曰：治之要極，無失色脈，用之不惑，治之大則。逆從到行，標本不得，亡神失國。去故就新，乃得真人。帝曰：余聞其要於夫子矣，夫子言不離色脈，此余之所知也。歧伯曰：治之極於一。帝曰：何謂一。歧伯曰：一者，因得之。帝曰：奈何。歧伯曰：閉戶塞牖，繫之病者，數問其情，以從其意，得神者昌，失神者亡。帝曰：善。 The emperor said, I wish to hear about the Dao. Boyi replied, Governing it is supreme, without failure of the vital energy, its use does not lead astray, and in governing great matters, one must be reverent. Follow and apply it thoroughly, establish models that cannot be lost, or else the spirit will wander and the country will perish. By abandoning the old and adopting the new, one can attain true humanity. The emperor said, I have heard of its essence from Master Zhou, and Master Zhou does not depart from the vital energy; this is what I know. Boyi replied, Governing it supremely lies in one. The emperor asked, What do you mean by one? Boyi replied, One means obtaining it through cause. The emperor asked, How so? Boyi replied, Close the doors and seal the windows, bind the sick person, ask them repeatedly about their condition, follow their intentions, and those who have the spirit will flourish, while those who lose their spirit will perish. The emperor said, Good.
