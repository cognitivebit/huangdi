    天年

    1: 黃帝問于歧伯曰：願聞人之始生，何氣築為基，何立而為楯，何失而死，何得而生？歧伯曰：以母為基，以父為楯；失神者死，得神者生也。 Yellow Emperor asked Yi Bo: I wish to hear about the beginning of human life. What kind of energy laid the foundation? What established the framework? What causes death? What leads to rebirth? Yi Bo replied: The mother serves as the foundation and the father serves as the framework; losing one's spirit results in death, gaining one's spirit results in life.

    2: 黃帝曰：何者為神？歧伯曰：血氣已和，營衛已通，五藏已成，神氣舍心，魂魄畢具，乃成為人。 Yellow Emperor asked: What is the nature of the spirit? Huzi replied: When the blood and Qi are balanced, the Wei and Yang are connected, the five organs have formed, the spirit Qi has left the heart, and the souls and bodies have completed their forms, one will become a human.

    3: 黃帝曰：人之壽夭各不同，或夭壽，或卒死，或病久，願聞其道。歧伯曰：五藏堅固，血脈和調，肌肉解利，皮膚致密，營衛之行，不失其常，呼吸微徐，氣以度行，六府化穀，津液布揚，各如其常，故能長久。 Emperor Yellow said: The lifespan of people varies, and they may die prematurely, pass away, or suffer for a long time. I wish to hear about the way to prevent this. Master Huan said: When the five viscera are firmly established, blood vessels are harmonious and balanced, muscles are relaxed and tender, skin is tight and smooth, vital defense functions operate properly, breathing is gentle, qi adjusts accordingly, grains are transformed in the six organs, fluids are evenly distributed, all functions function as usual, thus one can live long.

    4: 黃帝曰：人之壽百歲而死，何以致之？歧伯曰：使道隧以長，基牆高以方，通調營衛，三部三里起，骨高肉滿，百歲乃得終。 Yellow Emperor said: People live a hundred years and die, what causes this? Duke of Zhou replied: By practicing the way, one can prolong life; by establishing high foundations and making adjustments, create a regulated environment with three departments within three miles. With strong bones and well-nourished muscles, reaching a hundred years will be possible.

    5: 黃帝曰：其氣之盛衰，以至其死，可得聞乎？歧伯曰：人生十歲，五藏始定，血氣已通，其氣在下，故好走；二十歲，血氣始盛肌肉方長，故好趨；三十歲，五藏大定，肌肉堅固，血脈盛滿，故好步；四十歲，五藏六府十二經脈，皆大盛以平定，腠理始疏，滎貨頹落，髮頗斑白，平盛不搖，故好坐；五十歲，肝氣始衰，肝葉始薄，膽汁始減，目始不明；六十歲，心氣始衰，若憂悲，血氣懈惰，故好臥；七十歲，脾氣虛，皮膚枯；八十歲，肺氣衰，魄離，故言善誤；九十歲，腎氣焦，四藏經脈空虛；百歲，五藏皆虛，神氣皆去，形骸獨居而終矣。 Yellow Emperor said: Can someone tell me about the ebb and flow of vital energy leading to one's death? Master Qiao replied: At age ten, the five organs start to form, blood and qi circulate freely, vital energy is concentrated in the lower body, making people fond of walking. At age twenty, blood and qi become abundant, muscles grow strong, so people love movement. At age thirty, the five organs are fully formed, muscles are solid, and blood vessels are full, making people enjoy walking. At age forty, the five organs, six viscera, twelve primary channels, and seventy-two thousand meridian vessels all reach their peak, regulating functions become clear, essential substances accumulate, hair turns white, the body is well-balanced but not shaky, so people like to sit. At age fifty, liver qi starts declining, liver tendons start to weaken, bile decreases, vision becomes unclear. At age sixty, heart qi starts declining, one may become sad or grieving, blood stagnates, making people fond of lying down. At age seventy, spleen qi is deficient, skin becomes dry. At age eighty, lung qi declines, soul departs, so speech becomes confused. At age ninety, kidney qi is burnt, the five organs and their channels are empty and void; at a hundred, all five organs are empty, vital energy is gone, and the body remains alone until death.

    6: 黃帝曰：其不能終壽而死者，何如？歧伯曰：其五藏皆不堅，使道不長，空外以張，喘息暴疾；又卑基牆薄，脈少血，其肉不石，數中風寒，血氣虛，脈不通，真邪相攻，亂而相引，故中壽而盡也。 Yellow Emperor asked, What about those who cannot reach the end of their life and die? Huang Di said: Their five organs are not solid, causing the Dao to be short-lived. The exterior is flabby, there is wheezing and rapid breathing; furthermore, the foundation of the kidneys is weak and shallow, with little blood in the pulse, their muscles do not firm up, they often contract illnesses of wind and cold, their vital energy is deficient, the pulse is not connected, true and false forces attack each other, causing confusion and mutual attraction. Therefore, they die in the middle of their life.
