    五藏生成

    1. 心之合脈也，其榮色也，其主腎也。肺之合皮也，其榮毛也，其主心也。肝之合筋也，其榮爪也，其主肺也。脾之合肉也，其榮脣也，其主肝也。腎之合骨也，其榮髮也，其主脾也。 The harmonization of the heart with the pulse and its luster; the heart governs the kidneys. The harmony of the lung with the skin and its luster; the lung governs the heart. The harmony of the liver with the tendons and its luster; the liver governs the lungs. The harmony of the spleen with the flesh and its luster; the spleen governs the liver. The kidney's harmonization with the bones and its luster; the kidney governs the spleen.

    2. 是故多食鹹則脈凝泣而變色；多食苦則皮槁而毛拔；多食辛則筋急而爪枯；多食酸則肉胝䐢而脣揭；多食甘則骨痛而髮落，此五味之所傷也。故心欲苦，肺欲辛，肝欲酸，脾欲甘，腎欲鹹，此五味之所合也。五藏之氣，故色見青如草茲者死，黃如枳實者死，黑如尚炱者死，赤如衃血者死，白如枯骨者死，此五色之見死也。青如翠羽者生，赤如雞冠者生，黃如蟹腹者生，白如豕膏者生，黑如烏羽者生，此五色之見生也。生於心，如以縞裹朱；生於肺，如以縞裹紅；生於肝，如以縞裹紺；生於脾，如以縞裹栝樓實，生於腎，如以縞裹紫，此五藏所生之外榮也。色味當五藏：白當肺，辛，赤當心，苦，青當肝，酸，黃當脾，甘，黑當腎，鹹，故白當皮，赤當脈，青當筋，黃當肉，黑當骨。 Therefore, an excess of salty leads to the solidification and weeping of the veins, changing color; an excess of bitter causes the skin to thicken and hair to fall out; an excess of pungent makes tendons urgent and nails dry; an excess of sour makes the flesh swell and jaws protrude, and an excess of sweet causes bones to hurt and hair to fall, which are the damages caused by the five tastes. Thus, if the heart desires bitter, the lungs desire pungent, the liver desires sour, the spleen desires sweet, and the kidneys desire salty, these are the damages caused by the five tastes. The vital energy of the five organs, therefore, the person who sees colors like green grass will die, yellow like chestnuts will die, black like charcoal will die, red like vermilion blood will die, and white like dry bones will die, these are the colors that see death. Green like jade feathers is alive, red like chicken combs is alive, yellow like crab bellies is alive, white like pig lard is alive, black like crow feathers is alive, these are the colors that see life. Born in the heart, it's like wrapping with vermilion; born in the lungs, it's like wrapping with red; born in the liver, it's like wrapping with dark blue; born in the spleen, it's like wrapping with the color of lotus seeds; born in the kidneys, it's like wrapping with purple, these are the external honors of the five organs. Colors and flavors correspond to the five organs: white corresponds to the lungs, pungent, red corresponds to the heart, bitter, green corresponds to the liver, sour, yellow corresponds to the spleen, sweet, black corresponds to the kidneys, salty, so white corresponds to skin, red corresponds to blood vessels, green corresponds to tendons, yellow corresponds to flesh, and black corresponds to bones.

    3. 諸脈者皆屬於目，諸髓者皆屬於腦，諸筋者皆屬於節，諸血者皆屬於心，諸氣者皆屬於肺，此四支八谿之朝夕也。故人臥，血歸於肝，肝受血而能視，足受血而能步，掌受血而能握，指受血而能攝。臥出而風吹之，血凝於膚者為痺，凝於脈者為泣，凝於足者為厥。此三者，血行而不得反其空，故為痺厥也。人有大谷十二分，小谿三百五十四名，少十二俞，此皆衛氣之所留止，邪氣之所客也，鍼石緣而去之。 All the vessels are under the jurisdiction of the eyes; all the marrows are under the jurisdiction of the brain; all the tendons are under the jurisdiction of the bones; all the blood is under the jurisdiction of the heart; all the qi is under the jurisdiction of the lungs. The four limbs and eight vessels change day and night. Therefore, when a person lies down, blood returns to the liver, the liver receives blood and can see, the feet receive blood and can walk, the palms receive blood and can grip, and the fingers receive blood and can grasp. Upon rising from lying down and being exposed to the wind, if the blood congeals at the skin it is a case of rigidity; if it congeals in the vessels it is a case of weeping; if it congeals at the feet it is a case of stroke. These three cases are when the blood fails to return and reverse its pathway, resulting in rigidity and stroke. A person has large intestines divided into twelve parts and small intestines with 354 sections, some of which are missing, all of these being the locations where defensive qi resides and evil qi lodges. Use acupuncture and moxibustion to drive them out.

    4. 診病之始五決為紀，欲知其始，先建其母，所謂五決者五脈也。是以頭痛巔疾，下虛上實過在足少陰，巨陽甚則入腎。徇蒙招尤目冥耳聾，下實上虛，過在足少陽，厥陰甚則入肝。腹滿䐜脹，支鬲胠脇，下厥上冒，過在足太陰，陽明。欬嗽上氣，厥在胸中，過在手陽明太陰。心煩頭痛病在鬲中，過在手巨陽，少陰。 The onset of disease is determined by five signs, and in order to know its origin, one must first investigate its mother. The term five signs refers to the five pulse positions. Therefore, headache with sudden onset, pain that is acute, is due to an excess condition in the foot Shaoyang. If there is a strong excess, it will enter the Spleen. Deafness, blindness, and tinnitus, with internal symptoms and external manifestations, are due to an excess condition in the foot Shao Yang, with a strong Bladder involved, potentially entering the Liver. Abdominal fullness, distension, and constipation, with internal stagnation and external manifestations, is due to a deficiency-excess condition in the foot Taiyin, involving the Yin and Yang of the Stomach. Dysphagia and difficulty speaking, with Qi rising upward, is due to a deficiency-excess condition in the foot Shaoyang, involving the Yin of the Lungs. Heart palpitations and headache, with disease located in the region of the Gallbladder, are due to an excess condition in the Gallbladder, involving the Shao Yang and the foot Shaoyang.

    5. 夫脈之小大滑濇浮沈，可以指別；五藏之象，可以類推；五藏相音，可以意識；五色微診，可以目察。能合脈色，可以萬全。赤脈之至也喘而堅，診曰有積氣在中，時害於食，名曰心痺，得之外疾，思慮而心虛，故邪從之。白脈之至也喘而浮，上虛下實。驚，有積氣在胸中，喘而虛名曰肺痺寒熱，得之醉而使內也。青脈之至也長而左右彈有積氣在心下支胠，名曰肝痺，得之寒濕，與疝同法，腰痛足清頭痛。黃脈之至也大而虛，有積氣在腹中，有厥氣名曰厥疝，女子同法，得之疾使四支汗出當風。黑脈之至也上堅而大有積氣在小腹與陰，名曰腎痺，得之沐浴清水而臥。 Regarding the pulse, its smallness, largeness, smoothness, and turbidity can be distinguished. The signs of the Five Organs can be inferred; the sounds of the Five Organs can be discerned; subtle variations in color can be observed. One who can harmonize the pulse, color, and complexion can diagnose thoroughly.

    The superiority of the red pulse is that it becomes hard upon panting; it is diagnosed as having accumulated Qi within, which may harm eating, and is called Heart stagnation. Obtained externally, thinking and worrying lead to Heart vacuity, causing pathogens to invade.

    The superiority of the white pulse is that it becomes float upon panting, with emptiness above and fullness below. Shock, having accumulated Qi in the chest, is diagnosed as Lung stagnation, hot or cold; obtained from drunkenness, it indicates internal injury.

    The superiority of the blue-green pulse is that it becomes long and fluctuates with the presence of accumulated Qi in the lower abdomen and intestines, diagnosed as Liver stagnation, similar to dampness Dysuria, causing back pain, clear headache, and dizziness.

    The superiority of the yellow pulse is that it becomes large and empty, with accumulated Qi in the abdomen and deficiency of vital Qi, diagnosed as Kidney stagnation; similar for women, obtained from a sudden illness causing sweating, fever, and wind-stroke symptoms.

    The superiority of the black pulse is that it becomes hard above and large with the presence of accumulated Qi in the lower abdomen and Yin, diagnosed as Bladder stagnation; obtained from bathing in clear water while lying down.
