    口問

    1: 黃帝閒居，辟左右而問於歧伯曰：余已聞九鍼之經，論陰陽逆順，六經已畢，願得口問。歧伯避席再拜曰：善乎哉問也，此先師之所口傳也。黃帝曰：願聞口傳。歧伯答曰：夫百病之始生也，皆生於風雨寒暑，陰陽喜怒，飲食居處，大驚卒恐。則血氣分離，陰陽破敗，經絡厥絕，脈道不通，陰陽相逆，衛氣稽留，經脈虛空，血氣不次，乃失其常。論不在經者，請道其方。 Emperor Yellow was leisurely at home, he separated to the left and right and asked Master Qi of Guo: I have already heard the Nine Needles Classic, discussing yin and yang, order and disorder, the six classics are complete, I wish to ask with my mouth. Master Qi avoided the seat and bowed again, saying: How wonderful is this question! This is what the master teaches in person. Emperor Yellow said: I want to hear the teaching from his mouth. Master Qi answered: The hundred diseases all originate from wind, rain, cold, and heat; yin and anger, drinking, eating, dwelling, great shock and fear. Thus blood and qi are separated, yin and yang are broken, channels and vessels are cut off, the flow of the meridians is blocked, yin and yang reverse each other, defensive qi stagnates, the channels and vessels are empty, blood and qi do not circulate, and thus they lose their normality. The discussion is not in the classic; please explain its method

    2: 黃帝曰：人之欠者，何氣使然？歧伯答曰：衛氣晝日行於陽，夜半則行於陰，陰者主夜，夜者臥；陽者主上，陰者主下；故陰氣積於下，陽氣未盡，陽引而上，陰引而下，陰陽相引，故數欠。陽氣盡，陰氣盛，則目瞑；陰氣盡而陽氣盛，則寤矣。寫足少陰，補足太陽。 The Yellow Emperor asked: What causes people to be indebted? Zhou Yan answered: The Qi of Wei circulates during the daytime in the Yang aspect and at midnight in the Yin aspect. Yin governs night and rest; Yang governs above, while Yin governs below. Therefore, Yin Qi accumulates below, and Yang Qi is not yet exhausted. Yang pulls upward, and Yin pulls downward; Yin and Yang attract each other, so one often owes money. When Yang Qi is complete, and Yin Qi is abundant, the eyes are obscured. When Yin Qi is complete and Yang Qi is abundant, one will awaken. Prescribe more Kidney Yin and supplement Foot Yang.

    3: 黃帝曰：人之噦者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：穀入于胃，胃氣上注于肺。今有故寒氣與新穀氣，俱還入于胃，新故相亂，真邪相攻，氣並相逆，復出於胃，故為噦。補手太陰，寫足少陰。 Yellow Emperor said: What causes the speech of people? The Duke of Shou replied: Grains enter the stomach, and stomach qi rises to the lungs. Now there is both old cold qi and new grain qi, both returning to the stomach, with old and new mixed up, true and false attacking each other, qi going opposite directions, causing it to come out of the stomach, thus leading to speech disorders. Supplement the hand with the Conception vessel, and write the Foot Yang Ming with the Lesser Yin.

    4: 黃帝曰：人之唏者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：此陰氣盛而陽氣虛，陰氣疾而陽氣徐，陰氣盛而陽氣絕，故為唏。補足太陽，寫足少陰。 Yellow Emperor said: What causes people to feel fatigued? Qi Bo replied: When the yin qi is abundant and the yang qi is deficient, when the yin qi is rushing and the yang qi is slow, and when the yin qi is abundant and the yang qi is absent, this leads to fatigue. To supplement the great yang, write the foot-yang.

    5: 黃帝曰：人之振寒者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：寒氣客於皮膚，陰氣盛，陽氣虛，故為振寒寒慄，補諸陽。 Yellow Emperor said: What causes people to shake with cold? Hua Tuo replied: The cold qi resides in the skin, yin qi is abundant, and yang qi is deficient, thus causing shaking from the cold and trembling. It should be supplemented with yang

    6: 黃帝曰：人之噫者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：寒氣客於胃，厥逆從下上散，復出於胃，故為噫。補足太陰陽明，一曰補眉本也。 Yellow Emperor asked, What kind of qi causes people to belch? Huang Di replied, Cold qi accumulates in the stomach, and the evil qi rises from below and disperses, then exits through the stomach, which is why it results in belching. This also demonstrates the clarity of yin and yang.

    7: 黃帝曰：人之嚏者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：陽氣和利，滿於心，出於鼻，故為嚏。補足太陽榮眉本，一曰眉上也。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the cause of a person's sneezing? The Duke of Shou replied: Yin energy harmonizes and nourishes, filling the heart, and emanates from the nose, which is why it is called sneezing. [Note from the supplementary commentary: The brightness of the sun comes from its eyebrows.]

    8: 黃帝曰：人之嚲者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：胃不實則諸脈虛；諸脈虛則筋脈懈惰；筋脈懈惰則行陰用力，氣不能復，故為嚲。因其所在，補分肉間。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the cause of people being thin? Huangdi replied: If the stomach is not full, the vessels will be empty; if the vessels are empty, the muscles and vessels will become sluggish; when the muscles and vessels become sluggish, the vital energy cannot be restored, leading to thinness. Therefore, strengthen the area where it is lacking.

    9: 黃帝曰：人之哀而泣涕出者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：心者，五藏六府之主也；目者，宗脈之所聚也，上液之道也；口鼻者，氣之門戶也。故悲哀愁憂則心動，心動則五藏六府皆搖，搖則宗脈感，宗脈感則液道開，液道開，故泣涕出焉。液者，所以灌精濡空竅者也，故上液之道開則泣，泣不止則液竭；液竭則精不灌，精不灌則目無所見矣，故命曰奪精。補天柱經俠頸。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the Qi that causes people to shed tears? Zhang Bao replied: The heart is the master of the five zang and six fu organs; the eyes are where the conception vessel, the pathway for fluid, gathers. The mouth and nose are the gates of Qi. Therefore, when one feels sorrow or sadness, the heart moves, causing all the zang and fu to shake. When they shake, the conception vessel is affected, opening up the fluid pathway. This leads to tears flowing out. Tears flow because the fluid serves to moisten the channels, so when the fluid pathway opens up, tears are shed; when they do not stop, the fluid is depleted. When the fluid is depleted, Qi cannot be nourished, and without Qi nourishment, one will see nothing with their eyes. Thus it is called losing one's essence. This is from the Lingshui Jing: Treatise on the Celestial Pillar.

    10: 黃帝曰：人之太息者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：憂思則心系急，心系急則氣道約，約則不利，故太息以伸出之，補手少陰心主，足少陽留之也。 Yellow Emperor said: Why would excessive breathing lead to the loss of Qi? The Duke of Shou replied: When one is anxious and thinking, the heart becomes tense, and when the heart is tense, the meridians become constricted. Constricted meridians are not beneficial, so excessive breathing extends them outward. By supplementing the Kidney's deficient Yin and nourishing the Heart, and tonifying the Yang in the limbs.

    11: 黃帝曰：人之涎下者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：飲食者，皆入于胃，胃中有熱則蟲動，蟲動則胃緩，胃緩則廉泉開，故涎下，補足少陰。 Yellow Emperor asked: What Qi causes the fluidity from below? The Duke of Zhou replied: Those who consume food and drink, all enter the stomach. If the stomach has heat, the worms move, the stomach becomes relaxed, the stomach relaxes and the spring opens, thus causing the fluidity from below, supplementing the kidney yin.

    12: 黃帝曰：人之耳中鳴者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：耳者，宗脈之所聚也，故胃中空則宗脈虛，虛則下溜，脈有所竭者，故耳鳴，補客主人，手大指爪甲上與肉交者也。 Yellow Emperor said: What causes the sound in a person's ear? Huang Di replied: The ear is where the primeval Qi gathers, so if the stomach is empty, the primeval Qi will be deficient, causing it to leak. When there is a lack of Qi, one might experience ringing in the ears. To treat this condition, apply pressure with the tip of the thumbnail and the base of the nail on the flesh where the skin meets, as if supplementing the chief organ.

    13: 黃帝曰：人之自齧舌者，何氣使然？此厥逆走上，脈氣輩至也。少陰氣至則齧舌，少陽氣至則齧頰，陽明氣至則齧唇矣。視主病者，則補之。 Emperor Yellow said: Why do people grind their tongues? This is due to the rebellious qi rising up, and vital energy reaching a critical point. When deficient yin energy reaches this point, it causes tongue grinding; when deficient yang energy reaches this point, it causes tongue grinding as well; and when yang brightness energy reaches this point, it causes lip grinding. In treating the patient's main complaint, one should supplement accordingly.

    14: 凡此十二邪者，皆奇邪之走空竅者也。故邪之所在，皆為不足。故上氣不足，腦為之不滿，耳為之苦鳴，頭為之苦傾，目為之眩。中氣不足，溲便為之變，腸為之苦鳴。下氣不足，則乃為痿厥心悗。補足外踝下留之。 Regarding these twelve evils, all are of the strange and unusual type who walk through empty passages. Therefore, evil lies in what is insufficient. Therefore, with Qi insufficiency, the brain is not filled, causing ear pain and ringing, headache, and dizziness of the eyes. With middle-Qi insufficiency, the defecation changes, causing abdominal pain and rumbling. With lower-Qi insufficiency, it leads to flaccid paralysis and weak pulse. Supplementing the sole of the foot can help.

    15: 黃帝曰：治之奈何？歧伯曰：腎主為欠，取足少陰；肺主為噦，取手太陰、足少陰；唏者，陰與陽絕，故補足太陽，寫足少陰；振寒者，補諸陽；噫者，補足太陰陽明；嚏者，補足太陽眉本；嚲，因其所在，補分肉間；泣出補天柱經俠頸，俠頸者，頭中分也；太息，補手少陰、心主、足少陽，留之；涎下補足少陰；耳鳴補客主人，手大指爪甲上與肉交者；自嚙舌，視主病者，則補之。目眩頭傾，補足外踝下留之；痿厥心悗，刺足大趾間上二寸，留之，一曰足外踝下留之。 Emperor Yellow asked: What method should be used to treat this? Master Qi Bo replied: The kidney is responsible for deficiency, so take the foot Shaoyang; the lung is rebelling, take the hand Taiyang and foot Shaoyang; the one who stagnates, the yin and yang are blocked, so supplement the foot Taiyang, and write the foot Shaoyang; those who disperse cold, supplement the Zhong Yang; those who cough, supplement the foot Taiyang from the Mingmen; those who hawk, supplement the foot Taiji Yangming; those with a hoarse voice, supplement the foot Taiji; those who have difficulty breathing, supplement the hand Shaoyang and heart governor, leave it in; those who drool, supplement the foot Shaoyin; those with tinnitus, supplement the client master; those who self-eat saliva, treat the chief disease; dizziness and headache, supplement the foot Kunlun; paralysis of the extremities, supplement the foot outside the ankle and leave it in; weak pulse and shortness of breath, puncture the foot Jing well at the top of the big toe for two inches and leave it in, one says to supplement the foot outside the ankle and leave it in.
