    動輸

    1: 黃帝曰：經脈十二，而手太陰、足少陰、陽明，獨動不休，何也？歧伯曰：是明胃脈也。胃為五藏六府之海，其清氣上注于肺，肺氣從太陰而行之，其行也，以息往來，故人一呼，脈再動，一吸脈亦再動，呼吸不已，故動而不止。 Yellow Emperor said: There are twelve meridians, and the hands' ShaoYang, feet' Shaoyang, and Yang Ming move ceaselessly. What is the reason for this? Qi Bo replied: This is the manifestation of the Conception Vessel. The Conception Vessel is the sea of the Five Zang and Six Fu organs, its clear qi ascends to the lungs, lung qi flows through the ShaoYang, and this flowing corresponds with breathing. Thus, when a person inhales or exhales, the pulse moves again; when they inhale deeply, the pulse moves again, and their breathing is not constant, so it ceaselessly moves.

    2: 黃帝曰：氣之過於寸口也，上十焉息，下八焉伏，何道從還？不知其極。 Yellow Emperor said: The Qi is too excessive through the orifice above, and it rises ten steps to exhale, and eight steps to subdue below; how can one return the path? Not knowing its extreme.

    3: 歧伯曰：氣之離藏也，卒然如弓弩之發，如水之下岸，上於魚以及衰，其餘氣衰散以逆上，故其行微。 Obo said: The vital energy is like a bow and arrow released suddenly or water flowing down to catch fish and wither, the remaining vital energy wanes and disperses in reverse, so its movement is subtle.

    4: 黃帝曰：足之陽明，何因而動？歧伯曰：胃氣上注于肺，其悍氣上沖頭者，循咽，上走空竅，循眼系，入絡腦，出顑，下客主人，循牙車，合陽明，并下人迎，此胃氣別走於陽明者也。故陰陽上下，其動也若一。故陽病而陽脈小者，為逆；陰病而陰脈大者，為逆。故陰陽俱靜俱動，若引繩相傾者病。 Yellow Emperor asked: What causes the Yang Ming to move? Minister Qi replied: The Stomach Qi ascends to the Lung, and its bitter Qi flows upwards to the head, passing through the throat, it rises to the skylight, passes through the eye orbits, enters the brain, exits at the fontanelle, descends to the Grandmaster, passes through the teeth canal, merges with Yang Ming, and goes down together with the person. This is how Stomach Qi moves independently of Yang Ming. Thus, Yin and Yang's up-down movement is harmonious. Therefore, when there are Yang symptoms and the Yang channel is small, it is called counterflow. When there are Yin symptoms and the Yin channel is enlarged, it is also called counterflow. If both Yin and Yang are motionless or moving, like a swaying balance, it indicates illness.

    5: 黃帝曰：足少陰何因而動？歧伯曰：沖脈者，十二經之海也，與少陰之大絡，起於腎下，出於氣街，循陰股內廉，邪入膕中，循脛骨內廉，并少陰之經，下入內踝之後。入足下，其別者，邪入踝，出屬附上，入大指之間，注諸絡，以溫足脛，此脈之常動者也。 Yellow Emperor asked: Why does the kidney channel move? Huan Bo replied: The kidney channel, also known as the Bao Mai, originates from the lower part of the kidneys, passes through the Qi Street, follows the inner groove of the yin channel, enters the huiyin (perineum), and then merges with the conception vessel. Upon entering the sole of the foot, its function is to regulate the qi of the foot and ankle by dispersing pathogens along the tendons and meridians. This movement is a common phenomenon in the kidney channel

    6: 黃帝曰：營衛之行也，上下相貫，如環之無端，今有其卒然遇邪風，及逢大寒，手足懈惰，其脈陰陽之道，相輸之會，行相失也，氣何由還？歧伯曰：夫四末陰陽之會者，此氣之尤絡也；四街者，氣之徑路也。故絡絕則徑通，四末解則氣從合，相輸如環。黃帝曰：善。此所謂如環無端，莫知其紀，終而復始，此之謂也。 Yellow Emperor said: The practice of military medicine is like a circular orbit, pervading up and down without interruption. However, today there has been an unexpected encounter with evil winds and encounters great cold, and the hands and feet become sluggish. The flow of the yin and yang in the pulse has become confused, and the Qi cannot return to its course. Master Gao said: The convergence of the four extremities of yin and yang is the main pathway for Qi. The four channels are the paths of Qi. Therefore, if there is blockage, the paths will be open; if the four extremities are resolved, then the Qi will flow in harmony and be like a circular orbit. Yellow Emperor said: Good. This so-called circular orbit without end, with no record of beginning or end, refers to this.
