    本神

    1. 黃帝問于歧伯曰：凡刺之法，先必本于神。血、脈、營、氣、精神，此五藏之所藏也。至其淫泆離藏則精失、魂魄飛揚、志意恍亂、智慮去身者，何因而然乎？天之罪與？人之過乎？何謂德、氣、生、精、神、魂、魄、心、意、志、思、智、慮？請問其故。 The Yellow Emperor asked Yu Fang: In the practice of acupuncture, it must originate from the spirit. Blood, channels, Qi, Shen, and consciousness are what these five viscera hold. When they reach a state of depletion, the essence is lost, the soul flies upwards, intentions become confused, wisdom departs from the body, what causes this to happen? Is it a fault of Heaven or the fault of humans? What do we mean by virtue, Qi, life, essence, spirit, soul, substance, heart, intention, will, thought, wisdom, and understanding? Please explain its origin.

    2. 歧伯答曰：天之在我者德也，地之在我者氣也。德流氣薄而生者也。故生之來謂之精；兩精相搏謂之神；隨神往來者謂之魂；並精而出入者謂之魄；所以任物者謂之心；心有所憶謂之意；意之所存謂之志；因志而存變謂之思；因思而遠慕謂之慮；因慮而處物謂之智。 Duke Huan replied: The virtue of Heaven is within me, and the energy of Earth is also within me. When virtue flows with this energy, it generates what is subtle; when these two struggle, it becomes spirit. Following the spirit coming and going is called soul; pervading soul and body is called essence; all encompassing the essence entering and exiting is called spirit; because it directs things, it is called heart; having memories in the heart is called mind; where the mind dwells is called willpower; transforming with willpower is called thought; pursuing through thought is called discernment; through discernment interacting with things is called wisdom.

    3. 故智者之養生也，必順四時而適寒暑，和喜怒而安居處，節陰陽而調剛柔。如是，則僻邪不至，長生久視。 As a result, nurturing life is the way of the wise, who must adapt to the four seasons and be suitable for both heat and cold. They harmonize their pleasure and anger and live in peace, balance yin and yang, and regulate hardness and softness. Thus, abnormalities will not occur, and longevity and clear vision will be achieved.

    4. 是故怵惕思慮者則傷神，神傷則恐懼流淫而不止。因悲哀動中者，竭絕而失生。喜樂者，神憚散而不藏。愁憂者，氣閉塞而不行。盛怒者，迷惑而不治。恐懼者，神蕩憚而不收。 Therefore, those who ponder and think deeply will hurt their spirit; a hurt spirit will cause fear and confusion without end. Those who are moved by pity will exhaust themselves and die. Those who rejoice will cause their spirit to scatter and not hide it. Those who are anxious will close their energy and not let it flow. Those who are furious will be bewildered and not cure it. Those who are afraid will cause their spirit to wander and not gather it in.

    5. 心，怵惕思慮則傷神，神傷則恐懼自失。破䐃脫肉，毛悴色夭死于冬。 The heart, being perplexed and anxious, injures the spirit. When the spirit is injured, one fears losing oneself. The skin peels off and the hair turns white, dying in winter.

    6. 脾愁憂而不解則傷意，意傷則悗亂，四肢不舉，毛悴色夭死於春。 If the pectoral organ (heart) is worried and not understood, it will injure one's emotions. Emotions become confused and muddled, the limbs do not lift, and hair turns pale and dies in spring

    7. 肝悲哀動中則傷魂，魂傷則狂忘不精，不精則不正，當人陰縮而攣筋，兩脅骨不舉，毛悴色夭死於秋。 The liver mourning and sorrowful, injures the soul; the soul being injured leads to madness and forgetfulness, lack of clarity leads to impropriety, causing a person's vital yin to shrink while their muscles twitch, not lifting the two terrifying bones, hairs turning grey and dying in autumn.

    8. 肺喜樂無極則傷魄，魄傷則狂，狂者意不存人，皮革焦，毛悴色夭死於夏。 The lungs love pleasure without limits will harm the soul, and a damaged soul leads to madness. A person in madness loses their mind, their skin turns yellow and brittle, their hair turns grey and they die in summer

    9. 腎盛怒而不止則傷志，志傷則喜忘其前言，腰脊不可以俛仰屈伸，毛悴色夭死於季夏。 If the liver is filled with rage and cannot be stopped, it will harm the heart; when the heart is harmed, one forgets the previous words, cannot bend or stretch the backbone, becomes pale and wilted, and dies in the season of spring and autumn.

    10. 恐懼而不解則傷精，精傷則骨痠痿厥，精時自下。是故五藏主藏精者也，不可傷，傷則失守而陰虛；陰虛則無氣，無氣則死矣。 Fear and confusion can injure the vital essence. When the vital essence is injured, it leads to weakness of the bones and paralysis, and the vital essence will naturally descend when its time comes. Therefore, the five organs each govern the vital essence, which must not be injured, for if it is injured, the defense mechanism weakens, resulting in yin deficiency; with yin deficiency, there is no qi, and one dies.

    11. 是故用鍼者，察觀病人之態，以知精、神、魂、魄之存亡，得失之意，五者以傷，鍼不可以治之也。 Therefore, the skilled acupuncturist observes the condition of the patient to discern whether the mind, consciousness, soul, and spirit are present or absent, and whether there is a gain or loss. In such cases, acupuncture cannot be used to treat the injury.

    12. 肝藏血，血舍魂，肝氣虛則恐，實則怒。 The liver stores blood, blood sacrifices the soul, if the liver's qi is deficient there will be fear, and if it is abundant there will be anger.

    13. 脾藏營，營舍意，脾氣虛則四肢不用，五藏不安，實則腹脹經溲不利。 The spleen is housed in the spleen pavilion, and the spleen pavilion refers to the energy of the spleen. If the energy of the spleen is deficient, the limbs will not function properly, the five organs will not be secure, and in reality, the abdomen will be bloated and the bowels will move poorly.

    14. 心藏脈，脈舍神，心氣虛則悲，實則笑不休。 The heart houses the pulse, and the pulse gives rise to the spirit. If the heart's energy is insufficient, it will bring sadness; if it is abundant, one cannot stop laughing.

    15. 肺藏氣，氣舍魄，肺氣虛，則鼻塞不利少氣，實則喘喝胸盈仰息。 The lungs store the Qi, the Qi nourishes the soul, when the lung Qi is deficient, one will have a blocked nose and be disadvantaged in breathing, if it is real then there will be panting, wheezing, a full chest, and difficulty breathing.

    16. 腎藏精，精舍志，腎氣虛則厥，實則脹。五藏不安。必審五藏之病形，以知其氣之虛實，謹而調之也。 The essence of the kidney, essence treasuring aspiration, kidney qi being deficient results in lethargy, kidney qi being abundant results in distention. The five organs are not at ease. One must carefully examine the signs of the five organs to know the state of their qi, and proceed with caution in treating them
