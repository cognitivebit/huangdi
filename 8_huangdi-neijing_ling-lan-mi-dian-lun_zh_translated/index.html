    靈蘭秘典論

    1: 黃帝問曰：願聞十二藏之相使，貴賤何如。歧伯對曰：悉乎哉問也，請遂言之。心者，君主之官也，神明出焉。肺者，相傅之官，治節出焉。肝者，將軍之官，謀慮出焉。膽者，中正之官，決斷出焉。膻中者，臣使之官，喜樂出焉。脾胃者，倉廩之官，五味出焉。大腸者，傳道之官，變化出焉。小腸者，受盛之官，化物出焉。腎者，作強之官，伎巧出焉。三焦者，決瀆之官，水道出焉。膀胱者，州都之官，津液藏焉，氣化則能出矣。 Yellow Emperor asked: I would like to hear about the functions of the twelve organs, high and low. The Grand Preceptor replied: Indeed, this is a comprehensive inquiry, please allow me to explain. The heart is the ruler's office, from which spirit arises. The lung is the minister's office, responsible for regulating the pulse. The liver is the general's office, where strategy and planning emerge. The spleen is the central authority's office, making decisions. The urinary bladder is the official in charge of pleasure and enjoyment, from which laughter arises. The pancreas is the office responsible for managing grains and five flavors. The large intestine is the office that transmits the teachings, from which transformation emerges. The small intestine is the office that receives offerings, from which the transformation of things arises. The kidney is the office of strength-making, where agility and skill arise. The three burners are the offices of discernment and differentiation, from which the waterway arises. The gallbladder is the prefectural office, where fluids and essences are stored, and when they are transformed, they can be emitted.

    2: 凡此十二官者，不得相失也。故主明則下安，以此養生則壽，歿世不殆，以為天下則大昌。主不明則十二官危，使道閉塞而不通，形乃大傷，以此養生則殃，以為天下者，其宗大危，戒之戒之。 Of these twelve offices, none shall be lost. Therefore, when the ruler is enlightened, he will govern well and nurture life, which leads to long life, and the world will not perish; this being so, great prosperity will ensue. If the ruler is not enlightened, the twelve offices are in danger, and the Way will close and become blocked, causing great harm to life, and if this happens, the foundation of the world is in grave danger. Be warned! Be warned!

    3: 至道在微，變化無窮，孰知其原；窘乎哉，消者瞿瞿，孰知其要；閔閔之當，孰者為良。恍惚之數，生於毫氂，毫氂之數，起於度量，千之萬之，可以益大，推之大之，其形乃制。黃帝曰：善哉，余聞精光之道，大聖之業，而宣明大道，非齋戒擇吉日，不敢受也。黃帝乃擇吉日良兆，而藏靈蘭之室，以傳保焉。 Where the way is subtle, changes are infinite, who knows its origin? It is difficult indeed. Those who vanish do so quietly, who knows their purpose? In the midst of ambiguity, who is to be trusted? The many uncertainties arising from the tip of a needle, originate from measurement, ten thousandths of a degree can be further increased, and if pushed further, its form will be shaped. The Yellow Emperor said: Excellent! I have heard of the profound way and great works of the sages, but they do not declare the way, nor do they dare to prescribe auspicious days. The Yellow Emperor chose an auspicious day and hid the Spirit Room of the Jade Maiden in order to preserve it.
