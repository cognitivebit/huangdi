    五邪

    1. 邪在肺，則病皮膚痛，寒熱，上氣喘，汗出，欬動肩背。取之膺中外喻，背三節五藏之傍，以手疾按之，快然，乃刺之。取之缺盆中以越之。 If the evil is in the lung, there will be pain of the skin and flesh, fever and chills, shortness of breath, sweating, and difficulty in swallowing and shoulder and back pain. To remove it, locate it at the armpit outside the three junctions and five viscera, press firmly with your hand quickly, and then pierce it. To take it out, locate it at the lower abdomen and draw it out.

    2. 邪在肝，則兩脅中痛，寒中，惡血在內，行善掣節，時腳腫。取之行間，以引脅下，補三里以溫胃中，取血脈以散惡血；取耳間青脈，以去其掣。 If evil is in the liver, there will be pain in both flanks and chills. Cold exists inside, bad blood is present. Act virtuously and restrain yourself. At a proper time, the feet will swell. Take something from your pocket to pull down the pain, supplement with three li of warmth to disperse cold and evil in the middle energizer, take the green-colored blood vessel at the earlobe to remove the obstruction.

    3. 邪在脾胃，則病肌肉痛，陽氣有餘，陰氣不足，則熱中善飢；陽氣不足，陰氣有餘，則寒中腸鳴、腹痛；陰陽俱有餘，若俱不足，則有寒有熱，皆調于三里。 Evil lies in the spleen and stomach, causing muscle pain and stiffness. If yang qi is abundant but yin qi is deficient, one will have a preference for heat and be prone to hunger. If yang qi is deficient and yin qi is abundant, one will suffer from cold with abdominal rumbling and abdominal pain. If both yin and yang are abundant or deficient, one will experience both cold and heat symptoms, which can be treated by adjusting the balance within the three li (channels).

    4. 邪在腎，則病骨痛，陰痺。陰痺者，按之而不得，腹脹，腰痛，大便難，肩背頸項痛，時眩。取之湧泉、崑崙。視有血者，盡取之。 Evil resides in the kidneys, resulting in pain in the bones. This refers to yin deficiency. Symptoms include: when pressed, there is no relief; abdominal distention and lower back pain; difficulty with bowel movements; shoulder, back, neck, and jaw pain; and dizziness at times. Prescription: take from Qiong Mountain springs (medicine from Yunnan, China). If you see blood, take it all.

    5. 邪在心，則病心痛，喜悲時眩仆；視有餘不足而調之其輸也。 Evil in the heart causes pain, and the heart is weakened during times of joy or grief; if there is still some vision left, one should adjust it accordingly.
