    官鍼

    1: 凡刺之要，官鍼最妙。九鍼之宜，各有所為，長、短、大、小，各有所施也。不得其用，病弗能移。疾淺鍼深，內傷良肉，皮膚為癰；病深鍼淺，病氣不寫，支為大膿。病小鍼大，氣寫太甚，疾必為害；病大鍼小，氣不泄寫，亦復為敗。失鍼之宜。大者寫，小者不移。已言其過，請言其所施。 The art of acupuncture is most skillful with official implements. The suitable use of the nine needles varies, with different lengths and sizes for different applications. If you do not know how to apply them, illness cannot be alleviated. Shallow and rapid needling causes internal injury, leaving a wheal; deep and shallow needling leaves the qi unregulated, leading to a large wheal. Needling small areas with deep insertions causes harm; needling large areas with shallow insertions does not release the qi either, resulting in failure. In terms of the application of acupuncture, larger needles are inserted deeply and smaller ones are not moved. If this is wrong, please explain how it is applied incorrectly.

    2: 病在皮膚無常處者，取以鑱鍼於病所，膚白勿取。病在分肉間，取以圓鍼於病所。病在經絡痼痺者，取以鋒鍼。病在脈，氣少，當補之者，取以鍉鍼於井滎分俞。病為大膿者，取以鈹鍼。病痺氣暴發者，取以圓利鍼。病痺氣痛而不去者，取以毫鍼。病在中者，取以長鍼。病水腫不能通關節者，取以大鍼。病在五藏固居者，取以鋒鍼，寫於井滎分俞，取以四時。 For a skin illness in an unstable area, take a lancet at the site of the disease, do not take white skin. For a disease between layers of flesh, take a round lancet at the site of the disease. For a disease in tendons and ligaments, take a pointed lancet. For a disease in the meridians, with qi deficiency needing supplementation, take a flat-tipped lancet at the well point of the confluence of the channels. For a boil disease, take a corkscrew lancet. For a disease that causes acute pain due to qi outburst, take a round-pointed lancet. For a disease that causes acute pain and does not go away, take a needle lancet. For a disease in the center, take a long lancet. For water accumulation, causing swelling and unable to open the joints, take a large lancet. For a disease firmly rooted in the five organs, take a pointed lancet, write on the well point of the confluence of the channels, and take at the four times.

    3: 凡刺有九，以應九變。一曰俞刺，俞刺者，刺諸經滎俞藏俞也；二曰遠道刺，遠道刺者，病在上，取之下，刺府俞也；三曰經刺，經刺者，刺大經之結絡經分也；四曰絡刺，絡刺者，刺小絡之血脈也；五曰分刺，分刺者，刺分肉之間也；六曰大寫刺，大寫刺者，刺大膿以鈹鍼也；七曰毛刺，毛刺者，刺浮痺皮膚也；八曰巨刺，巨刺者，左取右，右取左；九曰焠刺，焠刺者，刺燔鍼則取痺也。 The Nine Needles: 1. Mu needle, used for treating Mu areas; 2. Yu needle, for diseases above the waist, insert below; 3. Jing needle, for treating major channels and points; 4. Luo needle, for minor vessels or points; 5. Fen needle, for treating between muscles; 6. Da zhe needle, for treating large lumps with a curved needle; 7. Mao needle, for treating floating swelling of the skin; 8. Jue needle, left side insert right and vice versa; 9. Zhen needle, for treating burns, using a red-hot needle

    4: 凡刺有十二節，以應十二經。一曰偶刺，偶刺者，以手直心若背，直痛所，一刺前，一刺後，以治心痺。刺此者，傍鍼之也。二曰報刺，報刺者，刺痛無常處也。上下行者，直內無拔鍼，以左手隨病所按之，乃出鍼，復刺之也。三曰恢刺，恢刺者1，直刺傍之，舉之前後，恢筋急，以治筋痺也。四曰齊刺，齊刺者，直入一，傍入二，以治寒氣小深者；或曰三刺，三刺者，治痺氣小深者也。五曰揚刺，揚刺者，正內一，傍內四，而浮之，以治寒氣之搏大者也。六曰直鍼刺，直鍼刺者，引皮乃刺之，以治寒氣之淺者也。七曰輸鍼，輸刺者，直入直出，稀發鍼而深之，以治氣盛而熱者也。八曰短刺，短刺者，刺骨痺，稍搖而深之，致鍼骨所，以上下摩骨也。九曰浮刺，浮刺者，傍入而浮之，以治肌急而寒者也。十曰陰刺，陰刺者，左右率刺之，以治寒厥；中寒厥，足踝後少陰也。十一曰傍鍼刺，傍鍼刺者，直刺傍刺各一，以治留痺久居者也。十二曰贊刺，贊刺者，直入直出，數發鍼而淺之，出血是謂治癰腫也。1. 者 : 舊脫。 There are twelve needling methods, each corresponding to one of the twelve channels.

    1. Even Needling: Insert the needle symmetrically on both sides, one in front and one behind, to treat heart pain or numbness. The needle is inserted near the handle.

    2. Reporting Needling: Needle any area where the pain is not constant, moving up and down along the channel, pulling out the needle slightly and then re-needling.

    3. Raising Needling: Insert the needle alongside the painful area, lift it up front and back to treat muscle stiffness or numbness.

    4. Uniting Needling: Directly enter one point and near-enter two other points for treating mild cold and shallow conditions; alternatively known as Three Needlings, treating soreness and weakness in the same depth.

    5. Lifting Needling: Insert the needle straight inward and slightly outward, causing it to float, for treating severe chills and shallow conditions.

    6. Straight Needling: Pierce the skin and directly insert the needle, treating mild chills and shallow conditions.

    7. Drawing Needling: Enter the needle straight in and straight out, deeply but intermittently, for treating heat with fullness.

    8. Short Needling: Insert the needle to treat bone pain, slightly swaying it while going deep, and positioning the needle at the point of the bone, massaging the bone up and down.

    9. Floating Needling: Enter near the insertion point and float the needle, treating stiff muscles with coldness.

    10. Yin Needling: Insert the needle at yin points on both sides, treating cold damage; for interior cold damage, it is specifically the foot yangming and bladder channel of foot taixi.

    11. Beside Needling: Directly insert a needle on one side and another needle on the opposite side to treat long-standing stagnation or numbness.

    12. Celebrating Needling: Insert the needle straight in and out, repeatedly pulling it out slightly but shallowly, causing bleeding, for treating swelling or tumors.

    5: 脈之所居，深不見者，刺之微內鍼而久留之，以致其空脈氣也。脈淺者，勿刺，按絕其脈乃刺之，無令精出，獨出其邪氣耳。 Where the pulse resides, if it is not visible, insert a fine needle slightly inside and leave it in for a long time, in order to make its meridian empty of qi. For a shallow pulse, do not insert needles; pressing to stop the pulse is the correct way of needling, do not let the spirit come out, only let out the pathogenic qi.

    6: 所謂三刺，則穀氣出者。先淺刺絕皮，以出陽邪，再刺則陰邪出者，少益深絕皮，致肌肉，未入分肉間也；已入分肉之間，則穀氣出。故刺法曰：始刺淺之，以逐邪氣，而來血氣，後刺深之，以致陰氣之邪，最後刺極深之，以下穀氣。此之謂也。 The so-called three punctures are when the grain qi comes out. First, shallowly pierce the skin to release Yang pathogenic factors; then pierce again to release Yin pathogenic factors. Superficially pierce and reach the muscles without going deep into the subcutaneous tissue. When you have entered the subcutaneous tissue, the grain qi will come out. Therefore, the method of acupuncture says: first shallowly pierce to chase away the pathogenic factors, followed by coming blood and qi, then deeply pierce to reach Yin pathogenic factors, and finally pierce extremely deep to release the grain qi. This is what it means.

    7: 故用鍼者，不知年之所加，氣之盛衰，虛實之所起，不可以為工也。 One who employs the acupuncture technique does not know how the years affect it, how changes in vital energy are manifested, or where true and false begin; therefore, it cannot be used for work.

    8: 凡刺有五，以應五藏，一曰半刺，半刺者，淺內而疾發鍼，無鍼傷肉，如拔毛狀，以取皮氣，此肺之應也。 Five needles in total, corresponding to the five organs, one is called half-needle. The half-needle is inserted shallowly and rapidly pierces through, without piercing the flesh, resembling plucking hair, used to obtain the qi of the lung, which corresponds to the lung organ.

    9: 二曰豹文刺，豹文刺者，左右前後鍼之，中脈為故，以取經絡之血者，此心之應也。 It is said that the tiger's word pierces, and the one who pierces with the tiger's word, thrusts left and right, front and back, with needles. When the central pulse fails, blood from the meridians is taken, thus matching the heart's nature

    10: 三曰關刺，關刺者，直刺左右盡筋上，以取筋痺，慎無出血，此肝之應也；或曰淵刺；一曰豈刺。 It is said, regarding the acupuncture point called Guán Cái (关刺): Guán Cái refers to directly inserting the needle into the muscles on both sides, targeting the muscle junctions, to treat muscular tension. Be cautious not to cause bleeding. This is the appropriate treatment for the liver. Another name for this point is Yǒu Cái (淵刺), and some call it Bǔ Cái (不刺).

    11: 四曰合谷刺，合谷刺者，左右雞足，鍼於分肉之間，以取肌痺，此脾之應也。 Says the text: Fourthly, it is called Merging Valley Needle. The Merging Valley Needle is applied to the sides of the chicken legs, piercing between the muscles, in order to remove blood stagnation, which is an indication for the spleen.

    12: 五曰輸刺，輸刺者，直入直出，深內之至骨，以取骨痺，此腎之應也。 Five says: Externalize the needle, the externalized needle is inserted straight in and out, deeply entering to the bone, taking away the bone obstruction, which is the corresponding function of the kidney.
