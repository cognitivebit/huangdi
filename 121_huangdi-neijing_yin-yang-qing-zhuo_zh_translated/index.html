    陰陽清濁

    1: 黃帝曰：余聞十二經脈，以應十二經水者，其五色各異，清濁不同，人之血氣若一，應之奈何？歧伯曰：人之血氣，苟能若一，則天下為一矣，惡有亂者乎？黃帝曰：余問一人，非問天下之眾。歧伯曰：夫一人者，亦有亂氣，天下之象，亦有亂人，其合為一耳。 Yellow Emperor said: I have heard of the twelve channels and their corresponding water courses. The five colors are different, with varying degrees of purity and impurity. Although human blood and qi are essentially one, how can they be balanced? Master Qiao said: If human blood and qi were truly one, the world would be unified, and chaos would ensue. Yellow Emperor asked: I am asking about one person, not the multitude of the world. Master Qiao replied: Even in one person, there is chaos within, and among the various examples in the world, there are also chaotic people. Their unity is simply for comparison

    2: 黃帝曰：願聞人氣之清濁。歧伯曰：受穀者濁，受氣者清。清者注陰，濁者注陽。濁而清者，上出於咽，清而濁者，則下行。清濁相干，命曰亂氣。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the distinction between clear Qi and turbid Qi. [The philosopher] Boyi replied: Those who receive grain-Qi are turbid, and those who receive clear-Qi are refined. The refined is stored in the Yin, while the turbid is stored in the Yang. If it becomes turbid within clarity, it ascends from the throat; if it becomes clear within turbidity, then it descends. The distinction between clear Qi and turbid Qi is called chaotic Qi.

    3: 黃帝曰：夫陰清而陽濁，濁者有清，清者有濁，清濁別之奈何？歧伯曰：氣之大別，清者上注于肺，濁者下走于胃。胃之清氣，上出于口；肺之濁氣，下注于經，內積于海。 Yellow Emperor said: When the yin is clear and the yang is turbid, which one is responsible for causing the other to be so, how can we distinguish between them? Guo Bo replied: The distinction lies in the different aspects of qi. Clear qi flows upward to the lungs, while turbid qi flows downward to the stomach. The clear qi from the stomach rises up through the mouth; the turbid qi from the lungs descends into the channels and accumulates within the sea.

    4: 黃帝曰：諸陽皆濁，何陽濁甚乎？歧伯曰：手太陽獨受陽之濁，手太陰獨受陰之清；其清者上走空竅，其濁者下行諸經。諸陰皆清，足太陰獨受其濁。 Yellow Emperor said: All the channels of Zhongyang are obscured, is that of Youji obscured even more so? Qi Bo replied: The left hand's Yang channel receives the obscurity alone, and the left hand's Yin channel receives the clarity alone. The clear Qi rises through the openings above, while the obscure Qi flows to the collaterals below. All the Yin channels are clear, and the foot Yin channel receives the obscurity alone.

    5: 黃帝曰：治之奈何？歧伯曰：清者其氣滑，濁者其氣澀，此氣之常也。故刺陰者，深而留之；刺陽者，淺而疾之；清濁相干者，以數調之也。 Yellow Emperor said: How should it be governed? Hua-Boo said: The pure one has smooth Qi, the murky one has thick Qi, this is the nature of Qi. Therefore, when needling the Yin, go deep and retain; when needling the Yang, go shallow and quick; for clear and turbid, adjust by numbers and timing
