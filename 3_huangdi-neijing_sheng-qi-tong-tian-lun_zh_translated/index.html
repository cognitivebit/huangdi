    生氣通天論

    1. 黃帝曰：夫自古通天者生之本，本於陰陽天地之間，六合之內，其氣九州九竅五藏十二節，皆通乎天氣。其生五，其氣三，數犯此者，則邪氣傷人，此壽命之本也。蒼天之氣清淨，則志意治，順之則陽氣固，雖有賊邪，弗能害也，此因時之序。故聖人傳精神，服天氣，而通神明。失之則內閉九竅，外壅肌肉，衛氣散解，此謂自傷，氣之削也。 Yellow Emperor said: The foundation of connecting with heaven is from ancient times. It comes from the space between yin and yang, within the six harmonies, nine zones and five layers, and twelve channels. Their qi connects to the celestial qi. When there are five openings and three layers, often falling into this, evil qi harms people, which is the basis of life and death. The pure and clear qi of the sky regulates intention and follows it, leading to the strengthening of yang qi, even if there are external evil influences, they cannot harm. This is due to the appropriate order of the times. Therefore, sages transmit spirit and serve celestial qi in order to connect with the divine light. Failing this, nine apertures close internally, exterior muscles harden, guarding and dispersing qi, which is called self-harm and the decline of qi.

    2. 陽氣者若天與日，失其所，則折壽而不彰，故天運當以日光明。是故陽因而上，衛外者也。因於寒，欲如運樞，起居如驚，神氣乃浮。因於暑，汗煩則喘喝，靜則多言，體若燔炭，汗出而散。因於濕，首如裹，濕熱不攘，大筋緛短，小筋弛長，緛短為拘，弛長為痿。因於氣，為腫，四維相代，陽氣乃竭。 If one is like the sun and heaven, losing their place will result in a bent life that does not shine brightly. Therefore, celestial stems correspond to solar illumination. Hence, the Yang Qi ascends above, and pertains to the outlying area (i.e., the periphery of the body). Because of Cold, one wishes to be like an axis, arising and settling as if startled; the spirit energy rises. Because of Heat, sweat vexes and causes panting and hoarse speech; in silence, it results in much speaking; the body is like scorched charcoal with sweat being released to disperse. Because of Dampness, the head is like a swaddling cloth, damp heat does not distinguish boundaries, large sinews are tightened and shortened, small sinews are loosened and lengthened; tightening and shortening leads to rigidity, while loosening and lengthening lead to weakness. Because of Qi, there is distention, with the four limbs taking turns in being distended; Yang Qi is depleted.

    3. 陽氣者，煩勞則張，精絕辟積，於夏使人煎厥。目盲不可以視，耳閉不可以聽，潰潰乎若壞都，汨汨乎不可止。陽氣者，大怒則形氣絕，而血菀於上，使人薄厥。有傷於筋縱，其若不容，汗出偏沮，使人偏枯。汗出見濕，乃生痤疿。高梁之變，足生大丁，受如持虛。勞汗當風，寒薄為皻，鬱乃痤。 Persons with excessive Yang Qi, when fatigued or overworked, their Essence is depleted and accumulated, causing them to be scorched in summer. Their eyesight cannot see, and they cannot hear with their ears, and saliva drools uncontrollably, like the ruins of a city. When Yang Qi becomes enraged, one's physical condition is disrupted, causing blood to flow upward, leading to dizziness. There are injuries to the tendons that cannot be tolerated; sweating occurs on one side, leading to emaciation in one part. Sweat is visible and generates boils and swelling. High fever leads to the formation of large blisters, which are as difficult to hold as emptiness. Laborous sweating in cold weather causes chills, while depression leads to boils

    4. 陽氣者，精則養神，柔則養筋。開闔不得，寒氣從之，乃生大僂。陷脈為瘻，留連肉腠。俞氣化薄，傳為善畏，及為驚駭。營氣不從，逆於肉理，乃生癰腫。魄汗未盡，形弱而氣爍，穴俞以閉，發為風瘧。故風者，百病之始也，清靜則肉腠閉拒，雖有大風苛毒，弗之能害，此因時之序也。故病久則傳化，上下不并，良醫弗為。故陽畜積病死，而陽氣當隔，隔者當寫，不亟正治，粗乃敗之。 The person with Yang Qi cultivates the spirit through refinement and nurtures the muscles through softness. If one cannot open or close, cold Qi will invade, leading to the formation of large swellings. Blood stagnation is like a piglet stuck in a drainpipe, leaving behind tendons and sinews. Yang Qi transforms into thinness, spreading fear and shock. If the energy does not circulate properly, it goes against the flesh's principles and causes boils and tumors. Before the soul sweat has evaporated, one's form is weak and energy is depleted, closing the acupuncture points leads to wind sickness. Therefore, wind is the source of all diseases; when calm and clear, the flesh and sinews close and reject even harsh winds and toxic poisons. If a disease persists, it will change forms and not parallel up and down, making it difficult for skilled doctors to treat. Thus, Yang Qi accumulates diseases leading to death, while Yang Qi must be separated; separation must be written, without delay in proper treatment, leading to failure

    5. 故陽氣者，一日而主外，平旦人氣生，日中而陽氣隆，日西而陽氣已虛，氣門乃閉。是故暮而收拒，無擾筋骨，無見霧露，反此三時，形乃困薄。 Individuals with a yang nature: by midday they are in charge, and the qi of the people is generated at night. The qi of yang becomes strong at noon, but as the sun sets, the yang qi has already become depleted, and the gate of qi is closed. Therefore, at dusk, one should gather and withdraw, have no disturbance to sinews and bones, not see mist and dew, and in contrast to these three times, the form will be weak and thin.

    6. 歧伯曰：陰者，藏精而起亟也，陽者，衛外而為固也。陰不勝其陽，則脈流薄疾，并乃狂。陽不勝其陰，則五藏氣爭，九竅不通。是以聖人陳陰陽，筋脈和同，骨髓堅固，氣血皆從。如是，則內外調和，邪不能害，耳目聰明，氣立如故。 The Yu Bode said: The Yin is the one that stores its essence and acts in a hurry, while the Yang is the one that defends outside and stands firm. If Yin does not overcome Yang, then the pulse will flow thinly and rapidly, leading to madness. If Yang does not overcome Yin, then the five organs struggle for Qi, and the nine orifices do not communicate. Thus, the sage person establishes Yin and Yang, coordinates tendons and vessels, and makes bone marrow strong and firm, allowing Qi and blood to flow well. In this way, the inner and outer are harmonized, evil cannot harm, and hearing and sight are acute, Qi stands tall as before.

    7. 風客淫氣，精乃亡，邪傷肝也。因而飽食，筋脈橫解，腸澼為痔。因而大飲，則氣逆。因而強力，腎氣乃傷，高骨乃壞。凡陰陽之要，陽密乃固，兩者不和，若春無秋，若冬無夏，因而和之，是謂聖度。故陽強不能密，陰氣乃絕，陰平陽秘，精神乃治，陰陽離決，精氣乃絕，因於露風，乃生寒熱。 The winds of the wayward roam and disperse, souls perish, the path of the demonic damages the liver. Hence indulgence in food leads to the sinews' dispersion, causing the intestines to form fistulas. Thus great drinking results in reversed Qi. Thus exerting oneself causes kidney Qi to be injured and bones to decay. In all matters of yin and yang, yang being sealed is key; if the two do not harmonize, it is like spring without autumn or winter without summer. Hence to harmonize them, this is called sheng du. Therefore, when yang is strong but cannot be sealed, yin Qi will vanish; yin is leveled and yang concealed, spirit will be treated, yin and yang separate, and Qi of spirit will vanish due to exposure to cold wind, thus giving rise to cold and heat.

    8. 是以春傷於風，邪氣留連，乃為洞泄。夏傷於暑，秋為痎瘧。秋傷於濕，上逆而欬，發為痿厥。冬傷於寒，春必溫病。四時之氣，更傷五藏。 Therefore, the harm caused by the spring wind gives rise to a cold condition; the pathogenic qi lingers and results in an excretion. In summer, harm from heat leads to rash; in autumn, it causes prickly heat. Harm from dampness in autumn causes headache with dizziness, leading to limb weakness. Harm from cold in winter necessarily brings onspring fever. The seasonal pathogenic qi further harms the five organs.

    9. 陰之所生，本在五味，陰之五宮，傷在五味。是故味過於酸，肝氣以津，脾氣乃絕。味過於鹹，大骨氣勞，短肌，心氣抑。味過於甘，心氣喘滿，色黑腎氣不衡。味過於苦，脾氣不濡，胃氣乃厚。味過於辛，筋脈沮弛，精神乃央，是故謹和五味，骨正筋柔，氣血以流，湊理以密，如是，則骨氣以精，謹道如法，長有天命。 The flavors that arise from yin are rooted in the five tastes, and the yin of the five orifices resides within the five tastes. Therefore, if the taste is excessively sour, liver qi will dominate with fluids, spleen qi will be exhausted. If the taste is overly salty, bone qi will labor, muscles will be short, heart qi will be repressed. If the taste is too sweet, heart qi will pant heavily, complexion will be black and kidney qi will not regulate. If the taste is excessively bitter, spleen qi will not moisten, stomach qi will become thick. If the taste is overly pungent, channels and vessels will collapse, spirit will be dispersed. Therefore, carefully harmonize the five tastes, so that bones will be upright and muscles pliable, qi and blood will circulate, essences will be subtle, practice the way according to law, and one can enjoy longevity by following heaven's decrees
