    金匱真言論

    1. 黃帝問曰：天有八風，經有五風，何謂。歧伯對曰：八風發邪，以為經風，觸五藏，邪氣發病。所謂得四時之勝者，春勝長夏，長夏勝冬，冬勝夏，夏勝秋，秋勝春，所謂四時之勝也。東風生於春，病在肝，俞在頸項；南風生於夏，病在心，俞在胸脇；西風生於秋，病在肺，俞在肩背；北風生於冬，病在腎，俞在腰股；中央為土，病在脾；俞在脊。故春氣者病在頭，夏氣者病在藏，秋氣者病在肩背，冬氣者病在四支。故春善病鼽衄，仲夏善病胸脇，長夏善病洞泄寒中，秋善病風瘧，冬善病痺厥。故冬不按蹻，春不鼽衄，春不病頸項，仲夏不病胸脇，長夏不病洞泄寒中，秋不病風瘧，冬不病痺厥飱泄，而汗出也。夫精者身之本也，故藏於精者春不病溫。夏暑汗不出者，秋成風瘧。此平人脈法也。 The Yellow Emperor asked, There are eight winds in heaven and five winds in the Classic of Changes. What do they mean? The Duke answered, The eight winds emit pathogenic factors, which are called the winds of the Classic of Changes. They strike the Five Organs, causing pathogenic Qi to give rise to diseases. Those who master the seasons will know which one triumphs over the others: spring triumphs over long summer, long summer triumphs over winter, winter triumphs over summer, and summer triumphs over autumn, and autumn triumphs over spring. This is known as the triumph of the four seasons. The east wind arises in spring, affecting the liver and knotting at the neck and jaw; the south wind arises in summer, affecting the heart and knotting at the chest and back; the west wind arises in autumn, affecting the lungs and knotting at the shoulder and back; and the north wind arises in winter, affecting the kidneys and knotting at the hips. The center is Earth, affecting the spleen and knotting at the spine. Therefore, diseases of Qi in spring affect the head; those of summer affect the abdomen; those of autumn affect the shoulder blades; and those of winter affect the limbs. Hence, spring is prone to skin eruptions, summer is prone to chest disorders, long summer is prone to diarrhea and cold damage, autumn is prone to windstroke, and winter is prone to numbness and paralysis. Consequently, one should not suppress urges in winter, nor indulge in skin eruptions in spring, nor suffer from neck and jaw pain in spring, nor chest disorders in summer, nor diarrhea and cold damage in long summer, nor windstroke in autumn, nor numbness and paralysis in winter. When the latter does occur, one should sweat it out, which is known as the Plain people's pulse method.

    2. 故曰：陰中有陰，陽中有陽。平旦至日中，天之陽，陽中之陽也；日中至黃昏，天之陽，陽中之陰也；合夜至雞鳴，天之陰，陰中之陰也；雞鳴至平旦，天之陰，陰中之陽也。故人亦應之。夫言人之陰陽，則外為陽，內為陰。言人身之陰陽，則背為陽，腹為陰。言人身之藏府中陰陽，則藏者為陰，府者為陽。肝心脾肺腎五藏，皆為陰。膽胃大腸小腸膀胱三焦六府，皆為陽。所以欲知陰中之陰，陽中之陽者何也。為冬病在陰，夏病在陽，春病在陰，秋病在陽，皆視其所在，為施鍼石也。故背為陽，陽中之陽，心也；背為陽，陽中之陰，肺也；腹為陰，陰中之陰，腎也；腹為陰，陰中之陽，肝也；腹為陰，陰中之至陰，脾也。此皆陰陽表裏內外雌雄相輸應也，故以應天之陰陽也。 Thus it is said: Within Yin there is Yin, within Yang there is Yang. At dawn, the sun's Yang is the Yang within Yang; at noon, the sun's Yang is the Yang within Yin; at the crowing of the cock, the sun's Yin is the Yin within Yin; and from the crowing of the cock to dawn, the sun's Yin is the Yin within Yang. Therefore, humans should follow this pattern. As for human Yin and Yang, the outside is Yang and the inside is Yin. As for the Yin and Yang of the human body, the back is Yang and the front is Yin. As for the Yin and Yang of the body's storage chambers, the stored element is Yin and the chamber is Yang. The five organs of storage—liver, heart, spleen, lung, and kidney—are all Yin. The six fu organs of the three burners—gallbladder, stomach, large intestine, small intestine, bladder, and triple burner—are all Yang. So, how does one know the Yin within Yin and the Yang within Yang? Treat winter diseases as being in the Yin, summer diseases as being in the Yang, spring diseases as being in the Yin, and autumn diseases as being in the Yang, all depending on where they are located. Therefore, the back is Yang, the Yang within Yang, which is the heart; the back is Yang, the Yang within Yin, which is the lung; the abdomen is Yin, the Yin within Yin, which is the kidney; the abdomen is Yin, the Yin within Yang, which is the liver; and the abdomen is Yin, the Yin within the utmost Yin, which is the spleen. All of these demonstrate the interplay between Yin and Yang in the human body's exterior and interior, as well as the male and female aspects, so humans should follow this pattern to correspond with heaven's Yin and Yang.

    3. 帝曰：五藏應四時，各有收受乎，歧伯曰：有。東方青色，入通於肝，開竅於目，藏精於肝，其病發驚駭，其味酸，其類草木，其畜雞，其穀麥，其應四時，上為歲星，是以春氣在頭也，其音角，其數八，是以知病之在筋也，其臭臊。南方赤色，入通於心，開竅於耳，藏精於心，故病在五藏，其味苦，其類火，其畜羊，其穀黍，其應四時，上為熒惑星，是以知病之在脈也，其音徵，其數七，其臭焦。中央黃色，入通於脾，開竅於口，藏精於脾，故病在舌本，其味甘，其類土，其畜牛，其穀稷，其應四時，上為鎮星，是以知病之在肉也，其音宮，其數五，其臭香。西方白色，入通於肺，開竅於鼻，藏精於肺，故病在背，其味辛，其類金，其畜馬，其穀稻，其應四時，上為太白星，是以知病之在皮毛也，其音商，其數九，其臭腥。北方黑色，入通於腎，開竅於二陰，藏精於腎，故病在谿，其味鹹，其類水，其畜彘，其穀豆，其應四時，上為辰星，是以知病之在骨也，其音羽，其數六，其臭腐。故善為脈者，謹察五藏六府，一逆一從，陰陽表裏，雌雄之紀，藏之心意，合心於精，非其人勿教，非其真勿授，是謂得道。 The emperor said: The five viscera correspond to the four seasons, each having their own function. Duke Mu replied: Yes. The eastern color is green, it enters through the liver, opens the orifices of the eyes, stores the essence in the liver, and when there is disease, it manifests as shock and fright; its flavor is sour, its category is grasses, its birds are chickens, its grains are wheat, its season corresponds to the four seasons, above it is the seasonal star, so spring qi is in the head, its sound is jiao, its number is eight, thus knowing the disease is in the sinews.

    The southern color is red, it enters through the heart, opens the orifices of the ears, stores the essence in the heart, so the disease is in the five viscera, its flavor is bitter, its category is fire, its animals are sheep, its grains are millet, its season corresponds to the stars, above it is the planetary star, thus knowing the disease is in the pulse, its sound is zhi, its number is seven, its smell is burnt.

    The central color is yellow, it enters through the spleen, opens the orifices of the mouth, stores the essence in the spleen, so the disease is in the tongue origin, its flavor is sweet, its category is earth, its animals are oxen, its grains are millet, its season corresponds to the four seasons, above it is the star of tranquility, thus knowing the disease is in the flesh, its sound is gong, its number is five, its smell is fragrant.

    The western color is white, it enters through the lungs, opens the orifices of the nose, stores the essence in the lungs, so the disease is in the back, its flavor is spicy, its category is metal, its animals are horses, its grains are millet, its season corresponds to the four seasons, above it is the star of the celestial terminator, thus knowing the disease is in the skin and hair, its sound is shang, its number is nine, its smell is fetid.

    The northern color is black, it enters through the kidneys, opens the orifices of the two yin channels, stores the essence in the kidneys, so the disease is in the channels, its flavor is salty, its category is water, its animals are donkeys, its grains are beans, its season corresponds to the four seasons, above it is the star of the celestial stem, thus knowing the disease is in the bones, its sound is yi, its number is six, its smell is putrid.

    Therefore, skilled in pulse diagnosis should carefully examine the five viscera and their six organs, one reversing and one following, yin and yang, exterior and interior, recording the female and male records, storing the essence in the heart-mind, combining the heart-mind with the essence, not teaching others unless one is skilled, not granting unless one is true - this is attaining the way.
