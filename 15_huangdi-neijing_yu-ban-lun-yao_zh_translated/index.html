    玉版論要

    1: 黃帝問曰：余聞揆度奇恒，所指不同，用之柰何。歧伯對曰：揆度者，度病之淺深也。奇恒者，言奇病也。請言道之至數，五色脈變，揆度奇恒，道在於一。神轉不回，回則不轉，乃失其機，至數之要，迫近以微，著之玉版，命曰合玉機。 Emperor Yellow asked, I have heard of the method of measuring the shallow or deep and distinguishing between ordinary and extraordinary diseases. How can it be used? The Duke replied, The method of measuring is for measuring the shallow or deep degree of illness, and distinguishing between ordinary and extraordinary diseases. When asking about the number, if the five colors of the pulse vary, the method of measuring and distinguishing applies. The spirit may not return or turn back and will lose its function. The essence of reaching the limit lies in approaching it subtly, engraving it onto the jade mold, which is called the Meeting of the Jade Function.

    2: 容色見上下左右，各在其要。其色見淺者，湯液主治，十日已。其見深者，必齊主治，二十一日己。其見大深者，醪酒主治，百日已。色夭面脫，不治，百日盡已。脈短氣絕死，病溫虛甚死。色見上下左右，各在其要。上為逆，下為從。女子右為逆，左為從；男子左為逆，右為從。易重陽死，重陰死。 One sees the color above, below, left and right, each in its place. If one sees a light color, use hot soup as treatment and it will be cured in ten days. If one sees a deep color, it must be treated by Qu, taking twenty-one days. If one sees a very deep color, treat with fermented milk, taking a hundred days. When the color becomes rotten and peels off, it cannot be cured, after a hundred days. Pulse is short and breath is cut off, leading to death. The color above, below, left and right, each in its place. Above refers to rebellion, below refers to obedience. For females, the right refers to rebellion and the left refers to obedience; for males, the left refers to rebellion and the right refers to obedience. Easy Rebellion leads to death, Easy Yin leads to death.

    3: 陰陽反他，治在權衡相奪，奇恒事也，揆度事也。搏脈痺躄，寒熱之交。脈孤為消氣，虛泄為奪血。孤為逆，虛為從。行奇恒之法，以太陰始。行所不勝曰逆，逆則死；行所勝曰從，從則活。八風四時之勝，終而復始，逆行一過，不復可數，論要畢矣。 Yin and Yang mutually oppose each other, with balance residing in their counteraction. This is a constant principle and a measure of degrees. Pulse palpitations are weak and irregular, characterized by the interplay of cold and heat. A solitary pulse indicates deficient Qi, while an empty pulse indicates bleeding. Solitariness represents reversal, and emptiness signifies following. By practicing the principles of constancy and odd-even, one can begin with the Lesser Yin. Acting contrary to what is beneficial is referred to as reversal, which leads to death; acting in accordance with what is advantageous is called following, which ensures life. The victories of the eight winds and the four seasons exemplify the cyclical nature of things. Once a cycle is completed, it starts anew. If one deviates from this pattern even once, it cannot be counted, and discussions on this subject are at an end.
