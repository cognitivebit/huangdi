    欬論

    1. 黃帝問曰：肺之令人欬，何也。歧伯對曰：五藏六府，皆令人欬，非獨肺也。帝曰：願聞其狀。歧伯曰：皮毛者，肺之合也，皮毛先受邪氣，邪氣以從其合也。其寒飲食入胃，從肺脈上至於肺，則肺寒，肺寒則外內合，邪因而客之，則為肺欬。五藏各以其時受病，非其時，各傳以與之。人與天地相參，故五藏各以治，時感於寒，則受病，微則為欬，甚者為泄為痛。乘秋則肺先受邪，乘春則肝先受之，乘夏則心先受之，乘至陰則脾先受之，乘冬則腎先受之。 Emperor asked: What is it about the lungs that makes people sneeze? The Duke answered: All the five organs and six sense organs can cause people to sneeze, it's not just the lungs. Emperor said: I would like to hear more about this. The Duke said: The skin and hair are what the lungs control. The skin and hair are the first to be affected by pathogenic qi, and the pathogenic qi follows their control. When cold drinks or food enter the stomach, they travel along the lung channel to the lungs, causing the lungs to become cold. Cold lungs cause internal and external control to merge, and the pathogenic factor attacks the body, resulting in a lung sneeze. Each of the five organs is affected by disease at different times, and none of them are unaffected. People and heaven harmonize with each other, so the five organs each have their ways of treatment. When it's cold, they are susceptible to disease, mild cases result in sneezing, while severe cases lead to leaking or pain. Riding autumn, the lungs are the first to be affected by pathogenic factors, riding spring causes the liver to be first affected, riding summer causes the heart to be first affected, riding yin results in the spleen being the first affected, and riding winter leads to the kidneys being the first affected.

    2. 帝曰：何以異之。歧伯曰：肺欬之狀，欬而喘息有音，甚則唾血。心欬之狀，欬則心痛。喉中介介如梗狀，甚則咽腫喉痺。肝欬之狀，欬則兩脇下痛，甚則不可以轉，轉則兩胠下滿。脾欬之狀，欬則右脇下痛陰陰引肩背，甚則不可以動，動則欬劇。腎欬之狀，欬則腰背相引而痛，甚則欬涎。 The emperor asked, What is the peculiarity of this? The marquis replied, The state of the lung is characterized by coughing and wheezing with a hoarse voice. Severe cases involve spitting blood. The state of the heart is characterized by chest pain upon coughing. In the throat, there is an obstructed feeling like choking, which in severe cases causes enlargement of the throat and soreness of the neck. The state of the liver is characterized by pain in the flanks upon coughing, with severe cases making it impossible to turn and, when turning, causing pain in the lower abdomen. The state of the spleen is characterized by right-flank pain upon coughing, which then radiates to the groin and makes the shoulders and back sore. In severe cases, movement is impossible and coughing becomes more pronounced. The state of the kidney is characterized by lower back pain that extends to the abdomen upon coughing, with severe cases involving excessive spitting.

    3. 帝曰：六府之欬柰何，安所受病。歧伯曰：五藏之久欬，乃移於六府，脾欬不已，則胃受之，胃欬之狀，欬而嘔，嘔甚則長蟲出。肝欬不已，則膽受之，膽欬之狀，欬嘔膽汁，肺欬不已，則大腸受之，大腸欬狀，欬而遺失。心欬不已，則小腸受之，小腸欬狀，欬而失氣，氣與欬俱失。腎欬不已，則膀胱受之，膀胱欬狀，欬而遺溺。久欬不已，則三焦受之，三焦欬狀，欬而腹滿，不欲食飲，此皆聚於胃，關於肺，使人多涕唾而面浮腫氣逆也。 The emperor asked: What is the meaning of the six palaces? How can it be treated? The Duke said: When there is a prolonged disturbance in the Five Organs, it moves to the six palaces. If the spleen is not relieved, it affects the stomach; when the stomach is disturbed, it causes nausea and vomiting. If this persists, worms will appear. If the liver is not relieved, it affects the gallbladder; when the gallbladder is disturbed, it causes qi to rise up and there will be bile secretion. If the lungs are not relieved, it affects the large intestine; when the large intestine is disturbed, there will be involuntary loss of feces. If the heart is not relieved, it affects the small intestine; when the small intestine is disturbed, there will be shortness of breath and loss of vital energy. If the kidneys are not relieved, it affects the bladder; when the bladder is disturbed, there will be incontinence. When there is a prolonged disturbance, it affects the triple burner; when the triple burner is disturbed, there will be abdominal fullness and no desire to eat or drink. All these symptoms accumulate in the stomach and obstruct the lungs, causing excessive secretion of tears and swelling of the face due to qi turning upwards.

    4. 帝曰：治之柰何。歧伯曰：治藏者，治其俞，治府者，治其合。浮腫者，治其經。帝曰：善。 The emperor asked: How should one govern? Duke of Zhou replied: To govern the people, govern their rituals; to govern the household, govern its members. To govern the floating and bloated, govern their meridians. The emperor said: Good
