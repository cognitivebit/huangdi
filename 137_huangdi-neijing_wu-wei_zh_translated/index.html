    五味

    1. 黃帝曰：願聞穀氣有五味，其入五藏，分別奈何？伯高曰：胃者，五藏六府之海也，水穀皆入于胃，五藏六府，皆稟氣於胃。五味各走其所喜，穀味酸，先走肝，穀味苦，先走心，穀味甘，先走脾，穀味辛，先走肺，穀味咸，先走腎。穀氣津液已行，營衛大通，乃化糟粕，以次傳下。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the five flavors of grain energy and how they enter the five organs and are distributed. Duke Mu replied: The stomach, which is the sea of the five organs and six palaces, is where all grains enter. All grains nourish the qi in the stomach. Each flavor goes to its preferred location. The flavor of grain sour first moves to the liver; the flavor of grain bitter first moves to the heart; the flavor of grain sweet first moves to the spleen; the flavor of grain pungent first moves to the lungs; and the flavor of grain salty first moves to the kidneys. Once the grain energy and fluids have moved, the defensive and nutritive qi circulate freely, transforming dregs and excreting waste.

    2. 黃帝曰：營衛之行奈何？伯高曰：穀始入于胃，其精微者，先出於胃之兩焦，以溉五藏，別出兩行，營衛之道。其大氣之搏而不行者，積於胸中，命曰氣海，出於肺，循咽喉，故呼則出，吸則入。天地之精氣，其大數常出三入一，故穀不入，半日則氣衰，一日則氣少矣。 Yellow Emperor asked: How about the exercise of the military and the palace? Bo Gao replied: The grain first enters the stomach, its subtle essence comes out from the two parts of the stomach, irrigates the five viscera, and forms two channels of the exercise. Those who have difficulty in their great Qi do not move, accumulate in the chest, command called Qi sea, coming out from the lungs, flowing through the throat, so that when one exhales it comes out and when one inhales it comes in. The vital qi of heaven and earth, its large numbers are constantly emitted from three places and enter into one, therefore grain does not enter, half a day then the qi declines, and one day the qi is deficient.

    3. 黃帝曰：穀之五味，可得聞乎？伯高曰：請盡言之。五穀：糠米甘，麻酸，大豆咸，麥苦，黃黍辛。五果：棗甘，李酸，栗咸，杏苦，桃辛。五畜：牛甘，犬酸，豬咸，羊苦，雞辛。五菜：葵甘，韭酸，藿咸，薤苦，蔥辛。 Yellow Emperor said: Can one hear about the five flavors of grain? Duke of Zhou replied: Please tell me in detail. Five grains: millet is sweet, sorghum is sour, soybeans are salty, wheat is bitter, and barley is pungent. Five fruits: date palm is sweet, pear is sour, chestnut is salty, apricot is bitter, and peach is pungent. Five livestock: cow is sweet, dog is sour, pig is salty, sheep is bitter, and chicken is pungent. Five vegetables: lettuce is sweet, celery is sour, mustard greens are salty, chrysanthemum greens are bitter, and scallions are pungent.

    4. 五色：黃色宜甘，青色宜酸，黑色宜咸，赤色宜苦，白色宜辛。凡此五者，各有所宜。五宜所言五色者，脾病者，宜食糠米飯，牛肉棗葵；心病者，宜食麥羊肉杏薤；腎病者，宜食大豆黃卷豬肉栗藿；肝病者，宜食麻犬肉李韭；肺病者，宜食黃黍雞肉桃蔥。 Five colors: yellow is suitable for sweet, green is suitable for sour, black is suitable for salty, red is suitable for bitter, and white is suitable for pungent. Each of the five has its proper match. The five suits refer to the five colors, and those with a spleen disorder should eat millet rice, beef, walnuts, and kale; those with a heart disorder should eat barley, mutton, apricot kernels, and cassia; those with a kidney disorder should eat soybeans, yellow noodles, pork, and knotweed; those with a liver disorder should eat sesame, dog meat, and loquat leaves and scallions; those with a lung disorder should eat glutinous rice, chicken, peaches, and garlic chives.

    5. 五禁：肝病禁辛，心病禁咸，脾病禁酸，腎病禁甘，肺病禁苦。 Five Restrictions: For liver disease, avoid pungent foods; for heart disease, avoid salty foods; for spleen disease, avoid sour foods; for kidney disease, avoid sweet foods; for lung disease, avoid bitter foods.

    6. 肝色青，宜食甘，糠米飯、牛肉、棗、葵皆甘。心色赤，宜食酸，犬肉、麻、李、韭皆酸。脾黃色，宜食咸，大豆、豬肉、栗、藿皆咸。肺白色，宜食苦，麥、羊肉、杏、薤皆苦。腎色黑，宜食辛，黃黍、雞肉、桃、蔥皆辛。 Liver is associated with the color green, and foods that are sweet are beneficial. Such as rice gruel, beef, dates, chrysanthemums are all sweet. Heart is associated with the color red, and sour foods are beneficial. Dog meat, sesame, apricots, scallions are all sour. Spleen is associated with the color yellow, and salty foods are beneficial. Beans, pork, chestnuts, mint are all salty. Lung is associated with the color white, and bitter foods are beneficial. Wheat, mutton, apricot kernels, cicely are all bitter. Kidney is associated with the color black, and spicy foods are beneficial. Millet, chicken, peaches, garlic are all spicy.
