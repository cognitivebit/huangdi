    熱病

    1. 偏枯，身偏不用而痛，言不變，志不亂，病在分腠之間，巨鍼取之，益其不足，損其有餘，乃可復也。 Dry and withered, the body feels pain where it is not used, speech does not change, resolve does not become disordered, the illness lies between the divisions of the hair, a large needle can be inserted to restore what is lacking, and remove what is in excess, thus it can be cured.

    2. 痱之為病也，身無痛者，四肢不收；智亂不甚，其言微知，可治；甚則不能言，不可治也。病先起于陽，復入于陰者，先取其陽，後取其陰，浮而取之。 Aches and pains are signs of illness; if the limbs are not contracted, one can still be treated when intelligence is slightly disordered. If it becomes more severe, the patient cannot speak and cannot be treated. Illness first manifests in the Yang and eventually enters the Yin; treat the Yang first, then treat the Yin, and administer the medicine by floating it down

    3. 熱病三日，而氣口靜、人迎躁者，取之諸陽，五十九刺，以寫其熱，而出其汗，實其陰，以補其不足者。身熱甚，陰陽皆靜者，勿刺也；其可刺者，急取之，不汗出則泄。所謂勿刺者，有死徵也。 The patient had a fever for three days, and the breath was quiet with noisy person; take from Zhongyang, 59 needles, to disperse the fever and induce sweating, and reinforce what is deficient. If the body's fever is severe, with both Yin and Yang being still, do not pierce. Those who can be pierced, take urgent action, without sweating, release it (the Qi). The one referred to as do not pierce has a sign of death.

    4. 熱病七日八日，脈口動，喘而短者，急刺之，汗且自出，淺刺手大指間。 On the seventh or eighth day of a fever, if the pulse is uneven and there is short, labored breathing, urgently perform acupuncture. Sweat will then spontaneously emerge, and lightly puncture between the knuckles of the thumb.

    5. 熱病七日八日，脈微小，病者溲血，口中乾，一日半而死。脈代者，一日死。 On the seventh and eighth day of the fever, the pulse was weak and small. The patient had blood in their stool, their mouth was dry, and they died half a day later. If there was a change in the pulse, they would die on the same day.

    6. 熱病已得汗出，而脈尚躁，喘且復熱，勿刺膚，喘甚者死。 The patient has a fever and is sweating, but the pulse is still agitated, and the person is panting and feels hot again. Do not puncture the skin, as those who are panting heavily are likely to die.

    7. 熱病七日八日，脈不躁，躁不散數，後三日中有汗，三日不汗，四日死。未曾汗者，勿腠刺之。 Hot disease seven days eight days, pulse not agitated, agitated not dispersed in number, after three days there is sweating, three days without sweating, four days death. Those who have never sweat, do not acupuncture the sinew.

    8. 熱病先膚痛，窒鼻充面，取之皮，以第一鍼，五十九，苛軫鼻，索皮于肺，不得，索之火，火者，心也。 A fever disease begins with skin pain and a closed nose, take the skin from the tip of the nose, use the first needle, number 59, prick the nostril, get the skin from the lung, do not let it go, burn it and it will be the heart.

    9. 熱病先身澀倚而熱，煩俛，乾唇口溢，取之皮，以第一鍼，五十九；膚脹口乾，寒汗出，索脈於心，不得，索之水，水者，腎也。 A feverish illness precedes a hot bodily sensation, followed by irritability. The mouth and lips are parched, saliva flows out, the skin is flushed, the pulse is taken at the wrist, using the first needle, number fifty-nine. The skin feels swollen in the mouth, the lips are dry, cold sweat appears, take the pulse at the heart, if it cannot be found, take it from the water (meaning the kidney).

    10. 熱病溢乾多飲，善惊，臥不能起，取之膚肉，以第六鍼，五十九，目眥青，索肉於脾，不得，索之水，木者，肝也。 The patient has a high fever, is thirsty, and cannot get up from bed. Take a piece of flesh from the sixth lumbar region, 59 points, and apply it to the eyes which turn green. Poke the flesh into the spleen; if not successful, draw water - wood represents the liver.

    11. 熱病面青，腦痛，手足躁，取之筋間，以第四鍼於四逆；筋躄目浸，索筋於肝，不得，索之金，金者，肺也。 The patient has a fever, feeling chilly, headache, and restless in hands and feet. For this condition, take a needle at the four corners of the patient and insert it into the sinew channels. The sinew channel is submerged in the eye, and when it cannot be obtained, seek gold. Gold refers to the lung.

    12. 熱病數驚，瘈瘲而狂，取之脈，以第四鍼，急寫有餘者，癲疾毛髮去，索血於心，不得，索之水，水者，腎也。 The fever was alarming, the patient delirious and incoherent. Upon taking their pulse with the fourth finger, if it was still rapid, shave the hair and beard, tie a ligature around the heart to stop the bleeding, and if this is not possible, tie one around the water (i.e., the kidney).

    13. 熱病身重骨痛，耳聾而好瞑，取之骨，以第四鍼，五十九，刺骨；病不食，齧齒耳青，索骨於腎，不得，索之土，土者，脾也。 The patient has a fever and is heavy with bone pain, deafness, and likes to sleep. Take the bones using the fourth needle, number 59, to pierce the bones. The illness does not allow eating, the teeth are grinding and the ears are green. Pierce the bone in the kidney; if unable, pierce the soil which is the spleen.

    14. 熱病不知所痛，耳聾，不能自收，口乾，陽熱甚，陰頗有寒者，熱在髓，死不可治。 The patient has a high fever with unknown pain, deafness that cannot be self-retracted, dry mouth, severe yang heat, a little yin cold. The heat is in the marrow, and it cannot be treated.

    15. 熱病頭痛，顳顬目𤸪，脈痛，善衄，厥熱病也，取之以第三鍼，視有餘不足，寒熱痔。 Hot disease with headache, dizziness, pain in the pulse, good scabbing, and severe fever; this is a case of yin deficiency and excess heat. The treatment is to use the third needle, observing that there is still some insufficiency, cold-heat sores.

    16. 熱病，體重，腸中熱，取之以第四鍼，於其俞，及下諸趾間，索氣于胃胳得氣也。 Hot disease, body weight, heat in the stomach, take it with the fourth lancet at the tip and below the toes, and extract gas from the abdomen and intestines

    17. 熱病挾臍急痛，胸脅滿，取之涌泉與陰陵泉，取以第四鍼，鍼嗌裏。 The patient has a fever and is experiencing severe pain in the liver area, feeling oppressed in the chest. Obtain the medicine called Jiuquan Yulin Wan from the fourth prong of the needle holder and insert it into the tongue's interior.

    18. 熱病，而汗且出，及脈順可汗者，取之魚際、太淵、大都、太白。寫之則熱去，補之則汗出，汗出大甚，取內踝上橫脈以止之。 For fever, if the sweat comes out and the pulse is regular, take from the herbs of fish-wing, great profundity, capital city, and Tai He. Write it down and the fever will disappear, apply it and the sweat will come out. If the sweat comes out in great quantity, stop it by taking the horizontal pulse at the inside of the ankle.

    19. 熱病已得汗而脈尚躁盛，此陰脈之極也，死；其得汗而脈靜者，生。 If a person has a fever and sweats, but their pulse is still rapid and strong, this indicates the extreme state of the yin pulse, meaning death; if they sweat but their pulse becomes calm, it means they will live.

    20. 熱病者，脈尚盛躁而不得汗者，此陽脈之極也，死；脈盛躁得汗靜者，生。 In the case of a feverish patient whose pulse is still strong and agitated but fails to perspire, this indicates an extreme state of the Yang-pulse, resulting in death. If the pulse is strong and agitated but the patient does eventually sweat and recover, they will survive.

    21. 熱病不可刺者有九：一曰：汗不出，大顴發赤噦者死；二曰：泄而腹滿甚者死；三曰：目不明，熱不已者死；四曰：老人嬰兒熱而腹滿者死；五曰：汗不出嘔下血者死；六曰：舌本爛，熱不已者死；七曰：咳而衄，汗不出，出不至足者死；八曰：髓熱者死；九曰：熱而痙者死。腰折，瘈瘲，齒噤齘也。凡此九者，不可刺也。 There are nine conditions in which one should not lance a fever:

    22. A person with high fever, red face and tongue, but no sweating will die;
    23. If there is discharge of pus from the abdomen accompanied by great fullness, that person will die;
    24. If the eyes are not clear and the fever persists, that person will die;
    25. If an elderly person or infant has a high fever with fullness in the abdomen, that person will die;
    26. If there is no sweating and vomiting of blood from below the mouth, that person will die;
    27. If the tongue is originally scorched and the fever persists, that person will die;
    28. If one coughs and has hemoptysis, with no sweating and not reaching sufficient amount, that person will die;
    29. If the marrow is affected by fever, that person will die;

    30. If there is a convulsion due to fever, that person will die. Dislocation of the waist, scrofula, and tooth decay also occur. All these nine conditions should not be lanced

    31. 所謂五十九刺者，兩手外內側各三，凡十二痏。五指間各一，凡八痏，足亦如是。頭入髮一寸傍三分各三，凡六痏。更入髮三寸邊五，凡十痏。耳前後口下者各一，項中一，凡六痏。巔上一，聰會一，髮際一，廉泉一，風池二，天柱二。 Regarding the fifty-nine needles [term], on the outer side of each hand there are three, making a total of twelve. Between the five fingers, there is one needle each, making a total of eight. The feet are also like this. For the head, one inch entering the hair and each side has three, making a total of six. Adding another three inches entering the hair and five at the edge, making a total of ten. There is one each for the front and back of the ears, the lower mouth, and the center of the throat, making a total of six. One for the top, one for understanding, one for the hairline, one for the modest wellspring, two for the wind pond, and two for the pillar of heaven.

    32. 氣滿胸中喘息，取足太陰大趾之端，去爪甲如薤葉，寒則留之，熱則疾之，氣下乃止。 The chest is filled with gasping breath, seize the tip of the great toe of the foot yin, remove the nails like pearl leaves, cold then keep it, hot then rush it, when the gasp stops.

    33. 心疝暴痛，取足太陰厥陰，盡刺去其血絡。 The pain in the chest is intense; take the foot too-yin and pinch it to remove its blood vessels.

    34. 喉痺舌卷，口中乾，煩心，心痛，臂內廉痛，不可及頭，取手小指次指爪甲下，去端如韭葉。 Throat constriction and tongue twisted, dry mouth, irritable heart, heartache, pain in the arms, cannot reach the head, take the hand little finger and ring finger nails, cut off the end like a radish leaf.

    35. 目中赤痛，從內眥始，取之陰蹻。 In the heart's red pain, from the inner corner [of the eye] it begins, taken from the yin secretion (tears).

    36. 風痙身反折，先取足太陽及膕中及血絡出血，中有寒，取三里。 The patient experiences chills and their body is twisting. First, take the moxa at the Sinew Channel points on the foot (such as Yanglingquan, Qiahai, and Shenmen) and apply bleeding cupping therapy. If there is coldness, take three li of medicine.
