    逆順肥瘦

    1. 黃帝問于歧伯曰：余聞鍼道于夫子，眾多畢悉矣。夫子之道，應若失，而據未有堅然者也。夫子之問學熟乎，將審察于物而心生之乎？歧伯曰：聖人之為道者，上合于天，下合于地，中合于人事，必有明法，以起度數，法式檢押，乃後可傳焉。故匠人不能釋尺寸而意短長，廢繩墨而起平水也，工人不能置規而為圓，去矩而為方。知用此者，固自然之物，易用之教，逆順之常也。 Yellow Emperor asked Guan Yu: I have heard that Confucius taught the art of the needle, and many have mastered it. Confucius's art, like a river flowing east or west, has no fixed direction. The Yellow Emperor asked about Confucius's study of knowledge, whether it is by scrutinizing objects to generate ideas within oneself? Guan Yu replied: A sage who practices the Way unites with heaven above, earth below, and human affairs in between, surely there is a method to guide the development, which can be established as a standard, and thus can be passed down. Artisans cannot fully reveal their skill in measuring dimensions, yet they create short and long objects skillfully; carpenters cannot establish rules but still achieve roundness and squareness. Knowing how to use these methods is the natural way of things, an easily learned teaching, and goes against what is considered orderly and conventional.

    2. 黃帝曰：願聞自然奈何？歧伯曰：臨深決水，不用功力，而水可竭也。循拙決沖，而經可通也。此言氣之滑澀，血水清濁，行之逆順也。 Yellow Emperor said: I want to hear about the nature of things. Guo Bo said: When it comes to water, one does not rely on strength but water can be exhausted; when it comes to clay, one does not rely on skill but a pot can be made. This talk discusses the smoothness and subtlety of Qi, the clarity and turbidity of blood and water, and the reversal of order and chaos in action.

    3. 黃帝曰：願聞人之白黑肥瘦小長，各有數乎？歧伯曰：年質壯大，血氣充盈，膚革堅固，因加以邪，刺此者，深而留之，此肥人也。廣肩腋項，肉薄厚皮而黑色，唇臨臨然，其血黑以濁，其氣澀以遲。其為人也，貪于取與，刺此者，深而留之，多益其數也。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the variations of people's complexions such as white, black, fat, thin, small, and long. Kuai Bo replied: People with robust constitution, rich blood and qi, firm skin, and are prone to evil influences. Those who practice acupuncture on them, insert needles deep and leave them in, resulting in a fat person. They have broad shoulders, arms, and thick skin, black complexion, their blood is black and turbid, and their qi is sluggish. Their nature is greedy for material possessions, and those who practice acupuncture on them insert needles deep and leave them in, which will increase their number (or amount).

    4. 黃帝曰：刺瘦人奈何？歧伯曰：瘦人者，皮薄色少，肉廉廉然，薄唇輕言，其血清氣滑，易脫于氣，易損于血，刺此者，淺而疾之。 Yellow Emperor asked: How can one reduce a person? Master Qi replied: A person who is reduced has thin skin and pale complexion, sparse hair, their flesh is meager, lips are thin, their blood is clear and qi smooth. It is easy to lose this condition through qi and easy to be harmed through blood. To pierce this condition, it should be shallow and quick.

    5. 黃帝曰：刺常人奈何？歧伯曰：視其白黑，各為調之，其端正敦厚者，其血氣和調，刺此者，無失常數也。 Yellow Emperor asked: What about a person with white or black hair? Hui Bao replied: Observe the whiteness or blackness, adjust accordingly, those who are upright, well-mannered, and generous in character will have balanced blood and qi. Piercing such a person would not lead to any disorder

    6. 黃帝曰：刺壯士真骨者，奈何？歧伯曰：刺壯士真骨，堅肉緩節，監監然，此人重則氣澀血濁，刺此者，深而留之，多益其數；勁則氣滑血清，刺此者，淺而疾之。 Yellow Emperor said: What if you are piercing a noble man with true bones? Huang Bo said: When piercing a noble man with true bones, the flesh is tough and the joints are rigid. If this person is heavy, the Qi will stagnate and the blood will be turbid. When piercing such a place, it is best to go deep and leave it in, which will increase its effectiveness. If there is strength, the Qi will be slippery and the blood will be clear. When piercing such a place, it is best to go shallow and act quickly.

    7. 黃帝曰：刺嬰兒奈何？歧伯曰：嬰兒者，其肉脆，血少氣弱，刺此者，以豪刺，淺刺而疾拔鍼，日再可也。 Yellow Emperor said: What about piercing an infant? Huchiobu replied: An infant's flesh is tender, blood is scarce, and vital energy is weak. If you pierce it, do so boldly, shallowly, and quickly remove the needle; it can be done repeatedly each day

    8. 黃帝曰：臨深決水，奈何？歧伯曰：血清氣濁，疾寫之則氣竭焉。黃帝曰：循拙決沖，奈何？歧伯曰：血濁氣澀，疾寫之，則經可通也。 Yellow Emperor said: What to do when facing deep waters? Qi Bo said: When the body's fluids are turbid and the breath is muddled, if one writes rapidly, it will exhaust the breath. Yellow Emperor said: What to do when facing choppy waters? Qi Bo said: When the fluids are turbid and the breath is sluggish, if one writes rapidly, the channels can be opened.

    9. 黃帝曰：脈行之逆順，奈何？歧伯曰：手之三陰，從藏走手；手之三陽，從手走頭；足之三陽，從頭走足；足之三陰，從足走腹。 Yellow Emperor asked: How does one interpret the flow of pulse? Hua Tuo replied: For the three Yin in the hand, follow the course from the Cun to the Hegu; for the three Yang in the hand, follow the course from the Hegu to the Benshen; for the three Yang in the foot, follow the course from the Benshen to the foot; for the three Yin in the foot, follow the course from the foot to the abdomen.

    10. 黃帝曰：少陰之脈獨下行，何也？歧伯曰：不然，夫沖脈者，五藏六府之海也，五藏六府皆稟焉。其上者，出于頏顙，滲諸陽，灌諸精；其下者，注少陰之大絡，出于氣街，循陰股內廉入膕中，伏行骭骨內，下至內踝之後屬而別。其下者，並于少陰之經，滲三陰；其前者，伏行出跗屬，下循跗，入大指間，滲諸絡而溫肌肉。故別絡結則附上不動，不動則厥，厥則寒矣。黃帝曰：何以明之？歧伯曰：以言導之，切而驗之，其非必動，然後仍可明逆順之行也。黃帝曰：窘乎哉！聖人之為道也。明于日月，微于毫釐，其非夫子，孰能道之也。 Yellow Emperor asked: Why does the Shao Yin vessel move downwards? Kuang Bo answered: This is not the case, for the Chong vessel is the sea of the Five Zang and Six Fu organs. All five zang and six fu originate from this sea. The upper part comes out from the lips and irrigates Yang; the lower part connects to the Shao Yin's major channel, issuing from Qi Street, follows the inside of the Yin canal, enters the Mingmen, sinks and runs through the Huan Gong, and finally terminates at the rear of the navel. The lower part is also in the Shao Yin channel, irrigating the three Yin; the front part hides and runs out from the tip of the toes, loops around the toes, enters the interspace between the great and second fingers, and irrigates the tendons and muscles. Hence, the branch vessels separate and attach to the upper Jue Ding, which does not move, thus causing Yin, and resulting in coldness. Yellow Emperor said: How can it be explained? Kuang Bo replied: By guiding with words and verifying through cutting, if it does not necessarily move, then it can still clarify whether its course is reversed or correct. Yellow Emperor exclaimed: What a difficult matter! This is the work of saints. Clear as day and night, subtle as hair tips; who can understand the way?
