    小鍼解

    1. 所謂易陳者，易言也。難入者，難著于人也。麤守形者，守刺法也。上守神者，守人之血氣有餘不足可補寫也。神客者，正邪共會也。神者，正氣也，客者邪氣也。在門者，邪循正氣之所出入也。未睹其疾者，先知邪正何經之疾也。惡知其原者，先知何經之病所取之處也。 Regarding the ease of explanation, it is easy because what is difficult to enter is also difficult to explain to people. For those who guard forms, they guard the needling technique. Those who guard spirits guard human life that cannot be supplemented with writing. Spirit guests are present at both right and wrong. The spirit is the right energy, and the guest is the evil energy. Those who stay by the door follow the right and wrong energy as it enters and exits. Not discerning its speed, the diviner does not know the speed of right and wrong energy. Recognizing its root cause, the diviner knows the location where right and wrong energy are taken from.

    2. 刺之微在數遲者，徐疾之意也。麤守關者，守四肢而不知血氣正邪之往來也。上守機者，知守氣也。機之動不離其空中者，知氣之虛實，用鍼之徐疾也。空中之機，清淨以微者，鍼以得氣，密意守氣勿失也。其來不可逢者，氣盛不可補也。其往不可追者，氣虛不可寫也。不可掛以髮者，言氣易失也。扣之不發者，言不知補寫之意也。血氣已盡而氣不下也。 The swiftness of a needle is like the speed of a arrow. Guarding the gate is like guarding the four limbs without knowing whether qi flows properly in and out. To guard machinery means to know how to guard qi. When machinery moves, but does not leave its empty space, one knows the difference between real and false qi, and uses needles with speed that is neither too fast nor too slow. The emptiness of machinery is clean and subtle; needles can reach the qi, sealing and guarding it so as not to lose it. When it arrives and cannot be met, qi is too abundant to supplement. When it departs and cannot be pursued, lost qi cannot be replenished. Do not hang it with hair, for qi can easily be lost. If the needle does not emit a sound, one does not know the intention of repairing or adding more. Blood and qi have been completely expended; the qi will not descend.

    3. 知其往來者，知氣之逆順盛虛也。要與之期者，知氣之可取之時也。麤之闇者，冥冥不知氣之微密也。妙哉！工獨有之者，盡知鍼意也。往者為逆者，言氣之虛而小，小者逆也。來者為順者，言形氣之平，平者順也。明知逆順正行無間者，言知所取之處也。迎而奪之者，寫也；追而濟之者，補也。 One who knows the coming and going of energy knows the reversal, orderliness, prosperity, and vacuity thereof. One who understands the appropriate time to act knows when to take advantage of energy. He who does not understand it is ignorant of the subtlety of energy. How wondrous! Only a craftsman fully understands the intention. Going with the reversal, one speaks of the energy as small and insignificant; coming with the orderliness, one speaks of the energy as balanced and harmonious. To know that there is no separation between reversal and orderliness, one can take advantage of it. By seizing it, one writes; by following after, one supplements.

    4. 所謂虛則實之者，氣口虛而當補之也。滿則泄之者，氣口盛而當寫之也。宛陳則除之者，去血脈也。邪勝則虛之者，言諸經有盛者，皆寫其邪也。徐而疾則實者，言徐內而疾出也。疾而徐則虛者，言疾內而徐出也。言實與虛若有若無者，言實者有氣，虛者無氣也。察後與先若亡若存者，言氣之虛實，補寫之先後也，察其氣之已下與常存也。為虛為實，若得若失者，言補者佖然若有得也，寫則怳然若有失也。 Regarding those who claim that empty becomes real, the mouth should be supplemented with breath when it is empty. When full, release it; when full and swollen, constrain it with breath. For those who claim to expel Qi and blood, this means they have lost blood. When evil prevails in those who claim emptiness, those who are proficient in the classics speak of abundance. Those who are swift within and slow without claim reality, while those who are slow within and swift without claim emptiness. Speaking of reality and emptiness as if they exist or do not exist, reality has breath while emptiness lacks it. In examining past and present as if they are lost or existent, the reality or vacuity of the breath is in supplementing or releasing, and examines whether the breath has already settled and persists constantly. To create emptiness and reality, to gain or lose, speaking of those who supplement it implies that they have gained it, while speaking of those who expel it suggests they may have lost it.

    5. 夫氣之在脈也，邪氣在上者，言邪氣之中人也高，故邪氣在上也。濁氣在中者，言水穀皆入于胃，其精氣上注于肺，濁溜于腸胃，言寒溫不適，飲食不節，而病生于腸胃，故命曰濁氣在中也。清氣在下者，言清濕地氣之中人也，必從足始，故曰清氣在下也。鍼陷脈，則邪氣出者取之上，鍼中脈則濁氣出者，取之陽明合也。鍼太深則邪氣反沉者，言淺浮之病，不欲深刺也。深則邪氣從之入，故曰反沉也。皮肉筋脈各有所處者，言經絡各有所主也。取五脈者死，言病在中氣不足，但用鍼盡大寫其諸陰之脈也。取三陽之脈者，唯言盡寫三陽之氣，令病人恇然不復也。奪陰者死，言取尺之五裏五往者也。奪陽者狂，正言也。 The Qi is in the pulse, evil Qi is on top. Therefore, evil Qi is on top. When Dampness is in the center, it means that all grains enter the stomach, and the refined Qi rises to the lungs, while Dampness lingers in the intestines and stomach, indicating cold and heat not being suitable, improper eating and drinking, and illness arising in the intestines and stomach. Thus it is called Dampness in the center. When Clear Qi is at the bottom, it means that the Qi of clear dampness is in a healthy person, and it must start from the foot, so it is said that Clear Qi is at the bottom. In piercing the pulse, take out the evil Qi from above; when piercing the Conception Vessel, take out the Dampness Qi from the Yang Brightness meeting point. If you pierce too deep, then the evil Qi will sink, indicating shallow and floating diseases, and not wanting to pierce deeply. Too deep, and the evil Qi will enter in, so it is called sinking. Each has a specific location for the skin, muscle, and vessel pulses, meaning that each meridian has its own master. Taking the five pulses when dying means illness lies in the central Qi insufficiency, but use the lancet to write all the Yin pulses. Taking the three Yang pulse means completely writing the Yang Qi, making the patient unconscious. Robbing Yin means death, referring to taking the Sinew Vessel's five places of five goings. Robbing Yang means madness, indicating correct meaning.

    6. 睹其色，察其目，知其散復，一其形，聽其動靜者，言上工知相五色於目。有知調尺寸小大緩急滑澀以言所病也。知其邪正者，知論虛邪與正邪之風也。右主推之，左持而御之者，言持鍼而出入也。氣至而去之者，言補寫氣調而去之也。調氣在于終始一者，持心也。節之交三百六十五會者，絡脈之滲灌諸節者也。 Examining its color, observing its shape, knowing its rise and disappearance, discerning its motion and stillness - these are the skills of one who understands the five colors in the eye. Knowing harmony, size, small or large, smooth or abrupt, slippery or tautness - these are the faults of speech. Knowing whether it is correct or not, knowing whether it is true or false, right or wrong - these are the signs of a doctor who uses questions and listens to the patient. The master guides from the right, while holding it from the left - this refers to the use of a lancet in and out. When the Qi arrives, it leaves - this refers to supplementing and adjusting Qi and leaving it. Balancing Qi is based on the beginning and end, and holding the heart. Regulating Qi is in accordance with the three hundred and sixty-five junctions - this regulates the meridians' nourishment and irrigation of the junctions.

    7. 所謂五藏之氣，已絕于內者，脈口氣內絕不至，反取其外之病處，與陽經之合，有留鍼以致陽氣，陽氣至則內重竭，重竭則死矣。其死也，無氣以動，故靜。所謂五藏之氣，已絕于外者，脈口氣外絕不至，反取其四末之輸，有留鍼以致其陰氣，陰氣至則陽氣反入，入則逆，逆則死矣。其死也，陰氣有餘，故躁。 The so-called five viscera's Qi has been completely exhausted within, and the Qi at the pulse points does not reach the outside; instead, taking the outer disease locations and merging with the Yang Meridian, needle retention causes the Yang Qi to arrive, and when the Yang Qi arrives, it leads to internal depletion, which ultimately results in death. Death occurs because there is no Qi to move, so it is still. When the so-called five viscera's Qi has been completely exhausted outside, the Qi at the pulse points does not reach the outside; instead, taking the four extremities as outlets and needle retention leads to the Yin Qi, the Yin Qi arrives when the Yang Qi reverses, which then leads to inversion, ultimately resulting in death. Death occurs because there is remaining Yin Qi, so it is agitated.

    8. 所以察其目者，五藏使五色循明。循明則聲章。聲章者，則言聲與平生異也。 To understand the meaning, one must examine its intention. The five organs revolve to bring clarity to the five colors. Clarity gives rise to sounds and words. Sounds and words are different from the ordinary sounds of life
