    論疾診尺

    1. 黃帝問于歧伯曰：余欲無視色持脈，獨調其尺，以言其病，從外知內，為之奈何？歧伯曰：審其尺之緩急小大滑澀，肉之堅脆，而病形定矣。 The Yellow Emperor asked Guan Pu: I want to examine the pulse without regarding color and observe it with the divining rod, in order to discern the disease and know internal from external, what should I do? Guan Pu replied: Consider the pulse's degree of fullness and urgency, its smallness or largeness, its smoothness or roughness, and the pulse's strength, which will define the form of the disease.

    2. 視人之目窠上微癰，如新臥起狀，其頸脈動，時咳，按其手足上，窅而不起者，風水膚脹也。 Looking at a person's eyes, if they have a slight flicker like one who has just woken up, with a throbbing pulse in the throat, sometimes coughing, and pressing on their hands or feet does not make them wake up, it is an indication of weak Qi and Blood.

    3. 尺膚滑，其淖澤者，風也。尺肉弱者，解併，安臥脫肉者，寒熱，不治。尺膚滑而澤脂者，風也。尺膚澀者，風痺也。尺膚麤如枯魚之鱗者，水泆飲也。尺膚熱甚，脈盛躁者，病溫也，其脈甚而滑者，病且出也。尺膚寒，其脈小者，泄、少氣。尺膚炬然，先熱後寒者，寒熱也；尺膚先寒，久大之而熱者，亦寒熱也。 The skin is smooth, and the fluid on it comes from the wind. The flesh is weak, a combination of heat and cold, and one who undresses while feeling heat or cold cannot be cured. Smooth skin with oiliness indicates the presence of wind. Dry, shriveled skin is due to dampness in drinking water. Skin that is heated excessively with throbbing pulse indicates illness from the environment. The person has an excessive and slippery pulse when the skin is hot. When it is cold and the pulse is small, this suggests a discharge of heat or deficiency of qi. Skin that glows fiercely, then cools down suggests that it is due to an alternating condition of heat and cold. When the skin first feels cold and then heated later, this also indicates a heat-cold condition.

    4. 肘所獨熱者，腰以上熱；手所獨熱者，腰以下熱。肘前獨熱者，膺前熱；肘後獨熱者，肩背熱。臂中獨熱者，腰腹熱；肘後麤以下三四寸熱者，腸中有蟲。掌中熱者，腹中熱；掌中寒者，腹中寒。魚上白肉有青血脈者，胃中有寒。 Whoever has a sole heat in the elbow, has heat above the waist; whoever has a sole heat in the hand, has heat below the waist. Whoever has a sole heat at the front of the elbow, has the front of the chest hot; whoever has a sole heat at the back of the elbow, has the shoulder blade and back hot. Whoever has a sole heat in the middle of the arm, has waist and abdomen heat; whoever has heat at the back of the elbow down to the third or fourth inch, the intestines have worms. Whoever has heat in the palm, the abdomen is hot; whoever has cold in the palm, the abdomen is cold. If there is white meat on the fish with a blue blood vessel, the stomach has cold.

    5. 尺炬然熱，人迎大者，當奪血；尺堅大，脈小甚，少氣，免有加，立死。 When the pulse is large and rapid, people will lose blood; when the pulse is hard and big, the pulse is small and weak, with little breath, one cannot avoid dying.

    6. 目赤色者病在心，白在肺，青在肝，黃在脾，黑在腎。黃色不可名者，病在胸中。 Colors corresponding to organs in traditional Chinese medicine: Red represents the heart, white represents the lungs, green represents the liver, yellow represents the spleen, and black represents the kidneys. The color that cannot be named corresponds to a disease in the chest.

    7. 診目痛，赤脈從上下者，太陽病；從下上者，陽明病；從外走內者，少陽病。 Upon examining the pain, if the pulsating redness moves from above to below, it is a disease of the Great Yang; if it moves from below to above, it is a disease of the Bright Yang; if it spreads outwards and gradually invades inwards, it is a disease of the Lesser Yang.

    8. 診寒熱，赤脈上下至瞳子，見一脈一歲死；見一脈半，一歲半死；見二脈，二歲死；見二脈半，二歲半死；見三脈，三歲死。 Diagnose cold or heat, the pulse feels rapid and strong at the wrist, seeing one pulse indicates a person will die in a year; seeing half a pulse indicates a person will almost die in a year; seeing two pulses indicates a person will die in two years; seeing two halves of a pulse indicates a person will almost die in two years; seeing three pulses indicates a person will die in three years.

    9. 診齲齒痛，按其陽之來，有過者獨熱，在左左熱，在右右熱，在上上熱，在下下熱。 Examining toothache, according to its Yang Qi circulation, those who have excess heat will have independent fever, left-sided fever, right-sided fever, upper fever, and lower fever.

    10. 診血脈者，多赤多熱，多青多痛，多黑為久痺，多赤、多黑、多青皆見者，寒熱。 The practitioner of pulse diagnosis, often sees red and hot, green and painful, black for stagnation, and red, black, and green all indicate cold and heat.

    11. 身痛而色微黃，齒垢黃，爪甲上黃，黃疸也。安臥小便黃赤，脈小而澀者不嗜食。 The individual has pale skin and is yellowish all over, with yellow teeth and nails. There are also yellow spots. When lying down and urinating, the urine is yellow or red. If the pulse is small and weak, this person does not like to eat.

    12. 人病，其寸口之脈，與人迎之脈小大等，及其浮沉等者，病難已也。 If someone is sick, compare the pulse at their wrist with the pulse of a healthy person: if it is larger or smaller and if it rises and falls differently, then there is difficulty in treating the illness.

    13. 女子手少陰脈動甚者妊子。 A girl with weak hands and moving Yin channels indicates pregnancy.

    14. 嬰兒病，其頭毛皆逆上者必死。耳間青脈起者掣痛。大便赤瓣飧泄，脈小者，手足寒，難已；飧泄，脈小，手足溫，泄易也。 Infant illness, if the hair on its head grows upside down, it will surely die. Reddish pulses in the ears will cause pain. If the stool is red and flows out easily, the pulse is weak, with cold hands and feet, difficult to recover; if it flows out easily, the pulse is weak, and the hands and feet are warm, easy to recover.

    15. 四時之變，寒暑之勝，重陰必陽，重陽必陰；故陰主寒，陽主熱，故寒甚則熱，熱甚則寒，故曰寒生熱，熱生寒，此陰陽之變也。 The four seasons, the dominance of hot and cold, heavy yin leads to light yang, and heavy yang leads to light yin; therefore, yin governs cold and yang governs heat. When cold is excessive, it gives rise to heat; when heat is excessive, it gives rise to cold. Therefore, it is said that cold generates heat, and heat generates cold - this is the change of yin and yang.

    16. 故曰：冬傷於寒，春生病熱；春傷於風，夏生飧泄腸僻，夏傷於暑，秋生痎瘧；秋傷於濕，冬生咳嗽。是謂四時之序也。 It is said: In winter, one suffers from cold and in spring, one gets sick with fever; in spring, one is harmed by wind, and in summer, one experiences diarrhea, weak limbs, and dizziness. In summer, one suffers from heat, in autumn, one gets ill from dampness, and in winter, one has a cough. This is the order of the four seasons.
