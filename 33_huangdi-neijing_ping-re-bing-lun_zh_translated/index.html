    評熱病論

    1: 黃帝問曰：有病溫者，汗出輒復熱，而脈躁疾不為汗衰，狂言不能食，病名為何。歧伯對曰：病名陰陽交，交者死也。帝曰：願聞其說。歧伯曰：人所以汗出者，皆生於穀，穀生於精，今邪氣交爭於骨肉而得汗者，是邪卻而精勝也，精勝，則當能食而不復熱。復熱者邪氣也，汗者精氣也，今汗出而輒復熱者，是邪勝也，不能食者，精無俾也，病而留者，其壽可立而傾也。且夫熱論曰：汗出而脈尚躁盛者死。今脈不與汗相應，此不勝其病也，其死明矣。狂言者是失志，失志者死。今見三死，不見一生，雖愈必死也。 Emperor asked: A person has a fever with profuse sweating and is restless and agitated, unable to eat, what is the name of this disease? Qi Bo replied: The name of this disease is yin-yang intersection, and intersection means death. Emperor said: I wish to hear your explanation. Qi Bo said: Sweat comes from grain, grain comes from essence. Now, pathogenic qi is struggling in the bones and flesh, causing sweat to appear, which means that the pathogenic factor has overcome the essence. If the essence wins, one will be able to eat without fever again. But if there is fever again, it means that the pathogenic factor has overcome, one cannot eat and is near death. It is said in the theory of fevers: Sweating and a full and rapid pulse indicates a dangerous situation. In this case, the pulse is not responding to the sweat, meaning it does not overcome the disease, so death is inevitable. If one is speaking madly, it means they have lost their mind, and losing one's mind leads to death. Now I see three causes of death but no cause of life; even if the condition improves, it must lead to death

    2: 帝曰：有病身熱汗出煩滿，煩滿不為汗解，此為何病。歧伯曰：汗出而身熱者，風也，汗出而煩滿不解者，厥也，病名曰風厥。帝曰：願卒聞之。歧伯曰：巨陽主氣，故先受邪，少陰與其為表裏也，得熱則上從之，從之則厥也。帝曰：治之柰何。歧伯曰：表裏刺之，飲之服湯。 The Emperor said: There is a disease with a fever, sweating, and discomfort. The discomfort does not alleviate with sweating; what kind of illness is this? [Kuo] Tu said: When there is sweating and a fever, it is Wind; when the discomfort does not resolve, it is Deficiency (Yin); this disease is called Wind Deficiency. Emperor said: I wish to hear more about it. [Kuo] Tu said: The Yang Ming governs the Qi, so it receives evil first and the Shao Yin and it form the exterior and interior. If it becomes hot, it will ascend along with it, causing Deficiency. Emperor said: How should we treat it? [Kuo] Tu said: Puncture and bleed the exterior and interior, and administer broth to take medicine

    3: 帝曰：勞風為病何如。歧伯曰：勞風法在肺下，其為病也，使人強上冥視，唾出若涕，惡風而振寒，此為勞風之病。帝曰：治之柰何。歧伯曰：以救俛仰。巨陽引精者三日，中年者五日，不精者七日，欬出青黃涕，其狀如膿，大如彈丸，從口中若鼻中出，不出則傷肺，傷肺則死也。 The Emperor asked: What is the illness caused by laboring in the wind? Hui Bao replied: The illness from laboring in the wind is located below the lungs, causing people to become short of breath and have a strong desire to look up at ghosts. They also produce saliva like tears, and the cold wind causes them to shiver. This is the disease caused by laboring in the wind. The Emperor asked: How do you treat it? Hui Bao replied: Use the method of raising the head slightly. Those who are strong can do it for three days, middle-aged people for five days, and those who are not strong should do it for seven days. When they spit out green and yellow saliva, their condition will be like a boil, as large as a thrown pulse, and it will come out of the mouth like a nosebleed. If it does not come out, it will injure the lungs, and if the lungs are injured, they will die

    4: 帝曰：有病腎風者，面胕痝然壅，害於言，可刺不。歧伯曰：虛不當刺，不當刺而刺，後五日其氣必至。帝曰：其至何如。歧伯曰：至必少氣時熱，時熱從胸背上至頭，汗出，手熱，口乾苦渴，小便黃，目下腫，腹中鳴，身重難以行，月事不來，煩而不能食，不能正偃，正偃則欬，病名曰風水，論在刺法中。帝曰：願聞其說。歧伯曰：邪之所湊，其氣必虛，陰虛者，陽必湊之，故少氣時熱而汗出也。小便黃者，少腹中有熱也。不能正偃者，胃中不和也。正偃則欬甚，上迫肺也。諸有水氣者，微腫先見於目下也。帝曰：何以言。歧伯曰：水者陰也，目下亦陰也，腹者至陰之所居，故水在腹者，必使目下腫也。真氣上逆，故口苦舌乾，臥不得正偃，正偃則欬出清水也。諸水病者，故不得臥，臥則驚，驚則欬甚也。腹中鳴者，病本於胃也。薄脾則煩不能食，食不下者，胃脘隔也。身重難以行者，胃脈在足也。月事不來者，胞脈閉也，胞脈者屬心而絡於胞中，今氣上迫肺，心氣不得下通，故月事不來也。帝曰：善。 Emperor said: There is a case of the patient with kidney disease, dizziness and headache. It would be better not to acupuncture. Qi Bo said: Vacuity should not be acupunctured, but if it has to be acupunctured, by the fifth day, the qi will definitely arrive. Emperor asked: What will happen when it arrives? Qi Bo said: When it arrives, the patient will have a deficiency of qi and fever, fever starts from the back and gradually moves towards the head, sweating, palms become hot, mouth becomes dry and thirsty, urine is yellow, eyes are swollen, there is a sound in the abdomen, the body feels heavy and difficult to move, menstruation does not come, the patient is irritable and cannot eat. This disease is called wind water, and it belongs to acupuncture treatment. Emperor said: I wish to hear your explanation. Qi Bo said: The pathogenic qi gathers, and its qi must be deficient, and if yin is deficient, yang will attack, so there is a deficiency of qi and fever with sweating. Yellow urine means that there is heat in the lower abdomen. Not being able to lie down properly means that the stomach is not harmonious. When lying down, the hiccups become more severe, which means that the lung is being pressed. All water-related symptoms are first seen as swelling below the eyes. Emperor asked: How do you know? Qi Bo said: Water is yin, and the area below the eyes is also yin. The lower abdomen is where yin resides, so when there is water in the lower abdomen, it causes swelling below the eyes. True qi rises upwards, causing the mouth to be dry and the tongue to be parched, making it difficult to lie down, and when lying down, hiccups bring out clear water. All water-related diseases cause difficulty in lying down, which leads to hiccups becoming more severe. A sound in the abdomen indicates that the disease is located in the stomach. The spleen is weak, causing irritability and not being able to eat. Difficulty in walking and carrying a heavy body are due to kidney qi in the foot. Menstruation does not come because the uterine vessel is closed. The uterine vessel belongs to the heart and is connected to the uterus, so today the qi rises and presses the lung, preventing heart qi from flowing down and causing menstruation to not come. Emperor said: Good
