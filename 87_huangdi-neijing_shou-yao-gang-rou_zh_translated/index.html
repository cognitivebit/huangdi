    壽夭剛柔

    1. 黃帝問於少師曰：余聞人之生也，有剛有柔，有弱有強，有短有長，有陰有陽，願聞其方。 Emperor Yellow asked the Master, I have heard that people are born with qualities such as strength and weakness, shortness and tallness, yin and yang. Can you please explain how these come into existence?

    2. 少師答曰：陰中有陰，陽中有陽，審知陰陽，刺之有方。得病所始，刺之有理。謹度病端，與時相應。內合於五藏六府，外合於筋骨皮膚。是故內有陰陽，外亦有陰陽。在內者，五藏為陰，六府為陽，在外者，筋骨為陰，皮膚為陽。故曰，病在陰之陰者，刺陰之滎俞，病在陽之陽者，刺陽之合，病在陽之陰者，刺陰之經，病在陰之陽者，刺絡脈。故曰，病在陽者名曰風，病在陰者名曰痺，陰陽俱病名曰風痺。病有形而不痛者，陽之類也；無形而痛者，陰之類也。無形而痛者，其陽完而陰傷之也。急治其陰，無攻其陽。有形而不痛者，其陰完而陽傷之也。急治其陽，無攻其陰。陰陽俱動，乍有形，乍無形，加以煩心，命曰陰勝其陽。此謂不表不裏，其形不久。 The master replied: Yin within Yin, Yang within Yang, discerning the Yin and Yang, acupuncture has methods. Once sickness begins, there is reason for acupuncture. Be cautious in treating the illness, correspond with the time. Internally unify the Five Zang and Six Fu, externally unify the tendons, bones, and skin. Therefore, internally there are Yin and Yang, and externally there are also Yin and Yang. What is inside, the Five Zang represent Yin, and the Six Fu represent Yang; what is outside, tendons, bones, and skin represent Yin, and skin represents Yang. Therefore, it says: If sickness is in the Yin of Yin, acupuncture at the Ling-Ju, if sickness is in the Yang of Yang, acupuncture at the He, if sickness is in the Yang of Yin, acupuncture at the Jing, and if sickness is in the Yin of Yang, acupuncture at the Luo-Vessels. Therefore, it says that sickness in Yang is called Wind, sickness in Yin is called Stagnation, and Yin and Yang together are called Wind-Stagnation. Sickness has form but no pain is of the Yang type; formless but painful is of the Yin type. Formless but painful means the Yang is complete and the Yin is damaged. Urgently treat the Yin without attacking the Yang. Formed but no pain means the Yin is complete and the Yang is damaged. Urgently treat the Yang without attacking the Yin. Yin and Yang moving, sometimes having form and sometimes formless, cause anxiety and are called Yin overpowering Yang. This refers to not showing on the outside, and the form will not last long.

    3. 黃帝問於伯高曰：余聞形氣之病先後，外內之應奈何？伯高答曰：風寒傷形，憂恐忿怒傷氣；氣傷藏，乃病藏，寒傷形，乃應形；風傷筋脈，筋脈乃應。此形氣外內之相應也。 Yellow Emperor asked Bo Gao, I have heard about diseases related to form and qi, as well as external and internal correspondences. How do they manifest? Bo Gao answered, Cold and heat damage the form, and fear and anxiety damage the qi; qi damage is the disease's root, cold damages the form, which is a response to the form; wind damages the channels and vessels, and channels and vessels are responses. This is an explanation of the correspondence between external and internal manifestations of diseases related to form and qi.

    4. 黃帝曰：刺之奈何？伯高答曰：病九日者，三刺而已；病一月者，十刺而已；多少遠近，以此衰之。久痺不去身者，視其血絡，盡出其血。 Yellow Emperor said: How shall I prick it? Duke Gao replied: If the illness lasts nine days, prick three times; if it lasts a month, prick ten times. The degree of distance depends on this to weaken it. If one has chronic paralysis that does not go away, observe their blood vessels and completely let out their blood.

    5. 黃帝曰：外內之病，難易之治奈何？伯高答曰：形先病而未入藏者，刺之半其日。藏先病而形乃應者，刺之倍其日。此月內難易之應也。 Yellow Emperor asked, What about diseases affecting the outer and inner parts? Which are difficult to treat? Master Gao replied, For a disease that affects the outer form before it enters the interior, puncture it halfway through the day. For a disease that affects the interior before it manifests in the form, puncture it twice a day. This is the difficult aspect of treating diseases internally.

    6. 黃帝問於伯高曰：余聞形有緩急，氣有盛衰，骨有大小，肉有堅脆，皮有厚薄，其以立壽夭奈何？伯高答曰：形與氣相任則壽，不相任則夭。皮與肉相果則壽，不相果則夭，血氣經絡勝形則壽，不勝形則夭。 The Yellow Emperor asked Bo Gao, I have heard that things have a time to flourish and a time to decline, bodies have periods of strength and weakness, bones come in different sizes, flesh can be hard or brittle, skin can be thick or thin. What determines whether one has long life or short?

    Bo Gao answered, Form and Qi must complement each other to sustain life, if they do not complement each other, there will be an untimely death. Skin and flesh must harmonize with each other for a long life, if they do not harmonize, there will be an untimely death. If the channels and vessels of Qi are in control over the form, one will sustain life, if not, there will be an untimely death.

    1. 黃帝曰：何謂形之緩急？伯高答曰：形充而皮膚緩者則壽，形充而皮膚急者則夭，形充而脈堅大者順也，形充而脈小以弱者氣衰，衰則危矣。若形充而顴不起者骨小，骨小則夭矣。形充而大肉胭堅而有分者肉堅，肉堅則壽矣；形充而大肉無分理不堅者肉脆，肉脆則夭矣。此天之生命，所以立形定氣而視壽夭者，必明乎此立形定氣，而後以臨病人，決生死。 Yellow Emperor said: What does it mean by the harmony of form? Duke Gao replied: If one's form is full and the skin is soft, then there will be longevity. If one's form is full but the skin is tense, then one will perish. If one's form is full and the pulse is firm and large, then it proceeds smoothly; if one's form is full but the pulse is small and weak, then vital energy declines, and decline means danger. If the form is full but the cheek does not rise, then the bones are small, and being small means perishing. If one's form is full and the flesh is thick and firm with divisions, then there will be longevity; if one's form is full but the flesh is without division and not firm, then it will perish. This is the way of heaven's life force, which establishes the foundation of form and determines vital energy based on longevity and perishing. By following this method, one can determine life or death for sick people.

    2. 黃帝曰：余聞壽夭，無以度之。伯高答曰：牆基卑，高不及其地者，不滿三十而死。其有因加疾者，不及二十而死也。 Yellow Emperor said: I have heard that people die of old age and untimely deaths. Master Bo Gao replied: If the foundation of a wall is weak, and it's height does not reach its base, one will die before the age of thirty. If there are additional illnesses causing death, one may die before the age of twenty.

    3. 黃帝曰：形氣之相勝，以立壽夭奈何？伯高答曰：平人而氣勝形者壽；病而形肉脫，氣勝形者死，形勝氣者危矣。 Yellow Emperor asked: If the appearance of Qi surpasses that of form, how can one establish longevity and avoid perishing? Master K'ao replied: One who is healthy with Qi superior to form will live a long life; but when one is ill and the flesh and skin are shed due to disease, even if the form prevails over Qi, it is dangerous.

    4. 黃帝曰：余聞刺有三變，何謂三變？伯高答曰：有刺營者，有刺衛者，有刺寒痺之留經者。 Yellow Emperor said: I have heard that there are three kinds of acupuncture. What are the three kinds? Bo Gao replied: There are acupuncturists who treat military injuries, those who treat guard injuries, and those who treat cold paralysis by needling the meridians.

    5. 黃帝曰：刺三變者奈何？伯高答曰：刺營者出血，刺衛者出氣，刺寒痺者內熱。 Yellow Emperor asked: What about the one who changes three times? Master Bo Gao answered: The one who stabs the camp will bleed, the one who stabs the guards will exhale, and the one who stabs the cold paralysis will have internal heat.

    6. 黃帝曰：營衛寒痺之為病奈何？伯高答曰：營之生病也，寒熱少氣，血上下行。衛之生病也，氣痛時來時去，怫愾賁響，風寒客於腸胃之中。寒痺之為病也，留而不去，時痛而皮不仁。 The Yellow Emperor asked, What is the nature of the illness caused by camp and guard? The Master of Baiyi replied, The illness caused by camp is due to cold and heat, with insufficient Qi and disrupted blood flow. The illness caused by guard is characterized by intermittent pain, accompanied by irritability and restlessness. The symptoms include abdominal cramping and pain, with the wind and cold lingering in the intestines.

    7. 黃帝曰：刺寒痺內熱奈何？伯高答曰：刺布衣者，以火焠之；刺大人者，以藥熨之。 Yellow Emperor asked: What if one is cold and has a numbness inside with heat? Master Gao replied: If you pierce the garment, burn it with fire; if you pierce the person of high rank, warm them with medicine.

    8. 黃帝曰：藥熨奈何？伯高答曰：用淳酒二十斤，蜀椒一斤，干姜一斤，桂心一斤，凡四種，皆嚼咀，漬酒中，用綿絮一斤，細白布四丈，並內酒中，置酒馬矢熅中，封涂封，勿使泄。五日五夜，出布綿絮曝乾之，干復潰，以盡其汁。每漬必晬其日，乃出乾。乾，並用滓與綿絮，復布為復巾，長六七尺，為六七巾，則用之生桑炭炙巾，以熨寒痺所刺之處，令熱入至於病所，寒復炙巾以熨之，三十遍而止。汗出以巾拭身，亦三十遍而止。起步內中，無見風。每刺必熨，如此病已矣。此所謂內熱也。 Yellow Emperor asked: How does one moxa-burn? Bo Gao replied: Use 20 pounds of pure wine, Sichuan peppercorns 1 pound, dried ginger 1 pound, and cinnamon bark 1 pound. All four kinds are chewed and soaked in the wine. Use a cotton ball of one pound and four rolls of white cloth to put inside the wine bottle and seal it tightly so that it doesn't leak. After five days and nights, dry the cotton ball and cloth outside, then let the wine evaporate completely. When it's dry, use a container with cassia bark and cotton balls, make a wrap about six or seven feet long, which makes six to seven wraps, then use them to heat the places where you were pricked for cold colic. Warm it up thoroughly so that the heat reaches the disease area, and use hot cassia bark and cotton balls to moxa again, 30 times in total. When you wipe your body with a cloth, do it 30 times until you finish. After walking inside, you won't see any wind. Whenever you get pricked, you must moxa, like this the disease will go away. This is what they call internal heat
