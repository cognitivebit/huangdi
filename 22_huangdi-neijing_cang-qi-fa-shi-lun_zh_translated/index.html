    藏氣法時論

    1. 黃帝問曰：合人形以法四時五行而治，何如而從，何如而逆，得失之意，願聞其事。歧伯對曰：五行者，金木水火土也，更貴更賤，以知死生，以決成敗，而定五藏之氣，間甚之時，死生之期也。帝曰：願卒聞之。歧伯曰：肝主春，足厥陰少陽主治，其日甲乙，肝苦急，急食甘以緩之。心主夏，手少陰太陽主治，其日丙丁，心苦緩，急食酸以收之。脾主長夏，足太陰陽明主治，其日戊己，脾苦濕，急食甘以緩之。心主夏，手少陰太陽主治，其日丙丁，心苦緩，急食酸以收之。脾主長夏，足太陰陽明主治，其日戊己，脾苦濕，急食苦以燥之。肺主秋，手太陰陽明主治，其日庚辛，肺苦氣上逆，急食苦以泄之。腎主冬，足少陰太陽主治，其日壬癸，腎苦燥，急食辛以潤之，開腠理，致津液，通氣也。 Yellow Emperor asked, Treating with the Five Phases of the Five Activities in order to regulate, what is it like following this approach and what is it like going against it? What is the intention of success or failure? I want to hear about this matter. The Duke answered, The Five Phases are gold, wood, water, fire, and earth. More exalted and more base, used to know life and death, used to determine success and failure, and to govern the five organs' energy, at a critical time, for life and death periods. Emperor said, I want to hear this quickly. The Duke said, The liver governs spring, the foot's yin and yang of the treatment govern treat, its days are yin and yang. The liver is bitter and urgent, eat sweet to moderate. The heart governs summer, the hand's yin of the great Yang govern treat, its days are bing and ding. The heart is bitter and slow, eat sour to collect. The spleen governs long summer, the foot's yin and Yang Ming of the treatment govern treat, its days are wood and metal. The spleen is bitter and damp, eat sweet to moderate. The heart governs summer, the hand's yin of the great Yang govern treat, its days are bing and ding. The heart is bitter and slow, eat sour to collect. The spleen governs long summer, the foot's yin and Yang Ming of the treatment govern treat, its days are wood and metal. The spleen is bitter and damp, eat bitter to dry. The lung governs autumn, the hand's yin and Yang Ming of the treatment govern treat, its days are metal and water. The lung is bitter and qi upward invasion, eat bitter to release. Kidney governs winter, the foot's yin of the great Yang govern treat, its days are water and wood. The kidney is bitter and dry, eat spicy to moisten, open channels, regulate fluid, pass gas.

    2. 病在肝，愈於夏，夏不愈，甚於秋，秋不死，持於冬，起於春，禁當風。肝病者，愈在丙丁，丙丁不愈，加於庚辛，庚辛不死，持於壬癸，起於甲乙。肝病者，平旦慧，下晡甚，夜半靜。肝欲散，急食辛以散之，用辛補之，酸寫之。 The illness is in the liver, cured in summer, but not cured in summer, much worse in autumn, not dying in autumn, sustained in winter, reviving in spring, prohibited by wind. For those with liver disease, they will be cured in yang meridian (Jupiter and Mars), but if not cured in Jupiter and Mars, reinforce with kidney meridian (Rabbit and Dragon), but if not dying, sustained in metal meridian (Water Rat and Water Ox), reviving in wood meridian (Tiger and Rabbit). For those with liver disease, the best time is at dawn, the most critical time is at noon, and the quietest time is midnight. If the liver desires to disperse, eat pungent food to disperse it and use pungent food to supplement it, and write sour to help it.

    3. 病在心，愈在長夏，長夏不愈，甚於冬，冬不死，持於春，起於夏，禁溫食熱衣。心病者，愈在戊己，戊己不愈，加於壬癸，壬癸不死，持於甲乙，起於丙丁。心病者，日中慧，夜半甚，平旦靜。心欲耎，急食鹹以耎之，用鹹補之，甘寫之。 The illness is in the heart, and it gets worse during the long summer. The long summer does not improve; rather, it's even worse than winter. Winter doesn't kill, and it continues into spring, starting anew in summer. To treat this condition, one should follow a regimen of moderate temperature, food, and clothing. For those with heart illnesses, recovery occurs during the years of Earth (Wu) and Metal (Jin), but if there is no recovery in Earth and Metal, it can be sustained in Yin Wood (Jia and Geng) and emerge in Yang Wood (Bing and Ding). A person with a heart condition will feel more acute symptoms during the daytime and have relief at midnight. At dawn, they should be quiet. If the heart is troubled, one should eat salty food to calm it, use salt as a supplement, and also consume sweet substances.

    4. 病在脾，愈在秋，秋不愈，甚於春，春不死，持於夏，起於長夏，禁溫食飽食濕地濡衣。脾病者，愈在庚辛，庚辛不愈，加於甲乙，甲乙不死，持於丙丁，起於戊己。脾病者，日昳慧，日出甚，下晡靜。脾欲緩，急食甘以緩之，用苦寫之，甘補之。 The illness is in the spleen, and recovery comes in autumn. If not recovered by autumn, it is more severe than spring, and if not cured by spring, it persists through summer, beginning in the long summer season. One must avoid hot and humid places, wear dry clothes, refrain from excessive eating and drinking, and maintain a temperate diet. For spleen diseases that do not recover in the period of Metal (Gengxin), add Wood (JiaYi) to the treatment, but if it still does not cure, continue with Fire (BingDing), starting in Soil (WuYin). Those who are ill should rise early and retire early. If the spleen is weak, eat sweet foods to strengthen it, use bitter foods to regulate it, and use sour foods as a supplement.

    5. 病在肺，愈在冬，冬不愈，甚於夏，夏不死，持於長夏，起於秋，禁寒飲食寒衣。肺病者，愈在壬癸，壬癸不愈，加於丙丁，丙丁不死，持於戊己，起於庚辛。肺病者，下晡慧，日中甚，夜半靜。肺欲收，急食酸以收之，用酸補之，辛寫之。 Lung disease, recovery in winter, if not recovered in winter, even worse than summer, and if not cured in summer, persists into the long summer, beginning in autumn. Treatment: prohibit cold drinks, food, and clothing. For lung disease, recovery in the years of Ren and Geng, if not cured in Ren and Geng, add the years of Bing and Ding, if not fatal, persist in Wu and Jia, starting in Geng and Xin. Lung patients should wake up early, be especially clear-headed during the day, and be quiet at midnight. To stop lung disease, eat sour food urgently to stop it, use sour foods to supplement it, and use herbs that promote digestion.

    6. 病在腎，愈在春，春不愈，甚於長夏，長夏不死，持於秋，起於冬，禁犯焠㶼熱食溫炙衣。腎病者，愈在甲乙，甲乙不愈，甚於戊己，戊己不死，持於庚辛，起於壬癸。腎病者，夜半慧，四季甚，下晡靜。腎欲堅，急食苦以堅之，用苦補之，鹹寫之。 The text translated to English: Kidney disease, recovery in spring, if not recovered in spring, it is more difficult than summer, if not dead in summer, it persists into autumn and begins in winter. Punish those who commit crimes of fire and heat, food, clothing. For kidney disease patients, recover in the yin months (Jia Yi), if not recovered in Jia Yi, it is more difficult in the metal months (Wu Xu), if not dead in Wu Xu, it persists into the water months (Geng Xin), and begins in the wood months (Ren Gui). Kidney disease patients are wise at midnight, difficult to cure in all seasons, and should wake up early. For kidney disease, eat bitter food urgently to strengthen it, use bitter medicine as a supplement, and write salt with it

    7. 夫邪氣之客於身也，以勝相加，至其所生而愈，至其所不勝而甚，至於所生而持，自得其位而起。必先定五藏之脈，乃可言間甚之時，死生之期也。 When evil qi enters the body, it increases by conquering the surface and becomes more severe when it is generated. When it becomes strong enough to be held, one will naturally attain its position and arise. Before being able to control the five viscera's meridians, one cannot speak of a critical time between life and death.

    8. 肝病者，兩脇下痛引少腹，令人善怒，虛則目䀮䀮無所見，耳無所聞，善恐如人將捕之，取其經，厥陰與少陽，氣逆，則頭痛耳聾不聰頰腫。取血者。 For patients with liver disease, pain in the lower flanks refers to the lower abdomen, causing anger and irritability. When it is deficient, the eyes cannot see and the ears cannot hear. They are easily frightened, as if someone is about to capture them. By taking the meridians, rein in the yin of the liver and the Yang of the stomach, with Qi reversal, there will be headache, deafness, dullness of the face, and swelling.

    9. 心病者，胸中痛，脇支滿，脇下痛，膺背肩甲間痛，兩臂內痛，虛則胸腹大，脇下與腰相引而痛，取其經者，少陰太陽，舌下血者。其變病，刺郄中血者。 For a patient with heart disease, there is pain in the chest, fullness in the flanks, lower abdominal pain, soreness between the shoulder blades and shoulders, inner arm pain. If it is empty, the chest and abdomen become enlarged, the lower abdomen and back are painful when pulled together. When seeking the meridian, it is related to the kidney Yang and red tongue with blood at the bottom. The transformation of disease is caused by bleeding in the Zangqiu point.

    10. 脾病者，身重善肌肉痿，足不收，行善瘈，腳下痛，虛則腹滿腸鳴，飱泄食不化，取其經，太陰陽明少陰血者。 For those with spleen disease, the body is heavy and has weak muscles, the feet do not recover, walking is difficult and painful, the soles of the feet hurt, when empty, the abdomen is full, the intestines are rumbling, food is not digested, their channels are the Yin and Yang of the Spleen with a deficiency of Yin blood.

    11. 肺病者，喘欬逆氣，肩背痛，汗出尻陰股膝髀腨胻足皆痛，虛則少氣不能報息，耳聾嗌乾，取其經，太陰足太陽之外厥陰內血者。 The patient with lung disease experiences wheezing, shortness of breath, and difficulty in breathing. They may also experience pain in the shoulders and back, excessive sweating, and pain in the hip joints, buttocks, thighs, waist, and feet. If the condition worsens, they may not be able to produce enough vital energy to maintain respiration, leading to deafness or tinnitus. The meridian associated with this condition is the Lung Meridian, which connects to the Large Intestine Meridian through the exterior and interior Yin channels, affecting internal blood flow.

    12. 腎病者，腹大脛腫，喘欬身重，寢汗出憎風，虛則胸中痛，大腹小腹痛，清厥意不樂，取其經，少陰太陽血者。 For patients with liver disease, the abdomen is distended and the legs are swollen; they pant and feel heavy, have difficulty sleeping due to night sweats and a dislike of wind; when deficient, there is pain in the center of the chest; both the large intestine and small intestine hurt, the clear yang fails to delight the mind, regulate their qi and take their meridians, nourish the yin of the spleen and stomach.

    13. 肝色青，宜食甘，粳米、牛肉、棗、葵皆甘。心色赤，宜食酸，小豆、犬肉、李、韭皆酸。肺色白，宜食苦，麥、羊肉、杏、薤皆苦。脾色黃，宜食鹹，大豆、豕肉、栗、藿皆鹹。腎色黑，宜食辛，黃黍、雞肉、桃、蔥皆辛。辛散，酸收，甘緩，苦堅，鹹耎。毒藥攻邪，五穀為養，五果為助，五畜為益，五菜為充，氣味合而服之，以補精益氣。此五者。有辛酸甘苦鹹，各有所利，或散或收，或緩或急，或堅或耎，四時五藏，病隨五味所宜也。 The colors of the organs indicate what flavors one should consume to maintain balance.

    Liver color: Green, eat sweet foods such as glutinous rice, beef, jujubes, and chrysanthemum, all of which are sweet. Heart color: Red, eat sour foods like soybeans, dog meat, loquats, and chives, all of which are sour. Lungs color: White, eat bitter foods such as barley, mutton, apricot kernels, and licorice, all of which are bitter. Spleen color: Yellow, eat salty foods like soybeans, pork, chestnuts, and astragalus, all of which are salty. Kidneys color: Black, eat spicy foods such as millet, chicken meat, peaches, and garlic, all of which are spicy.

    The properties of these flavors are dispersing, gathering, calming, strengthening, and clearing. Toxic drugs attack evil; grains nourish; fruits aid; animals benefit; vegetables fill; combining the proper tastes in one's diet will help to replenish essence and vital energy.

    These five elements have their specific properties, which are beneficial in different ways. They can be dispersing or gathering, calming or urgent, firm or purgative, according to the seasons and the organs involved. Disease can be treated according to these flavors.
