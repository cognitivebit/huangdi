    大惑論

    1. 黃帝問於歧伯曰：余嘗上於清冷之台，中階而顧，匍匐而前，則惑。余私異之，竊內怪之，獨瞑獨視，安心定氣，久而不解。獨博獨眩，披髮長跪，俛而視之，後久之不已也。卒然自上，何氣使然？歧伯對曰：五藏六府之精氣，皆上注于目而為之精。精之窠為眼，骨之精為瞳子，筋之精為黑眼，血之精為絡其窠，氣之精為白眼，肌肉之精為約束，裹擷筋骨血氣之精，而與脈并為系。上屬於腦，後出於項中。故邪中於項，因逢其身之虛，其入深，則隨眼系以入于腦。入于腦則腦轉，腦轉則引目系急。目系急則目眩以轉矣。邪其精，其精所中不相比也，則精散。精散則視歧，視歧見兩物。目者，五藏六府之精也，營衛魂魄之所常營也，神氣之所生也。故神勞則魂魄散，志意亂。是故瞳子黑眼法於陰，白眼赤脈法於陽也。故陰陽合傳而精明也。目者，心使也。心者，神之舍也，故神精亂而不轉。卒然見非常處精神魂魄，散不相得，故曰惑也。 Yellow Emperor asked Guan Yu: I have once ascended a high and cool platform, stood on the middle step, looked in front and staggered, feeling dizzy. I privately and secretly observed it, closed my eyes and gazed steadily, calmly and peacefully for a long time without understanding. I was alone, staring and looking at it for a long time that did not stop. Suddenly, I came down from above, what qi made this happen? Guan Yu answered: The essence of the five viscera and six bowels is all sent up to the eyes to nourish them. The nest of essence is the eyes, the essence of bones is the pupils, the essence of sinews is the black eyes, the essence of blood is the cords of the eyes, the essence of qi is the white eyes, the essence of muscle and tendons is the binding, and the essence of arteries wraps around the bones, blood, qi, and essence. The superior resides in the brain, and comes out from the neck. Therefore, pathogenic factors are present in the neck, and by encountering its own emptiness, it enters deeply, then the eyes become bound to enter the brain. Entering the brain causes the brain to turn, turning the brain leads to tightening the eye bindings. The eye bindings become dizzy and turn, resulting in dizziness. If the pathogenic factor's essence is not harmonious within itself, then the essence scatters. Scattered essence leads to double vision, and seeing two objects. The eyes are the essence of the five viscera and six bowels, the dwelling place of defending the soul and spirit, and the source of qi. Therefore, when the mind is exhausted, the soul and spirit scatter and intentions become chaotic. This is why black pupils and red pulse methods are used in yin and yang, so when yin and yang merge, there is clarity. The eyes are the heart's use. The heart is the palace of the spirit, so when the qi and essence are disordered, it does not turn, and that is why it is called being dizzy.

    2. 黃帝曰：余疑其然。余每之東苑，未曾不惑，去之則復，余唯獨為東苑勞神乎？何其異也？歧伯曰：不然也。心有所喜，神有所惡，卒然相惑，則精氣亂，視誤，故惑，神移乃復。是故間者為迷，甚者為惑。 Yellow Emperor said: I suspect it is so. I have always been puzzled by the Eastern Yard, not understanding it well; if I remove it, it returns; am I the only one who labors for the Eastern Yard? How strange that would be! Grand Preceptor replied: It is not so. There are things one likes and things one dislikes in one's heart, and if they suddenly clash, one's vital energy becomes confused, and one sees mistakes and therefore becomes puzzled; when the god moves, it returns. Therefore, those who are confused are lost, and those who are more so are even more puzzled.

    3. 黃帝曰：人之善忘者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：上氣不足，下氣有餘，腸胃實而心肺虛。虛則營衛留於下，久之不以時上，故善忘也。 Yellow Emperor said: Why is it that people who are good at forgetting have such qi? Duke of Zhou said: When there is insufficient qi above and excess qi below, the stomach and intestines are full while the heart and lungs are empty. The emptiness causes the defensive qi to remain below, not ascending in time, thus leading to good forgetting.

    4. 黃帝曰：人之善飢而不嗜食者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：精氣并於脾，熱氣留於胃，胃熱則消穀，穀消故善飢。胃氣逆上，則胃脘寒，故不嗜食也。 Yellow Emperor said: What kind of energy makes a person who is good at hunger but does not like to eat? Master Qi Bo replied: The vital energy converges in the spleen, and the heat energy remains in the stomach. When the stomach heat dissipates the grain, the grain disappears and one becomes good at hunger. The stomach energy flows upward, then the stomach area is cold, which is why one does not like to eat.

    5. 黃帝曰：病而不得臥者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：衛氣不得入于陰，常留於陽。留於陽則陽氣滿，陽氣滿則陽蹻盛，不得入于陰則陰氣虛，故目不瞑矣。 The Yellow Emperor said: What causes a person to be unable to rest when they are sick? The Duke of Shou replied: The vital energy does not enter the yin, and always remains in the yang. When it remains in the yang, the yang vital energy is filled, and when it is filled, the yang flourishes. Since it does not enter the yin, the yin vital energy becomes weak, which is why the eyes do not close.

    6. 黃帝曰：病目而不得視者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：衛氣留於陰，不得行於陽，留於陰則陰氣盛，陰氣盛則陰蹻滿，不得入于陽則陽氣虛，故目閉也。 Yellow Emperor said: If a person is sick but cannot see, what Qi causes this? Qi Bo replied: The Wei Qi resides in the Yin and does not move to the Yang. Residing in the Yin results in an abundance of Yin Qi. An abundance of Yin Qi leads to the Yin orifices being filled, and if it cannot enter the Yang, then the Yang Qi will be weakened. Thus, the eyes are closed.

    7. 黃帝曰：人之多臥者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：此人腸胃大而皮膚濕，而分肉不解焉。腸胃大則衛氣留久；皮膚濕則分肉不解，其行遲。夫衛氣者，晝日常行於陽，夜行於陰，故陽氣盡則臥，陰氣盡則寤。故腸胃大，則衛氣行留久；皮膚濕，分肉不解，則行遲。留於陰也久，其氣不清，則欲瞑，故多臥矣。其腸胃小，皮膚滑以緩，分肉解利，衛氣之留於陽也久，故少瞑焉。 The Yellow Emperor said: Why do so many people sleep? Qi Bo replied: This person's stomach and intestines are large, and their skin is moist. They cannot differentiate the muscles. The large intestine and stomach cause the qi to linger for a long time; the moist skin causes the muscles not to differentiate, resulting in sluggishness. Qi that circulates in the daytime through Yang and at night through Yin will cease when the Yang qi is exhausted and sleep will come, and when the Yin qi is exhausted, one will awaken. Thus, when the intestines and stomach are large, the qi lingers for a long time; moist skin causes the muscles not to differentiate, resulting in sluggishness. When this lingers in the Yin for a long time, the qi does not become clear, causing one to be drowsy, thus many people sleep. People with smaller intestines and stomachs, smooth skin that moderates, and muscles that differentiate clearly, the qi that lingers in Yang for a long time will cause them to be less drowsy.

    8. 黃帝曰：其非常經也，卒然多臥者，何氣使然？歧伯曰：邪氣留於上焦，上焦閉而不通，已食若飲湯，衛氣留久於陰而不行，故卒然多臥焉。 Yellow Emperor said: This is not a serious illness, but those who sleep frequently, what Qi allows this? Kuang Bo said: Evil Qi accumulates in the upper abdomen (Dan Zhong), the upper abdomen closes and does not communicate, already eating like drinking soup, defensive Qi accumulates for a long time in Yin and does not disperse, therefore sleep frequently.

    9. 黃帝曰：善。治此諸邪，奈何？歧伯曰：先其藏府，誅其小過，後調其氣，盛者寫之，虛者補之，必先明知其形志之苦樂，定乃取之。 The Yellow Emperor said, Good. How should one regulate this perverseness? The Duke of Zhou replied: First, eliminate its root cause, exterminate its minor vices, then adjust its energy. For those who excel, record their achievements; for those who are deficient, supplement their deficiencies. One must first clearly understand its form and nature, its bitterness and pleasure, before one can truly take it.
