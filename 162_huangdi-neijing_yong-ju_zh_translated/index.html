    癰疽

    1. 黃帝曰：余聞腸胃受穀，上焦出氣，以溫分肉，而養骨節，通腠理。中焦出氣如露，上注谿谷，而滲孫脈，津液和調，變化而赤為血。血和則孫脈先滿溢，乃注于絡脈，皆盈，乃注于經脈，陰陽已張，因息乃行。行有經紀，周有道理，與天合同，不得休止。切而調之，從虛去實，寫則不足，疾則氣減，留則先後。從實去虛，補則有餘，血氣已調，形氣乃持。余已知血氣之平與不平，未知癰疽之所從生，成敗之時，死生之期，有遠近，何以度之，可得聞乎？ Yellow Emperor said: I have heard that the intestines and stomach receive grain, the upper cavity expels gas, warming to nourish flesh, strengthening bones and joints, and regulating sinews. The middle cavity expels gas like dew, rising to the valleys, nourishing the mother veins, harmonizing body fluids, transforming into blood, and becoming red. When the blood is in harmony, the mother veins are filled first and then distributed to the nutritive veins, yin and yang are balanced, and breathing can proceed. There are records of this process, with principles that align with heaven, and cannot be neglected.

    Adjusting this process requires starting with emptiness and moving towards fullness. Writing too little will not suffice; acting too hastily may deplete qi. If one lingers, there will be a sequence of deficiencies. Conversely, starting from fullness and eliminating emptiness leads to having more than enough. When blood and qi are balanced, the body's energy can be maintained.

    I know how to balance blood and qi, but I do not know where the origin of boils and carbuncles lies, nor when success or failure, life or death will occur. Are there any teachings that can shed light on this?

    1. 歧伯曰：經脈留行不止，與天同度，與地合紀。故天宿失度，日月薄蝕；地經失紀，水道流溢，草萓不成，五穀不殖；徑路不通，民不往來，巷聚邑居，則別離異處。血氣猶然，請言其故。夫血脈營衛，周流不休，上應星宿，下應經數。寒邪客於經絡之中，則血泣，血泣則不通，不通則衛氣歸之，不得復反，故癰腫。寒氣化為熱，熱勝則腐肉，肉腐則為膿。膿不寫則爛筋，筋爛則傷骨，骨傷則髓消，不當骨空，不得泄寫，血枯空虛，則筋骨肌肉不相榮，經脈敗漏，熏於五藏，藏傷故死矣。 Obi said: The meridians and vessels [of the body] remain unceasing, harmonizing with heaven and earth. When the heavens are out of order, the sun and moon become faint; when the earth loses its records, rivers overflow, grass does not thrive, and crops do not sprout; roads become inaccessible, and people cannot come and go, lanes becoming densely populated areas and cities. Thus, different places become alienated. Regarding blood and qi, please explain the cause. For the blood vessels nurture and defend [the body], circulating without rest, going up to correspond with stars and constellations, and going down to correspond with meridians and numbers. Cold pathogens invade the channels of the meridians, causing the blood to weep, which in turn does not flow, and so the qi energy returns to itself, unable to reverse [the situation], thus leading to swelling and tumors. Cold turns into heat, and heat, if it prevails, rots the flesh. Rotting flesh leads to ulcers. If ulcers are not treated, tendons become weakened and damage bones, damaging bone marrow, which does not flow into the empty spaces, causing blood to dry up, leading to emptiness in muscles, sinews, and flesh, and the meridians and vessels cannot complement each other, causing the five organs to be scorched, leading to injury and resulting in death.

    2. 黃帝曰：願盡聞癰疽之形，與忌曰名。歧伯曰：癰發於嗌中，名曰猛疽。猛疽不治，化為膿，膿不寫，塞咽，半日死。其化為膿者，寫則合豕膏，冷食，三日而已。 Yellow Emperor said: I wish to hear about the appearance of carbuncles and their names. Duke of Shao said: Carbuncles appear in the groin area and are called malignant boils. Malignant boils that cannot be treated turn into abscesses, untreated abscesses can obstruct the throat, leading to death within half a day. For those who have turned into abscesses, if treated, apply pig fat ointment, eat cold food, and they will recover in three days

    3. 發於頸，名曰夭疽。其癰大以赤黑，不急治，則熱氣下入淵腋，前傷任脈，內熏肝肺。熏肝肺，十餘日而死矣。 Originating at the neck, it is called dantian. Its swelling is large and red-black in color. If not treated promptly, the heat will descend into the armpit, causing damage to the conception vessel, and internally scorching the liver and lungs. Scorching the liver and lungs, one will die within twenty days.

    4. 陽留大發，消腦留項，名曰腦爍。其色不樂，項痛而如刺以鍼。煩心者，死不可治。 Yangliu Dafa, Xiao Nianli Qi, Mingyao Nao Ma. QixSE color bule, Jiacheng yiyanxiao. Fannaxin Zhe, Sisuo Ganqi.

    5. 發於肩及臑，名曰疵癰。其狀赤黑，急治之，此令人汗出至足，不害五藏。癰發四五日，逞焫之。 Located on the shoulders and chest, it is called a blemish or rash. Its appearance is red and black, and if treated promptly, this can cause excessive sweating. However, it does not harm the internal organs. A rash appears within four to five days and eventually disappears.

    6. 發於腋下赤堅者，名曰米疽。治之以砭石，欲細而長，疏砭之，塗以豕膏，六日已，勿裹之。其癰堅而不潰者，為馬刀挾癭，急治之。 If there is a hard, reddish nodule under the arm, it is called miliary tuberculosis. Treat it with chalkstone, let it be detailed and long-lasting, thinly apply chalkstone, after six days do not wrap it up. If the swelling becomes hard but does not soften, use a horse knife to squeeze the swelling and treat it urgently.

    7. 發於胸，名曰井疽。其狀如大豆，三四日起，不早治，下入腹，不治，七日死矣。 Arising from the chest, it is called well-boil. Its appearance resembles that of a large bean. It emerges within three to four days and if not treated promptly, it descends into the abdomen and remains untreated, leading to death within seven days.

    8. 發於膺，名曰甘疽。色青，其狀如穀實𦸈𧁾，常苦寒熱，急治之，去其寒熱，十歲死，死後出膿。 From the lung, it is called gan3qi2. It is green in color and resembles a grain swelling up, always bitterly cold and hot. If treated urgently, it removes the cold and heat, at ten years old one dies, after death there is pus.

    9. 發於脅，名曰敗疵。敗疵者，女子之病也，灸之，其病大癰膿，治之，其中乃有生肉，大如赤小豆，坐陵翹草根各一升，以水一斗六升煮之，竭為取三升，則強飲厚衣，坐於釜上，令汗出至足已。 Published in The Cracks, it is called Bai Ye. Bai Ye is a woman's illness. When treated with cupping, the lesion becomes large and swollen. After treatment, there will be flesh inside the lesion, as big as a red small bean. Take one liter of the roots of Ling Xiang Cao and one liter of Jiao Cao, boil them in six liters of water, strain it to get three liters. Have the patient sit above this pot, making him sweat profusely until his feet are wet.

    10. 發於股脛，名曰股脛疽。其狀不甚變，而癰膿搏骨，不急治，三十日死矣。 Located at the heel, called heel abscess. Its appearance does not change much, and the swelling pulsates against the bone. If not treated promptly, it will result in death within thirty days.

    11. 發於尻，名曰銳疽。其狀赤堅大，急治之，不治，三十日死矣。 Located on the neck, it is called Sharp Carbuncle. Its appearance is red and hard, large in size, urgent treatment is necessary; if not treated, one will die within thirty days.

    12. 發於股陰，名曰赤施。不急治，六十日死。在兩股之內，不治，十日而當死。 Located in the scrotum, it is called red shelf. If not treated promptly, death occurs within sixty days. If located inside the scrotum and not treated, death will occur within ten days.     13. 發於膝，名曰疵癰。其狀大，癰色不變，寒熱，如堅石，勿石，石之者死，須其柔，乃石之者，生。 Arising from the knee, it is called Yuan Chou. Its shape is large, the color of the Yuan Chou does not change, cold and hot like a stone, not like a stone, those who are like stones will die, there must be something soft about them, and they will survive.

    14. 諸癰疽之發於節而相應者，不可治也。發於陽者，百日死；發於陰者，三十日死。 The eruptions occurring at the joints and corresponding in number [with each other] cannot be treated. If it occurs in the Yang aspect, one will die within a hundred days; if it occurs in the Yin aspect, one will die within thirty days.

    15. 發於脛，名曰兔嚙，其狀赤至骨，急治之，不治害人也。 It is located on the ankle and is called rabbit's tooth. Its color is bright red to the bone. It must be treated urgently, or it will harm people.

    16. 發於內踝，名曰走緩。其狀癰也，色不變，數石其輸，而止其寒熱，不死。 Originating from Nanjing, it is called Walking Calm. Its appearance is abnormal, the color does not change, it counts how many stones it passes, and stops its cold and heat, not dying.

    17. 發於足上下，名曰四淫。其狀大癰，急治之，百日死。 Located on both the feet, it is called the Four Licentiousness. Its appearance is large and ugly, and if treated urgently, one will die within a hundred days

    18. 發於足傍，名曰厲癰。其狀不大，初如小指，發，急治之，去其黑者；不消輒益，不治，百日死。 Located on the foot, it is called Xi Liang. Its appearance is not large, initially resembling a small finger, and if treated immediately after the black part appears, it will be cured; otherwise, if left untreated for a hundred days, it will result in death.

    19. 發於足趾，名脫癰。其狀赤黑，死不治；不赤黑，不死。不衰，急斬之，不則死矣。 Appearing on the toenails, it is called a fungus. Its condition may be red or black and untreatable; if not red or black, it is not fatal. If it does not wither away, cut it out immediately, for otherwise, death will ensue.

    20. 黃帝曰：夫子言癰疽，何以別之？歧伯曰：營衛稽留於經脈之中，則血泣而不行，不行則衛氣從之而不通，壅遏而不得行，故熱。大熱不止，熱勝，則肉腐，肉腐則為膿。然不能陷，骨髓不為燋枯，五藏不為傷，故命曰癰。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the distinction between boils and carbuncles? Zhi Bó replied: The wei qi accumulates in the channels of the meridians, causing the blood to stagnate and not circulate. This leads to a blockage, preventing it from flowing freely, resulting in heat. If the heat persists and becomes dominant, the flesh begins to decay, leading to abscesses. However, one cannot become critically ill; the bone marrow does not dry up nor do the internal organs get damaged, so it is called a boil.

    21. 黃帝曰：何謂疽？歧伯曰：熱氣淳盛，下陷肌膚，筋髓枯，內連五藏，血氣竭，當其癰下，筋骨良肉皆無餘，故命曰疽。疽者，上之皮夭以堅，上如牛領之皮。癰者，其皮上薄以澤。此其候也。 Yellow Emperor said: What is a boil? Bóu said: The vital energy is abundant and overflowing, sinking into the muscles and skin, the tendons and marrow are dry, internally connected to the five organs, blood and vital energy are exhausted, at the point where the boil emerges, all the tendons, bones, and flesh have no remaining strength, so it is called a boil. In a boil, the skin is tightened and firm like a cow's neck, while in a furuncle (癰), the skin is thin and moisturized. This is its stage.
