    骨度

    1. 黃帝問於伯高曰：脈度言經脈之長短，何以立之？伯高曰：先度其骨節之大小、廣狹、長短，而脈度定矣。 Yellow Emperor asked Bo Gao: How can one determine the length, shortness, and other aspects of a pulse based on the classics? Bo Gao replied: First, determine the size, width, length, and shortness of the bones and joints, and the pulse measurement will be established.

    2. 黃帝曰：願聞眾人之度。人長七尺五寸者，其骨節之大小長短各幾何？伯高曰：頭之大骨圍，二尺六寸，胸圍四尺五寸。腰圍四尺二寸。髮所覆者顱至項，尺二寸。髮以下至頤，長一尺，君子終折。 Yellow Emperor said: I want to hear how the people measure their height. A person's height is seven feet and five inches, how big or small are the bones and joints? Bo Gao said: The circumference of the skull is two feet six inches, the chest circumference is four feet five inches. The waist circumference is four feet two inches. From the forehead to the nape, it is two inches in length. The hair below reaches the chin, which is one foot long, and a gentleman would cut it off.

    3. 結喉以下至缺盆中，長四寸。缺盆以下至𩩲𩨗，長九寸，過則肺大，不滿則肺小。𩩲𩨗以下至天樞，長八寸，過則胃大，不及則胃小。天樞以下至橫骨，長六寸半，過則迴腸廣長，不滿則狹短。橫骨，長六寸半。橫骨上廉以下至內輔之上廉，長一尺八寸。內輔之上廉以下至下廉，長三寸半。內輔下廉，下至內踝，長一尺三寸。內踝以下至地，長三寸。膝膕以下至附屬，長一尺六寸。附屬以下至地，長三寸。故骨圍大則太過，小則不及。 From the larynx below to the cavity of want, it measures four inches. From the cavity of want below to the point of the sternum, it measures nine inches, exceeding it becomes large, falling short it becomes small. At the point of the sternum below to the pivot, it measures eight inches, exceeding it becomes large, not reaching it becomes small. At the pivot below to the transverse process, it measures six and a half inches, exceeding it becomes wide and long, falling short it becomes narrow and short. The transverse processes measure six and a half inches. From the upper transverse process to the lower transverse process at the level of the pelvis, it measures one foot eight inches. From the lower transverse process to the pubic symphysis, it measures three and a half inches. Below the pubic symphysis to the ground, it measures one foot three inches. From the ground below to the knees, it measures three inches. The kneecap is below the patella, measuring one foot six inches. Below the patella to the ground, it measures three inches. Therefore, if the skeleton is too wide, it is excessive; if it is not large enough, it is insufficient.

    4. 角以下至柱骨，長一尺。行腋中不見者，長四寸。腋以下至季脅，長一尺二寸。季脅以下至髀樞，長六寸，髀樞以下至膝中，長一尺九寸。膝以下至外踝，長一尺六寸。外踝以下至京骨，長三寸。京骨以下至地，長一寸。 From the angle bone down to the pillar bone, a foot long. If it cannot be seen at the armpit, four inches. From the armpit down to the place of the elbow, a foot and two inches. From the place of the elbow down to the hip joint, six inches, from the hip joint down to the knee pit, a foot and nine inches. From the knee pit down to the outer ankle, a foot and six inches. From the outer ankle down to the capital bone, three inches. From the capital bone down to the ground, one inch.

    Translation: The length measurements of different body parts according to traditional Chinese medicine.

    1. 耳後當完骨者，廣九寸。耳前當耳門者，廣一尺三寸。兩顴之間，相去七寸。兩乳之間，廣九寸半。兩髀之間，廣六寸半。 The length of the ear lobe is 9 inches. The width of the area in front of the ear, where the earlobe is attached, is 1 foot 3 inches. The distance between the two nostrils is 7 inches. The distance between the two nipples is 9 and a half inches. The distance between the two hip bones is 6 and a half inches

    2. 足長一尺二寸，廣四寸半。肩至肘，長一尺七寸；肘至腕，長一尺二寸半。腕至中指本節，長四寸。本節至其末，長四寸半。 The following literary Chinese text has been translated to English:

    Length: one foot and two inches, width: four inches and a half. From shoulder to elbow, length is one foot and seven inches; from elbow to wrist, length is one foot and two inches and a half. From the wrist to the base of the middle finger, length is four inches. From the base of the middle finger to its end, length is four inches and a half.

    1. 項髮以下至背骨，長二寸半，膂骨以下至尾骶，二十一節，長三尺，上節長一寸四分分之一，奇分在下，故上七節至于膂骨，九寸八分分之七。此眾人骨之度也，所以立經脈之長短也。是故視其經脈之 The hairline to the spine is two and a half inches long, from the pectoral girdle down to the tailbone there are twenty-one vertebrae, with each vertebra one inch and a quarter in length. The odd one is at the bottom, so from the top seven vertebrae reach down to the pectoral girdle, measuring nine inches and three-quarters. This is the standard length of human bones, and this is why we use it to determine the length of meridians. Thus when examining the meridians in the body, one must take into account the lengths of the corresponding channels

    2. 在於身也，其見浮而堅，其見明而大者，多血，細而沉者，多氣也。 In the body, those who appear to be superficial and firm, those who appear bright and large, have more blood; those who are subtle and deep have more qi.
