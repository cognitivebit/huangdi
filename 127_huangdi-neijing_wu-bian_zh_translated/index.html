    五變

    1. 黃帝問於少俞曰：余聞百疾之始期也，必生於風雨寒暑，循毫毛而入腠理，或復還，或留止，或為風腫汗出，或為消癉，或為寒熱，或為留痺，或為積聚。奇邪淫溢，不可勝數，願聞其故。夫同時得病，或病此，或病彼，意者天之為人生風乎，何其異也？少俞曰：夫天之生風者，非以私百姓也，其行公平正直，犯者得之，避者得無殆，非求人而人自犯之。 Emperor Yang asked Shao Yong: I have heard that the origin of the hundred diseases is generated by exposure to wind, rain, cold, and heat. They may come and go, linger or stop, manifest as wind-swelling and sweating, or as consumption, or as chills and fever, or as paralysis, or as accumulation. The strange and perverse nature of this phenomenon cannot be counted; I wish to hear about its cause. As for the cases where people contract illnesses simultaneously, some may contract this illness, while others may contract another one. Is this not evidence that God bestows suffering upon humans in accordance with their actions, how strange is that? Shao Yong replied: The way in which God creates wind is not out of malice towards ordinary people; it acts impartially and justly. Those who violate it will suffer the consequences, while those who avoid it will suffer little or none, and this is not something they seek but rather something that happens due to their own actions.

    2. 黃帝曰：一時遇風，同時得病，其病各異，願聞其故。少俞曰：善乎其問！請論以比匠人。匠人磨斧斤，礪刀削斷材木。木之陰陽，尚有堅脆，堅者不入，脆者皮弛，至其交節，而缺斤斧焉。夫一木之中，堅脆不同，堅者則剛，脆者易傷，況其材木之不同，皮之厚薄，汁之多少，而各異耶。夫木之蚤花先生葉者，遇春霜烈風，則花落而葉萎；久曝大旱，則脆木薄皮者，枝條汁少而葉萎；久陰淫雨，則薄皮多汁者，皮漉而淺；卒風暴起，則剛脆之木，枝折杌傷；秋霜疾風，則剛脆之木，根搖而葉落。凡此五者，各有所傷，況於人乎！ Yellow Emperor said: At one time, encountering wind and at the same time contracting illness, with various symptoms, I wish to hear of their causes. Shao Yong said: What a good question! Please discuss it by comparing it to a craftsman. The craftsman sharpens an axe, hones a knife, cuts and shapes wood material. The yin and yang of the wood still have hardness and brittleness; the hard one does not enter, while the brittle one is flexible, until reaching the joints where the axe is lacking. Since the hardness and brittleness of one piece of wood are not the same, the hard one is rigid, while the brittle one is easily damaged; how much more for different kinds of wood materials, the thickness of the bark, and the amount of sap, each being different? When the wood's buds sprout before the leaves open, encountering spring frost or strong wind, the flowers fall and the leaves wither. If there is a long drought, then the brittle wood and thin-skinned trees have branches with little sap and their leaves wither; if there is an excessive amount of rain, then the thin-skinned trees with more sap will peel and become shallow-rooted; suddenly encountering strong wind or storms, then the hard and brittle trees have broken branches and damaged trunks; in the autumn, when the frost and wind are rapid, then the hard and brittle trees have shaking roots and their leaves fall. All of these five cases have been harmed; how much more for people!

    3. 黃帝曰：以人應木，奈何？少俞答曰：木之所傷也，皆傷其枝。枝之剛脆而堅，未成傷也。人之有常病也，亦因其骨節皮膚腠理之不堅固者，邪之所舍也，故常為病也。 Yellow Emperor said: What if a man corresponds to wood, how could that be? Shao Yong answered: The places where the wood is injured are all on its branches. The branches are brittle and strong but not yet fully injured. In humans, there are constant diseases which are due to their bones, joints, skin, hair follicles not being firm and secure, so evil spirits have a place to lodge, hence they constantly become diseased

    4. 黃帝曰：人之善病風厥漉汗者，何以候之？少俞答曰：內不堅，腠理疏，則善病風。黃帝曰：何以候肉之不堅也？少俞答曰：膕肉不堅，而無分理。理者麤理，麤理而皮不致者，腠理疏。此言其渾然者。 Yellow Emperor said: How do you know if a person is prone to disease, fever, and chills due to sweat? Shao Yong answered: If the interior is not solid, the sinews are scattered, then one is prone to diseases such as fever. Yellow Emperor asked: What is the sign of flesh not being solid? Shao Yong answered: The muscles are soft and not separate. Separating means separating the tendons, separating the tendons so that the skin does not fit, resulting in scattered sinews. This refers to someone who is quite like this.

    5. 黃帝曰：人之善病消癉者，何以候之？少俞答曰：五藏皆柔弱者，善病消癉。黃帝曰：何以知五藏之柔弱也？少俞答曰：夫柔弱者，必有剛強，剛強多怒，柔者易傷也。黃帝曰：何以候柔弱之與剛強？少俞答曰：此人薄皮膚，而目堅固以深者，長沖直腸，其心剛，剛則多怒，怒則氣上逆，胸中蓄積，血氣逆留，髖皮充肌，血脈不行，轉而為熱，熱則消肌膚，故為消癉。此言其人暴剛而肌肉弱者也。 Yellow Emperor asked: How can one determine a person who is good at treating illnesses and preventing diseases? Shao Yong answered: Those who are skilled in treating illnesses and preventing diseases are the ones who are familiar with the Five Virtues. The Yellow Emperor asked: How do you know that the Five Virtues are soft and weak? Shao Yong answered: Softness and weakness necessarily have strength and rigidity, strength and rigidity often lead to anger, and anger causes Qi to rise and flow inversely, accumulating in the chest, causing the blood and Qi to become stagnant and blocked, leading to the thickening of skin and muscles, and the obstruction of blood vessels, resulting in fever that leads to the wasting of muscles and skin. This refers to a person who is prone to anger and has weak muscles.

    6. 黃帝曰：人之善病寒熱者，何以候之？少俞答曰：小骨弱肉者，善病寒熱。黃帝曰：何以候骨之小大，肉之堅脆，色之不一也？少俞答曰：顴骨者，骨之本也。顴大則骨大，顴小則骨小。皮膚薄而其肉無䐃，其臂懦懦然，其地色殆然，不與其天同色，污然獨異，此其候也。然後臂薄者，其髓不滿，故善病寒熱也。 Yellow Emperor said: What is the sign that a person is susceptible to cold and fever? Shao Yong replied: Those with weak bones and flesh are susceptible to cold and fever. Yellow Emperor asked: How can we determine whether someone has small or large bones, hard or soft flesh, or different skin color? Shao Yong replied: The hip bone is the basis of all bones. If the hip bone is large, then the bones are large; if the hip bone is small, then the bones are small. If the skin is thin and the flesh lacks substance, the arms are flabby and limp, the complexion is almost lost, not harmonious with heaven's colors, dirty and unique, this is the sign of their condition. Then, if the arms are weak, the marrow is insufficient, so they are susceptible to cold and fever.

    7. 黃帝曰：何以候人之善病痺者？少俞答曰：麤理而肉不堅者，善病痺。黃帝曰：痺之高下有處乎？少俞答曰：欲知其高下者，各視其部。 Yellow Emperor asked: How can one judge whether a person is good at contracting diseases or not? Shao Yong replied: Those with thin and delicate bodies but weak muscles are good at contracting diseases. Yellow Emperor asked: Is there any high or low aspect to contracting diseases? Shao Yong replied: To know whether it is high or low, one must examine its corresponding part

    8. 黃帝曰：人之善病腸中積聚者，何以候之？少俞答曰：皮膚薄而不澤，肉不堅而淖澤。如此，則腸胃惡，惡則邪氣留止，積聚乃傷脾胃之間，寒溫不次，邪氣稍至。蓄積留止，大聚乃起。 Yellow Emperor asked: What is the cause of people's susceptibility to diseases, and how can it be predicted? Shao Yong answered: The skin is thin and lacks luster, and the flesh is soft and moist. As a result, the spleen and stomach are in poor condition. This leads to the accumulation of evil qi, which can cause harm between the spleen and stomach. If the cold and heat do not subside, the evil qi will gradually gather, eventually leading to the onset of disease.

    9. 黃帝曰：余聞病形，已知之矣！願聞其時。少俞答曰：先立其年，以知其時。時高則起，時下則殆，雖不陷下，當年有沖道，其病必起，是謂因形而生病，五變之紀也。 Yellow Emperor said: I have heard about the manifestations of disease, and I know them. I wish to hear about its time. Shao Yong answered: First determine their age, in order to know its time. If it is high, it will rise; if it is low, it will perish; although it does not collapse, there was a change in the year, and its disease must arise, which is called causing the form to produce illness, and the record of the five transformations.
