    寒熱病

    1. 皮寒熱者，不可附席，毛髮焦，鼻槁臘。不得汗，取三陽之絡，以補手太陰。肌寒熱者，肌痛，毛髮焦而唇槁臘。不得汗，取三陽于下，以去其血者，補足太陰，以出其汗。 For those with chills and fever, they should not attend court sessions. The hairs on the body are brittle, and the nose cartilage is shriveled. No sweating; take the sinews of the Triple Burner, to supplement the hand-yang. For those with muscle chills and fever, there is muscle pain and the hairs are brittle while the lips are shriveled. No sweating; take the Triple Burner below to remove its blood, and supplement the yin of the foot-Taiyang, causing it to sweat.

    2. 骨寒熱者，病無所安，汗注不休。齒未槁，取其少陰于陰股之絡；齒已槁，死不治。骨厥亦然。骨痺，舉節不用而痛，汗注、煩心。取三陰之經，補之。 Those who suffer from bone cold and heat disorders have no peace from their illnesses, with perspiration unceasing. Before the teeth are loose, take the Shao Yin at the Yin Groin channel; once the teeth are loose, it is incurable. The same applies to Bone Paralysis. Aches in the bones that prevent the use of joints, accompanied by perspiration and restlessness. Take the Qi of the San Jiao channel and supplement it.

    3. 身有所傷，血出多及中風寒，若有所墮墜，四肢懈惰不收，名曰體惰。取其小腹臍下三結交。三結交者，陽明太陰也，臍下三寸關元也。厥痺者，厥氣上及腹。取陰陽之絡，視主病也，寫陽補陰經也。 If one has an injury, with blood flowing out and being affected by cold wind, if there is a fall or stumble, the limbs become limp and fail to withdraw, this is called body weakness. At the lower abdomen, below the umbilicus, are three knots intertwined. These three knots intertwined represent Yang Ming and Yin Qi. The umbilical cord is located at the center of these three knots. If there is a blockage in the channels, the vital energy ascends to the abdomen. Examining the relation between yin and yang, one can diagnose the primary cause and write the Treatment of Yang Deficiency and Yin Supplementation.

    4. 頸側之動脈人迎。人迎，足陽明也，在嬰筋之前。嬰筋之後，手陽明也，名曰扶突。次脈，足少陽脈也，名曰天牖。次脈，足太陽也，名曰天柱。腋下動脈，臂太陰也，名曰天府。 On the front of the neck, there is the Radiant Pulse. The Radiant Pulse corresponds to the Yangming channel, which lies in front of the Governing Vessel. After the Governing Vessel, there is the Taiyang channel, also known as Heaven's Pillar. Then comes the ShaoYang channel, also known as Heaven's Wall. At the armpit, there is the TaiYang channel, also known as the Celestial Pivot.

    5. 陽迎頭痛，胸滿不得息，取之人迎。暴瘖氣鞭，取扶突與舌本出血。暴襲氣蒙，耳目不明，取天牖。暴攣癎眩，足不任身，取天柱。暴痺內逆，肝肺相搏，血溢鼻口，取天府。此為天牖五部。 Sunny welcome headache, chest full and unable to breathe, the one who takes it is welcomed. Violent itchiness from gas, taking a whip, bleeding from the tongue base. Sudden attack dizziness, unstable feet, taking heaven pillar. Violent spasms leading to confusion, legs not supporting, taking heaven pavilion. Violent paralysis of internal organs, liver and lung fighting, blood overflowing from the nose and mouth, taking heaven residence. These are the five departments of the Heaven Pavilion.

    6. 臂陽明，有入頄遍齒者，名曰大迎。下齒齲，取之臂。惡寒補之，不惡寒寫之。足太陽有入頄遍齒者，名曰角孫。上齒齲，取之在鼻與頄前。方病之時，其脈盛，盛則寫之，虛則補之。一曰取之出鼻外。 Beams of the Arm (Ulnar) - Great Reception In cases where there is entering and spreading to the teeth, it is called Great Reception. When the lower teeth are involved, take from the arm. In cases where there is not an involvement of the lower teeth, do not take from the arm. Beams of the Foot (Lesser Yang) - Horny Son In cases where there is entering and spreading to the teeth, it is called Horny Son. When the upper teeth are involved, take from the nose and the area in front of the throat. In times of disease, the pulse will be full. If full, write (the needle); if empty, supplement (with moxa). Alternative: Take from outside the nostrils.

    7. 足陽明有挾鼻入于面者，名曰懸顱。屬口，對入繫目本，視有過者取之。損有餘，益不足，反者益其。足太陽有通項入于腦者，正屬目本，名曰眼系。頭目苦痛，取之在項中兩筋間。入腦乃別陰蹻、陽蹻，陰陽相交，陽入陰，陰出陽，交于目銳眥，陽氣盛則瞋目，陰氣盛則瞑目。 There is a point on the foot Yinning called Sūiyǐng, which is located near the root of the nose. Its name means suspended nostril. It belongs to the channel of the mouth and intersects with the channel of the eyes. When there are symptoms, take it. If there are remnants, increase what is insufficient and decrease what is excessive. There is a point on the foot Yangming called Tōngtícóng, which enters the brain. It belongs to the channel of the eyes and is named Eye Chain (Yǎnshē). For headache pain, take it at the level of the two muscles of the neck. Entering the brain separates Yin and Yang, and they exchange with each other. When Yang enters Yin, the pupil becomes dull; when Yin enters Yang, the pupil becomes clear.

    8. 熱厥取足太陰、少陽，皆留之；寒厥取足陽明、少陰於足，皆留之。舌縱涎下，煩悗，取足少陰。振寒洒洒鼓頷，不得汗出，腹脹煩悗，取手太陰，刺虛者，刺其去也；刺實者，刺其來也。 In the case of fever chills, take the Sinew Chrono (Taiyin) and Lesser Yang Ming (Shaoyang) points on the feet and leave them. In the case of cold chills, take the Foot Yang Ming (Jingwell) and Lesser Yin on the foot and leave them. If the tongue is dry with saliva dripping down, and there is vexation and discomfort, take the Sinew Chrono (Sanyinjiao) point on the foot. Shake off the cold, sprinkle, and pound the throat, do not let sweat come out, if there is fullness and discomfort in the abdomen, take the Hand Taiyin (Quepen) points, and pierce the empty points, that is to say, pierce them away; pierce the real points, that is to say, pierce them coming.

    9. 春取絡脈，夏取分腠，秋取氣口，冬取經輸。凡此四時，各以時為齊。絡脈治皮膚，分腠治肌肉，氣口治筋脈，經輸治骨髓。五藏，身有五部：伏兔一；腓二，腓者腨也；背三，五藏之輸四；項五。此五部有癰疽者死。 In the spring, take the luo-connecting vessels, in summer, take the sinews, in autumn, take the qi channels, and in winter, take the meridians. At each of these four seasons, one should regulate according to the time. The luo-connecting vessels treat the skin, the sinews treat the muscles, the qi channels treat the tendons and joints, and the meridians treat the bone marrow. The five organs, the body has five parts: genitals (1), bladder (2) which is also called back, back (3), four meridians of the five organs, neck (5). If any of these five parts have boils or carbuncles, they will die.

    10. 病始手臂者，先取手陽明、太陰而汗出；病始頭首者，先取項太陽而汗出；病始足脛者，先取足陽明而汗出。臂太陰可汗出，足陽明可汗出，故取陰而汗出甚者，止之于陽，取陽而汗出甚者，止之於陰。 For someone whose illness begins with the arm, first take the hand's Shao Yang and Tai Yin channels and make them sweat. For someone whose illness begins with the head, first take the Jiao Yang channel and make it sweat. For someone whose illness begins with the leg, first take the Zusanli (ST-36) point of the Yang Ming channel and make it sweat. The Tai Yin channel can make one sweat from the hand, and the Yang Ming channel can make one sweat from the foot, so if sweating becomes excessive when taking the Shu points of the Yin channels, stop at the Yang levels, and if sweating becomes excessive when taking the Shu points of the Yang channels, stop at the Yin levels.

    11. 凡刺之害，中而不去則精泄；不中而去則致氣。精泄則病甚而慪，致氣則生為癰疽也。 All injuries that do not go away when pierced will cause the vital essence to be depleted; if it is not pierced correctly, it will lead to a buildup of qi. Depletion of vital essence can result in severe illness and emotional distress, while a buildup of qi can lead to the development of boils or abscesses
